SUBCHAPTER 1: OVERVIEW CONCEPTS

9.01 GENERAL

a. Overview. The Work Study allowance program offers an additional payment to students in return for performing services in Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) related activities. As a supplemental educational benefit, VA Work Study allowance can often serve as that special increment that makes a program of education or training feasible. In this way, Work Study benefits may provide the educationally disadvantaged or unemployed student with an opportunity, the value of which can greatly exceed the actual monetary amount of the allowance itself.

b. Program Administration. The Work Study program has been centralized to the VA Regional Processing Office (RPO) in Muskogee, Oklahoma. VA personnel administering the Work Study program will target information about Work Study availability to those students who may be eligible for the program. During outreach efforts, Work Study benefits should be stressed as part of an education package; a package which combines the learning experience with part-time employment. VA should not limit outreach efforts to students currently enrolled in school, but include all students who are potential beneficiaries, especially those who seem to be experiencing persistent readjustment difficulties.

c. Work Study Management System. Education Service uses a web-based application to support the Work Study program. This application is called the Work Study Management System (WSMS). It has the capabilities for users to access, search, and retrieve online information through a web browser, and to modify and store the data into a database. Currently WSMS can handle data information on Work Study applications for Veterans and their dependents, including the functionalities for collecting, processing, maintaining, managing, and sharing data information pertaining to the Work Study program (such as student information, contracts, time records, Work sites, supervisors, etc.). It has the basic capability for the Work Study time and payment calculations, the printing of awards, contracts, letters, and labels, and the various lookup capabilities.  It also provides the utilities to serve in maintenance of data information related to work sites, supervisors, and schools.

 d. Work Study Forms. The Work Study program uses these forms:

1. VA Form 22-8690, Time Record (Work Study Program).

2. VA Form 22-8691, Application for Work Study Allowance.

3. VA Form 22-8691d, Application for Work Study Allowance to Repay an Education Debt (Under 38 U.S.C. 3485).

4. VA Form 22-8692, Student Work Study Agreement (Student Services).

5. VA Form 22-8692a, Extended Student Work Study Agreement.

6. VA Form 22-8692b, Work Study Agreement (Student Services).

7. VA Form 22-8692d, Work Study Agreement (Repayment of Education Debt).

8. VA Form 22-10270, VA Lump Sum Participation Agreement Form.

9. VA Form 22-10219, VA Work Study Work Site Application.

NOTE: The WSMS can generate the appropriate forms or form letters.

9.02 WORK STUDY DUTIES AND SUPERVISION INFORMATION

“Qualifying Work Study activities” include but not limited to:

  • Duties directly related to VA
  • Duties under the direct supervision of a VA employee
  • Any activity of a state Veterans’ agency related to providing benefits assistance to Veterans under VA law
  • A position working in the Center of Excellence for Veteran Claimant Success, as established under Part T, Title VIII of the Higher Education Act of 1965
  • A position working in a cooperative program administered by the VA and an Institution of Higher Learning (IHL)
  • Any other Veteran-related position in an IHL.
  • Driving can be approved as a qualifying activity of a Work Study position at a VA facility.
  • Mentoring and/or tutoring duties are allowable with a VetSuccess on Campus (VSOC) program
  • Assisting with dissemination of general information regarding Veteran benefits and/or services
  • Aiding Veteran students with general inquiries about Veteran benefits via phone, email, or in person
  • Maintain and organizing Veteran related files
  • Assisting SAAs with outreach to Veterans
  • Providing hospital, domiciliary, or medical treatment to Veterans in State homes, when VA pays an allowance to the state for such care
  • Any activity relating to the administration of a national cemetery or a State Veteran’s cemetery.

a. Outreach Activities.  Given that educational institutions are non-VA organizations, VA outreach activities performed at an educational institution must conform to the non-VA organization requirements. Work Study students performing VA outreach activities may be supervised by an official of the educational institution but only in a cooperative supervisory effort with a VA employee who is controlling the work activities.

(1) Outreach Limited.  "Outreach" refers to the outreach services authorized by 38 USC, Chapter 63, as amended or added by Public Law (PL) 109-233, Section 402(a).  Except when performed to assist State Approving Agency (SAA) employees in carrying out their outreach duties, outreach activities must be performed as part of a VA outreach services program. If approved by the Secretary, VA outreach activities may include other governmental programs, such as other federal, state, or local programs beneficial to Veterans and eligible dependents. Outreach activities do not include administrative duties or the processing of paperwork.

Outreach activities by Work Study students are limited to those activities that are an element of VA’s outreach services program. Non-VA worksites can only be approved if the Work Study student is engaged in outreach activities as part of a plan organized by VA to fulfill its commitment for outreach to Veterans. A Work Study student may not work for a non-VA organization just because that organization is engaged in rendering services to Veterans and eligible dependents. Effective August 1, 2021, P.L. 116-315, Section 1006 now allows for approved outreach services to be performed in Congressional facilities.

NOTE:  Work Study Coordinators (WSCs) should pay particular attention to proposed Work Study positions at military bases that support the Transition Assistance Program (TAP) or Disabled Transition Assistance Program (DTAP) briefing. The Work Study student may assist with any aspect of the VA portion of these briefings. The Work Study student may assist with the briefings for non-VA programs such as the TRICARE medical program since it is a benefit afforded the Veteran. However, the Work Study student may not assist with such duties as presentations on how to write resumes or how to prepare for civilian life.

(2) Non-VA Facilities

(a)  The WSC should be aware of non-VA facilities such as employment services offices, Department of Defense (DoD) facilities, and other community organizations that may present logical points for VA outreach Work Study assignments.

(b)  It is essential that Work Study duties at non-VA work sites be strictly limited to outreach duties. Work Study students may provide information about VA programs and non-VA governmental programs. They should not be performing the services associated with any non-VA program and should not be performing administrative duties or processing paperwork for the non-VA facility. For example, an allowable duty would be to distribute information about job programs, whereas assisting a Veteran with locating a job would not be an allowable duty.

NOTE:  Work Study students directly supervised by VA employees of Regional Offices (ROs) may perform outreach activities at non-VA facilities.

(3) Supervision Required 

 (a)  Outreach activities provided by Work Study students at non-VA organizations, such as a military facility, must be under the "supervision" of a VA employee (unless those duties are performed to assist SAA employees in carrying out their outreach duties). 

(4) RPO Responsibilities.  The Muskogee RPO should:

  • Request, on a regular basis, each educational institution using the Work Study program provide an estimate of the total number of Work Study hours they need during the upcoming school year. A notice should be included explaining applications may be denied for the Work Study benefit if contract hours requested on individual claims exceeds the number of approved hours for the institution.
  • Routinely compare the Work Study hours authorized for individual contracts at an institution with the total Work Study hours approved for that institution.
  • Deny Work Study applications requesting work at an educational institution when, by approving those contracted hours, the total hours of all contracts would exceed the total number of estimated hours approved for that institution per school year. When denying individual claims from students, appellate rights should be included.
  • Use the supervision procedure required for Work Study students performing outreach functions at non-VA facilities as previously stated. 
  • Review new Work Study position descriptions to ensure they include the limitation on duties which may be performed at educational institutions and the limitation on the outreach function.

NOTE:  If outreach duties are included in a Work Study position at an educational institution, VA supervision of Work Study students is required. VA compliance surveys cannot be used as a means of direct supervision due to the infrequency of such surveys. Compliance surveys may be used only as a secondary means of verifying that Work Study students are properly utilized for approved activities. 

b. Educational Institutions.  Work Study students at educational institutions may assist with the preparation and processing of necessary papers and other documents.  All duties in Work Study position descriptions must deal directly with assisting in the processing of VA paper work or the gathering of information needed to fulfill VA reporting requirements. Examples of processing VA paper work include assisting with the completion of education applications, enrollment certifications, and notices of change in enrollment status or doing monthly check-ins whereby students routinely verify their course load information with the appropriate school official. A school official (usually the certifying official) must supervise these students.

c. Hospital and Domiciliary Care. Work Study students may assist in providing hospital and domiciliary care and medical treatment under Chapter 17 of Title 38. This includes such care provided to Veterans in a state home if the state home receives any payment from VA for such care. A hospital or domiciliary employee, or a state home employee must supervise these students. These positions were authorized beginning June 30, 2013

d. National Cemetery or State Veteran’s Cemetery.  Work Study students may assist with the administration of National Cemeteries or state Veteran's Cemeteries.

e. Other VA Activity.  Work Study students may assist with any other VA activity as the Secretary of the VA determines appropriate. This means that Work Study students can be used anywhere within VA such as*:

  • Regional Offices;
  • Hospitals;
  • Outpatient Centers;
  • Data Processing Centers;
  • Central Office;
  • The Board of Veterans Appeals; 

*This list is not inclusive.

NOTE: As stated earlier, driving can be approved as a qualifying activity of a Work Study position at a VA facility.  However, it is never appropriate to allow a Work Study student to drive a Veteran or other individual to a designated location (such as a VA hospital) as part of their duties.

f. Chapter 1606 Administration.  In the case of an individual who is receiving educational assistance under chapter 1606, Work Study students may assist with an activity relating to the administration of this chapter (for which he or she is receiving benefits) at DoD, Department of Homeland Security (DHS), or National Guard facilities. Appropriate tasks include completing Notice of Basic Eligibility (NOBE), also known as DD Form 2384-1, or reporting information through channels when individuals make a six-year commitment or in the event someone fails to carry out his or her commitment. A DoD, DHS, or National Guard official must supervise these students.

g. Student Duties.  Depending on which facility the Work Study student is assigned, the duties may vary.

(1) General.  Work Study students may be assigned a variety of duties related to VA activities. These assignments should be consistent with, and capitalize upon, a student’s area of study so the services he or she performs become a practical extension of their education. Training for Work Study assignments should not exceed more than 20 percent of the authorized Work Study hours for the specified contract period. As an operating policy, the use of Work Study students should supplement the regular work force. Regular employees should not be displaced because of Work Study students.

(2) At VA Facilities.  In general, view any VA facility as having the potential to offer appropriate VA Work Study employment. Work Study students may be assigned to suitable activities at any VA facility. VA facilities do not have any limitations as to duties for Work Study students. 

NOTE: A student who is receiving VA education benefits for an internship at a VA facility cannot receive Work Study benefits for the same work/position. Based on 38 U.S.C. 3485(d), an individual who is performing Work Study will only be considered a United States employee for the purposes of benefits of Chapter 81 of Title 5 (Compensation for Work Injuries), and not for the purposes of laws administered by the Office of Personnel Management (OPM). Since the intern (a VA employee) falls under the laws administered by OPM, this is another reason why he/she would not qualify for Work Study.

(3) At DoD Facilities.  DoD facilities have limitations as to duties for Work Study students. If the WSC assigns a student receiving educational assistance under title 10, U.S.C., chapter 1606 to work at a DoD facility, that student can be authorized to perform activities relating to the administration of chapter 1606 benefits. Otherwise, a DoD facility can only use Work Study students for disseminating information on VA benefits and services and assisting individuals to obtain these benefits. Duties may also include, to the extent feasible, providing information on other governmental programs determined to be beneficial to Veterans.

(4) At Other Non-VA Facilities. Other non-VA facilities have limitations as to duties for Work Study students. A non-VA facility can only use Work Study students for disseminating information on VA benefits and services and assisting individuals in obtaining these benefits. The non-VA facility cannot use the Work Study students to do paper work. (Work Study students assigned to VA facilities or educational institutions may do paper work.) Duties may also include, to the extent feasible, providing information on other governmental programs determined to be beneficial to Veterans. The WSC should be aware of non-VA facilities such as Employment Services Offices, DoD facilities, and other community organizations which may present logical points for VA outreach Work Study assignments. 

h. VA Work Study Positions in Foreign Countries (Overseas Locations).  Approval of VA Work Study positions in foreign countries will be done by the Muskogee RPO and is subject to the following: 

  • Work Study Position Assisting VA Employee Administering VA Program. VA has very few employees who administer VA programs overseas. These employees are usually at specific sites, such as at military installations or at specific overseas locations. A VA Work Study position directly related to assisting a VA employee in the administration of VA programs may be approved at an overseas location. Two examples of these approved programs are the VA portion of the Transition Assistance Program (TAP) and the Benefits Delivery at Discharge (BDD) program.  
  • Work Study Position at A Branch Facility Located Overseas. Work Study positions at a branch of a U.S. school located overseas may be approved. Examples of these schools are the University of Maryland European Division, or the University of Maryland Asian Division. Work Study students in these overseas positions have the same assigned duty limitations as do Work Study students at a school located in the United States. 
  • Work Study Position in the Federal Benefits Unit (FBU) of a United States Embassy or Consulate.  Work Study positions in the FBU of a United States Embassy or Consulate may be approved. These positions are restricted to performing outreach services.
  • Work Study Position at VA Facilities in the Philippines.  Work Study positions providing administrative support at VA facilities in the Philippines may be approved.  

NOTE: Chapter 35 beneficiaries do not qualify for the Work Study program at overseas locations.  This is because of the restriction in 38 USC, section 3537(a).  This  states, in part, “the services of any eligible person who is pursuing, in a State, at least a three-quarter…“.  [A “State” means each of the several States, territories, and possessions of the United States, the District of Columbia, and the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico (38 USC 101(20).]

9.03 Eligibility

a. General

(1)  Students: To participate in the Work Study allowance program, the student must be receiving benefits under one of the following education programs:

  • Title 38, USC Chapter 30: The Montgomery GI Bill
  • Title 38, USC Chapter 31: Veteran Readiness and Employment Service
  • Title 38, USC Chapter 32: The Post-Vietnam Era Veterans’ Educational Assistance Program (VEAP)
  • Title 38, USC Chapter 33: The Post-9/11 GI Bill/Fry Scholarship/STEM Scholarship      
  • Title 38, USC Chapter 35: Dependents’ Educational Assistance Program (DEA)
  • Title 10, USC Chapter 1606:  The Montgomery GI Bill – Selected Reserve 

The student must be enrolled in and pursuing an approved college degree, vocational or professional program of education at a rate of pursuit of ¾ training time or greater. For a student under chapter 33, he/she must be enrolled in a rate of pursuit (ROP) of 75% or greater. For the purposes of this chapter, the term ¾ training time or greater will also include chapter 33 students enrolled in a ROP of 75% or greater. 

Per General Counsel Precedent Opinion 16-90, Work Study benefits are potentially payable to beneficiaries serving on active duty. These individuals must meet all other eligibility criteria and train at least at the ¾ time rate. Work Study benefits are not available to Chapter 35 beneficiaries who are serving on active duty.

Payments to a student under the Work Study program are not allowed for services performed on or after the student’s delimiting date (if that student has one). However, students who have a delimiting date, with an education debt, who attend school after that delimiting date can have Work Study allowance applied to this education debt.

The Work Study team must verify a student is training at the ¾ or greater rate when the student signs the Work Study agreement in reenrollment cases.  This is important if the student wishes to start working before the date classes begin.  The student’s school must confirm the student is preregistered at the ¾ time or greater training rate. Upon verification by the school, and VA has approved the Work Study contract, the student may begin to work up to 30 days before the actual start of classes.


b. Reduction or Termination

(1) If the student reduces his or her training time to less than ¾ time after signing the Work Study agreement, completion of services under the agreement may be allowed with the approval of VA. A new contract, however, will again require pursuit of ¾ training time or greater at the time the student signs his or her new agreement.

(2) If the student terminates training, the student may only complete those hours of uncompleted service during the month in which he or she terminates training.

(3) If the student has other educational benefits suspended because of unsatisfactory progress, the student may only complete those hours of uncompleted services during the month in which VA suspended his or her benefits.

EXAMPLE: If the VA suspended a student’s benefits on April 14, 2015 for unsatisfactory progress, the student could only complete those hours of uncompleted service scheduled for the month of April 2015. VA cannot pay for any hours scheduled or completed during any following months.

c. Advance Payment Cases

Students may request an advance payment for Work Study benefits when determined to be eligible for the program.  In most circumstances, a student may request an advance payment up to 45 days before a term begins.  If a request for Work Study is received more than 45 days in advance, the request should be returned for resubmission.

NOTE: In situations where a Work Study student is deployed for active duty and is not able to complete the number of hours required by his or her advance Work Study payment, the WSC should create the overpayment as usual unless there is clear indication the student intends to perform the required work.

When this overpayment is created, the suspension of debt collection will apply, and the Debt Management Center (DMC) will be asked to suspend collection of the debt

d. Delimiting Date Payments. If a student has a delimiting date, then payments under the Work Study program are not allowed for services performed on or after that student’s delimiting date. Work Study allowance is potentially payable to claimant who owe an education debt.  The amount of the debt considered in this program is the total debt to the government, to include all accumulated interest, administrative costs and other fees.  (Reference 38 CFR 1.929).  The debt must have been created under chapter 30, 31, 32, 33, 34, 35, or 36 of title 38, U.S.C., or chapter 1606 under title 10 U.S.C.  claimants under this program do not have to be enrolled in school.  Claimants may also qualify for this program if they are beyond their delimiting dates.

9.04. POSITION DESCRIPTIONS

VA must ensure position descriptions (PDs) exist for each Work Study position whether it is at a VA facility, non-VA facility, or education institution. It is the responsibility of the Muskogee RPO to review these PDs and ensure the mission of VA is represented. The Muskogee RPO will keep a copy of each PD description. This copy should be maintained in an electronic file by facility. In addition, the law clearly establishes clear criteria for selection of Work Study students should there be more applicants than positions.

a. VA must ensure Work Study students are effectively utilized in the activities permitted by law.  The law does not assume that an unlimited number of Work Study positions may be filled.  The law establishes clear criteria for selection of Work Study students when there are more applicants than positions. 

b. Work Study PDs must include an estimate of the number of work hours that a school can effectively use for the academic year plus the following summer term. The estimate should include the use of Work Study students during all intervals between terms as well. 

EXAMPLE:  A school indicates it needs 3 Work Study students per semester and for the summer term. The average length of a Work Study contract is 400 hours for these students. Therefore, the school should estimate that it needs 3,600 Work Study hours for the academic year (400 hours X 3 students X 3 terms (2 semesters and one summer term)).

NOTE 1:  VA approves Work Study positions. VA does not approve Work Study sites. VA may have to disapprove a Work Study position simply because of where that position is located. However, it is still the Work Study position that may not be approved. Once a position is approved, any number of people may be hired. However, Work Study supervisors should limit the number of people assigned to these positions, so the total hours worked is coordinated with the work to be performed.

NOTE 2:  The Muskogee RPO may not impose an arbitrary formula on the use of Work Study students at schools, e.g., one Work Study contract per 100 students per semester. The need for Work Study students may vary widely among institutions (even institutions with the same VA student enrollment) depending on local circumstances. For example, an institution with a highly automated records system with students who rarely change their enrollment might need fewer Work Study students than an institution with a paper bound system with students who frequently change their enrollment. Compliance surveys should routinely verify that Work Study students are properly utilized for approved activities.

c. Schools should annually submit a new PD (VA Form 22-10219) to show the estimated Work Study hours needed.

NOTE: Even when a facility has no changes, an annual PD submission is still required. However, schools can simply put a remark on the form noting this and provide an updated estimate of the number of hours needed for the upcoming school year.

Muskogee RPO should accept the new estimate if it is lower than the previous estimate or if the updated estimate is no more than 10% higher than the previous estimate. Schools must provide justification for increases in total Work Study hours that exceed 10% over the previous estimate. The school should submit a new PD along with the estimated hours if a school wishes to add new duties to its PD (such as a VA outreach function).

NOTE: If a WSC has questions as to whether a non-VA position qualifies for approval, he or she should send specific information about that position to the Procedures team.

9.06 WORKSTUDY APPLICATION PROCEDURES

a. VA Form 22-8691. A student applying for a VA Work Study benefit must complete VA Form 22-8691, Application for Work Study Allowance.  Once VA receives the student’s Work Study application, the form is reviewed for accuracy. If all documentation is complete and eligibility is confirmed, the Work Study application should be processed.   

Generally, WSMS correctly calculates the approved hours for the application on single enrollment periods. However, the approved hours may need to be calculated when processing multiple terms or overlapping terms, or when verifying hours on previously written contracts. The WSC may use the attached Work Study Approved Hours Calculator job aid to check the maximum number of hours approved. The first tab of this job aid is a calculator for a single period, and the second tab allows the WSC to enter multiple and overlapping periods.

Example Scenario for using the job aid:

  • Qualifying terms are:
    • 12-01-23 to 12-31-23
    • 12-15-23 to 01-15-24
    • 01-25-24 to 05-10-24
  • An extension request was processed 12-08-23
  • The Date of Claim is 12-04-23

WSC should use the multiple line tab. On line one, enter 12-04-23 in the begin date field and 12-31-23 in the end date field. On line two, enter 12-15-23 in the begin date field and 01-15-24 in the end date field. The job aid will calculate the maximum hours payable in the hours allowed field for each line and the total hours fields, as indicated in the figure below.

b. Approval of VA Form 22-8691. Upon approval of the Work Study application, the WSC will initiate the:

  • VA Form 22-8692, Work Study Agreement (Claimant Services) and
  • VA Form 22-8690, Time Record (Work Study Program).

The agreement (also referred to as a contract) should be signed by the Education Officer or designee and clearly state the maximum number of hours approved and the timeframe in which the hours must be worked. If the student wishes to request consideration of advance payment, refer to 9.06(g).

VA will email the contract and time record to the Work Study Site

Supervisor. The Site Supervisor should obtain the Work Study student’s signature on the contract. The student-signed contract should be returned to VA, via the Ask VA (AVA) website for completion.

Upon receipt of the student’s signed contract, the WSC will capture the document into the student’s Imaging Management Folder (TIMS) folder and process any pending time sheets.

IMPORTANT: The student can only begin working after the contract has been approved and the Site Supervisor has been notified. Hours worked prior to the approval date will not be paid by VA.

c. Period Covered by Work Study Application. A Work Study application should cover the period of the student’s current certified period of enrollment, not to exceed an ordinary school year, (i.e., two semesters or three quarters), or 12 months, for a course not organized on a term basis.

EXAMPLES:

(1) If the student is currently certified as enrolled for only one semester, the application would be limited to the services that could be provided during that semester.

(2) If the student is currently certified as enrolled for a full academic school year consisting of two semesters or three quarters (usually 9 months), the contract will be limited to the services that can be provided during the entire academic school year.

(3) If the student is currently certified as enrolled in a 24-month Non-College Degree (NCD) program, the contract will be limited to the services that can be provided during a twelve-month period. If the student completes those hours of the Work Study contract and remains continuously enrolled in the 24-month program, the student may request an extension of the original contract.

NOTE: The timeframes are the maximum hours that a student may contract. The WSC should approve a shorter contract if he or she has reason to believe that the student may not be eligible for Work Study benefits for the entire length of his or her current enrollment.

EXAMPLE: If a student is enrolled for an academic year consisting of three quarters, and the WSC’s review of the student’s Benefits Delivery Network (BDN), Long Term Solution (LTS), or Corporate WINRS (CWINRS) record shows that he or she has a history of reducing training time to less than ¾ time, the WSC should limit the contract to the number of hours that are supported by the first quarter only. The WSC should extend the contract for additional hours, one quarter at a time, if the student remains eligible for Work Study benefits after completing the number of hours in the shorter contract.

d. Time for Application Submission. A student may submit a Work Study application at any time of the year. The WSC should consider each application and notify the student within 7 business days of selection or non-selection of Work Study benefits.

e. Number of Applications. A student should file one application per year with extension requests being requested for each certified period of enrollment during that one-year period.

(1) If a student submits more than one application during the certified period of enrollment, process the subsequent application as a request for an extension of hours.

(2) If the student submits a request for additional hours under a previous contract, the WSC should process the request.  A student can request the additional hours by various means, such as letter, phone call, submitting another VA Form 22-8691, etc.

f. Retention of Application.  The student’s TIMS folder is the official record for a student’s Work Study history. All applications, timesheets, etc. should be retained in the student’s TIMS folder. If the WSC is unable to capture the document, it should be printed and routed for priority scan drop.

g. Advance Payment Requested

If an applicant requests advance payment on VA Form 22-8691 (box 9), then VA Form 22-8692 (Claimant Work Study Agreement (Advance Payment)) must be submitted.

Work Study students may elect to be paid in advance for 40% of the number of hours in their Work Study agreement, or 50 hours, whichever is less. The advance payment is recouped from the first regular Work Study payment. Students must be working to fulfill their 50 hours advancement requirement within 60 days of starting their contract.  This is because the benefit is designed to work around the students’ school schedule. 

If the Work Study student requests advance payment and is presently working under an existing contract, the current contract must be closed, and a new contract opened.  The final time record for the current contract should be accompanied by a new application for the new contract. Advance pay cannot be issued on an extended contract. A new contract is required.

If a student requests an advance payment, the WSC will prepare the contract and send the contract to the student, via the Site Supervisor, for the student to sign and return to the WSC.  When the WSC does sends the contract to the student, they will close the claim token in TIMS. 

When the student returns the signed contract, the WSC will enter the signed contract information into WSMS and release the advance payment. The signed contract is then imaged into TIMS.

h. Non-Selection Actions. If an applicant is not selected for the Work Study program, the WSC should notify the applicant in writing that his or her application for the Work Study program was not approved. This notice should include the specific reason(s) for non-selection and the applicant's right to reapply for the Work Study allowance program during a later term. The disallowance letter must include the VA Form 20-0998, Your Rights to Seek Further Review of Our Decision, and a copy of the letter must be captured to the applicant’s TIMS folder.

9.07 FACTORS IN THE SELECTION PROCESS

a. Factors for Consideration. In selecting students for participation in the Work Study allowance program, the following factors will be carefully considered and evaluated:

(1) Service-Connected Disabilities. Veterans with service-connected disabilities rated at 30 percent or more will be afforded a priority in the awarding of Work Study contracts. All applicants with service-connected disabilities rated 30 percent or more must be hired before other applicants are hired, whenever feasible.

(2) Financial Need

(a) To efficiently reach the target group of individuals who can benefit most substantively from the additional allowance that the Work Study allowance program provides then the degree of financial need of an individual applicant must be assessed.

(b) Consideration of financial need should include, but should not be limited to, the following:

  1. Number of dependents;
  2. Living expenses, including dependents' living expenses;
  3. Unusually high tuition and fees; and/or
  4. Unusual family medical expenses.

(c) Consideration of financial need should focus on those needs of the applicant that may be met by the Work Study allowance. A fundamental question to be asked in each case is whether or not the Work Study allowance will enable the applicant to enter or continue in a program of education or training.

(3) Availability of Transportation. In determining job site location, determine if the applicant is able to obtain reliable and affordable transportation to a particular work site. The selected work site should be within a normal commuting distance.

(4) Motivation

(a) Evaluating the motivation of the Work Study applicant can be a largely subjective process. Generally, determine the applicant's motivation by his or her statement of interest in the program as well as from any interview information provided by the referring official or supervisor.

(b) A primary motivation for a student pursuing a course of study should be attainment of a particular educational or training objective. In those instances, in which an applicant for Work Study employment is either receiving or has a history of receiving VA tutorial assistance allowance, carefully consider the suitability of such employment. If the WSC determines that the time that the applicant has available for performing Work Study services would have an adverse effect upon the overall pursuit of an educational or training objective, then the applicant should not be offered a Work Study agreement.

(5) Work Assignments and Disabilities. If an individual has a disability, ensure that any Work Study assignment is compatible with the disability. Consult the Veteran Readiness & Employment (VR&E) Officer if there is any doubt about the compatibility of the disability and the work assignment for an individual with a disability (either service-connected or not). In all other cases, have the agency or organization using the Work Study student determine if the individual can perform the duties of the job.

NOTE 1: If the student wishes to limit his or her work to one or two work sites and those sites only have limited positions available, the WSC should discuss this with the student. If a position is not available and the student will not work at another site, disallow the claim.

NOTE 2: If the student wishes to limit his or her work to one work site and that site is not approved, the WSC should discuss this with the student and the school. If the school submits a request for approval, the WSC should expedite action on that approval. If the site is not approved and the student will not work at another site, disallow the claim.  

(6) VR&E Division Assistance in Job Modification and Reasonable Accommodation. Refer cases to the VR&E Officer at the regional office of jurisdiction if they require evaluation for job modification and reasonable accommodation.

(7) Delimiting Date. Ensure that sufficient time is available within the agreement period to enable the student to complete the hours entailed in the contract before the student’s delimiting date.

NOTE: Effective August 1, 2018, the “Forever GI Bill” removes the 15-year time limit to use the Post-9/11 GI Bill benefits for Veterans with a last discharge or release from active duty on/after January 1, 2013, children of deceased service members who became entitled on/after January 1, 2013 and, all Fry Scholarship spouses

b. Avoidance of Bias. When the WSC is considering applicants for the program, they need to avoid any preselection bias which would tend to favor program experience over any of the other selection factors.

  • Give all qualified applicants equal consideration.
  • Take efforts to encourage the broadest possible participation in the program.
  •  Make reasonable attempts to place all qualified disabled Veteran applicants in Work Study positions.

  • Student and site supervisor initials,

  • The calculation of cumulative hours returned via email to the site supervisor for correction. VA cannot make time record corrections.

If the student does not have direct deposit established, a paper check will be issued.  Paper checks are generally received in 7 to 10 business days after the payment information is sent to the Department of Treasury. It is critical the student’s mailing address is kept current. Students must report any address changes to VA as soon as possible.

Payment notifications are electronically emailed to the Site Supervisor and the student’s email address from WSMS.  These notifications are automatically generated and released upon Fiscal Authorization (FAUT) processing.

(2) If no enrollment certification has been received for the period in question, deny the Work Study application and send a letter to the student and notification to the Site Supervisor stating there’s no enrollment.

Payments for New Contracts (Non-Advance Payment Cases). A student selected for the Work Study program who elects not to receive an advance payment will be paid for the hours shown on his or her time record. (2) Bi-weekly, whichever comes first.  

The final time record may be for less than 50 hours if less than 50 hours remain to complete the contract, or if a student ceases to perform services before the completion of the contract.

b. Payments under the Lump Sum payment program. Public Law 116-154, Section 6, is the Improvement to the Work Study Allowance Program that allows approved Educational Institutions to become a 154 Administering Facility (154 AF) and receive a lump sum payment amount to be used for the purposes of paying VA approved Work Study students who receive GI Bill benefits at their institution.

9.10 MAXIMUM HOURS OF SERVICE

a. Total Hour Limitation. The maximum number of hours of Work Study service that a student may perform during or between a period of enrollment is limited to 25 times the number of weeks in the applicable enrollment period excluding the period between the enrollments. (38 CFR 21.4145d)

b. Semester, Quarter or Term Limitation. For courses organized on a semester, quarter or term basis, the total number of hours of Work Study service is limited to 25 times the number of weeks shown on the enrollment certification. If the enrollment certification shows 12 weeks, the limitation would be 25 x 12 weeks or 300 hours. (If the student is certified for an entire school year of two semesters or three quarters, the limitation is 25 hours times the number of weeks shown on the enrollment certification and subject to the maximum of 1,300 hours for 52 weeks.  

c. Calendar Year Limitation. For enrollments for an entire calendar year (52 weeks), the total number of hours of Work Study service is limited to 1,300 hours in any 12-month period.

9.12 UNSCHEDULED TERMINATIONS AND REDUCTIONS IN TRAINING TIME

a. Written Notice of Termination. If an agreement is terminated by either the student or VA, the WSC must take the appropriate action to terminate the record and give written notice of this termination to the student and the Site Supervisor. If the student ceases to perform the agreed upon service and does not notify VA, the WSC will process this termination through the WSMS. The system will send the student a letter providing him or her with notice of termination stating why the agreement was terminated and the effective date of termination. This letter will include a VA Form 20-0998, Your Rights to Seek Further Review of Our Decision. In the event an unscheduled termination cannot be avoided, use the date that the student last performed service as the date for computing the allowable cumulative hours on the time record .

b. Reduction Actions (Advance Payment Cases)

(1) If a student reduces his or her training time to less than ¾ time for non-chapter 33 benefits or a ROP less than 75%  for chapter 33 benefits after receiving an advance payment and continues training at the reduced rate after he or she signs a Work Study agreement, the WSC will allow the student to complete the number of hours for which an advance payment was issued unless this is not in the best interest of the VA and the student.

NOTE: In the event the student reduces his or her training to less than ¾ time before completing an agreement, the student, with the approval of the RPO Director or designee, may be permitted to complete the portions of an agreement in the same or immediately following the term, quarter, or semester in which a student ceases to train at ¾ time.  No new contracts will be written if the enrollment remains below ¾ training time or 75% ROP. Follow local RPO procedures on receiving approval on these situations

(2) If the student is not allowed to complete the number of hours for which an advance payment was issued, the contract should be terminated, and the student should receive a letter notifying him or her of the specific reason(s) for the action taken. This letter should include a VA Form 20-0998. The WSC can do this in the WSMS system. The WSC should ensure copies of all actions and letters are placed in the student’s TIMS folder.

(3) If the student is allowed to complete the full number of hours specified in the agreement, the WSC should send a letter stating that he or she can complete those hours either during the same term, quarter or semester in which he or she ceased to train at ¾ time or a ROP of 75%. In no event may the student perform services beyond his or her delimiting date. It is the responsibility of the WSC to ensure the student does not Work more hours than was on the original agreement for which an advance payment was received.  Follow local RPO procedures before sending a student letter in these situations.

c. Reduction Actions (Non-Advance Payment Cases)

If a student reduces the rate of his or her training to less than ¾ time, has not received an advance payment and continues training at the reduced rate of training after he or she signs a Work Study agreement, the student, with the approval of the RPO Director or designee, may be permitted to complete the portions of an agreement. A prepared memo signed by the Muskogee RPO Director or designee must be placed in TIMS folder to show this was approved.  Follow the local Muskogee RPO procedures on receiving approval on these unique situations.

d. Termination Actions (Advance Payment Cases)

(1) If a student terminates his or her training after signing a Work Study agreement, the student will not be allowed to complete any additional hours beyond the hours represented by the advance payment. If the hours represented by the advance payment have not been completed and the WSC believes that it is in the best interest of VA and the student to complete those hours, the WSC should terminate the contract at the end of the month the student reduced to less than ¾ time training, and send a letter advising of the action taken and the specific reasons. This letter should include VA Form 20-0998, Your Rights to Seek Further Review of Our Decision.  These actions can be done in the WSMS and copies of all actions and letters should be placed in the student’s TIMS folder or a copy sent to the VR&E office of jurisdiction. A memo signed by the Muskogee RPO Director or designee must be placed in TIMS folder to show this was approved.  Follow the local Muskogee RPO procedures on receiving approval on these unique situations.

 (2) The WSC should take action to terminate the contract on the appropriate date and send the student a letter notifying him or her of the specific reason(s) for the action taken. This letter should include a VA Form 20-0998. Be sure a copy of this letter is sent to the Site Supervisor and a copy is placed in the student’s TIMS folder or sent to the appropriate VR&E Division.

e. Termination Actions (Non-Advance Payment Cases)

(1) If a student terminates his or her training after signing a Work Study agreement and has not received an advance payment, the student will not be allowed to complete any additional hours beyond the hours during the month in which he or she terminates training.  

(2) If a student has education benefits suspended because of unsatisfactory progress, the student may only complete those hours of uncompleted service during the month in which VA suspended his or her benefits.

(3) The WSC should take action to terminate the contract on the appropriate date and send the student a letter notifying him or her of the specific reason(s) for the action taken. The letter should include a VA Form 20-0998 and a copy of the letter sent to the Site Supervisor a copy placed in the student’s TIMS folder or sent to the appropriate VR&E Division.

f. Actions on Debts (Advance Payment Cases)

(1) Consider any hours of unperformed service for which the student received an advance payment as a debt subject to recovery. The amount of the debt will be an amount equal to the number of hours of unperformed services multiplied by the hourly rate of the advance payment. Collection of these overpayments will be actively pursued.

 (2) In the event of a Work Study overpayment, the WSC will notify the Muskogee RPO’s Support Services Division (SSD) to ensure the overpayment is processed in the appropriate education master record.

NOTE: In the event a Work Study student is placed in active duty status and is not able to complete the number of hours required by his or her advance payment, the WSC should still create an overpayment in this situation; however, the suspension of debt collection may apply.  Contact the Debt Management Center (DMC) for additional questions regarding the suspension of the collection of the debt.

g. Actions on Debts (Non-Advance Payment Cases)

(1) Consider any hours of unperformed service for which the student received payment as a debt subject to recovery. The amount of the debt will be an amount equal to the number of hours of unperformed services multiplied by the hourly rate of the payment. Collection of these overpayments will be actively pursued.

(2) In the event of a Work Study overpayment, the WSC must notify the Muskogee RPO’s SSD to ensure the overpayment is processed in the appropriate education master record.

APPENDIX A: PAYMENT OF WORK STUDY ALLOWANCE FOR OFFSETTING EDUCATION DEBTS

9A.01 GENERAL

The Work Study allowance program offers an additional way to repay an education debt to the government for claimants who owe under one of the education programs administered by the VA. (38 CFR 1.929.)

Go to Top


9A.02 ELIGIBILITY

a. Work Study allowance is potentially payable to students who owe an education debt. The amount of the debt considered in this program is the total debt to the government, to include all accumulated interest, administrative costs, and other fees. (38 CFR 1.929.) The debt must have been created under Chapters 30, 31, 32, 33, 34, 35, or 36 of title 38, U.S.C., or Chapter 1606 or 1607 of title 10, U.S.C.  Students under this program do not have to be enrolled in school. Students may also qualify for this program if they are beyond their delimiting date.

b. If the student requests a waiver of his or her education debt, the student does not qualify for this program while the waiver request is pending. If the student appeals the denial of this request to Board of Veterans Appeals (BVA), or to Court of Veterans Appeals (CVA), the student loses eligibility for this program. However, if VA, BVA, or CVA denies the waiver request or the appeal, the student again qualifies for this program

c. VA may not offer a Work Study contract to an individual who is receiving compensation from another source for the Work Study services the individual wishes to perform.

d. VA may not offer a Work Study contract to an individual if VA determines that the debt can be collected through other means such as collection in a lump sum, collection in installments, or compromise of the debt

9A.04. APPLICATION PROCEDURES

a. Application Requirement. A claimant applying for a VA Work Study benefit under this program must complete VA Form 22-8691d. Upon receipt of an application, the WSC should:

(1) Contact the Committee on Waivers and Compromises (COWC)  , and DMC to determine the following:

a. If the claimant has submitted a request for waiver or compromise of the education debt (or an appeal of the denial of a waiver request).

NOTE: DMC does not handle requests for waivers or compromises but refers these requests to the station having jurisdiction over the education debt. DMC would know if the claimant has submitted a request for waiver or compromise.

b. If there is any evidence that the claimant's education debt can be collected through other means such as collection in a lump sum, collection in installments, or compromise.

(2) If each staff has a negative reply to the inquiry for information shown above  the Work Study Coordinator should continue processing the application.

(3) If any staff has a positive reply to the inquiry for information shown above, the RO Work Study Coordinator must deny the application.

b. Period Covered by Application. The student must contract for sufficient hours to become entitled to a sum large enough to liquidate the whole education debt; however, the contract cannot exceed 52 weeks. In determining the number of hours per week and the number of weeks necessary to liquidate the education debt, VA will use the amount of the education account receivable, including all accrued interest, administrative costs and marshal fees outstanding on the date the contract is offered to the student and all accrued interest, administrative costs and marshal fees VA estimates will have become outstanding on the date the debt is to be liquidated.

EXAMPLE: If the student owes $2,000.00, the student will have to enter into a contract to perform services for a total of 275.86 hours, based on the Federal minimum wage of $7.25 hourly. (2,000 / 7.25 = 275.86) Change the fraction of an hour to minutes. (.86 X 60 = 52) Round up the minutes. In this example, the student would have to contract to work 275 hours and 52 minutes. If the education debt exceeds the amount that can be liquidated within 52 weeks, the student does not qualify for this program.

NOTE: Currently, only chapter 35 students are charged accrued interest, administrative costs, and marshal fees. Use these calculations only on chapter 35 students under this program. SSD should make these calculations.

c. Time for Application Submission. A student may submit a Work Study application at any time following creation of an education debt. There is no time limit set by law for submission of an application.

d. Number of Applications. A student only needs to file one application for this program.

e. Retention of Application. Ensure  the VA Form 22-8691d is placed in the student’s TIMS folder or send a copy to the VR&E office of jurisdiction.

f. Actions after Selection

(1) Upon selection of a student, notify DMC of the selection. Send the student’s name and file number to VAVBASPL/DMC/EDU.

(2) Also send DMC the contract’s beginning and ending dates and dollar amount of the debt to be recouped. Request that all debt collection efforts pertaining to the education debt, including having the student’s name and social security number removed from the IRS list, be suspended until after the ending date of the contract.

(3) Upon selection of a student, prepare VA Form 22-8692d and a locally prepared letter. In addition to informing the student of where and when to report, the locally prepared letter must also inform the student that he or she can elect to have part of his or her other VA benefits used to recoup the debt. Request the student to return all copies of the signed agreement within 10 days from the date of the letter.

(4) The WSC will coordinate with the Muskogee’s RPO SSD to ensure that education benefits will not be offset by the education debt while the student is in this program.

g. Non-Selection Actions. If a student is not selected for this program, promptly notify the student in writing that his or her application for the Work Study program has not been approved. Include the specific reason or reasons for non-selection and a summary of the evidence considered. Also include VA Form 20-0998, Your Rights to Seek Further Review of Our Decision. Ensure a copy of this letter is placed in the student’s TIMS folder (or a copy sent to the VR&E office of jurisdiction)

9A.07 PAYMENTS

a. Payments for New Contracts.

(1) A student selected for this program will not qualify for an advance payment.

(2) Ensure that all amounts earned under this program are applied to the student's education debt. Authorize the Work Study allowance at the Federal minimum wage or the State minimum wage (if the State has a minimum wage), whichever is greater.

(3) If there is an increase in the rate (such as an increase in the Federal or State minimum wage) that will increase the rate of recovery of the debt, increase the rate of Work Study payments to the increased rate. Also, reduce the number of hours to reflect the increased rate of recovery of the debt.

(4) Thirty days after entry into this program, contact the Site Supervisor to ensure that the student has worked or is in the process of working the number of hours for which he or she contracted. If there is no evidence that the student is working, verify the employment and the anticipated completion date of the hours. If appropriate, terminate the Work Study contract.

(5) Reduce the debt after the student completes 50 hours of work or the balance if less than 50 hours remain in the contract.

b. Request for Additional Hours. A student cannot request additional hours as the initial contract must have contracted for sufficient hours to recoup the education debt, or for a maximum of 52 weeks.

NOTE: While no additional hours can be authorized, the WSC can consider a second application from a student if he or she was previously selected for this program, performed Work Study services, or quit for a valid reason. The WSC can approve a second contract only for those hours that remain not performed during the first contract.

EXAMPLE: A student was accepted into this program for a total of 1,000 hours. He performed 600 hours satisfactorily and quit because of an illness in his immediate family. The WSC can approve a second contract for the remaining 400 hours needed to recoup the student’s education debt.

c. Specific BDN Actions for Applying Work Study Allowance to Education Debt. The WCS should review BDN to determine the current amount of the student's education debt. The WSC should review the total number of hours authorized and adjust those hours to equal the amount of the education debt. After adjustment of those hours to equal the education debt, authorize Work Study allowance to recoup the entire debt and terminate the Work Study award. Once it is determined the student is eligible to be enrolled in this program, refer to the WSMS guide for additional processing details to ensure the number of hours worked is used to offset any education debt.

d. Payments for Additional Hours (Beyond Hours Needed to Repay Debt). If the student has completed additional hours beyond those hours needed to recoup the debt, determine if the student was at fault in working the additional hours.

(1) If the student is at fault in working additional hours (he or she knew they were working additional hours over and above those needed to recoup the education debt), prepare a memorandum for the signature of the RPO Director or  designee. Once approved , notify the student by a locally produced letter denying any payment for those hours.

(2) If the student is not at fault in working additional hours (he or she did not know they were working additional hours over and above those needed to recoup the education debt), authorize payment for those additional hours and terminate the Work Study contract.  

NOTE: The WSC should use judgment whether the student could reasonably know if he or she was working these additional hours. Apply the "reasonable doubt" criteria where applicable.

9A.08 MAXIMUM HOURS OF SERVICE

a. Total Hour Limitation. The maximum number of hours of Work Study service that a claimant may perform under this program is limited to the lesser of the following: the time needed to repay the total amount of the claimant's education debt, or 52 weeks. (The claimant is limited to working 40 hours weekly. Reference: 38 CFR 1.929(e)(1).)

b. Semester, Quarter or Term Limitation. Claimants under this program do not have to be attending school. There is no semester, quarter, or term limitation for these claimants.

9A.09 REPORTING HOURS WORKED

The submission of a time report and number of the hours is the same for a student who is not in the education debt program.  The site supervisor must submit the hours when 50 hours have been performed or every two weeks, whichever comes first.

9A.10 UNSCHEDULED TERMINATIONS

a. Written Notice of Termination. If an agreement is terminated by either the student or VA, the WSC should take the appropriate action to terminate the Work Study award. Give written notice of this termination to the student and the supervisor. If the student ceases to perform the agreed-upon service and does not notify VA, send the student a dictated letter providing him or her with notice of termination stating why the agreement is terminated and the effective date of termination. Include VA Form 20-0998, Your Rights to Seek Further Review of Our Decision. In the event an unscheduled termination cannot be avoided, use the date that the student last performed service as the date for computing the allowable cumulative hours on the timecard.

b. Termination Actions

(1) If the student’s education debt is recouped by any other means (such as recovery by the IRS by withholding of an income tax refund check), or if the debt is waived or otherwise liquidated, the student automatically loses eligibility for this program. Terminate the Work Study account after first authorizing payments for any hours worked after the debt is recouped, waived, or otherwise liquidated. (38 CFR 1.929(d)(6).)

(2) If a student is receiving education assistance while liquidating a debt under this program, terminate the contract effective the last date that he or she received such education benefits if the BDN review shows the student either reduces the rate of pursuit or terminates such assistance and creates an additional education debt.         (38 CFR 1.929(d)(7).)

(3) If a student under this program does not satisfactorily perform Work Study services, terminate the contract effective the last date that he or she satisfactorily performed these services. (38 CFR 1.929(d)(8))

(4) The WSC should take action to terminate the contract on the appropriate date and send the student a locally produced letter notifying him or her of the specific reason or reasons for the action taken. Include VA Form 20-0998, Your Rights to Seek Further Review of Our Decision.

c. Actions on Debts. Notify DMC in the following instances:

(1) If the student has terminated his or her Work Study contract and has not repaid the entire education debt, the WSC should notify DMC with the amount of the remaining debt and request that DMC resume appropriate collection action.

(2) If the student has fulfilled his or her Work Study contract and BDN shows total recovery of the education debt, the WSC should provide this information to DMC.

(3) Payments to a student under the Work Study program are not allowable for services performed on or after the student's delimiting date in chapters 30, 31, 32, 33, 35 or 1606. This includes payments to a chapter 31 student who is considered in a rehabilitation program (employment assistance). However, a student with an education debt that attends school after their delimiting date can have Work Study allowance applied to this education debt.

Appendix B. Additional Questions and Clarifications

Question: Can a student who is receiving VA education benefits for an internship at a VA facility also receive VA benefits as Work Study for the same Work/position?

Answer: No. The total number of hours required for Work Study cannot exceed 25 times the number of weeks during or between a period of enrollment. Since the student is already working as an intern at a VA facility (thus exceeding this number of hours required for Work Study), the same work at the VA facility cannot be approved for Work Study. Therefore, since the Work Study program is not approvable, the student cannot receive VA benefits for Work Study and internship simultaneously. (38 U.S.C. 3485(a) (3))

Based on 38 U.S.C. 3485(d), an individual who is performing Work Study will only be considered a U.S. employee for the purposes of benefits of Chapter 81 of title 5 (Compensation for Work Injuries), and not for the purposes of laws administered by the Office of Personnel Management (OPM). Since the intern (a VA employee) falls under the laws administered by OPM, this is a second reason he or she would not qualify for Work Study.

Question: What do we do in a situation where a Work Study student is called to active duty and is not able to complete the number of hours required by his or her advance Work Study payment?

Answer: WSCs should follow the intent of 38 CFR 21.4145(g) (1) (i), which is to allow the student to complete the Work Study agreement "in the same or immediately following term, quarter, or semester, in which the Veteran, reservist, or eligible person terminates training." This means do not create a debt until after the term, quarter, or semester which follows the one in which the Work Study program was terminated. Do not apply this principle but instead create a debt immediately if there is clear indication that the individual does not intend to perform the required work (38 CFR 21.4145(h)).

When an overpayment is created in this situation, the suspension of debt collection rule may apply. If the person remains in an activated status when the overpayment is created and is assigned at that point, then it would be proper to ask DMC to suspend collection of the debt.
