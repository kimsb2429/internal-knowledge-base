SUBCHAPTER I. GENERAL

10.01 INTRODUCTION

a. Purpose. This chapter explains direct deposit for all education benefit programs.

NOTE: "Direct deposit" and Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT) mean the same thing in this chapter.

b. Limitations

1. Performance. Direct deposit does not mean same day or next day service. Direct deposit payments will take between 3 to 5 days to reach the bank once a payment is authorized. (A payment is considered authorized in the Benefits Delivery Network (BDN) on the date a certification transaction (35, 35X, 35Z) is processed or in the Long Term Solution (LTS) when the work product is authorized). The BDN M22 screen (Transaction History) will show these transactions immediately after they are processed.

           NOTE: The Schedule of Operations now provides a projected payment date for direct deposits.

2. Determining Receipt of Direct Deposit. Since payments are not issued at the same time each month, beneficiaries will need to check their bank accounts to verify receipt of a direct deposit.

10.02 HOW CAN A BENEFICIARY SIGN UP FOR DIRECT DEPOSIT?

a. Financial instution approved for direct deposit participation. Beneficiaries must indicate they are using an acceptable financial institution for direct deposit. 

1. Acceptable financial institutions include banks, savings and loans associations, credit unions, and mutual savings banks.

2. Unacceptable financial institutions include credit card companies, finance companies, mutual fund companies, brokerage firms, insurance companies, and other non-traditional financial services companies.

b. Minimum Required Information. Four pieces of information are required in addition to the beneficiary's name and file number. They are:

1. Bank Forms. These include deposit slips, canceled checks, or voided checks OR

2. The Routing number of the beneficiary’s financial institution.

3. The account number at the beneficiary’s financial institution.

 4. Whether the account is checking or savings.

c. Acceptable Enrollment Methods. Any of the following enrollment methods is acceptable:

1. Telephonic enrollment. The beneficiary may call the Education Call Center (ECC) at 1-888-442-4551.  As always, ECC personnel must verify the identity of callers for direct deposit in the same manner as they would for all case specific calls received.

2. Online using self-service. The beneficiary may access the eBenefits website at https://www.va.gov/change-direct-deposit to update and/or change their direct deposit information.

3. Signed Statement. Any signed statement that includes the necessary enrollment information is acceptable, as long as a proper bank form is attached (e.g. If a beneficiary attaches direct deposit information to an application, this would be acceptable).

4. Standard Form 1199A (Direct Deposit Sign-up Form). If stated form is received from a beneficiary, accept this as a request to update/change/add EFT. Section 3 of the form must be completed by the financial institution, not the beneficiary. This is only for beneficiaries who have an international account or do not have access to the internet or phone.

5. VA Application. Accept EFT on VA applications that contain direct deposit information fields.

10.03 HOW TO FIND DIRECT DEPOSIT DATA ON A CHECK

a. Routing Number. This is a nine-digit all numeric field at the lower left corner used to identify the financial institution.

b. Account Number. This is the beneficiary's account number assigned by the financial institution (lower middle area of the check). This number can be up to 17 positions with a combination of both alpha and numeric characters.

NOTE: Some account numbers may contain dash (-) symbols. Enter as provided, including the dash (-).

c. Check Serial Number. This is the number of a particular check (upper right hand corner). In most situations, the check serial number is coded separately from the account number. However, in some cases it may be coded between the routing number and the account number. In any instance, be careful that the check serial number is not recorded as part of the account number. If in doubt, contact the financial institution.

d. Name of Financial Institution. This identifies the financial institution the veteran is using.

e.  Account Title Information. This indicates the person or persons entitled to the proceeds of the account. The name of the beneficiary must be shown in the account title. Joint accounts are acceptable.

10.04 HOW TO PROCESS REQUESTS FOR DIRECT DEPOSIT

a. Review Direct Deposit Data. The VA employee should ensure the minimum required information as listed in Section 10.02b is available. All documentation regarding direct deposit must be filed in the Image Management System (TIMS) folder. This includes any telephonic documentation.

b. BDN 601 Screen.

            1. Use the BDN CADD (Change Address) command to access the 601 screen.

The required entries are CADD, password, and file number. (Veteran Claims Examiners (VCEs) may also access the 601 screen from the CADJ (Claims Adjudication), STOP, SUSP (Suspend), or RESU (Resume) commands by entering "601" in the NEXT SCREEN field.

NOTE 1: If more than one record exists, the S02 screen (Payee Selection) will display. Select the appropriate record for which the direct deposit is being updated.

NOTE 2: You can change the beneficiary’s address and add or change direct deposit data at the same time (one CADD session).

c. BDN 601 Screen Entries. The BDN 601 screen fields require an entry.

1. DD/EFT (Direct Deposit/Electronic Funds Transfer).

CHG     CANCEL

To establish or change direct deposit data, enter an "X" after CHG.

To cancel direct deposit data, enter an "X" after CANCEL. Click "ENTER" to save.

Actions 2-6 below are not required when cancelling direct deposit data.  

         NOTE: Accept EFT cancelation on VA Form 20-572.

2. ROUTING. Enter the first eight digits of the financial institution's routing number.

3. CHECK DIGIT. Enter the 9th digit of the financial institution's routing number. (The check digit is used to verify the routing number.)

4. TYPE ACCT. Enter "C" (Checking) or "S" (Savings) as appropriate. 

NOTE: If unsure or claimant did not specify, enter "C"

5. ACCT NO (Account Number). Enter the veteran's account number at the financial institution.

6. DD/EFT LTR SUPPRESS. Enter an "X" only if the direct deposit acknowledgment letter or special paragraphs should be suppressed (see Figures 10.06, 10.08 and 10.10-10.12). This field should seldom be used.

NOTE 1: If EFT numbers won’t accept on the BDN 601 screen (or partially provided): do not input EFT; edit award/eligibility letter or send CO FIS-3 letter (direct deposit info invalid option) explaining that beneficiary should contact the Education Call Center or visit VA.gov to make direct deposit updates.

NOTE 2: International/Foreign Bank (including Philippines) issues: Only available for VSC payments. Beneficiary needs to contact his/her bank and see if there is a “sister bank” in the United States with which VA can set the direct deposit. The “sister bank” would transfer the funds to the foreign bank. The VCE should send the CO FIS-3 letter and edit as applicable.

NOTE 3: The Edith Nourse Rogers Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math (STEM) Scholarship, Veteran Rapid Retraining Assistance Program (VRRAP) and Veteran Employment Through Technology Education Courses (VET TEC) benefits are paid through the Finance and Accounting System (FAS). If the beneficiary is using any of these benefits, changes in EFT on the VA Form 22-10203, 22-0994 or 22-1990S will also impact their Compensation & Pension (C&P) benefits (the data is linked to FAS). 

NOTE 4: For any STEM, VRRAP, or VET TEC beneficiaries, any change to their Compensation & Pension (C&P) EFT will also update their STEM or VET TEC direct deposit (the data is linked to FAS).

SUBCHAPTER II - DIRECT DEPOSIT IN BDN


10.05 SUMMARY OF EFT IN BDN

Direct deposit required extensive changes to the chapter 30 BDN system. These include:

a. Inquiry and Transaction History.          

1. The BDN M21 screen (Status and Award History) has a one-position direct deposit indicator field in the middle of the screen. A "Y" means the record has direct deposit data. An "N" means it does not.  Refer to Figure 10.02.  

2. The M01 screen (Deduction/Receivable/Balance Data) displays the full direct deposit data in the record.  Refer to Figure 10.03.  The data fields are as follows:

i. DD/EFT STATUS. This will be either blank, "Active", or "Inactive".  Blank means VA has not received direct deposit data or that direct deposit data previously on file has since been canceled. "Active" means VA has active direct deposit data. "Inactive" means VA has direct deposit data but this data is considered inactive because the most recent direct deposit payment was returned from the financial institution. 

ii. ROUTING. This shows the first eight digits of the financial institution's routing number. 

iii. CHECK DIGIT. This shows the ninth digit of the financial institution's routing number. 

iv. TYPE ACCOUNT. This will be either C (Checking) or S (Savings). 

v. ACCT NO. This is the beneficiary’s account number at the financial institution. 

3. The M23 screen (Claimant Transaction History) displays a "22D" transaction when direct deposit data is added, changed or deleted on the master record (chapter 33, 30 and 1606 only). Refer to Figure 10.04.  

 NOTE:The “22D” is recorded in addition to any other transaction that may be processed.  

 NOTE: EFT is not available for Chapter 32 beneficiaries.

b. Direct Deposit Payment Standard. All payments will be released via direct deposit (except for non-chapter 33 advance payments) if there is active direct deposit data in the master record. This includes out-of-system payments for correspondence, flight, apprenticeship, and on-the-job training as well as tutorial and work-study payments.

There is an exception for fiscal payments. A field has been added to the F15 screen (Special Payments). Refer to Figure 10.05 and the F45 screen (Unassociated Clearance) giving the employee the choice of issuing a payment by direct deposit or by check if appropriate. 

c. Returned Payment. 

1. If a direct deposit payment is returned for "bad address"* (that is, the bank did not accept the payment), BDN will automatically reissue the payment by check unless the master record is in a terminated status. When a direct deposit is returned, the DD/EFT STATUS field on the M01 screen will be changed to "Inactive". (Any subsequent entry of direct deposit data on the 601 screen will reactivate the direct deposit status.) If a direct deposit is returned for a reason other than bad address, or if the master record is terminated, the payment will go into proceeds and no check will be reissued.

* for direct deposit, “bad address” means some part of the banking record was incorrect or no longer valid. It is not associated with the beneficiary’s mailing address.

2. If a check is returned due to a "bad address", BDN will reissue the payment via direct deposit if the DD/EFT STATUS field shows "Active".

d. Letters. Letters and paragraphs for existing letters were developed to keep the beneficiary advised regarding direct deposit. See Sections 10.06 and 10.07.
