SUBCHAPTER I.  JURISDICTION PROCEDURES


1.01  GENERAL

a. RPO Responsibility. All RPOs (Regional Processing Offices) are responsible for processing education benefits under Chapters 30, 32, 33, 35 of Title 38, U.S. Code, Chapter 1606 and 1607 of Title 10, U.S. Code, National Call to Service (Section 531 of PL 107-314) and, Section 903 of Public Law 96-342. The Muskogee RPO is responsible for Section 901 cases.

In addition, the RPOs are responsible for processing Chapter 32 refunds for Veterans within its jurisdiction. This includes death refund development and payments.

b. RO Responsibility. All ROs (Regional Offices) are responsible for the following:

(1) Handling requests for general information. (ROs should send inquiries regarding specific claims and related information to RPOs.)

(2) Provide character of service determinations for Veterans with service not characterized as Honorable or Under Honorable Conditions

NOTE: Veteran Claims Examiners (VCEs) should follow local RPO procedures regarding how to send a request to a RO.

c. Education Folders. Paper folders were used for claimant records in the pre-TIMS (The Image Management System) era but are seldom created or used in current processing. Paper folders may be stored at an RPO, off site under a RPO's supervision, or at a Record Management Center. RPOs may recall paper folders at their discretion. An RPO may occasionally need to review documents in a paper folder that are missing from the TIMS record. The types of folders that exist are listed below

(1) Electronic Folder Management System. All Education processing is done within TIMS. This is the primary folder for storing claims related information. For additional information regarding creating folders in TIMS, refer to Chapter 14, Upstream Procedures.

NOTE: The only types of folders that can be recorded in BDN (Benefit Delivery Network) are claims folders, Chapter 30 folders, DEA (Dependents’ Educational Assistance) folders, and EDU (Education) folders. BDN does not indicate whether a TIMS folder or a paper folder exists.

(2) Claims Folder. This is a three-part red paper folder known as red-rope. It has three flaps with fasteners attached on the inside. Claims folders were used to process Chapter 32, Section 903, and Chapter 1606 benefits before June 1994. Since June 1994, Education (EDU) folders were used for these benefits. The Waco RO used paper claims folders for Section 901 claims until the Muskogee RPO assumed jurisdiction for this benefit in 2005. Since then, Section 901 records have been converted to TIMS. The Chapter 34 benefit of Title 38 (which expired on December 31, 1989) was processed entirely with claims folders.

(3) CH30 (Chapter 30) Folder. This is a two-part paper folder containing Chapter 30 material only. Initially, RPOs built two-part gray folders for Chapter 30 claims until the stock of those folders was depleted. Then the RPOs used Kraft folders (light brown in color) as Chapter 30 folders. These folders were discontinued in the 1990s and replaced with EDU (Education) folders.

(4) EDU Folder. This is a two-part Kraft brown paper folder containing material for Chapters 30, 32, 1606, or Section 903. The physical EDU folders have not been created since the 1990s. However, the TIMS folders for all education benefits (except Chapter 35) are recorded as EDU folders in BDN.

NOTE 1: For claims established before October 16, 2000, a Veteran could have two folders (both an EDU and a CH30 folder) shown in BDN and in some instances two paper folders. In this situation, RPOs had two options: to retain both folders, or to destroy one folder and retain only one folder. If the RPO decided to destroy a folder, the RPO needed to request and adjustment in BDN from the Records Management Center (RMC). A claimant should only have one electronic folder.

NOTE 2: The multiple folders in BIRLS (Benefits Information Record Location System) or BDN occurred because before October 1, 2000, the CEST (Claims Establishment) processing for Chapter 30 caused BIRLS to indicate the existence of a CH30 folder and the CEST processing for Chapters 32 and 1606 caused BIRLS to indicate the existence of an EDU folder. CEST processing was changed effective October 1, 2000, to automatically create only an EDU folder in BIRLS for these education benefits.

(5) Dependents' Educational Assistance (DEA) Folder. This is a two-part blue paper folder created for Chapter 35 benefits. These paper folders are seldom used except in rare situations when a dependent has no SSN (Social Security Number), there is a second (subsequent) spouse, or a 10th (subsequent) child and TIMS is unable to support the paperless folder.

d. Folder Required. All claims are required to have the TIMS folder before any award is processed. VCEs (Veteran Claim Examiner)  should follow local RPO procedures regarding folder transfer procedures. The RPO may process a hardship claim working with the RPO where the current folder is located to review the file for any discrepancies, and request expedition of the folder to the new jurisdiction.

1.02  JURISDICTION - REGIONAL PROCESSING OFFICE (RPO)

a. General. The following jurisdiction rules apply for all education benefits except Section 901:

(1) School Known. If the claim is for enrollment in a specific school or training establishment, the RPO with jurisdiction over the school (or the primary school in situations where the claimant is attending more than one facility) will have jurisdiction over the claim. This does not apply to correspondence training or licensing and certification tests. Guest Student certifications (transient or secondary school locations) should be processed by the RPO having jurisdiction over the school that will confer the degree, diploma or certificate for the program being pursued.

(2) School Not Known. If the claim does not specify enrollment in a particular school or training establishment, the RPO with jurisdiction over the claimant’s stated residence will have jurisdiction over the claim. If an enrollment certification is later received showing jurisdiction of a different RPO, the claimant's folder will be transferred to the RPO having jurisdiction over the school's address. Once an enrollment certification is received, the school will dictate the actual RPO jurisdiction. RPOs should appropriately transfer the electronic claims folder for processing.

(3) Correspondence School. If the claim is for enrollment in a correspondence or combination correspondence and residence school, the RPO with jurisdiction over the claimant's stated residence will have jurisdiction over the claim.

(4) Licensing and Certification Tests and National Tests. The RPO with an existing education folder will have jurisdiction over the licensing and certification or national test claim. If no education folder exists, the RPO with jurisdiction over the claimant's stated residence will have jurisdiction.

(5) Tuition Assistance Top-Up: The RPO with an existing education folder will have jurisdiction over any tuition assistance top-up claim.  If no education folder exists, the RPO with jurisdiction over the beneficiary's residence address will have jurisdiction.

(6) Original Claims—Army Post Office (APO), Diplomatic Post Office (DPO), and Fleet Post Office (FPO) Addresses. If the claimant has an APO, DPO, or FPO address, the above rules will not apply. Jurisdiction will be determined as follows:

  • Buffalo – APO/DPO/FPO AA (Armed Forces Americas) and APO/FP AE (Armed Forces Europe, Canada, Middle East, Africa)
  • Muskogee -- APO/DPO/FPO AP (Armed Forces Pacific)

SUBCHAPTER II.  MAIL AND FOLDER PROCEDURES


1.07  MAIL ANALYSIS

a.  General.  The overall mail management program is the responsibility of the Support Services Division following the procedures in M23-1, part I, Chapter 1. Education mail is any form with a 22 prefix or any document that pertains to a benefit covered by this manual. All mail must be properly analyzed to determine the appropriate action. Some mail must be controlled by a pending issue in the BDN.

b.  Electronic Processing.  Documents identified as education mail are scanned and indexed for electronic processing. The paper document may be destroyed six months after scanning according to the Records Control Schedule (RCS) reports. For additional issues regarding Upstream processing, refer to Part 3: Chapter 14 - Upstream Manual .

NOTE 1: The RPOs receive most claims through an online process [i.e. VONAPP (Veterans Online Application) or vets.gov] and enrollment certifications and notices of change in student status through the Veteran Administration Online Certification of Enrollment System (VA-ONCE) rather than by paper mail. Electronic certifications received through VA-ONCE are processed through BDN to some extent by the Electronic Certification Automated Program (ECAP). In some cases, ECAP will set up the pending issue. The electronic data from these claims and certifications is retained in the claimant's TIMS folder.

NOTE 2: Chapter 33 VA-ONCE [and information from WEAMS (Web Enabled Approval Management System] files are handled by EIP (Enterprise Integration Pattern/Process) which is a part of the Long Term Solution (LTS). This complete system facilitates an end-to-end process which will establish and clear an end product when fully automated or will only establish the end product control when the claim is "off-ramped" for additional review and manual processing by a VCE/SVCE (Senior Veterans Claim Examiner). There is no electronic data of the pending issue captured into the TIMS folder for claims that are fully automated. Only the enrollment certification or changes to enrollment documents are downloaded from a file into the TIMS folder.

1.08  DIVISION MAIL MANAGEMENT

Once the Support Services Division delivers the paper mail to the Education Division, the Upstream section is responsible for ensuring the mail is associated with an education claims folder.

NOTE: Each RPO may locally determine who will perform each action below, presuming the VA Employee has received the appropriate training and has the appropriate commands. The use of the phrase of Upstream team and/or Program Support Assistants (PSAs) is to provide basic guidance.

a.  There are 4 types of incoming paper mail:

(1) Active mail which includes all forms and letters not considered priority mail and received from outside the Education division. Active mail could be received via Post Office, fax machine, or other sources.

(2) File mail which includes BDN/LTS-generated award or denial letters and copies of other letters to be filed in the TIMS folder. All BDN/LTS-generated award or denial letters received in paper form are to be placed in the claimant's folder within 60 days of date of receipt or 90 days during extreme heavy peak periods.

(3) Priority mail which requires immediate processing. Priority processing could include but is not limited to Veterans and claimants under extreme financial hardship, homeless claimants, special requests determined by the Education Officer or the RO Director.

(4) Unidentified mail includes any mail that does not identify the claimant or cannot be associated with any claimant.

b.  The Upstream section is generally responsible for batching the mail into categories as defined by the RPO, scanning the active mail into TIMS, indexing the mail into the appropriate TIMS folders which ensures the work is assigned through TIMS. File mail is also batched and scanned into TIMS but as drop mail and is not assigned to processing.

c.  The Upstream section is also generally responsible for establishing control in BDN for all active mail. The employees assigned should CEST appropriate original end products when indexing and creating new folders in TIMS. All other active mail should be placed under control in TIMS within 7 days from the date of receipt. However, due to the nature of Education work, during extreme heavy volume periods, this may be expanded to 14 days. If additional time is needed to place all mail under control in TIMS is needed, the RPO Director must coordinate the request with the Director of Education Service or his/her designee.

d.  If mail is unable to be associated with one particular claimant (herein called unidentified mail), the document(s) must be returned to the sender with a cover letter explaining the reason for the return. See Figure 1.03, Return Mail Cover Letter Sample. If the correspondence cannot be returned to the sender, the RPO must retain the document according to the RCS Schedule.

1.09  UNDELIVERABLE MAIL PROCEDURES

In April 2020, all returned mail was forwarded to the CM Portal. In order to process the returned mail, RPOs will need to separate the work into three categories during the indexing stage in The Image Management System (TIMS). 

Informational System Warning: All letters sent to the print vendor through DGI(LTS) are mailed using United States Postal Service (USPS) automated batch mailing. This runs letters through the USPS database to ensure that any forwarding address for the recipient is used. There is no way for EDU to know that the mail is being forwarded automatically unless it is returned as undeliverable. If returned mail has a different address than what our records show was used to send the letter, typically the cause is a forwarding address on file with USPS.

a.  DEFINITIONS

1.  Actionable Returned Mail: There are two types of actionable mail. (formerly known as essential) 

Letters which require a response from the beneficiary (i.e. Development letters) 

Letters in which a decision is made on eligibility or payment or that may have an adverse impact on the beneficiary. 

Examples of actionable returned mail include, but are not limited to, development letters, debt notices, award adjustment letters (i.e. adverse action letters), letters developing for mitigating circumstances, and any award letter which grants eligibility, etc. Notices of eligibility and entitlement, including Certificates of Eligibility or supplemental award letters are also considered to be actionable returned mail. 

2.  Non-Actionable Returned Mail: Any letter serving as a notification to the beneficiary and requires no action on their part and has no adverse impact on the beneficiary.

Typically, these are informational payment notifications, Cost of Living Adjustment (COLA) notices, new legislation notices, etc. 

Mail deemed Non-Actionable Returned mail will not require additional review. This type of mail may be closed in TIMS without further action. 

3.  Returned School Mail: Any letter which was addressed to a school (i.e. training establishment or State Approving Agency) associated with one beneficiary. 

b.  UPSTREAM PROCEDURES 

Mail in the CM portal is the responsibility of the RPO Upstream section. This section is responsible for identifying what document type to use and where to route each token. While daily sorting is not required, it is encouraged to ensure the flow of the returned mail remains timely. 

The three categories of mail must be identified by the Program Support Assistant (PSA)

1.  Development letters

(A)  Open Claim Token. Once reviewed and added by the PSA as a “DEVLTR”, will be routed in TIMS to the VCE’s queue who requested the information or will attach to an open token if in an aggregate group queue.

(B)  Closed Claim Token. Since the beneficiary’s record has been closed, a Veteran Claims Examiner (VCE) must review this development letter. The PSA will index this returned letter as “DEVLTR” and route it to a new queue entitled “DEVLTR”.

2.  Actionable letters

(A)  Open Claim Token. Once reviewed and added by the PSA as a “ERTN”, will be routed in TIMS to the VCE’s queue who has the open claim token or to an aggregate queue.

(B)  Closed Claim Token. Since the beneficiary’s record has been closed, this letter must be reviewed to determine why it was returned. The PSA will index it as “ERTN” and it will be routed to the Returned Mail queue for VCE processing.

3.  Non-Actionable letters

All letters which meet the description of non-actionable letters will be uploaded from CM and indexed as “NONLET”. No claim token is needed as these are considered scan/drop.

NOTE: The document “NONLET” in TIMS should be labelled “scan/drop”.

c.  CLAIMS PROCESSING

1.  All Actionable Return Mail. Development letters (DEVLTR) and Actionable Returned Mail (ERTN) should be treated the same by VCEs. RPO management is expected to ensure the DEVLTR queue is managed on a weekly basis and letters are reviewed in a timely manner. Any time return mail is resent, according to the following procedures, the attached Returned Mail Cover Letter should be adapted and used.

2.  Address correct, formatting issues. The VCE needs to validate the mail was returned for either an obvious formatting error or grammar issues. Additionally, the VCE should ensure that applicable room, floor, apartment, suite, etc numbers are included after the street address, preferably on the same line. If the mail was returned only because it is obvious the address failed to display properly in the envelope window or something specifically cosmetic, the VCE should ensure BDN/LTS (the system from which the letter was generated) is correct in displaying the address. VCEs should follow current procedures to release a copy of the returned letter to the beneficiary.

3.  Forwarding label contains new address. The VCE must review the address in all appropriate VA systems which may contain a usable address.

NOTE 1: When the phrase “all appropriate VA systems” is used, it includes LTS, BDN, FOCAS, SHARE, TIMS, VBMS, etc. Any VA system which may have an address is acceptable.

  • If the address has already been updated, place a remark next to the returned letter in the TIMS folder stating, “Letter dated mm-dd-yyyy returned, letter resent to new address on mm-dd-yyyy”.
  • VCEs should follow current procedures to release a copy of the returned letter to the beneficiary.

NOTE 2: Verify date on forwarding label; if the address has been updated after this date (review BDN M23 screen – look for 22 or 25 transaction code) do not change the address.

If the address has not been updated, update the address in all Education systems (to which VCE has access) and follow current procedures to release a copy of the returned letter to the beneficiary.

If there is an End Product Code (EPC) pending, the suspense date should be updated for an additional 30 days from the date the letter was reissued. If the EPC has already been cleared, do not re-establish an EPC.

4.  No new address provided from Post Office. The VCE should review the address in all appropriate VA systems.

(A)  If a different address is found, update Education systems with the correct address.

  • Create a remark next to the ERTN document in the TIMS folder stating; “Letter dated mm-dd-yyyy returned, letter resent to new address on mm-dd-yyyy”.
  • VCEs should follow current procedures to release a copy of the returned letter to the beneficiary.”

(B)  If a more current address is not found, the VCE should review TIMS or LTS for a current email address.

(1) Email address found. If an email address is found, the letter may be sent to the beneficiary following current procedures.

NOTE: If the letter sent to an email address is returned, follow the procedures for paper returned mail or move to step below.

(C)  No email address found. The VCE should attempt to contact the beneficiary via the phone and document the event using either a VA Form 119 or 0820 in TIMS. If the VCE leaves a voice message, the message should include the following:

This is XXX from the Department of Veterans Affairs regarding your education benefits. Before a decision can be made regarding your claim, we must have your current mailing address. Please call 1-888-GIBILL1 and update your address as soon as possible. Unfortunately, without a current mailing address, we may be unable to complete the processing of your claim.

NOTE:  All phone calls to contact the beneficiary (successful or not) must be documented in the beneficiary’s TIMS folder as stated above.

i.  If the beneficiary answers the phone, the VCE should confirm the identity of the beneficiary by requesting information regarding enrollment. (NOTE: VCEs must NEVER give Personal Identifiable Information (PII) over the phone. VCEs may confirm information from the beneficiary only.)

ii.  Once the beneficiary has been appropriately identified, the VCE should update the mailing address as well as an email address but also inform the beneficiary what is needed in order to complete the claim. In certain circumstances, the verbal contact with the beneficiary may suffice and resolve the development issue.

5.  No New Address Found: When the address cannot be located, and beneficiary has not responded to phone contact, AND a response is required from beneficiary, the VCE should make a decision on the claim based on facts found in the TIMS file. In rare situations, disallowing as failure to furnish or whereabouts unknown may be appropriate. If a decision has already been made and notice released, no action is necessary. Annotate in remarks of DEVLTR “Undeliverable (mm-dd-yyyy)”.

NOTE: The VCE should allow the award decision letter to issue to the last known address. This will result in additional returned mail which will require processing to review again, but it will also indicate VA has continued in “good faith” to notify the beneficiary.

6.  Returned School Mail. Schools have requested using the school email address since the paperless initiative began. Please refer to the System Advisory: VA-ONCE Workaround to Assist with Paperless Processing Due to COVID-19, dated April 23, 2020.

Whenever possible, the letter which was returned should be emailed to the address in WEAMS or printed on enrollment certifications from VA ONCE.

TIMS must be updated with a remark next to the ERTN, stating the letter was released to the email address and date.

NOTE: In rare situations, when the school mailing address is wrong AND there is no known email address, the VCE must contact the ELR of jurisdiction for additional guidance.

7.  Non-Actionable letters. If a VCE sees a document labelled “NONLET” when processing a claim, they do not need to research the correct address.

1.10  REMOVAL OF MATERIAL IN FOLDERS

Only documents and forms with probative value will be maintained in a claimant's education folder. Dispose of material which does not directly support a claim or a VA award action, including duplicate or redundant material, in accordance with Records Control Schedule VB 1, Part I, Item # 13-052.200.

NOTE: All individuals who handle education claims and folders should be careful to not destroy or discard any material which may have value to the claimant. When such material is no longer necessary for documentation, return the material to the claimant along with an explanatory letter.

1.11 CONTROL OVER SENSITIVE CASES

All Veterans and their family members have a right to privacy by virtue of their (or their family members’) service and are guaranteed that right through laws and regulations (e.g. 38 CFR 1.576, 5 U.S.C 522a, etc.). They have the right to expect VBA employees and users will only access records as the result of a legitimate business need. VBA has put into place internal controls to prevent unauthorized disclosure by limiting access to records based on business need and monitoring/recording access to these records.

As provided in 1.04 of this chapter, Veteran-employee folders will be processed at stations other than the home station. In addition, records in BDN, Share, DGI, Benefits Manager (BM), and The Image Management System (TIMS) will be sensitized to prevent unauthorized access.  Records for Veteran-employees, relatives of Veteran-employees, Office of General Counsel (OGC) employees, and Power of Attorney (POA) users located at a VBA facility will be assigned sensitivity level “3” in TIMS and sensitivity level “7” in BDN, Share, DGI, and BM.

1.13  PENDING ISSUE CONTROL

Pending issues in BDN must be established for all formal claims (see Part 3: Chapter 3 - Processing Applications for Benefits ) and for all other issues that require adjudicative action. A Pending Issue File (PIF) is established in BDN through the CEST and CADJ (Claims Adjudication) commands. For more information regarding CEST, refer to Part 3: Chapter 14 - Upstream Manual Subchapter 5. Pending issue controls are cleared under the CAUT (Claims Authorization) and PCLR (Pending Issue Clear) commands. Pending issues are cancelled using the PCAN (Pending Issue Canceled) command.

a. Suspense Dates. Every pending claim or issue is assigned an EPC (End Product Code) which has an automatic suspense date (i.e., date when the pending issue requires review). Currently, every pending EPC will have an automatic suspense date of 30 days except for EPC 270 (Notice of Disagreement) which will have a suspense date of 60 days. If the automatically generated control period is inappropriate for a particular issue, enter the desired suspense date in the SUSPENSE DATE field on the 101 screen. (See M22-3, Appendix A for a list of the EPCs for education issues.)

b. Date of Claim. The date of claim entry required on the BDN 101 screen will be the earliest date of receipt in any VA facility, to include eBenefits and scanning vendor sites. VA employees should identify this date from the earliest VA date stamp or equivalent. (This is not necessarily the date of claim in the legal sense. See Part 3: Chapter 3 - Processing Applications for Benefits). If multiple documents are received on one claim, use the earliest date of receipt as the date of claim. In some cases, it is appropriate to use the date an issue arose as the date of claim as explained in examples 2 and 3 that follow.

EXAMPLE 1: An application is received in the Buffalo RPO on July 1, but not actually received in the Muskogee RPO (the office of jurisdiction) until July 15. When establishing the pending issue control, the Muskogee office must use July 1 as the date of claim.

EXAMPLE 2: VA receives an enrollment certification for reenrollment in the same program. There is no claim (in the legal sense). Therefore, use the date the enrollment certification was received in the RPO for the date of claim entry.

EXAMPLE 3: Education Service forwards a "fugitive felon" case to the RPO for action. Education Service received the referral from the OIG. In this situation, the RPO should use the date of referral from the Education Service as the date of claim.

c. Timeliness. All mail should be reviewed and placed in TIMS within 7 calendar days from the date of receipt. A PIF must be established for original claims within this period (if one does not already exist). This is true even if a claim cannot be adjudicated within the 7-day period. However, before establishment of the pending issue, all master records and PIFs already existing for the file number should be reviewed through the SINQ (Status Inquiry) command. This will reduce the possibility of the establishment of an improper end product. The 7-day timeliness period will not be extended if a pending issue must be changed or canceled and reestablished.

d. Final Disposition of BDN Pending Issue Control. Leave the control pending in BDN until final resolution of all related issues.

e. ECAP. In some case, ECAP establishes the pending issue but VCEs must process the award. VCEs need to review for the existence of additional master records and pending issues to reduce the possibility of an improperly established end product. See  OLAF (ON–LINE APPROVAL FILE) FOR WINDOWS (Training Guide 22-99-4) for background on ECAP.

1.14  FUTURE DIARY CONTROL

a. A BDN future diary control is generally used when a control period of more than 30 days is appropriate.

FIGURE 1.05 FUTURE PENDING ISSUE CONTROL ESTABLISHMENT SCREEN

b. When establishing a future diary control, the EPC entered on the Ready screen is the appropriate supplemental end-product based on the benefit when the diary matures. This will help identify these types of claims easier on work management reports. If a diary cannot be established in BDN because the EPC has or is being PCLRd, the VCE should establish the proper diary the following day.

On the 101 screen, control code 39 is entered in the FUTURE DATE REASON field, and the desired suspense date (BDN has a limitation of at least 10 calendar days but not more than 1 year in the future) is entered in the SUSPENSE DATE field.

NOTE: If the diary cannot be established in BDN because the future date is less than 10 days from “today’s” date, the VCE should use the original date received as the date of claim in this situation.

      c. Upon maturity, the future diary control converts to a pending issue control which may be cleared under the CAUT or PCLR command or canceled under the PCAN command. Before maturity, a future diary control may be canceled under the PCAN command.

  • O-L/S- Used for lump sum awards under Non-Chapter 33 benefits. The diary is set for the first day of the term as indicated on the enrollment certification.
  • OIGRD- Used to identify the end of the period the school has indicated the student has to complete an incomplete grade OR one year from the date the term ends, whichever is earlier.
  • OFUTR- Used to diary first Chapter 35 awards or any future terms for other benefits**. The diary is set for the first day of the term as indicated on the enrollment certification. ** for enrollment certifications received for terms which start more than 120 or 180 days in the future, refer to Part 4: Chapter 1 - Enrollment Certification for processing instructions.
  • O-RAD- Used to track a future RAD if more than 10 days from today’s date.
  • O-FGN- Used in Foreign School Currency conversions. Use the rate effective on the first day of the month of July that precedes the beginning date of the individual’s enrollment period.

EXAMPLE 1: The VCE previously set the suspense date as 05/01/2016. On March 1, 2016, the VCE reviews the EP after receiving the information on February 25, 2016, needed to process an award. The VCE should cancel the pending issue (using the PCAN command) as it has not yet matured. Then the VCE should process an award with a supplemental EP with a date of claim of February 25, 2016.

EXAMPLE 2: The VCE previously set the suspense date as May 1, 2016. On May 10, 2016, the VCE reviews the EP after receiving the information needed on May 2, 2016, to process an award. The VCE can use the EP previously set by the pending issue as it “matured” on May 1, 2016. The VCE should process an award with the supplemental EP previously established by the pending issue.

1.15  TIMS CONTROLS

VCEs should use TIMS to supplement BDN controls or as a sole means of control when BDN cannot be used. There are two methods in TIMS for establishing controls: Await Mail and Individual Suspense. Await Mail is generally used when the control date is more than 30 days in the future. Individual Suspense is generally used when the control date is less than 30 days.

1.19  SINGLE SIGNATURE AUTHORITY

The Education Officer, at his or her discretion, may designate single signature authority. This authority can be to specific individuals or for specific types of actions. Such authority allows individuals to approve award actions with only the signature of a Veterans Claim Examiner (VCE). Single signature authority will be given based on demonstrated competence in education award processing. The actions listed below, however, must be reviewed and signed by two or more persons unless otherwise indicated:

NOTE: The use of Blind Sign awards is not permissible. Any award authorized as eligible for single signature but BDN/LTS rejects must have two signatures for authorization

a. Original basic eligibility determinations. It should be noted ALL original awards MUST have two signatures. Original Chapter 35 COEs must have two signatures.
b. Changes in basic eligibility. Changes in basic eligibility include any change in which eligibility was modified, i.e. a change in Veterans Information System (VIS), service dates, etc. An example for chapter 33 would be any change on the Service Data Screen or the Entitlement & Kickers Screen.
c. Awards resulting from administrative decisions
d. Awards involving use of an override indicator for the monthly rate in non-chapter 33 awards
e. Awards involving incarcerated claimants
f. Awards involving entitlement exhausted.
g. Awards involving extensions of delimiting date.
h. Awards over big payment thresholds as in Section 1.20 below.

NOTE: If adding or modifying service periods does not change a claimant's benefit level, entitlement, delimiting date or impact payments, a second signature is not required.  

For information regarding procedures for granting or removing single signature authority, refer to M22-3 Appendix B.

1.20  EDUCATION BIG PAY REVIEW

(Updated March 2025)

Education payments over specific thresholds are mandated to have multiple levels of approval before payment should be issued. This includes any payment using the Benefit Delivery Network (BDN) CERT command or fiscal transactions done by other divisions, such as Support Services. Each benefit has a different threshold; however, in every situation the Regional Processing Office (RPO) must adhere to the following procedures.

a. Definition. For the purposes of this section, the phrase “big pay” means any big or large retroactive payment, in which an award generates one or more payments cumulatively which reaches the threshold amounts.  Due to system limitations some multiple awards or multiple payments are released concurrently. It is the cumulative total of the payments which drives the decision regarding when “big pay” procedures should be followed.

b. Appropriate Review levels. Big pay awards require, at minimum, a third level of review. The process of big pay review is the same regardless of the benefit type. The only difference is the definition of the dollar amount by benefit type. A third level of review mandates three different persons have reviewed, approved, and concurred on the award or awards. The RPO can determine proper level of delegation regarding the three levels. Typically, the next level approval should come from a higher graded employee. However, depending on the experience and grade level of the first person, the other levels for approval and concurrence can be Senior VCEs, Lead SVCEs, or Education Quality Training Specialists (EQTS). RPOs can include Supervisory VCEs as a final approver as necessary.

Due to limitations within the systems and approval access, the GS-11 employee or higher must be the person who authorizes the award, once ALL appropriate concurrence is on the note in the file.

When a fourth level approver is required, this must be a Supervisory VCE.

c. Big Pay Thresholds:

1. The following table reflects when additional signatures are required for any award action which meets or exceeds Big Pay thresholds:

NOTE: When the BDN payment screen exceeds award line limitation to deliver payments for an entire non-chapter 33 award, the RPO may use multiple end products, but only one original or one supplemental EP to authorize big payment awards. Subsequent end products must be a 930 EP.

NOTE: Under Chapter 33, a VCE may process a single payment to a school if it is less than $45,000 and may process a single payment to an individual if it is less than $25,000, without additional signatures.

d. LTS and BDN system limitations.  Due to multiple system limitations in both the Long Term Solution (LTS) and BDN, VCEs may find it necessary to split the award lines into multiple work products (WPs) to allow the proper payment(s) to release. VCEs should never use the practice of breaking up big payment awards on multiple work products to justify NOT requiring proper signature authorization

A big or large retroactive payment is an award where the cumulative payment reaches the threshold amounts.  As an example, if several single payments appear on the same award, the cumulative amount would, therefore, qualify as a big pay and must adhere to proper authorization procedures.  

LTS payments currently will fail if a payment over $99,999.99 is attempted. Due to this system limitation, it is acceptable to break the payments into multiple work products to ensure the payments are authorized. One of the payments must be over the threshold but under $99,999.99. The remaining payments may be under the threshold. However, the requirement for signature authorization remains for the entire cumulative total of payment due to the student or school.

EXAMPLE:   Chapter 33 Tuition and Fees (T&F) amount totals $120,000
                              First WP is authorized with T&F input as $95,000
                              Second WP is authorized with T&F corrected to $120,000 (releasing the additional $25,000)
                              Total T&F paid to school is $120,000

When processing chapter 33 claims with future maturation dates, and the award exceeds the threshold, process the claim following all big pay procedures.  After the award is authorized in LTS, suspend the claim in The Image Management System (TIMS) until the maturation date. (There is no need to establish a diary/future end product in BDN for additional control).  At this time, appropriate station and individual end product credit are authorized.

On the maturation date, the VCE should ensure the first payment was released.  On a new WP, correct the T&F to the total amount reported, to release the remaining T&F.  Suppress the letter, authorize LTS and follow RPO guidance to alert the Director’s office (Refer to paragraph f). Individual and station end product credit are authorized.

NOTE: When using multiple WPs, the final letter must be adjusted to reflect all of the enrollment terms.  **Pro Tip: Save the first letter showing the full amount, prior to reducing the T&F.

e. Authorization Procedures for Big Pay.  Once the big pay threshold has been met, the VCE must create an audit worksheet for non-chapter 33 benefits to verify the payment amount is correct. For chapter 33 benefits, the payment sheet LTS creates suffices. The VCE should capture this document as MISC in the claimant’s TIMS folder. The VCE should follow local RPO guidance regarding where to route the TIMS folder for authorization.

NOTE: All supporting documents for the payment must be captured in the TIMS folder. This includes T-sheets from the Flight On-the-Job Correspondence Apprenticeship (FOCAS) system and the authorized Work Product Summary (WPS) screen from LTS. The information contained with the T-Sheet and LTS must include the term dates, proof of the monthly certification (non-chapter 33) and total payment amount being released.

The VCE should create a note in TIMS which includes the following:

  • Beginning and ending dates of the award
  • Facility name and facility code
  • Cumulative payment amount
  • Verification the audit worksheet (if required) is correct and date of case review by all levels of review assigned by RPO leadership

The second, third and, when required, fourth level reviewer will verify all the previous steps including the audit worksheet and note in the TIMS folder are completed and accurate. All levels of appropriate authorization are expected to add concurrence to the note, and annotate comments, if applicable. Initials of each reviewer and the date reviewed must be added to the Big Pay note at each level of review.

Once final review is completed and the note has been properly updated to include all reviewers’ information, the payment may be authorized and released by the appropriate authorizer. Follow local RPO procedures.

The authorizer must ensure all proper letters have been released and should finish the claim token as appropriate. As stated above, before the award is authorized, all appropriate approvals must be annotated on the note in TIMS.

f. Notice to the Director’s Office. After authorization, the appropriate reviewer will send notification to the Director’s office of the award following local RPO procedures. Each RPO should include an email notification to the EDU Quality Assurance team.  All information regarding the big pay must be sent to the Director’s office within 3 business days of the claim being authorized. The Director’s office must archive the documents as appropriate. RPOs are encouraged to follow local procedures to ensure VCEs receive appropriate end product credit for all big pay awards. 

g. Director’s Review of Big Payments. To strengthen the integrity of the Education program, an additional review at the Director level is required for all payments that exceed $25,000 for student payments and $45,000 for school payments.

1. Each RPO will receive an e-mail report of cases where education payments over $25,000 (or $45,000 as appropriate) were released. This report is generated from Data and Information Systems (D&IS). The e-mails are sent to a mailbox established for this purpose at each RPO named VAVBALLL/EDUPMT, with LLL representing the station identifier. These e-mails are sent on a regular basis and will generally not exceed two per week. Each e-mail includes the payments for review along with the checklist below each payment. If no payment greater than the designated amount was generated for the period covered then the RPO will receive a negative e-mail report.

2. The RPO Director is responsible for ensuring the checklist, is completed for each case on the e-mail.

3. If there is a “no” response to any of these questions, especially if there is no documentation to support the payment, and it is suspected there was a fraudulent payment, the RPO Director’s office should contact the Inspector General’s office, and the EDU Quality Assurance team. Provide a brief explanation of the problem(s) found.

4. RPO Directors must ensure each e-mail will be reviewed, with a response completed, no later than 15 days from the day the list is generated. The response should be returned to the EDU Quality Assurance team.

5. Completed e-mails, signed, and sent by the Director or Assistant Director must be printed and maintained in chronological order in the Director’s office so they are available for review upon request.

6. Each RPO must conduct an annual Strategic Oversight and Analysis Review (SOAR) on the review process of these large payments. When reporting local findings, include an analysis of the third or fourth signature procedures, the accuracy of payments, and outline a plan of action to address inaccuracies and fraud.  Follow the procedures outlined in M22-3, chapter 4 regarding the SOAR.
