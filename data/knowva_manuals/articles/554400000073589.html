5.01  CHAPTER 32 NOE PROCESSING - GENERAL

a.  After chapter 32 awards or other transactions are processed, they are sent to the TCS (BDN Central System) for further editing and storage.  TCS generates chapter 32 reject, action, and informational NOE (Notice of Exception) messages upon completion of benefit update processing.  These messages are transmitted to the appropriate BDNC (Benefit Delivery Network Center).  After further processing, the messages are available for regional office retrieval.  Regional office personnel should obtain NOE messages on a daily basis.  See part II, chapter 8, for procedures for using the NOES (Notice of Exception Subsystem) command to retrieve messages.

b.  Each BDNC only retains NOE messages for 30 processing days.  These centers also compare every message against the PIF (Pending Issue File) to determine whether affected pending issues should be cleared or updated (to reflect a reject).

(1)  If a pending issue exists and it is "pending acceptance" and no message is posted for that master record when BDN completes update processing and produces messages, BDN will clear the pending issue.

(2)  If a pending issue exists and it is "pending acceptance" and a message is posted for that master record, BDN will determine whether to clear the pending issue based on the message number.

(i)  If the message is in the 8000-series range, BDN clears the pending issue as these messages do not indicate a rejected transaction

(ii)  If the message is not in the 8000-series range and was generated by award processing, BDN updates but does not clear the pending issue.  The regional office must either cancel the pending issue or clear the pending issue through generation of a corrected transaction.

NOTE 1:  Some of the messages in this chapter cannot be issued as the result of input from a BDN terminal; however, these messages can be issued as a result of DOD (Department of Defense) changes to a claimant's  Contributions Account.

NOTE 2:  Corrections (02V transactions) to some fields in master records can be made directly on a BDN screen.  See part II, paragraph 8.13, for allowable entries.

2950

ACTION - REVIEW AWARDS - CONTRIBUTIONS ADJ FOR MMM-YY

NOTE:  MMM = a three-letter abbreviation for a month; Y = the year.

Explanation:  Bank file processing has caused a change to the master record.  One or more allotments have been changed.  The month and year shown in the NOE legend reflect the earliest allotment affected.  The master record contained award lines which extended beyond adjusted allotment month(s), so the master record's monthly rates may require adjustment.  BDN could not automatically adjust the rate(s).

Action:  Carefully review the claimant's folder and master record.  Award action may be necessary to adjust the monthly pay rates in existing periods.  (See par. 4.18g.)  Identify the changes to the Contributions Account and compare the affected allotment months to the claimant's award periods.  If the allotment adjustment changes the monthly rate payable in any award line, process an amended award, repeating the affected award line and all subsequent award lines (with the same award information).  BDN will then recalculate the monthly rates.  The rates are likely to require adjustment in the following instances:

The allotment adjustment increased the claimant's original number of monthly contributions (from less than 36 to more than 36); or

The allotment adjustment decreased the claimant's original number of monthly contributions (from more than 36 to less than 36); or

The allotment adjustment causes the Contributions Account to include allotments of differing amounts (e.g., before adjustment all allotments were $50 and after adjustment there are three new allotments of $65); or

The Contributions Account previously contained allotments of differing amounts, and the adjustment changed the total number of allotments.

If the allotment adjustment does not change the existing monthly rate of any award line, no action is necessary.

See footnote 1.

ACTION - AWARD SUSPENDED FOR CONTRIBUTIONS ADJUSTMENT

NOTE:  This message can be issued when due process must be followed as provided by Public Law 100-203, The Computer Matching and Privacy Protection Act of 1988.  This legislation provides that VA may not take adverse action to a benefit recipient based on information received by an agency from an automated system of another agency until VA notifies the recipient of the pending action and the information received from the automated system is verified independently.  (See pt. IV, par. 13.14.)

Action:  Process an amended award to remove the suspense only after carefully reviewing the claimant's record to determine the proper enrollment period (or periods) to be included in the amended award.  If the allotment months which have been adjusted fall within an award period, resubmit that award period and all subsequent award periods.  If the allotment months which have been adjusted do not fall in an award period, all later award periods should be resubmitted.  If school changes or course objective changes are involved, multiple award actions will be required.  The processing of the amended award action will allow BDN to recalculate the monthly rates and adjust the ending date so that the claimant does not use excessive entitlement.

INFO - ADVANCE PAY NOT PAID

Explanation:  BDN processed an advance pay award and computed the payment amount.  The claimant had an accounts receivable which completely offset the advance pay amount, so no check was released.  Also, no COD form was sent to the school.

Action:  Upon receipt, route the message to VSD for confirmation of school attendance.  After obtaining school attendance information, VSD will annotate the message and return it as follows:

If there is no change in school attendance, VSD will return the message to the Finance activity.  The Finance activity will submit the certification.

If there is a change in school attendance, VSD will return the message to the Adjudication Division for processing an amended award or a "quit" certification as appropriate.  Any award prepared should lift the COD bar.

8909

INFO - DISALLOWANCE PROCESSED BUT NO SYSTEM LETTER GENERATED

Explanation:  BDN did not generate a disallowance letter because of the entered reason code(s).

Action:  Associate the message with the claimant's folder.  Determine if a dictated letter was appropriate and whether one was sent to the claimant.  If a letter has not been sent, send an appropriate dictated letter.

FOOTNOTES:

1       Messages 2950, 2951, 2952, 2953, and 2960 involve changes to a claimant's Contributions Account.  To properly review these messages, it is necessary to determine which allotment months have been affected, to what extent that the allotment months have changed, and whether an adjustment (i.e., increase or decrease) is necessary.  Maintain a current copy of the M27 screen (and the M28 screen if the claimant has a DOD contribution) in the claimant's folder as a normal part of the chapter 32 claim documentation.  Review these screen prints during routine claims processing.   If the Contributions Account has changed or the existing prints are more than 6 months old, print the appropriate screen(s) to replace the screen(s) in the claimant's folder.   In many instances, the Contributions Account changes which generate the above-mentioned messages will affect all award lines and rates.  In such instances, any amended award action must repeat all award periods.

        When allotments are being made at a low monthly rate (e.g., $25 per month), the allotments can extend beyond a 6-year period.  However, BDN has a 6-year maximum field for allotments.  If additional allotments are received (to exceed the 6-year maximum), BDN posts these allotments to retroactive months.  A retroactive award should not be made in such a case because the new allotments only affect remaining entitlement.  BDN automatically adjusts the award DLP (Date Last Paid) and issues a message.  File the message without action provided that BDN had properly adjusted the award DLP.

2       The transaction code for messages 8024, 8025, and 8026 will be 88G.  This code signifies a system-generated transaction which occurs when a Bank file processing action adjusts a claimant's name or file number (or merges two Contributions Accounts), and the master record also includes a Benefit Account.  The 88G transaction updates the Benefit Account so it reflects the change(s) made during Bank file processing.
