4.01 GENERAL

a. Individuals eligible under the various laws described in this manual have a period of eligibility during which they must use their entitlement. The first day after an individual's period of eligibility expires is known as his or her delimiting date.

Effective August 18, 2017, per Public Law 115-48, individuals who qualify under Chapter 33 benefits and separate from Active Duty with an Honorable discharge after January 1, 2013, do not have a period of eligibility to complete their education benefits.  These individuals do not have a delimiting date and are, therefore, not subject to delimiting date restrictions. 

b. An individual's period of eligibility may be extended under certain situations. Extensions to an individual's period of eligibility are frequently called "delimiting date extensions." Figure 4.01 provides a summary of the various types of delimiting date extensions for claimants under Chapters 33, 30, 32, 35, 1606, 1607, and Section 903. Other paragraphs in this chapter explain each type of extension.

c. Reservists receiving benefits under Chapter 1606 do not have a delimiting date if they remain actively drilling.  Once they are no longer drilling, they are no longer entitled to benefits and do not have a period of eligibility unless one of the specific situations outlined below occurs.

NOTE: In Ozer v. Principi, the United States Court of Appeals for Veterans Claims (CAVC) decided Chapter 35 surviving spouses do not have a delimiting date. Prior to the Ozer decision, both spouses and surviving spouses had a 10-year window to use their Chapter 35 benefits.  The Ozer decision was invalidated by Public Law 107-103, which reinstated the delimiting date for spouses effective December 28, 2001. Therefore, a delimiting date extension remains an issue for Chapter 35 spouses. Those affected by the Ozer decision, continue to not have a delimiting date as long as they have not changed their program.

4.02 EXTENSIONS BASED ON A LATER PERIOD OF ACTIVE DUTY

a. General. Typically, individuals eligible under Chapters 30, 32 or Section 903 may receive education benefits for a period not to exceed 10 years from date of discharge; however, a later period of qualifying active duty service may give the individual a new 10-year delimiting period for these benefits.  Veterans Claims Examiners (VCEs) should verify this service in the same manner as any other period of service.

Typically, individuals eligible under Chapter 33 may receive education benefits for a period not to exceed 15 years from date of discharge; however, a later qualifying period of active duty service may give the individual a new 15-year delimiting period for this benefit. The period of eligibility for Chapter 33 is based on the individual’s last date of discharge from any qualifying period.  VCEs should verify this service in the same manner as any other period of service.

NOTE: If the individual’s date of discharge is after January 1, 2013, the individual will have no delimiting date under Chapter 33.

b. Length of Later Period of Active Duty. For purposes of giving an individual a new period of eligibility (and a new delimiting date), a later period of active duty must be at least 90 days in length if it ended on or after December 18, 1989. The only exceptions to this 90-day requirement are for individuals who are discharged or released due to a service-connected disability, for a non-service connected medical condition which pre-existed the later active duty service, for hardship, or because of a Reduction in Force (RIF) for convenience of the government. (38 Code of Federal Regulations (CFR) 21.7050(a), 21.5041(a), 21.5745(a)) Under Chapter 33, only a service-connected disablity discharge may be for a period less than 90 continious days. (38 USC 3321)

NOTE:  VA’s General Counsel determined VA is not extending the delimiting date, but actually granting a new eligibility period beginning on the date of the claimant’s last release from active duty. Consequently, if the member re-enters active duty after his original delimiting date has passed, that member would not qualify for benefits until their 90th day back on active duty. The new 10-year eligibility period would begin once the claimant is released from active duty for the subsequent period.

EXAMPLE 1: An individual who established eligibility for Chapter 30 served from July 1, 1990, to June 30, 1993. His original delimiting date was July 1, 2003, based on this period of service. He reenters active duty on May 1, 2005 and continues on active duty.

ACTION: Once the individual completes 90 days of service after the reentry on active duty, eligibility is restored at the 90-day point. The individual remains eligible for benefits while on active duty and then receives a new period of eligibility from the date of discharge from the new service. In this example, payment could resume on July 29, 2005, the 90th day of service from the point of reentry. Payments could continue through the end of the call-up service and then a new delimiting date would exist. The same reasoning applies to a third or subsequent period of active duty. This applies to Chapter 32, Chapter 33, Chapter 30, and Section 903.

EXAMPLE 2:  An individual served on active duty from July 1, 2012, to June 30, 2015. His original delimiting date under Chapter 30 was July 1, 2025, based on this period. He is called up to active duty January 15, 2017 and is released from active duty on November 14, 2017. He is now applying for Chapter 33 benefits based on the call-up service, and he also expects a Chapter 30 delimiting date extension based on that same service.

ACTION: Unfortunately, periods of active duty beginning on or after August 1, 2011, may only be used once to establish eligibility for an education benefit. While this individual’s 2017 mobilization would automatically be applied to extend Chapter 30 (this is not optional), his earlier service used to establish Chapter 30 could not be used to establish Chapter 33. Also, unless he was able to relinquish Chapter 1606 or 1607, he would be required to relinquish Chapter 30 in order to establish Chapter 33 at the 50% benefit level which may not be in the individual’s best interest.  

EXAMPLE 3: A Chapter 30 claimant originally had a delimiting date of July 1, 2003.  He reenters active duty on May 1, 2005, and is discharged on April 30, 2008.

ACTION: If an individual completes 90 days of service after reentry on active duty, eligibility is restored at the 90-day point.  The individual remains eligible for benefits while on active duty and then receives a new 10-year delimiting period from the date of discharge from the service.  In this example, the VCE should resume payment beginning July 29, 2005, the 90th day of service from the point of reentry.  Payments should continue through the end of the call-up service and for another 10 years from date of discharge from that service.  The same reasoning applies to subsequent periods of active duty and other benefit programs. 

EXAMPLE 4: A Veteran has the following information:

  • June 1, 1989: Veteran enlists and participates in Chapter 30.
  • May 31, 1993: Veteran receives honorable discharge and joins the Selected Reserve.
  • August 29, 2003, to December 15, 2003: Veteran attends college.
  • February 1, 2004: Veteran called to active duty.
  • June 4, 2004: Veteran receives an honorable discharge from last period of service.

Can this individual be paid for the 2003 term? Can he/she be paid for any training taken while on active duty in 2004? If not, can he/she be paid for training after the 90th day of active duty in 2004?

ACTION: The individual's original 10-year time limit has expired. The original delimiting date was June 1, 2003. The individual only requalifies for any VA educational benefits on the day after completing 90 days of any reentry or call-up service on active duty.  In this example, the individual would be eligible to begin receiving education benefits for training as of May 1, 2004, if enrolled in program.  The new period of eligibility would begin following last discharge from active duty.

c. Character of Service for Later Period of Service

(1) Once an individual has already qualified for basic educational assistance under Chapter 30 based on an honorable period of service and then has another period of service with anything less than an honorable discharge, VA should apply 38 United States Code (USC) 3031(a). This statute does not require characterization of a subsequent discharge or release from active duty for individuals using MGIB-AD.

(2) Once an individual has already qualified for basic educational assistance under Chapter 33 based on an honorable period of service and then has another period of service with anything less than an honorable discharge, VA should apply 38 United States Code (USC) 3321(a). This statute does not require characterization of a subsequent discharge or release from active duty for individuals using Post-9/11 GI Bill.

Workaround. Until the LTS is updated, VCEs must select "Under Honorable Conditions" for any characterization which is not honorable.

(3) VCEs must use an individual’s last period of active duty in these situations when determining the delimiting date. These individuals will get a delimiting date 10 years and one day (or 15 years and one day for Chapter 33) from the date of the end of their last period of active duty, regardless of the character of discharge from the active duty.

NOTE: For claimants receiving Chapter 33 benefits, if the (RAD) date is on or after January 1, 2013, the individual does not have a delimiting date, therefore, there is no need to process an extension. 

d. Procedures for Entering a Later Period of Service.

(1) Chapter 30 and Chapter 32/903. The VCE should enter the additional service period on the Benefits Delivery Network (BDN) 310 screen on the second line under the service dates used to established eligibility.

(2) Chapter 35 Children.  A child receives an 8-year extension from the release from active duty, if this date occurs between the child’s 18th and 26th birthdays.  This extension may never go beyond the child’s 31st birthday.

In order to extend a child’s delimiting date, the VCE should input the child’s release date in the Child Data field on the BDN 311 screen.

(3) Chapter 33. The VCE should enter the additional service period on the Service Data screen in the LTS.

4.03 EXTENSIONS FOR DISABILITY PREVENTING TRAINING

 a. General.  A Veteran eligible for Chapter 30, 32, 33 or Section 903 benefits, a Reservist eligible for Chapter 1606 benefits, Chapter 33 Marine Gunnery Sergeant John David Fry (Fry scholarship) dependent or Chapter 35 spouse may have their delimiting date extended if VA determines he or she was prevented from initiating or completing a chosen program of education because of a physical or mental disability. Under Chapter 1606, this type of delimiting date extension for disability may be approved only if the disability was incurred in or aggravated by service in the Selected Reserve.

(1) Physical or mental disabilities which result from a claimant's own misconduct do not qualify the claimant for a delimiting date extension.  Public Law 100-689, provides that, for purposes of delimiting date extensions for disability, the disabling effects of chronic alcoholism will not be considered to be due to willful misconduct. This means a Veteran, or Reservist may be authorized a delimiting date extension for disability if he or she was prevented from training because of the disabling effects of chronic alcoholism.

(2) The length of any extension granted will equal the length of time VA determines the claimant was prevented from initiating or completing a program of education within the basic period of eligibility because of the disability.

(3) Although a child eligible for Chapter 35 benefits is not eligible for an extension for disability under the provision described in this paragraph, he or she may be granted an extension under reasons beyond their control (see Para. 4.08).

b. Applications. A claimant may apply for a delimiting date extension due to disability on any current VA application for education benefits, by submitting a letter requesting an extension which properly identifies the beneficiary, and/or by submitting a request through the GI Bill Help Portal (a.k.a. Right Now Web (RNW)). The request should include the evidence described below. If the supporting evidence is not submitted, the VCE must develop for it appropriately.

To determine if the claim was timely filed for a delimiting date extension based on disability, it must have been received in VA by the latest of either (1) One year after the claimant's delimiting date; or (2) One year after the date on which the claimant became able to initiate or continue training following recuperation from the disability.

Establish an end product to control the claim until a decision is made.

EXAMPLE: A claimant's original delimiting date was June 12, 2017; the first date training was feasible was February 23, 2018. In this case, add one year to the later date of February 23, 2018, to obtain February 23, 2019.

c. Required Information to Support Claim. A statement from the claimant and supporting medical evidence is required to establish eligibility for an extended delimiting date based on disability.  In most situations, VCEs should use Personal Computer-Generated Letter (PCGL) Dev-12, Extension of DD Dev.  If the VCE chooses to create a locally produced letter, it must contain the following information:

(1) The claimant must submit a signed statement which contains the following:

(A) The type of disability claimed;

(B) The beginning and ending dates of any period(s) during which, he/she was unable to pursue training because of the claimed disability and his/her reason(s) for being unable to begin or continue this training program because of the disability;

(C) The claimant's statement of how the disability was incurred, if known; and

(D) A statement of the claimant's employment history during the period that he or she was unable to pursue training because of the disability. The claimant should specify dates and weekly hours of employment, names and addresses of employers, and types of jobs held.

(2) The claimant must submit medical evidence clearly establishing the nature and duration of the disability. Such evidence will consist of:

(A) A statement by an attending physician, reporting diagnosis and treatment, the period(s) of disability, and the physician's prior and current evaluation of the feasibility of employing or training the claimant. The statement should also indicate the date(s) when, in the opinion of the physician, training was first medically feasible

(B) Other supporting evidence, such as hospital reports or results of laboratory tests.

(3) If, during initial development (30 days), the claimant returns evidence supporting a disability, but does not provide all the information requested, send a follow-up development letter requesting only the missing information. 

  • VCEs must control and update the disposition of the claim in BDN and TIMS throughout this process
  • When additional development is required, allow the claimant 30 days from the follow-up to respond before taking further action. 

(4) If, after full development and no medical evidence of a disability is furnished, the VCE should deny the claim for failure to prosecute, without referral to the rating board. 

  • If any medical evidence is submitted along with responses to the questions asked, send the referral to the rating board. 

NOTE: VCEs are not able to determine feasibility or interpret evidence for rating and may not deny a claim unless untimely or for failure to prosecute when requested items are not provided.

d. Determinations. A favorable decision to extend a claimant's delimiting date requires a finding of the existence of a physical or mental disability which precluded training, establishes the duration of the disability, and concludes the absence of willful misconduct on the part of the claimant for incurrence of the disability.

e. Rating Required. A Rating Veterans Service Representative (RVSR) at the Veterans Service Center (VSC) associated with the Regional Processing Office (RPO) is authorized to make these determinations.  Coordination between the Education Officer (EO), Veterans Service Center Manager (VCSM), and RPO Director will be necessary to ensure these ratings are completed within timeliness goals associated with Education claims.  The VCE must control the delimiting date determination on a supplemental end product associated with the claimed benefit. The date of claim should be the date the application (request) was received. In addition, the VCE should place the claim in AWAIT Mail in TIMS for 30 days.  Since most VSCs do not have access to TIMS, the VCE should print from TIMS the applicable information for the RVSR to make a decision, route the entire packet to the VCSM requesting a decision is necessary.  Care should be taken to only refer these once the entire packet is fully developed and ready for the RVSR.  The VSCM, in coordination with the EO, will ensure a timely decision is made regarding this claim (no more than 30 days from the date it is referred to VSC).  Once the decision is rendered, the RVSR, through the VCSM, will return the claim to the EO.  Priority scanning of the rating decision as active mail will insure the VCE receives it in TIMS and can proceed based on the decision.

(1) Rating Decisions. The rating board is responsible for determining the existence of a physical or mental disability and establishing the beginning and ending dates for the period(s) during which training was precluded by disability. These decisions will be presented in a memorandum rating.  RVSRs should follow current rating guidance on memorandum ratings.

(2) Willful Misconduct. When necessary, VCEs will make determinations concerning willful misconduct in accordance with current administrative decisions. If the case involves disease, the rating board will determine the issue of willful misconduct. In other cases, the VCE is responsible for the determination and will prepare the necessary administrative decision.

f. Chosen Program of Education. The claimant may select any approved program of education during an extended period of eligibility provided the program selected would have been available to the claimant during his or her basic period of eligibility at the time disability prevented training. Changes of program may be granted during the extended period as applicable.

g. Determining Beginning and Ending Dates

(1) Beginning Date. The claimant will elect the beginning date of the extended period of eligibility. This elected date may not be earlier than the claimant's initial delimiting date or later than the following:

(A) The first day of the first ordinary term following the 90th day after notice of the approval of the delimiting date extension if enrolled in a course organized on a term, quarter, or semester basis during the extended period of eligibility.

(B) On or before the 90th day after notice of the approval of the delimiting date extension if attending a course not organized on a term, quarter, or semester basis during the extended period of eligibility.

(2) Ending Date. Determine the ending date of the extended period by adding the length of the period of disability determined above to the beginning date of the extended period. The day after that ending date becomes the claimant's new delimiting date for education benefit purposes.

NOTE: An election may not be needed if a previous term was interrupted due to the delimiting date.  A VCE would add the extended period to the old delimiting date to allow for full payment of the term.

h. Procedures to Enter an Extension Due to a Disability.

(1) For Chapter 30, 32, and 1606, VCEs should update the field DELIM DATE ADJ TYPE on the BDN 310 Eligibility and Entitlement screen.  In this field, calculate the length of the extension in months and days and then input it. Under “FROM” field, input the date the claimant chose.  

(2) For Chapter 1607, there is no extension field on the BDN 310 screen.  The payment should be continued and a message should be input on the BDN 312 screen stating

STOP: DEL DT IS XX-XX-XX.  BDN award letters should be suppressed and VCEs should use the PCGL CO AWD23 to provide the claimant the correct delimiting date

NOTE:  No Chapter 1607 claimant may be paid beyond November 25, 2019, due to the National Defense Authorization Act of 2016.

(3) For Chapter 35 spouse, VCEs should update on the BDN 311 screen under Spouse Data/Period of Eligibility field.  In this field, VCEs should input the months and days of the extension and then under Date Elected by Claimant, input the date the spouse elected his/her extension to begin.

VCE will need to repeat an award line on BDN 312 screen or input the next term to process the change in delimiting date.  If it appears the BDN letter will not generate with the correct information, the VCE should suppress the letter and prepare a locally produced letter which provides the spouse the correct delimiting date.

(5) Chapter 33 Fry Child

The VCE should adjust the date of birth on the LTS BIO screen so that age 33 falls on the new delimiting date.

(6) Chapter 33 Fry Spouse

The VCE should adjust the Veteran’s date of death so the new delimiting date is generated.

i. Further Extension. Once an extended delimiting date has been granted, it will not be extended further because of either recurrence of disability or the onset of a new disability when such recurrence or onset is after the claimant's basic delimiting date. Although benefit payments may be made up to the new delimiting date, payments will not be continued beyond the limits of the claimant's entitlement with the exception of extensions of entitlement.

j. Notice to Claimant

(1) Denials. VCEs should prepare a locally produced letter to inform the claimant of the denial of a delimiting date extension. This letter must include a copy of the Rating Decision (be careful to NOT attach the one with the names of the Rating Specialists). Please note that if the disallowance is because the reason given is non-qualifying, make sure the letter lists all the qualifying reasons. The VCE should enclose VA Form 20-0998, Your Rights to Seek Further Review of Our Decision. The VCE should clear the appropriate supplemental end product for whichever benefit was requested.

(2) Approvals. VCEs should prepare a locally produced letter to inform the claimant of the approval of a delimiting date extension. Generally, the PCGL Award 19 letter will be used to grant a delimiting date extension and attaching a copy of the Rating Decision is sufficient. VCEs should be careful to not include the copy of the Rating Decision which has the Rating Specialist’s name on it. If the information is not complete on the Rating Decision, this letter must also:

(A) Indicate the length of time how long the extended time will be and a clear explanation of how this period was determined.

(B) Explain the possible beginning dates for the extended delimiting period and a request for the claimant's election of a beginning date. The letter must explain the election of a beginning date including which date appears most advantageous to the claimant (if it can be determined). The letter should include a VA Form 21-4138 as an enclosure for the claimant's statement of election. The letter should include language indicating once the election is made, it may not be changed and the extended delimiting period will run continuously from the beginning date he or she chooses.

(C) Inform the claimant of the approval or denial of the program of education for which he or she has applied. If the claimant has not indicated a program, request the claimant to furnish a statement which identifies his or her chosen program. Explain the availability of VA counseling.

(D) Enclose VA Form 20-0998.

k. Appeals. If a Supplemental Claim, Higher Level Review, or a legacy Notice of Disagreement is received on a claim which was denied an extension of the delimiting date, the VCE must assess if the reason was due to the Rating Board’s decision regarding medical evidence or Education’s decision to not extend. If the disallowance reason was solely due to the Rating Board’s decision associated with medical evidence, the request should be referred to the Veterans Service Center to review and forward to the appropriate area based on the Appeals Modernization Act (AMA) rules.

If the supplemental claim, higher level review request, or notice of disagreement refers to the decision made by the Education Division, the VCE should review and follow the AMA rules.

NOTE: As a reminder, all NODs, (non-legacy) are to be referred to the Board of Veterans Appeals. Refer to Part 3, 2.04, part II, 9c for proper procedures.

4.04 EXTENSIONS TO END OF TERM OR COURSE

a. General. If a claimant's period of eligibility expires during a term or course, his or her delimiting date may be extended in certain circumstances. These extensions apply only to Chapter 35 children and spouses, claimants receiving Chapter 1606 benefits, claimants receiving Chapter 1607 (where separated due to a disability) and former captives, spouses, surviving spouses, and children eligible for benefits under the Antiterrorism Act of 1986. Such an extension may not cause the claimant's original entitlement to be exceeded. 

NOTE:  No Chapter 1607 claimant may be paid beyond November 25, 2019, due to the National Defense Authorization Act of 2016.

b. School Operates on Quarter or Semester Basis. If a claimant is enrolled in an educational institution which operates on a quarter or semester basis and his or her period of eligibility expires during the quarter or semester, his or her delimiting date may be extended to the end of the quarter or semester for claimants under Chapter 1606, Chapter 1607 (when separated due to a disability) or Chapter 35 claimants. Other benefits cannot extend the delimiting date to the end of the semester.

NOTE 1: BDN has been programmed to extend to the end of a term for Chapter 1606 and Chapter 1607 claimants. The VCE should review the extension and send the award to an authorizer for second signature.

NOTE:  No Chapter 1607 claimant may be paid beyond November 25, 2019, due to the National Defense Authorization Act of 2016.

NOTE 2: For Chapter 35 children, this is the rare instance when a delimiting date may be extended past the 31st birthday if the child needs to complete a term.  In those rare situations, the VCE needs to adjust either the eligibility or date of birth fields on the BDN 311 screen so that BDN will allow the extension.

NOTE 3: For Chapter 35 spouses, the BDN 311 screen will have to be adjusted to allow this extension.  The date of eligibility field will have to be slightly modified to allow for the extension to end of term.

c. School Does Not Operate on Quarter or Semester Basis. If a claimant is enrolled in an educational institution that does not operate on a quarter or semester basis and his or her period of eligibility expires after having completed more than one half of the course, the delimiting date may be extended until the end of the course or for 12 weeks (84 days) from the original delimiting date, whichever occurs first.  This is only available for claimants pursuing Chapter 1606 or claimants pursuing Chapter 35 benefits. Other benefits cannot extend the delimiting date.

NOTE: For Chapter 35 claimants, the BDN 311 screen will have to be adjusted to allow the system to calculate a new delimiting for the extension to the end of term.

NOTE: For Chapter 35 spouses, more than one half of the course does not need to be completed.

d. Flight and Correspondence Courses. For claimants pursuing Chapter 1606 benefits enrolled in a course pursued exclusively by correspondence or flight and the period of eligibility expires after having completed more than one half of the course, the delimiting date may be extended for the additional amount of instruction that 84 days will provide at the full-time rate. Use the Flight, On-the-Job Training, Correspondence, and Apprenticeship System (FOCAS) to compute the extension.

e. Apprenticeship and On-the-Job Training. No delimiting date extensions are allowed for claimants who are pursuing a program of apprenticeship or on-the-job training.


4.05 EXTENSION FOR POW OR MIA STATUS

a. POW or MIA Status. If a Chapter 33 or Chapter 30 Veteran is detained by a foreign government or power after his or her last discharge or release from active duty, VA may extend the delimiting date by the length of time the Veteran was detained. This extension may include any period immediately after release from captivity during which the Veteran was hospitalized in a civilian, military or VA medical facility. 

This applies to claimants receiving Chapter 33 Fry benefits if the Veteran was in either POW or MIA status as well.

4.06 EXTENSION FOR UPGRADED DISCHARGE/ RECORDS CORRECTION

 a. General. Service members who use VA education benefits while on active duty and Veterans who were potentially eligible, but chose not to use their benefits, while awaiting service correction or upgraded characterization, would not be eligible for a delimiting date extension under this section. (38 CFR 21.9625(e), 21.7050(f))

b. Qualifications. A Veteran with one period of service, who either (1) has never applied for VA education benefits or (2) was previously denied by VA in the past, may become eligible as a result of the correction of his or her military records and/or upgrade of their character of discharge. (10 USC 1552,1553)

c. Chapter 33 Veterans. Depending upon the release from active duty (RAD) date of the service period in question, Veterans applying for benefits under the Post-9/11 GI Bill (Chapter 33) may be processed using different means, as described below.

NOTE 1: A Veteran whose service previously established eligibility under Chapter 33 is not entitled to a delimiting date extension under this section. If eligibility had been previously established, the beneficiary will retain their original eligibility period, unless other action, outside of a military records correction or discharge upgrade, allows for an extension.

NOTE 2: An upgraded discharge, for service ending prior to January 1, 2013, will remain subject to 38 USC 3321(a)(1), still requiring a 15-year eligibility period beginning upon the date of the upgrade.

Example: An individual served on active duty from January 17, 2006, to September 13, 2012. They were released with a character of discharge of “Under Honorable Conditions” (UHC) with a separation reason of “Other”. Their service characterization was upgraded to “Honorable” on June 10, 2015. The individual would be eligible under Chapter 33 until their delimiting date on June 10, 2030.

Currently, the Chapter 33 Processing System (DGI) does not allow for data entry to indicate a change in Character of Service to a fully Honorable status.  Consequently, DGI does not generate the correct Date of Eligibility or Delimiting Date (when one is required).

Workaround. Until DGI is modified to allow for a military records correction or an upgrade to the character of service, Veterans Claims Examiners (VCEs) should:

(1) Correct the service period, which may or may not include a Character of Service update, in the Add/Edit Service Period pop-up.

(2) Ensure that all Training and Excluded Service periods have been entered and the accuracy of benefit calculation verified.

(A) If the beneficiary is not eligible due to Insufficient Qualifying Service, no additional action is required.

(B) If the beneficiary is now eligible for Chapter 33 benefits, due to the records correction or upgraded discharge, proceed to step 3.

(3) Check to see if the beneficiary relinquished a benefit.

(A) If the beneficiary relinquished a benefit and the Election Date is after the date on which the discharge was upgraded, proceed to step 4.

(B) In all other cases, enter the date on which the discharge was upgraded (or August 1, 2009, whichever is later) in the Chapter 33 “Start Date” field on the "Entitlement and Kickers" screen, and click submit to hold the entry.  This will prevent DGI from paying benefits prior to the actual date of benefit eligibility.

(4) If the upgraded discharge is for service prior to January 1, 2013, add 15 years from the date of the discharge upgrade, this will be the eligibility period and the new delimiting date will be the day following the eligibility period.

EXAMPLE:

Service Dates June 24, 2004, to June 23, 2008

Upgraded discharge approved May 15, 2022

Eligibility begins on date of upgrade and continues for 15 years. The delimiting date is the day after the period of eligibility.

  • May 15, 2022, to May 14, 2037 is the 15 year period of eligibility.
  • May 15, 2037 is the delimiting date.

Enter the new delimiting date in the “Chapter 33 Delimiting Date” field on the “Entitlement and Kickers” screen in DGI, and click submit to hold the new entry.

(5) In the DGI Comments field, indicate the dates for the period of service which was upgraded, the date of the upgrade, and new delimiting date. Add a corresponding Flash message in The Image Management System (TIMS) folder.

d. Chapter 30 Veterans. The delimiting date for those beneficiaries receiving Chapter 30 will be adjusted to ten years from date of the Department of Defense (DOD) determination to correct and/or upgrade their service and the beneficiary will only be able to use the benefits effective beginning on the date of DOD’s decision. 

NOTE: As with Chapter 33, this ONLY applies if the beneficiary’s eligibility has not been previously established.  If eligibility had been previously established, the beneficiary will retain their original eligibility period, unless other action, outside of a military records correction or discharge upgrade, allows for an extension.

VCEs should enter the date of the record correction and/or discharge upgrade (generally found on DD Form 214 or a similar service document) on the BDN 310 screen in the Discharge Upgrade field.  The entry in this field must be a date later than the latest RAD (entered on the BDN 310 screen) but not later than the current date.  The field In Service must be blank. BDN will automatically extend by 10 years (not an extra day).

4.07 Extension for Involuntary Separation.

Public Law 102-484 provided that certain persons involuntarily separated from the Selected Reserve will be eligible for Chapter 1606 benefits for 14 years from their date of eligibility if they meet the following criteria:

a. Separation is by reason of

            (1) Inactivation of the person’s unit of assignment, or

            (2) Involuntary separation of the person from the Selected Reserve and

b. Basic eligibility for Chapter 1606 is established before separation.

VCEs should follow normal award processing procedures and retain the original date of eligibility on the BDN 310 screen.

VCEs need to add “STOP” in the message field on the BDN 312 screen and put the new delimiting date in the message field.

VCEs also must suppress all award letters and release a locally produced letter which will notify the claimant of the corrected delimiting date. Currently Chapter 1606 BDN letters no longer reflect a delimiting date.

4.08 EXTENSION FOR SUSPENSION OF TRAINING DUE TO REASONS BEYOND CONTROL.  

Only Chapter 35 children can qualify for an extension of delimiting date if the claimant's training was suspended due to reasons beyond his or her control. 

VCEs should remember the extension cannot be extended beyond the child’s 31st birthday. An extension for failure to begin training may not be granted either.  The request must be received timely, either by one year from the original delimiting date, or one year from the date on which the condition that caused the reason the child could not continue training ceased, whichever is later.

In these situations, VCEs should use CO DEV12 letter to develop to the claimant. These decisions are conducted by the VCE, not the Rating Board. The extension will begin from the last delimiting date. The claimant does not choose the beginning date of the extension.  The BDN 311 screen (Figure 4.02) is programmed to extend delimiting dates for Chapter 35 children when necessary.  BDN will automatically extend from the last delimiting date.  BDN will cut the extension at age 31.  On the screen, the VCE should input the begin date the circumstances interrupted the claimant’s training in Time Lost Beyond Control Begin Date field and the date the claimant could resume schooling in the End Date field. The VCE should suppress the BDN award letter and release the PCGL CO AWD-19 letter. The VCE must be sure to exclude any statement regarding the claimant and an election.

4.09 Extension for Call-up.

a. Chapter 30 and Chapter 33 Veterans. The procedures to extend a claimant’s delimiting date for reasons of a call-up are the same as extending for later period of duty. Refer to M22-4, Part 3, 4.02.

b. Chapter 1606. For those claimants who have separated from the Reserves/Guard with remaining entitlement under chapter 1606 and eligible for an extension to a delimiting date due to a call-up, these orders must be under Title 10 Sections 12301(a), 12301(d), 12301(g), 12302, 12304, 12304a, or 12304b. 

If the orders show the active service was under any of those authorities, the claimant is entitled to receive an extension for time on active duty plus four months for each call up.  VCEs must account for the time while claimant was on active duty and then input the date the claimant left the Selected Reserves on the BDN 310 screen (under Delim Date Adj From) and the total amount of call-up time. The VCE will need to override the eligibility on the BDN 310 screen.

The BDN award letter must be suppressed and the VCE should release a locally produced letter advising of the correct new delimiting date because of the call-up period(s).

c. Chapter 35 children. A child may receive an extension equal to the period of the call-up plus four months for each call-up. These orders must be under Title 10 Sections 688, 12301(a), 12301(d), 12301(g), 12302, 12304 or if involuntarily ordered to full-time National Guard duty under section 502(f) of title 32, United States Code. (see CFR 21.3041).

Calculate the new delimiting date. Enter the service period (equal to time served plus 4 months) in the Time Lost Beyond Control field on the BDN 311 screen. If the extended delimiting date should go beyond age 31, adjust the child's date of birth on the BDN 311 screen to allow the delimiting date to be extended. Hit enter and review the 311 screen for accuracy.

In either circumstance the VCE should suppress the BDN award letter and release a locally prepared letter advising the claimant of the new, adjusted delimiting date.

d. Chapter 35 spouse.  A Chapter 35 spouse may receive an extension equal to the period of call-up plus four months for each call-up. These orders must be under Title 10 Sections 688, 12301(a), 12301(d), 12301(g), 12302, 12304 or if involuntarily ordered to full-time National Guard duty under section 502(f) of title 32, United States Code. (see CFR 21.3046).

The VCE should input on the BDN 311 screen, the total months and days of the extension in Period of Disability Mos/Days field and the spouse’s original delimiting date in the Date Elected by Claimant field.

As with other adjustments to the BDN 311 screen, the VCE must process an award on the BDN 312 screen to complete this action and produce a letter with the new delimiting date for the spouse.  If the BDN generated letter will not provide the correct information, the VCE should suppress the BDN letter and prepare a locally produced letter with the appropriate information.

4.10 EXTENSION DUE TO SEPARATION FOR DISABILITY.

a. General. Certain claimants eligible for Chapter 1606 or Chapter 1607 may qualify for an extension of their delimiting date due to separations for disability (whether service-connected or not). It must be distinguished from the extension described previously which requires for Chapter 1606 that the disability be service-connected

b. Chapter 1606.  In the event the claimant is separated from the Selected Reserves due to a disability, the delimiting date will be set at 14 years from the basic date of eligibility.  The VCE should place a STOP message in the message field on the BDN 312 screen and include the correct delimiting date.  The VCE should suppress all award letters and notify the claimant with a locally produced award letter of their adjusted delimiting date.

c. Chapter 1607. The Veterans Information System (VIS) will reflect an “EA” code. The claimant will have a 10-year period of eligibility beginning the date of Chapter 1607 eligibility.  If the claimant is separated before serving 90 days, the date of eligibility is the date on which the person was separated. 

On the BDN 310 screen, VCEs should repeat the Date of Eligibility (DOE) in S/C DOE field, input the date the claimant left the Reserves in the RSR field and put DIS in the REASON field.  BDN will automatically give 10 years from DOE.

NOTE:  No Chapter 1607 claimant may be paid beyond November 25, 2019, due to the National Defense Authorization Act of 2016.

4.11 EXTENSION WHEN VETERAN DIES.

a. Chapter 35 children. If the Veteran (for whom the child is receiving Chapter 35 benefits), dies between the child’s 18th and 26th birthdays, the child is allowed an extension of 8 years from the Veteran’s date of death. In addition, this is one of the rare instances when the extension may be beyond age 31.

The VCE should put the date of death on the BDN 311 screen and either repeat the last award line on the BDN 312 screen to release a BDN letter with the appropriate delimiting date OR input the next award on the BDN 312 screen and allow the BDN letter to release. 

b. Chapter 35 spouse. If the Veteran dies, the spouse is entitled to an extension of ten years from the Veteran’s date of death. In certain circumstances, if the Veteran dies from a service-connected disability, the spouse must elect a new beginning date.

The VCE should input the Veteran’s date of death on the BDN 311 Screen.  This will create the proper delimiting date in BDN.  The VCE should ensure an award is produced on the BDN 312 screen to ensure BDN releases a letter to the spouse advising of the new date.  If the BDN generated letter will not provide accurate information, the VCE should suppress the BDN generated letter and prepare a locally produced letter with the correct information.

4.12 Extension for Those who Complete their Service Contract.

a. Chapter 1607. VIS will reflect “EB” code.  The claimant will receive 10 years from the date of separation from the Selected Reserves (this is not active duty). The VCE should add the STOP message on the BDN 312 screen along with the correct delimiting date.  VCEs should suppress BDN award letters and use the PCGL CO AWD23 letter to notify the claimant of the correct delimiting date.

NOTE:  No Chapter 1607 claimant may be paid beyond November 25, 2019, due to the National Defense Authorization Act of 2016.

4.13 Extension for Primary Caregivers

a. Background: Public Law 111-377 allowed extensions to a claimant’s period of eligibility for certain claimants who acted as the primary provider of claimant care services for Veterans or members of the Armed Forces under Section 1720G (a) of Title 38 and who, as a result, were prevented from pursuing a program of education on or after August 1, 2011.

b. Qualifications. To qualify for an extension of a delimiting date, a claimant must be:

Eligible under the Montgomery GI Bill (Chapter 30),

An eligible child dependent under the Survivors’ and Dependents’ Educational Assistance program (Chapter 35), or

An eligible child dependent receiving Transfer of Entitlement (TOE) benefits under the Post-9/11 GI Bill (Chapter 33)

Additionally, the claimant must:

Have a delimiting date on or after August 1, 2011,

Be currently serving, or served as a primary caregiver under Section 1720G(a) of Title 38 during the eligibility period (before delimiting date)

Have been prevented from training or suspended pursuit of a program of education on or after August 1, 2011

c. VCE Processing. The Family Caregivers Program of the Veterans Health Administration (VHA) determines whether claimants qualify as a primary caregiver under Section 1720G (a) of Title 38. If a claimant submits a request for a delimiting date extension as a result of being a primary caregiver, the VCE should do the following:

1.  Review the claimant’s delimiting date and ensure that the delimiting date is on or after August 1, 2011. If the claimant’s delimiting date is prior to August 1, 2011, then the claimant does not qualify for the delimiting date extension. The VCE should send a locally produced letter disallowing the claimant’s request for a delimiting date extension.  Be sure to include VA Form 20-0998.

2.  If the delimiting date is on or after August 1, 2011, the VCE should submit the names and Social Security Numbers of the Veteran (or member of the Armed Forces who was seriously injured) and of the claimant requesting the delimiting date extension via an encrypted email to the designated Point of Contacts (POC) from each RPO. The VCE should suspend the end product for 14 days and place the TIMS folder in AWAIT MAIL for 14 days.

3. The POC will develop with VHA and provide the POC with eligibility determinations and the POC will provide the response to the VCE.  If the claimant has not applied for the primary caregiver designation or if the claimant is not eligible for the delimiting date extension, the VCE should send the claimant the letter in Attachment A.  This letter will include favorable findings for the claimant.

4.  If the claimant is found to be eligible for a delimiting date extension, the VCE should extend the claimant’s delimiting date by the length of time that the claimant was prevented from completing his or her chosen program of education and send the claimant the letter in Attachment B.

4.15 Extension for Public Law 116-140

Due to Public Law 116-140 Eligibility and Processing, certain beneficiaries who were prevented from pursuing their chosen program of education due to a school closure (temporary or permanent) may be authorized an extension to their delimiting date (i.e. period of eligibility). 

a. General. A Veteran eligible for chapter 30 or 33, a Reservist eligible for chapter 1606 benefits, a chapter 33 Fry Scholarship Child, or Transfer of Entitlement eligible spouse or child may have their delimiting date extended if VA determines he or she was prevented from pursuing their individual chosen program of education due to a school closure (temporary or permanent) before the expiration of their previous delimiting date during the period March 1, 2020 through December 21, 2021. (Extended by Public Law 116-154)

b. Applications. The beneficiary must apply for a delimiting date extension due to Public Law 116-140 on any current VA application for education benefits or may submit the request on any document.  The request for a delimiting date extension, will be considered timely filed, if the request for the extension is received in the VA within one year after the beneficiary’s original delimiting date. 

c. Required information to support claim. A statement from the beneficiary is required to consider an extended delimiting date based on Public Law 116-140.  The beneficiary may request an extension through the Education Call Center documented on a VA Form 119 or 0820, a statement submitted through Oracle Client Applications (i.e. Ask A Question) or submit a statement to VA which should include:

  • A statement indicating the reason is due to COVID-19 related school closure 
  • The beginning and ending dates showing the beneficiary was unable to attend training AND
  • In addition, enrollment certifications will be required from the educational institutions to support those dates. 

NOTE: Once the dates are known AND the student states the lapse was due to COVID-19, the VCE is authorized to extend the delimiting date. A statement from a beneficiary does not require any additional verification. 

d. Extending the Delimiting Date for all benefits, the VCE should: 

  • Determine the length of the extension and the new delimiting date.
    • If the beneficiary’s delimiting date is after when he or she was able to resume training, December 21, 2021 or later, adjust the length of the period he/she could not train during the covered period (March 1, 2020, to December 21, 2021) from their current delimiting date.
    • If the beneficiary’s delimiting date occurs during the covered period (March 1, 2020, to December 21, 2021) and while he or she could not train, the VCE must develop for an election to ask when the beneficiary wishes to begin their extension. Inform the he or she may choose to begin their extension the earliest of the date they were able to resume training if prior to December 21, 2021, or the later of 90 days from the date of the election letter for training not offered on term basis, the first term offered after 90 days for training offered on term basis, or any date in between these dates. Advise the beneficiary their delimiting date will be adjusted beginning 90 days after the date of the election letter if we do not receive an election within 60 days and may not be changed. (Modified PCGL AWD-19 Letter)
  • Add a FLASH to the TIMS folder stating the delimiting date has been extended to (new date) due to PL 116-140.
  • Update all systems appropriately. (i.e. BDN, LTS, FOCAS)

In this field, calculate the length of the extension in months and days and then input it. Under “FROM” field, input the date the beneficiary indicated he/she could resume training.

(2)  Chapter 35 children claims. VCEs should remember the extension cannot be extended beyond the child’s 31st birthday. An extension for failure to begin training may not be granted either. The extension will begin from the last delimiting date. The beneficiary does not choose the beginning date of the extension. The BDN 311 screen is programmed to extend delimiting dates for Chapter 35 children when necessary. BDN will automatically extend from the last delimiting date. BDN will cut the extension at age 31.

The VCE should suppress the BDN award letter and release the PCGL CO AWD-19 letter. The VCE must be sure to exclude any statement regarding the child and an election. 

(3)  Chapter 33 Veteran/TOE Spouse claims. Be cautious when modifying the Long Term Solution (LTS) for periods before 2013 to ensure the action does not activate section 112 of the Colmery Act which removed delimiting dates for those Veterans who were released from active duty after January 1, 2013. If this is unavoidable, control the ending period manually allowing benefit payments only for the additional period extended.   • “Other” for reason
