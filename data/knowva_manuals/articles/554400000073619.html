2.01  OVERVIEW

Performance of compliance surveys involves:

a. Selection of schools and training establishments to be surveyed and individual beneficiaries to be reviewed.

b. Review of records maintained by VA, schools, and training establishments.

c. Personal visits to schools and training establishments.

d. Reports and correspondence with school and training establishment officials, the appropriate SAA, and other VA elements.

For the purposes of this chapter, the term Education Compliance Survey Specialist (ECSS) includes any position performing the duties of that position. This includes, but is not limited to, Education Liaison Representatives (ELR) and State Approving Agencies (SAA).

2.02 SCHEDULE OF COMPLIANCE SURVEYS (Reference Title 38 U.S.C. §3693)

a. General. The Approvals, Compliance & Liaison Team Chief, will establish and maintain a schedule of compliance surveys to ensure that surveys are conducted as required, and whenever the Director of Education Service determines a survey is required.

A compliance survey schedule will be created and updated in Salesforce before the start of each fiscal year. The schedule must meet the minimum requirements established by law and the VA. It will be based on the number of schools and training establishments utilizing the following:

  • Active Facilities Report
  • Salesforce Scheduled Facility and Report Modules
  • DGI (Digital GI Bill DGI (DGI)
  • Other resources identified by VA Central Office (VACO)

b. Scheduling Requirements. Title 38 U.S.C.§ Section 3693(a) requires:

1. Conducting an annual compliance survey at an educational facility or training establishment offering one or more courses approved for the enrollment of eligible Veterans or persons if at least 20 such Veterans or persons are enrolled.

2. Survey each such educational institution and training establishment not less than once during every 2-year period; and

3. Assign not fewer than 1 education compliance specialist to work on compliance surveys in any year for each 40 compliance surveys required to be made under this section for such year.

Additionally, Education Service has a variety of compliance options including (Risk-Based Survey (RBS), Targeted Risk Based Survey (TRBR), Supervisory Visits (SV), and Section 3693 surveys) that overlap in scope.

The following will be taken into consideration when the Section 3693 survey schedule is developed:

1. Identification of Active Facilities. Each fiscal year the compliance survey schedule will be developed based on the Active Facilities Report (Record Control Schedule (RCS) 20-0260)). Active facilities are defined as any facility that had a beneficiary entitled to receive payment at any point during the designated year.

2. Waiver Requirements. Compliance visits will be waived in the following facilities due to the long standing demonstrated record of compliance and monitoring by other agencies:

  • High schools
  • Department of Labor (DOL) Registered Apprenticeships
  • Other facilities determined by VACO

All other educational facilities and training establishments may be considered for a waiver of compliance per Title 38 U.S.C. § 3693(b) based on their record of compliance.

3. Remote Surveys. Onsite compliance surveys are preferred. Remote surveys cannot be conducted on facilities where flight or correspondence programs are approved. In all cases, the remote survey must be approved by the Approvals, Compliance & Liaison Team Chief in writing and a copy of the authorization letter uploaded to the Salesforce survey file.

4. Centralized Compliance Surveys. The Approvals, Compliance & Liaison Team Chief may direct that centralized surveys be conducted at some corporate schools with locations in multiple states.

5. VA GI BILL Feedback System. As part of every compliance survey, a review of the feedback systems will be conducted, and a summary statement(s) will be entered into the narrative report

6. WEAMS (Web Enabled Approval Management System). Enrollment limits in WEAMS will be reviewed as part of every compliance survey, reset as appropriate, and reflect the approved institution and program types.

7. Annual Reporting Fees. As part of every compliance survey, Annual Reporting Fees paid to an ETI under Title 38 U.S.C. 3684 must be reviewed. Schools will receive the amount listed in § 3684(c)(2) per eligible student. If a school receives a reporting fee for 100 or more eligible students, then the reporting fee paid to the school cannot be used for, or merged with, the school’s general fund.

Compliance Procedures:

    1. Education Compliance Survey Specialists (ECSSs) will review how the reporting fee paid to a school is utilized during Compliance Survey visits.

a) The Surveyor will interview the primary School Certifying Official (SCO) and confirm how the school utilizes the reporting fee and document the findings in the Compliance Survey Narrative report.

b) Schools should document how they use the reporting fee (a formal ledger, receipts to/from a SCO training conference, invoices for equipment for the school’s Veteran Center, formal documentation the school uses the reporting fees as part the SCOs salary, etc.). The surveyor should review these documents and ensure the school has not spent the reporting fee on unapproved expenses.

c) If the school has over 100 Veteran students, the surveyor will interview the primary SCO and confirm the reporting fee is not merged with any other funds at the school. The SCO is required to have documentation the reporting fee is not merged with any other fund at the school. The document should include the account number (and if available a copy of the ledger) for the Reporting Fee account.

  1. The Surveyor will document how the school uses the reporting fee in the Compliance Survey Narrative.
  2. If the reporting fee is not utilized properly the Surveyor must:

a) Specifically identify the discrepancy in the Exit Briefing with the SCO.

b) Submit a referral to the SAA of jurisdiction, citing the regulatory authority 38 U.S.C. § 3684. Furnish any facts regarding the discrepancy that were discovered during the survey. This referral is for informational purposes. The SAA will take corrective action as the SAA deems necessary. Controls should not be established for a response from the SAA.

c) Annotate the error in the Compliance Survey Narrative and the Compliance Survey Findings Letter sent to the school.

8. Priority Enrollment. As part of every compliance survey, a review of Priority Enrollment will be conducted in accordance with Title 38 U.S.C. § 3698(c)(1)(c).

a) If the “Priority Enrollment” box is checked but there is no evidence of priority enrollment for Veterans/Servicemembers, then the surveyor should report the discrepancy to the ELR of jurisdiction. The ELR should reach out to the school and either obtain an updated catalog/addendum or, if the school fails to provide documentation, should uncheck the “Priority Enrollment” checkbox in WEAMS. If the ELR unchecks the “Priority Enrollment” checkbox in WEAMS, then the finding should be noted in the Compliance Survey Narrative.

b) If the “Priority Enrollment” box is not checked but there is evidence of priority enrollment for Veterans/Servicemembers, then the surveyor should notify the school that they should submit notice to the ELR of jurisdiction of the Veteran/Servicemember priority enrollment policy so that WEAMS can be updated accord.

9. Reporting Requirements. All compliance activities will be processed and recorded within the Salesforce application.

10. School Findings Letter.  A copy of the narrative report will be sent to the school after the completion of the compliance survey. It will accompany the letter sent to the school to provide the institution detailed findings. Prior to sending, the narrative report will be thoroughly reviewed to remove PII, reference of fraud, school liability, or referrals to OIG.

11. SCO Beneficiaries.  Records of any SCO who has received education benefits for attending the same facility will be included in the review sample.

CFR 21-9674d states the VA will not pay benefits for courses certified to the VA by the individual taking the course. This applies to Ch 33 students only, however due to the potential for fraud, the records of any SCO who is also receiving benefits at the same facility being surveyed will be reviewed. Regardless of the type of institution, those records will be selected as part of the sample. The surveyor must identify any SCOs who received benefits by:

• Reviewing WEAMS for the current list of SCOs and checking TIMS, DGI, EM, and FOCAS to determine if they have received benefits;
• Inquiring during the initial notification to the school if any SCOs are also beneficiaries
• Inquiring during the survey in-brief if any SCOs are beneficiaries

Ch 33 students who have certified their own enrollment will have those certifications referred for correction unless another designated SCO can certify the enrollment data during the survey. Other than those situations, no additional scrutiny, other than deemed warranted during the survey, needs to be exercised for the records of SCOs receiving benefits. Only SCOs who attended the facility during the compliance survey review period will normally be selected for review.

A statement will be included in all Narrative reports that a review was accomplished and no SCOs were in receipt of education benefits for attending that facility or that SCOs, by name, were identified as having received benefits for their attendance at the facility.

Additionally, see Title 38 CFR 21.4202(c) “Restrictions; proprietary schools. Enrollment will not be approved for any veteran or eligible person under the provisions of Chapter 34 or 35 respectively, in any proprietary school of which the veteran or eligible person is an official authorized to sign certificates of enrollment or monthly certificates of attendance, an owner or an officer.”

2.03  SELECTION OF CASES

a. Resources. The following resources will be utilized to select cases for review:

  • COIN EDU 289
  • Annual Reporting Fees (ARF) listing
  • VA Computer Applications
    • FOCAS (Flight, On-the-Job, Correspondence, Apprenticeship System)
    • The Image Management System (TIMS)
    • LTS (The Long Term Solution)
    • VA-ONCE (VA Online Certification of Enrollment) reports
    • Other Applicable Applications

1. Compliance Survey Listing, COIN EDU 289. The Compliance Survey Listing, Computer Output Identification Number (COIN) Report 289 is generated by Hines Data Processing Center (DPC) during end-of-month processing in October, December, and March of each year and mailed directly to Regional Offices (ROs). The listing includes selected master record information for all cases which were paid during the month with the exception of those under the programs listed below. See Part 9, Chapter 8 for additional information. For instructions on obtaining records not included in the listing, see VA Applications below.

  • Chapter 30 correspondence and on-the-job(OJT)/apprenticeship (APP)
  • Chapter 33 correspondence and OJT/APP
  • Chapter 1606 correspondence, OJT/APP, and Coast Guard

2. Annual Reporting Fee (ARF) Listing. The ARF Listing is generated during the second quarter of the fiscal year and reflects all students who were in receipt of benefits the previous calendar year, January through December. The pay list is arranged alphabetically by student name and shows the file number and the amount of payment made for each VA student counted in the reporting fee computation.

3. VA Applications. There are several applications used in Education award processing that will be used in preparing for compliance survey schedules.

i. FOCAS. This is a non-Chapter 33 payment system for Flight, OJT, Correspondence, and APP awards

NOTE: The Education Service OJT Flight and Correspondence Report is generated quarterly by the Performance Analysis & Integrity (PA&I) Team. This report contains a list of beneficiaries in receipt of benefits for Flight, OJT, Correspondence, and APP training during the quarter and is disseminated to the CELOs to assist with compliance survey scheduling within their individual regions.

ii. TIMS. This is an imaging and workflow processing application used by the Regional Processing Offices (RPOs) and Education Service to establish and maintain electronic folders for recipients of educational benefits.

iii. LTS. This is the Chapter 33 Post-9/11 GI Bill payment system.

iv. VA-ONCE. This is the online certification program for VA.

b. Initial Selection of Cases. The ECSS will select the sample for review during the compliance survey in a way which does not prejudice the randomness of the sample. To accomplish this, simply select cases using a set pattern (e.g., every case, or every other case, or every third case) until the desired number of cases has been selected. A similar pattern must be used to select cases from all sources.

c. Selection of Cases for Expanded Surveys and 100 Percent Audits. If additional cases are needed to expand the compliance survey, continue using the procedures outlined in this chapter to select the cases, except these cases need not be random. Cases will be selected from no more than two previous lists. Lists would include ARF, Education Service OJT Flight and Correspondence Report, and COIN 289.

2.05  TIMELINESS REPORTING FOR COMPLIANCE ACTIVITIES 

a. Tracking of Workload: All assignments and recorded completion of compliance activities are tracked within the Salesforce, Scheduled Facilities module. 

b. Timeliness: VACO Education Service (EDU) coordinates with VA leadership annually to establish performance metrics for compliance activities. Compliance Activities Conducted by: 

1.  VA Employee: Specific timeliness requirements will be published within employee performance standards. The number of days to complete will be calculated using the following “Days to Complete” formula: Salesforce (Completed date (-) Exit Briefing date + 1 Day = Total number of days to complete). 

2. Contracted Vendor: Timeliness for compliance activities performed by contractors will meet standards and methods identified within the contract.

3. SAA Cooperative Agreement: Timeliness for compliance activities performed by SAAs will meet standards and methods identified within the signed cooperative agreement.
