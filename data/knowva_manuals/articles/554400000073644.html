SUBCHAPTER I.  ENROLLMENT ADJUSTMENTS


11.01 REQUIREMENT FOR REPORTING ENROLLMENT CHANGES

a. School reporting requirements for specific enrollment situations are found in 38 Code of Federal Regulations (CFR) 21.4203. This part describes the requirements for reporting enrollment changes. It also describes how the "last date of attendance" is to be determined.

b. Schools must report interruptions or terminations of training, for any reason, as well as changes in the number of credit or clock hours (38 CFR 21.4203). The basic rule is that the school must initiate these reports in time for VA to receive them within 30 days of the date on which the change occurs. This 30-day rule is qualified in the following ways:

(1) Non-punitive Grades. A school may assign a non-punitive grade for a course or subject in which a student is enrolled even though the student does not withdraw from the course or subject. When this occurs, the school must report the assignment of the non-punitive grade before the earlier of the following dates:

(a) Thirty days from the date on which the school assigned the grade, or

(b) Sixty days from the last day of the enrollment period for which the non-punitive grade is assigned.

(2) Failure to Reenroll. For students whose enrollment was certified for the school year, and the student attends through the end of one term but fails to return the following term, the school must report the terminated enrollment within 30 days of the last officially scheduled registration date for the subsequent term.

EXAMPLE: A student was originally certified for the 2014-2015 school year; he or she completes the term ending December 20, 2014, but fails to reenroll for the term beginning January 14, 2015. The last officially scheduled registration date for the second term is January 20, 2015. The school would have until February 19, 2015 to submit a timely report.

(3) Changes near the Beginning of the Term. Sometimes changes in enrollment occur during a "drop" period. In such cases, the school must report the enrollment change to VA within 30 days from the last date of the "drop" period or within 60 days from the first day of the enrollment period, whichever occurs first.

EXAMPLE: A term begins on September 15, 2015, and there is a "drop" period which ends on October 7, 2015. A reduction in training occurs on September 25, 2015. The school would have until November 6, 2015, to submit a timely report.

(4) Unsatisfactory Progress or Conduct. In general, a school must report unsatisfactory progress or conduct within 30 days from the date on which progress or conduct becomes unsatisfactory. However, if unsatisfactory grades are involved, the 30-day period will begin from the date on which the school official responsible for determining whether a student is making satisfactory progress received the final report indicating that approved satisfactory progress standards have not been met. However, in no event will a report of unsatisfactory progress (because of unsatisfactory grades) be considered timely if received more than 60 days after the end of the enrollment period.

EXAMPLE: A student completed a term on January 25, 2015. The school received the final grade reports on March 1, 2015, which showed that the student's progress was unsatisfactory as of January 25, 2015. The school would have until March 26, 2015, to submit a timely report.

11.02 DETERMINING THE LAST DATE OF ATTENDANCE

Schools must report the last date of attendance to VA when a claimant terminates enrollment in resident training. Likewise, schools must report the last day of attendance in particular resident courses when the claimant reduces training time but continues in school (38 CFR 21.4203(d)). For changes in independent study courses, the school need only report the official date of change in status under the practices of the institution (38 CFR 21.4135(e) (2) (ii)).

a. College-Level Courses. Even though daily attendance records are not required, the school must establish an adequate reporting system to detect enrollment changes soon after they occur and to provide timely reports to VA. When an enrollment change occurs, any of the following methods may be used to determine the last date of attendance:

(1) attendance records;

(2) grading reports;

(3) last date on which examination or other papers filed;

(4) last day of activity in the instructor's records; or

(5) a statement from the student as to the last day of his or her attendance.

b. Non-College Degree Courses. For terminations, the school may use the same methods listed in subparagraph a above.

11.03 REPORTING REDUCTIONS AND TERMINATIONS

a. Method of Reporting

(1) Schools must report changes in the enrollment status of VA students. They should use VA Form 22-1999b or 22-1999b-1, Notice of Change in Student Status. The preferable means of submission is through VA-ONCE but paper copies are acceptable.

(2) To expedite processing of reductions in training time and withdrawals from training, Veteran Claims Examiners (VCEs) may accept telephonic, or email notices of these changes. VCEs should encourage certifying officials at educational institutions to use VA-ONCE to report enrollment changes.

(3) Only the certifying official at an institution has the authority to originate a notice of reduction or termination. However, the certifying official may authorize another employee of the institution or a VA work-study student to transmit the information to the RPO.

(4) VCEs should record telephonic notices of reduction or termination on VA Form 119, Report of Contact. The form should be clearly identified as a telephonic notice and include all the information required to process a reduction or termination of benefits, the name and title of the person providing the information, and the date the notice is received. The person recording the information should capture the document in the claimant’s TIMS folder.

b. Notification from School of Withdrawal or Reduction. School officials must promptly notify VA when students change their hours or attendance, withdraw from school, or complete a unit course but receive a non-punitive grade (38 CFR 21.4203).

(1) When a student reduces training time or withdraws from training after the end of the school's drop period and receives a non-punitive grade(s), the school should complete item 7, Mitigating Circumstances, on any form submitted.

(2) All schools are encouraged to submit a statement regarding the evidence of mitigating circumstances at the time the reduction or withdrawal is reported.

(3) If an NCD course is operated on a block or unit basis and the student withdraws and accrues no credit toward completion, the school should report the date the student last accrued credit in the Remarks item.

NOTE 1: Since a school is required to report the last date of attendance, this must be the student's actual last date of attendance as can best be determined. A school may not report the first day of the term as the last day of attendance if the facts are otherwise. If it appears a school has incorrectly certified the last date of attendance, development is required to determine the correct date.

NOTE 2: Some schools report a termination for non-payment of tuition and fees but the student continued to attend classes. VA educational assistance is based on actual school attendance. If the school allows the student to continue attending classes, do not terminate benefits.

c. Notification from School of Changes in Credit Hours which do not Affect Full Time Payment for Non-33 benefits. School officials do not need to notify VA of changes in credit hours which do not change student's full time training status during a standard quarter or semester unless the student is a serviceperson and there is a change in the amount of tuition and fees (38 CFR 21.7156(b)). This means that a school does not have to report changes from 12 to 15 hours or vice versa except for servicepersons who have a change in tuition and fees.

d. Notification from School of Changes in Credit hours which do not Affect Full Time Payment for Chapter 33 benefits. Schools need to notify VA of changes in credit hours, whether or not tuition and fees change since this may have an impact on the student’s book stipend. For example, a student reduces from 14 hours to 13 hours effective the first day of the term. The school does not report any change to tuition and fees. However, the student received the book stipend based on enrollment for 14 hours on the first day of the term. The student is only due a book stipend for 13 hours. Therefore a book and supplies debt should be created.

11.04  EFFECTIVE DATES OF REDUCTION AND TERMINATION

a. General. VCEs must consider many factors when reducing or terminating educational assistance benefits.

(1) The type of training,

(2) The type of grade assigned for a course,

(3) The timing of a student's withdrawal from training,

(4) Whether or not payment has already been made,

(5) Whether or not monthly certification is required,

(6) Whether any debt will be created

(7) The presence or absence of acceptable mitigating circumstances,

(8) The 6-credit exclusion rule and,

(9) Call-ups to active duty

b. Regulations. The effective dates for adjustments for all possible circumstances are specified in the following regulations:

Chapter 33: CFR 21.9630-35

Chapter 1606: CFR 21.7630-35

Chapter 30: CFR 21.7130-35

Chapter 35: CFR 21.4131-35

Chapter 32: CFR 21.5130

Section 901: CFR 21.5830-35

Section 903: CFR 21.5290

For most circumstances, the rules are the same for all programs. However each program has some unique circumstances requiring reduction or termination of benefits.

c. Other Than IHL and NCD. If a student terminates training in a correspondence, flight, cooperative, farm cooperative, or job training program, terminate benefits on:

(1) The date the last lesson was serviced for correspondence training.

(2) The date the last instruction was received for flight training.

(3) The date the last training was received for cooperative training.

(4) The date of the last classroom attendance for farm cooperative training.

(5) The date the last training was received for on-the-job or apprenticeship training.

d. IHL and NCD Courses Offered on a Term, Quarter, or Semester Basis. If a student—

(1) Withdraws from all courses before the end of the institution's drop period (not to exceed 30 days), terminate benefits effective the last date of attendance.

(2) Withdraws from all courses after the end of the institution's drop period and punitive grades are assigned for the courses or acceptable mitigating circumstances are shown, terminate benefits effective the last date of attendance. If the student was taking independent study courses, terminate benefits according to the official date of change in status under the practices of the institution.

(3) Withdraws from all courses after the institution's drop period, receives non-punitive grades for the courses and acceptable mitigating circumstances are not found, terminate benefits effective the first date of the enrollment period.

NOTE: This rule does not apply if the 6-credit exclusion will be granted. If the 6-credit exclusion applies, pay for up to 6 credit hours through the last date of attendance or for those not required to perform monthly certifications. The rule also does not apply if training is discontinued as the result of being ordered to active duty.

(4) Completes a course but at the end of the term, semester, or quarter receives a non-punitive grade for the course and acceptable mitigating circumstances are not found, terminate benefits from the first date of the enrollment period. (The 6-credit exclusion is not a factor in such cases.)

(5) Reduces training time and VA receives information on this reduction before the student has been awarded benefits for the period that includes the reduction, award benefits based upon the actual dates of attendance for each of the training times. Therefore, the reduction in rate will be effective on the date the training was reduced. Apply this rule to all benefits covered by this manual. However, do not apply this rule if the reduction occurs after the drop period and the student fails to establish mitigating circumstances.

(6) Reduces training time by withdrawing from fewer than all courses on the first day of the term, reduce benefit payments from the first date of the term.

(7) Reduces training time by withdrawing from fewer than all courses under one of the conditions listed below, reduce payments at the end of the month in which withdrawal occurs (or effective the date of reduction if monthly certification is required, except for chapter 33 before March 17, 2025), or at the end of the term, whichever is earlier.

(a) Withdrawal occurs before the end of the institution's drop-period.

(b) Withdrawal occurs after the end of the institution's drop period and acceptable mitigating circumstances are shown or the 6-credit exclusion applies or the training is discontinued as the result of being ordered to active duty (c) A punitive grade is assigned for the course from which the student withdraws.

(8) Reductions and terminations that occur on the first day of an enrollment period should be processed in the same manner as if the student never attended the course(s). This applies to all benefit programs.

e. NCD Courses Not Operated on a Term, Quarter, or Semester Basis

(1) Determining Reduction or Termination Date

(a) If an NCD course is operated on a block or unit basis and a student withdraws from all courses or reduces training time by withdrawing from fewer than all courses during the block or unit, earns no credit for attending that portion of the block or unit that preceded his or her withdrawal and the school does not submit information or evidence of mitigating circumstances on the VA Form 22-1999b, terminate benefits as stated above. If the school does submit information or evidence of acceptable mitigating circumstances, terminate or reduce benefits effective the last date the student accrues credit toward class completion.

(b) If an NCD course is not operated on a block or unit basis or on a term, quarter, or semester basis, do not develop for mitigating circumstances. Terminate or reduce such awards effective the last date the student accrues credit toward course completion.

(2) Determining the Date the Student Last Accrued Credit. When the reason for a student's termination from training or reduction in training time is other than "end of term" or "completion of training" and the date the student last accrued credit is not included in the school's report, contact the school by telephone to obtain the correct date. Document the information on VA Form 0820 Report of Contact. Capture this document in the TIMS folder.

f. Chapter 33 End of Month Protection

(1) Historically, VA has applied different effective dates for protected reductions depending on the requirement for monthly verification of enrollment under the benefit or training type. For non-chapter 33 training requiring monthly verification, awards are reduced effective the date of the reduction. For non-chapter 33 training which does not require monthly verification, awards are reduced effective the last day of the month which the reduction occurred in, or the end date of the term, whichever occurs first.

(2) Chapter 33 began requiring monthly verification of enrollment for beneficiaries enrolled in NCD training effective August 1, 2021, and for beneficiaries enrolled in IHL training effective December 17, 2021. . Prior to this change, monthly verification of enrollment was not required for chapter 33.

(3) Unlike non-chapter 33 programs requiring verification, the release of payment each month under chapter 33 is not directly tied to verification. Instead, payments are released automatically near the end of each month and failure to verify for two consecutive months will result in a payment being withheld. Instead, the effective date of a protected chapter 33 reduction is determined by 38 C.F.R. § 21.9635 and 9636, as follows:

(a) Protected reductions for enrollments beginning prior to August 1, 2011, should have end of month protection applied.

(b) Protected reductions for enrollments beginning on or after August 1, 2011, which were processed prior to March 17, 2025, should have the end of month protection applied.

(c) Protected reductions for enrollments beginning on or after August 1, 2011, which are processed on or after March 17, 2025, will not have the end of month protection applied.

SUBCHAPTER II.  6-CREDIT HOUR EXCLUSION

 

11.05  DEFINITION AND BACKGROUND

Public Law 100-689 changed the procedures for how VCEs should review and consider mitigating circumstances when a student withdraws or reduces their first time. The provision is referred to as the "6-credit exclusion" since up to 6 credits may be excluded from the requirement to provide mitigating circumstances to VA. The rule does not apply if the student withdraws during the institution's drop period or if the student receives a punitive grade for the withdrawn courses.

a. The 6-credit exclusion applies to a course withdrawal only if each of the following requirements is met:

(1) The student is enrolled under an education benefit provided under title 38 or title 10.

(2) The withdrawal is the first instance of withdrawal from courses on or after June 1, 1989;

(3) The student has been awarded benefits for the withdrawn courses;

(4) Mitigating circumstances would normally be an issue (e.g., the withdrawal was beyond the drop period and a non-punitive grade was assigned for the course).

b. The 6-credit exclusion does not apply if the student:

(1) Withdraws from a course during the drop period;

(2) Completes a course and receives a non-punitive grade;

(3) Withdraws from a course and receives a punitive (failing grade);

(4) Withdraws from a course and there is no debt established based on the change, or

(5) Was previously granted 6-credit exclusion for the same or a different benefit.

(6) Withdraws from a course(s) and VA has not awarded benefits yet for that course(s).

NOTE: VCEs should use care in applying exception (4). If it is to the claimant's advantage, apply the 6-credit exclusion.

EXAMPLE: A serviceperson is taking 14 hours and receiving reimbursement for tuition and fees under chapter 30. She reduces to 12 hours shortly after the drop date. If not applying the 6-credit exclusion would result in an overpayment due to the difference in tuition and fees for 2 hours, the VCE should apply the exclusion.

11.06  CLARIFICATION OF "FIRST INSTANCE"

The following examples illustrate the term "first instance."

EXAMPLE 1: A school reports a student withdrew from 3 credit hours on March 5, 2015, and from an additional 3 credit hours on March 7, 2015. The student was enrolled in a regular spring term at an IHL. Both drops were after the drop/add period.

ACTION: The 6-credit exclusion applies to the first 3-credit withdrawal but not to the second. This is because the 6-credit exclusion applies only to the first instance of withdrawal.

EXAMPLE 2: A student drops a course on March 9, 2015, and asks VA to consider his or her mitigating circumstances for this withdrawal and "save" the exclusion for some future withdrawal.

ACTION: The VCE will apply the 6-credit exclusion since this is the first instance of withdrawal. The exclusion cannot be "saved" for some future occasion which might be more beneficial to the student.

11.07  CLARIFICATION OF "6 CREDIT HOURS OR THE EQUIVALENT"

a. A maximum of 6 credit hours or the equivalent will qualify for the exclusion, regardless of the training time for the 6 credit hours. If more than 6 credit hours are dropped in one incident, the additional hours will be subject to the mitigating circumstances rule. The student will be asked to provide mitigating circumstances for the entire incident. The VCE will automatically exclude 6 credits and will consider whether or not mitigating circumstances apply to the remaining credits. Six graduate training hours may be excluded regardless of the training time to which it may equate.

EXAMPLE 1: A student enrolls in 2 courses for 6 credit hours and is awarded benefits at the 1/2-time rate. The student's school reports that he drops both courses on the same day after the drop period with non-punitive grades. The school does not report evidence of mitigating circumstances on VA Form 22-1999b.

ACTION: The VCE should apply the exclusion for all 6 credits. The VCE should prepare an award which will terminate the student's benefits effective the date of termination. The VCE must ensure the award letter includes the appropriate notification which states the student has been allowed the one time exclusion.

NOTE: BDN (Benefit Delivery Network) will generate the appropriate letter when using the STOP command and reason code 6C. This does not apply to chapter 35 cases.

EXAMPLE 2: A student enrolls in 3 courses for 9 credit hours and is awarded benefits at the 3/4-time rate. The courses are for 2, 3, and 4 credits. The student's school reports that he dropped the 3-credit and the 4-credit courses on the same day after the drop period with non-punitive grades. The school does not report evidence of mitigating circumstances on VA Form 22-1999b.

ACTION: The VCE will apply the exclusion for 6 credits and develop for mitigating circumstances for the remaining one credit hour. Since mitigating circumstances are not shown, the VCE should amend the award to pay for 8 hours (the 6 credits excluded from the mitigating circumstances plus the 2 unaffected hours). The award should then reflect the 6 hours drop off the day the student dropped them leaving only the 2 hours for the remainder of the term. The VCE will need the total charges for the remaining two credits. The VCE should release the appropriate letter which includes the request for mitigating circumstances and the notice that the student has been allowed the one time exclusion.

EXAMPLE 3: A student enrolls in 7 graduate credit hours which the school certifies is full-time training. The student's school reports that he dropped 6 graduate credit hours after the drop period. The school does not report evidence of mitigating circumstances on the VA Form 22-1999b.

ACTION: The VCE will apply the exclusion for the 6 graduate credits. However, the VCE must contact the school to determine the total charges for the one remaining credit and the training time the school is granting for the one hour. Once this is learned, the VCE should amend the award to reflect the drop of the 6 graduate credit hours on the date of drop. The VCE should release the appropriate letter which must include the notice that he has been allowed the one time exclusion.

NOTE: BDN will generate the appropriate letter when using reason code 7C as the beginning reason on the date of the drop. This does not apply to chapter 35 cases.

b. For the purpose of this provision, the following will be considered the equivalent of 6 credit hours:

(1) Six semester or quarter hours

(2) Nine clock hours if the course is measured on a clock hour basis with 18 clock hours equal to full-time training. This applies to a non-degree program offered on a term, semester, or block basis.

(3) Eleven clock hours if the course is measured on a clock hour basis with 22 hours equal to full-time training. This applies to a non-degree program offered on a term, semester, or block basis.

c. For non-standard terms, consider only credit hours (semester or quarter hours), not "equivalent credit hours."

EXAMPLE: A student enrolls in one 5-semester hour course during a 6-week summer term. The student drops all 5 semester hours after the drop period with non-punitive grades. This is the first instance of withdrawal.

ACTION: All 5 semester hours are within the maximum of the 6-credit hour exclusion. The VCE should apply the exclusion to all 5 semester hours. The VCE should prepare an award which terminates the master record effective from the date of withdrawal and notify the claimant that she has been allowed the one-time exclusion.

NOTE: BDN will generate the appropriate letter when using the STOP command and reason code 6C. This does not apply to chapter 35 cases.

11.08  DOCUMENTATION PROCEDURE

The VCE must document each case in which the 6-credit hour exclusion (6X) is granted to prevent erroneous granting of additional exclusions in the future.

a. For chapter 30, 1606, and 1607, add the date the 6-credit hour exclusion was granted in the 6 CR EXC DATE field on the 312 screen if processing an award. Add this date on the M21 screen using the CORR command screen if not processing an award.

b. For chapters 32, 35, and Section 903, set the file pull indicator to "W" after the 6-credit hour exclusion has been granted and no folder pull is otherwise required. Set the file pull indicator to "Z" when the exclusion has been granted and folder pull is required for other reasons. The chapter 32 and 35 systems include the following folder pull messages: "6-credit exclusion granted" for code "W"; and "6-hr excl. granted/other folder pull req" for code "Z."

c. For chapter 33, if the 6-credit hour exclusion (6X) was previously granted outside of DGI, enter that date in the 6X Date field. For example, if the claimant has used the 6-credit hour exclusion (6X) in BDN on a M21 screen for chapters 30, 1606, or 1607, then input the date the exclusion was used.

NOTE: It is no longer necessary for VCEs to FLASH TIMS Folders regarding the granting of 6 credit exclusion. The controls set in BDN or DGI are sufficient notification. However it is essential that each student be granted the one-time exclusion when applicable but that a student receives the 6 credit exclusion only one time. When a school reports that a drop in credit hours occurred, the VCE must always consider the possibility that the exclusion rule applies.

11.09  PROCEDURES WHEN THE EXCLUSION APPLIES

When the 6-credit hour exclusion applies, the VCE must take different actions depending on the number of hours dropped. Take an end product on any appropriate adjustment of the claimant's master record and concurrent notification of all issues. The most important factor, the VCE must consider, no matter which benefit, is does the reduction in hours (or complete withdrawal), create an overpayment from benefits previously paid? If there is no debt created, the 6-credit hour exclusion does not apply.

a. Drop is 6 or Fewer Hours. If the dropped courses total 6 or fewer credit hours, the VCE should adjust the award as if mitigating circumstances were established for these hours. The VCE will adjust (terminate or reduce) the award as of the date last attended or at the end of the month as appropriate and concurrently notify the claimant that he or she has been allowed the one time exclusion. The VCE should prepare the appropriate PCGL letter if they are unable to use BDN code 6C or 7C when processing the termination or reduction.

b. Drop is More Than 6 Credits—No Debt Created. If more than 6 credit hours are dropped but no debt was created because of the additional hours, follow the previous procedure.

EXAMPLE 1: A student receiving chapter 30 is taking 14 semester hours at a school where 12 semester hours are considered full time. He drops two courses and reduces his hours to 7 semester hours during the middle of a semester. He has never used the 6-credit hour exclusion.

ACTION: Although this reduction is for more than 6 hours, the existence or nonexistence of mitigating circumstances for the 7th credit hour would have no effect on any debt created for this term. The student is due full-time benefits from the beginning of the term (since he remains enrolled in 7 semester hours and 6 semester hours of the 7 dropped hours are payable because of the exclusion) and 1/2-time benefits from the date of the drop.

EXAMPLE 2: Same example however the student is receiving chapter 33 benefits and the amendment was processed before March 17, 2025.

ACTION: As previously stated, the student is due full time benefits from the beginning of the term (since he remained enrolled in 7 semester hours and 6 semester hours of the 7 dropped hours are payable because of the exclusion) and then half time training benefits would be from the first of the month following the date of the reduction. VCEs need to remember the number of hours the student is enrolled in, is not as large of a factor as the result of any debt regarding the drop from 14 hours to 7. In addition, the drop to the existing 7 hours would be from the beginning of the month following the reduction, since chapter 33 beneficiaries receive end of month rule when there is a reduction in hours.

EXAMPLE 3: Same example however the student is receiving chapter 33 benefits and the amendment was processed on or after March 17, 2025.

ACTION: The student is due full time benefits from the beginning of the term (since he remained enrolled in 7 semester hours and 6 semester hours of the 7 dropped hours are payable because of the exclusion) and then half time training benefits would be from the date of the reduction. The drop to the existing 7 hours would be from the effective date of the reduction, since the amendment was processed after discontinuance of end of month protections for chapter 33.

c. Drop is More Than 6 Credits—Mitigating Circumstances Is an Issue since a Debt will be Created. If the student drops more than 6 credits and the school does not submit evidence of mitigating circumstances with the report of reduction, the VCE should adjust the student’s award retroactively for the dropped hours in excess of 6 to the beginning date. Concurrently ask the claimant for mitigating circumstances. Notify the claimant that he or she has been allowed the one time exclusion and send the appropriate system generated letter.

EXAMPLE 1: A chapter 35 spouse who is enrolled for 9 credits from February 9, 2015 withdraws from all 9 credits on March 24, 2015.

ACTION: The VCE should amend the master record by awarding benefits for 6 hours (1/2 time) from the beginning of the term until the day of the withdrawal. After adjusting the award, the VCE should send the appropriate letter requesting mitigating circumstances and also granting the 6-credit hour exclusion. In addition, the student may provide acceptable mitigating circumstances which could reinstate the hours to 9 from the beginning of the term until the date of the withdrawal.

EXAMPLE 2: A chapter 33 student enrolls in a standard term (January 18, 2016 to May 10, 2016) with 13 semester hours. After the Drop/add Period, he reduces to 5 hours on February 11, 2016 with non-punitive grades. However, the school did not furnish this information until April 1, 2016.

ACTION: Since Tuition and Fees are based on total charges, the VCE must have the tuition and fees broken down as follows:

• 5 hours that student still attending

• 6 hours that cover the 6-credit hour exclusion.

Since the student withdrew from 8 hours, the 6-credit hour exclusion only applies to the first 6 hours. The student would be required to submit mitigating circumstances for the remaining 2 hours he dropped since these are not protected. In addition, Books and Supplies (B&S) would be adjusted based on the number of hours as of the first day of the term. On the first day of the term the student would be paid for enrollment in 11 hours (5 hours still attending plus 6 hour exclusion = 11 hours). DGI will generate a B&S overpayment for the 2 unprotected hours. Finally the reduction to the 5 hours will affect the monthly housing allowance (BAH). The school did not submit the notice of the change in hours until April 1, 2016, therefore; the BAH for February and March had already been released. (DLP/DLC would be 04/01/16). The student’s BAH will be adjusted from the first day of the term for the 2 unprotected hours. The student’s rate of pursuit (ROP) on the first day of the term will be based on his enrollment in 11 credit hours (11/12 = 0.9167 which rounds to 90% ROP).

The VCE should amend the award to reflect 5 hours effective February 11, 2016 and save these changes. VCE should then click the “Add” (6X placeholder) and enter tuition and fees for 11 hours (5 remaining hours plus 6 hours for the exclusion).

DGI will make the necessary adjustments based on the information entered and create the appropriate overpayments. For more information, refer to Part 12.

NOTE 1: DGI will apply the 6-credit hour exclusion unless the “Do Not Apply 6X” is marked or the exclusion has previously been used either under chapter 33 or under another benefit.

NOTE 2: If the VCE fails to enter the 6X placeholder information, a Red Banner will display the following message on the WPS screen:

ERROR: This Work Product contains 6X place holder. Please revisit the Enrollment screen and input these values.

EXAMPLE 3: A chapter 33 student enrolls in a standard term (January 18, 2025, to May 10, 2025) with 13 semester hours. After the drop/add period, he reduces to 5 hours on February 11, 2025 with non-punitive grades. Mitigating circumstances are not provided. The amendment is processed on or after March 17, 2025.

ACTION: The VCE should enter the tuition and fees associated with 11 hours (the remaining 5 hours plus 6 hours for the exclusion) in 6x placeholder information in DGI. DGI will re-calculate the books and supplies stipend payable from the beginning of the enrollment based on 11 hours. The tuition and fees award will also be re-calculated from the beginning of the enrollment based on the 6x placeholder value and the tuition and fees value associated with the reduction amendment. The housing award will be re-calculated from the beginning of the enrollment based on 11 hours, and will stop effective February 10, 2025, the last day the student was training above 0.5 RoP. Both the tuition and fees and housing awards are adjusted based on the effective date of the reduction, rather than the end of the month in which the reduction occurred, as they were processed after discontinuance of end of month protections for chapter 33.

11.10  ADDITIONAL EXAMPLES

EXAMPLE 1: The school submits a VA Form 22-1999 for 6 credit hours from May 23, 2015, and a VA Form 22-1999b which notifies of withdrawal from all courses on June 3, 2015 (the drop date). Both notices are processed together on June 15, 2015.

ACTION: Do not apply the 6-credit hour exclusion rule since this student has not yet been awarded benefits for the dropped courses. No benefits should be awarded to the claimant for this enrollment unless mitigating circumstances are received and accepted. In the award letter, the VCE needs to include a paragraph asking if mitigating circumstances were involved. If they were, they will be considered. If additional information is received regarding the existence of mitigating circumstances, the VCE should adjust the award accordingly. The VCE should not control for the existence of the mitigating circumstances.

EXAMPLE 2: A student enrolls in a term from January 15, 2016, through May 10, 2016, for 6 credits and drops one course (3 hours) on March 2, 2016, receiving a punitive grade (“F”).

ACTION: Since the student received a PUNITIVE grade, the 6-credit hour exclusion does not apply. The VCE should reduce the award on the date of drop, March 2, 2016, for non-33 benefits. For chapter 33 benefits, the VCE should reduce the award effective March 2, 2016, to reflect the change in hours and a reduction in tuition and fees (as appropriate).

NOTE: DGI is rule-based and will make the adjustment effective the appropriate date, dependent on when the amendment was processed. However the VCE should always enter the actual date reported by the school in DGI.

EXAMPLE 3: A student enrolls in a summer quarter on June 7, 2015, and withdraws on June 21, 2015, which is within the school's drop period.

ACTION: Do not apply the 6-credit hour exclusion. Only withdrawals after the drop period qualify for the exclusion.

EXAMPLE 4: A chapter 33 student enrolls in 12 semester hours from September 5, 2015, to December 19, 2015. On January 5, 2016, the school submits VA Form 22-1999b which shows the student withdrew from 6 hours on December 10, 2015. The school did not submit information or evidence of mitigating circumstances.

ACTION: The VCE should apply the 6-credit hour exclusion for the credits dropped on December 10, 2015.

SUBCHAPTER III.  MITIGATING CIRCUMSTANCES

 

11.11  GENERAL

a. Overview. Reducing and stopping education benefits are major issues in the adjudication of education claims. These award actions involve very complicated issues which include but are not restricted to whether or not mitigating circumstances apply to that individual award action. Veterans Claims Examiners (VCEs) must also consider other factors described in this subchapter.

b. Summary. The law prohibits payment of VA benefits for a course from which a student withdraws and received a grade that will not be used in computing requirements for graduation (38 USC 3680(a) (3)). There are exceptions:

(1) the beneficiary was ordered to active duty or

(2) the beneficiary withdrew during the “drop” period or

(3) the beneficiary can establish that the failure to complete the course was due to mitigating circumstances (by statute, withdrawals that fit into the 6-credit exclusion are considered to be with mitigating circumstances).

NOTE: The purpose of the original legislation creating these rules was to curb abuse of VA educational assistance programs by certain beneficiaries who were using the non-punitive grading and liberal withdrawal policies of some schools to receive VA benefits for several terms without making any academic achievement. Therefore, Congress directed VA to enforce the restrictions of 38 USC 3680(a) (3) in a manner which eliminated federal payments to beneficiaries who are not seriously pursuing an education. Congress also expressed its intent that VA exercise compassion when considering the domestic difficulties and problems of beneficiaries making a serious attempt to obtain an education.

c. Definition. The term "mitigating circumstances" means events beyond the beneficiary's control. These events either prevented the beneficiary from completing the course or, although the circumstances did not prevent the beneficiary from completing the course, they did impact the beneficiary’s ability to perform.  Evidence must show the impact was significant enough that it would be fair to say that the circumstances caused the beneficiary to receive a non-punitive grade due to unsatisfactory achievement. It is the responsibility of the beneficiary to demonstrate the mitigating circumstances were unavoidable and unforeseen.

d. Mitigating Circumstances Examples. Different types of potential mitigating circumstances are listed below. These are standard VBA remarks found in the VA-ONCE enrollment certification system which School Certifying Officials (SCOs) are encouraged to use. The list is not all inclusive. A VCE should apply the reasonable doubt rule and presumptive provisions by considering the evidence provided and the intent by submitting mitigating circumstances.

(1) An illness or injury afflicting the student during the enrollment period. The illness or injury must have been  serious enough it either prevented the beneficiary from completing the course or resulted in a non-punitive grade due to unsatisfactory achievement.

  • No documentary evidence is required if the school or beneficiary states this illness occurred during the enrollment period.

(2) An illness or death in the student's immediate family. It is assumed such a death either prevented the beneficiary from completing the course or resulted in a non-punitive grade due to unsatisfactory achievement.

  • No documentary evidence is required if the school or beneficiary states this illness or death was in the beneficiary’s immediate family. For the purposes of this chapter, the beneficiary’s immediate family includes their spouse, parents, children (including wards/foster children), siblings, or grandparents. Exceptions exist to this and should be reviewed on a case by case basis.

(3) An unavoidable change in the student's conditions of employment. The condition must have either prevented the beneficiary from completing the course or resulted in a non-punitive grade due to unsatisfactory achievement.

  • No documentary evidence is required if the school or beneficiary states this change occurred during the enrollment period.

(4) An unavoidable geographical transfer resulting from the student's employment that prevents the student from completing the course.

  • No documentary evidence is required if the school or beneficiary states this change occurred during the enrollment period.

(5) Immediate family or financial obligations beyond the control of the beneficiary which require him or her to suspend pursuit of the program of education to obtain employment.

  • No documentary evidence needs to be submitted if the school or student states it occurred during the enrollment period.

(6) Discontinuance of the course by the school.

  • This must be substantiated by the school, the State Approving Agency (SAA), or the Education Liaison Representative (ELR). These procedures are outlined: Advisory: Post-9/11 GI Bill Claims Involving Disapproval of the Program of Education.

(7) Unanticipated active military service, including active duty for training. It is important for the VCE to understand this event should be unanticipated military service. Regularly scheduled weekend drilling is not unanticipated.

  • Acceptable documentary evidence would include military orders showing the dates of activation. It is the VCE’s responsibility to verify the activation orders coincide with at least one day of the school enrollment and are under a qualifying Title 10 section.

(8) Unanticipated difficulties with childcare arrangements the student made for the period during which he or she is attending classes. (It is assumed that such difficulty will prevent the student from completing the course).

  • No documentary evidence needs to be submitted.

11.12  EVIDENCE REQUIREMENT FOR MITIGATING CIRCUMSTANCES

a. As a general rule, beneficiaries are expected to document their claimed mitigating circumstances with evidence that substantiates the seriousness or the unavoidable nature of the situations which interfered with their enrollments (38 CFR 21.4136).

b. VCEs must be able to ascertain if the reported mitigating circumstances meet these three criteria:

1. What occurred? Essentially what and when was the event which caused the beneficiary withdraw or reduce training time?

2. Was it unavoidable? The onus on the beneficiary to prove the event was unforeseen and unavoidable.

3. What was the impact of the event? Was the withdrawal or reduction in training time the only option due to the event?

The statement from a beneficiary is the beginning of the evidence. The statement should always be accepted but the VCE may have to develop to ensure the veracity of the event is documented. The reason for the reduction or termination must be stated. If there is a reason to believe the statement needs additional documentation to support the event, the VCE should develop as appropriate.

A VCE should automatically accept a beneficiary’s statement if the specific mitigating circumstances (the actual event) stated was of such a sensitive nature it would be insensitive to insist on the submission of corroborative evidence. A clear example of such a situation is the reported death of the beneficiary’s spouse or child. VCEs must exercise discretion in determining which mitigating circumstances may be accepted without documentary verification.

As previously noted in 11.11, a VCE should apply the reasonable doubt rule and presumptive provisions by considering the evidence provided and the intent of a person submitting mitigating circumstances. Unless the evidence provided indicates a lack of a justifiable reason, presume the issue was beyond the beneficiary’s control and prevented the beneficiary from completing the course.

c. Additionally the school may act as an agent for the beneficiary. Schools are required to submit a description of the mitigating circumstances when reporting the reduction or termination. If the school reports the reason for the reduction or withdrawal, the VCE should consider it as the beneficiary’s statement. If additional corroborative documentation is needed to substantiate the reason for the withdrawal or reduction, the VCE should develop directly to the beneficiary using the MITC-1 letter.

Schools using VA-ONCE have the following reasons to select from the drop down menu when providing mitigating circumstances.

  • An illness or death in the student’s immediate family. VA will not request any additional documentary evidence. 
  • An illness or injury afflicting the student during the enrollment period. VA will not request any additional documentary evidence.  
  • An unavoidable change in the student’s conditions of employment. VA will not request any additional documentary evidence.
  • An unavoidable geographical transfer resulting during the student’s enrollment. VA will not request any additional documentary evidence.
  • Immediate family or financial obligation beyond the control of the student. VA will not request any additional documentary evidence.
  • Unanticipated active military service, including active duty training. VA will request documentary evidence using the MITC-1 letter if information on VIS conflicts. See 11.11 for acceptable documentary evidence.
  • Discontinuance of the course by the school. This is automatically acceptable and 6-credit exclusion should not be consumed with this reason (already substantiated by the school, the State Approving Agency (SAA) or the Education Liaison Representative (ELR)).
  • Unanticipated difficulties with childcare arrangements. VA will not request any additional documentary evidence.

VCEs should accept all sensitive mitigating circumstances reports submitted through the VA-ONCE process. If a beneficiary submits reasons for the drop or termination, separately from the school’s submission, the VCE should be judicious in consideration of the reason submitted. (See M21-1, Part III, Subpart iv, Chapter 5, Section A - Principles of Reviewing Evidence and Decision Making). Generally, explanations of mitigating circumstances should be accepted and seldom require additional documentation to be submitted.

In the situation when the VCE cannot automatically accept the stated reasons, the VCE must reduce or terminate the education benefits retroactively to the first day of the term (The use of the 6-credit exclusion was previously defined in this chapter. See Subchapter 2). Doing so will create any debts necessary.  The VCE must send the proper mitigating circumstance letter (MITC-1 letter) which may encourage submission of additional written information from the beneficiary to reduce the debt.

NOTE: When a compliance survey is held at the educational institution, the compliance specialist will verify all required documentation is on file to support the mitigating circumstances reported by the School Certifying Official (SCO).

11.13  DEVELOPMENT AND AWARD ACTIONS

a. Development for Evidence of Mitigating Circumstances and Concurrent Actions

(1) Award Action. If mitigating circumstances are an issue and the school or student does not submit adequate evidence of mitigating circumstances with a report of reduction, withdrawal, or assignment of non-punitive grades, do not pay for the course or courses in question. Adjust the award (subject to the 6-credit hour exclusion), creating an overpayment if appropriate, and concurrently ask the student for mitigating circumstances.

NOTE: Many schools allow students to withdraw without penalty up to the middle of a school term. 38 CFR 21.4135(l) restricts a drop period to a maximum of 30 days. Never accept a drop period of more than 30 days after the beginning date of the term. If the exact drop period is not readily available, assume it is 30 days

(2) Development—Specific Letters and Control. When the VCE properly uses the ‘7M’ or ‘6M’ BDN change reason codes, BDN will release the appropriate development letter. DGI is also designed to generate the appropriate letter when mitigating circumstance development is required. If the VCE believes the system generated letter is inappropriate or incomplete, a locally produced letter will be necessary. In all situations though, do not control for receipt of mitigating circumstances.

b. Subsequent Submission of Mitigating Circumstances

(1) Acceptable Mitigating Circumstances. If the student submits acceptable mitigating circumstances (complete with any necessary supporting evidence), restore education benefits for the affected courses from the beginning date of the term until the last date of attendance. Send the PCGL letter M/C-3.

NOTE: The PCGL letter M/C-3 is not needed if the VCE processing an amendment properly uses the BDN reason 7R or 6E when re-entering the enrollment. (This does not apply to chapter 35).

EXAMPLE: A student attending his third consecutive quarter at State University as a full-time student withdrew from all courses on February 8, 5 weeks after the beginning of the winter quarter. The student had already been granted the 6-credit exclusion. The school does not submit information or evidence of mitigating circumstances with the report of reduction. VCE creates an overpayment and asks the student for mitigating circumstances by sending the appropriate system generated letter. In response, the student states that he had been attending school during the day and working as a stock clerk during the evening. He indicates that his employer discontinued the evening shift and required all evening employees to begin daytime work effective February 9. He submits a signed statement from his employer verifying that this change in hours of employment was necessitated by changes in the operation of the business.

ACTION: Restore benefits for the overpaid courses Using BDN begin reason 6R when re-entering enrollment for this term. For a chapter 35 claim, restore benefits and send student PCGL letter M/C-3. VCE is authorized to amend award on a supplemental end product.

(2) Unacceptable Mitigating Circumstances. If the student claims but does not establish acceptable mitigating circumstances (or does not submit any necessary supporting evidence), do not restore education benefits for the affected courses. Send the claimant PCGL letter M/C-2.

EXAMPLE: A student who had previously used the 6-credit exclusion received an "NC" grade in a 4-hour class. This occurred after the drop period. The school does not submit information or evidence of mitigating circumstances with the report of reduction. VCE creates an overpayment and asks for mitigating circumstances by means of the appropriate system generated letter. In response, the student reports that he had to miss two class sessions each week because of transportation problems. Consequently, he was unable to remain current with his course work.

ACTION: Deny the claim of mitigating circumstances. Send the PCGL letter M/C-2. VCE is authorized to PCLR a supplemental end product for this action.

(3) BDN Letter. Regardless of whether mitigating circumstances are accepted or disallowed, if an award adjustment is made, allow the computer-generated award letter to issue. Take the supplemental end product.

11.14  SCHOOL LIABILITY

Schools may become liable for overpayments to students under certain circumstances. If an award action is going to create an overpayment, the VCE must determine if all or part of the overpayment resulted from a late or false report from school officials. This determination will be made based upon the school reporting requirements outlined in Part 1: Chapter 7 - School Liability. 

a. For non-chapter 33 claims processing, VCEs must update BDN when potential school liability is considered:

(1) When award action is going to create an overpayment, the VCE must place a "Y" or "N" in the "POTENTIAL SCHOOL LIABILITY?" field on the BDN 312/406 or 412 screen.

(a) If an award will create an overpayment, failure to make an entry of "Y" or "N" will result in the display of the message "ENTRY REQUIRED."

(b) If an entry is made and no overpayment situation exists, the message "DATA INCONSISTENT-REVIEW" appears and the field is highlighted. Removal of the inappropriate entry will allow processing to continue.

(2) If the VCE finds no reporting error that could lead to potential school liability, he or she will place an "N" in the field. The message "NO SCHOOL LIABILITY" will be displayed on the screen during GAD (Generate and Display) processing and on the award after GAP (Generate and Print) processing.

(3) If the VCE determines that a reporting error exists which could result in potential school liability, he or she will place a "Y" in the field. The message "POTENTIAL SCHOOL LIABILITY EXISTS" will appear on the screen during GAD processing and on the award after GAP processing.

(4) The indicator will appear in the master record to indicate that the debt involves, at least in part, potential school liability.

b. There is currently no prompt in chapter 33 processing to indicate potential school liability.

11.16  REPORTING INCOMPLETE GRADES

a. Some institutions allow the assignment of an "Incomplete" grade on a provisional basis only. These institutions require a student to remove the "Incomplete" grade within a specified time (e.g., by the end of the term following the term for which the "Incomplete" grade was assigned). If the student does not replace the "Incomplete" grade within the prescribed time, the institution records a punitive failing grade in the student's academic record. Institutions that have and enforce such a policy and prescribe a maximum time for grade replacement of less than 1 year, are not required to report the assignment of an "Incomplete" grade as a non-punitive grade.

NOTE: Different schools indicate incomplete grades in different ways. Schools can designate incomplete grades by "I", "W", "Z" or by other means.

b. Many institutions have a grading policy that permits an "I" grade to be replaced with a non-punitive grade or which allows a non-punitive "I" grade to remain in a student's record indefinitely. At these institutions, "I" grades must be reported as non-punitive grades at the time the grades are assigned. This reporting requirement is not intended to force institutions into adopting a particular academic policy regarding "I" grades. It is necessary, however, to ensure compliance with statutory restrictions on benefit payments.

(1) When a report is received that an "I" grade has been assigned and the number of credit hours affected by the "I" grade would reduce the student's training time causing an overpayment for VA benefit payments, the VCE should establish a future diary control to mature 1 calendar year after the date the "I" grade was assigned.

NOTE: If evidence of mitigating circumstances is received with the report of the assignment of an "I" grade, determine the acceptability of the mitigating circumstances and do not establish a future diary control.

(2) If the "I" grade is replaced in the student's academic record with a creditable passing grade or with a punitive failing grade, the institution should report this grading action. Such reports should clearly indicate that the change in enrollment status being reported is the replacement of an "I" grade previously reported. There may be occasions when reports of grade replacements are not received until after a VA determination that benefits may not be paid for the course in question due to the absence of acceptable mitigating circumstances. In these situations, the official report of grade replacement constitutes evidence of the removal of the payment prohibition of 38 USC 3680(a)(3), and benefit payments will be authorized for the course in question.

(3) If at the end of the 1-year control period no report has been received from the institution to indicate that the "I" grade has been replaced, assume that the "I" grade has not been changed. The VCE should reduce benefits retroactively, creating an overpayment. Mitigating circumstances could influence the award so VCE should send appropriate letter asking the claimant for any evidence of such. As previously stated, no control for receipt of mitigating circumstances would be necessary. The 6 credit exclusion provision does not apply in incomplete grade situations. In such cases, the date of claim entered in BDN should be the date the diary control matured.

NOTE: RPOs must take care to ensure the diary controls are monitored through the BDN WIPP system or through VOR.

11.19  GRADUATION REQUIREMENTS

Schools are highly encouraged to report graduation or program completion information to VA. Once a student graduates from a degree program or completes a Non-College Degree program, the School Certifying Official (SCO) should submit a Notice of Change in Student Status (VA Form 22-1999b). If the student is graduating from a degree program, “Graduation” should be selected as the termination reason. If the student is completing a Non-College Degree program, "End of Term or Course" should be selected as the termination reason. The last date of the term selected will be pre-populated as the date of termination if using VA-ONCE. If subsequent terms were previously certified, VA-ONCE will prompt the SCO to terminate those enrollments, or to leave as previously certified.

Graduation should only be reported for students who graduate while using benefits.

a. Graduation/Course Completion.

(1) Chapters 30, 1606, 1607, 32, and 35 awards: VCEs should enter the appropriate End Reason Code on the 312 screen when a school or training establishment indicates that a claimant has graduated from a degree program or has completed a non-degree program. Unless otherwise indicated below as unnecessary, the BDN master record must be adjusted to reflect graduation or course completion under the appropriate supplemental End Product. (Required, even if there are no subsequent enrollment periods in BDN.) Select the appropriate End Reason Code as follows:

1. If the student graduated from a degree program use End Reason Code 68.

2. If the student completed a non-degree program use End Reason Code 67.

If the adjustment only involves repeating the last award line to indicate graduation or course completion (i.e. no change to past or future payments), the BDN generated letter should be suppressed as no notification is required. However, if the adjustment shortens the term in question (or removes subsequent terms), the letter must be released (chapters 35 and 32 require an appropriately edited PCGL (SR-1) Adverse Action Letter).

NOTE: When subsequent terms are removed temporarily (due to BDN limitations), the BDN letter should not be released.

If there are subsequent enrollment periods at a different school (or at same school; student is still enrolled), the VCE must reenter terms. The VCE will enter these terms using a 930 series end product. The different school or program often required different codes. (i.e. facility, objective, and or course codes) When processing this additional step, the BDN generated letter should be released.

(2) Chapter 33 awards: VCEs will use the “add amendment” button in DGI. The VCE will enter the last day of term as the effective date and tuition and fees. DGI will reflect “unchanged” on the amended term. The VCE will authorize the Work Product and clear a supplemental end product. No letter is required, provided the enrollment period has not changed.

If term (that includes graduation) was not paid in DGI, no action is necessary. The VCE should be sure to close the any work product which may have been created. No end product credit is authorized.

(1) Termination “end of term or course” effective on the last day of the enrollment period

Non-college degree: When a student graduates or completes the course from a non-college degree program, the school will select “end of term or course”.

Non-Chapter 33 benefits: No changes required in BDN when system end reason is 61, 66 or 65.

Chapter 33 benefits: No changes required in DGI, unless document is in the VA-ONCE inbox. If it is, the VCE should delete the document from the VA-ONCE inbox and authorize the work product. No letter is released. The VCE is not entitled to an end product for clearing the VA-ONCE inbox in DGI.

Non-Chapter 33 benefits: VCEs will process award in BDN using STOP command changing the no-pay date appropriately. For chapters 35 and 32, the VCE will allow the BDN system generated letter to be released and send an appropriately edited PCGL (SR-1) Adverse Action Letter.

Chapter 33 benefits: VCEs will enter this change as a “correction” on the original enrollment in DGI. The VCE should ensure the DGI letter is released.

(3) Termination “end of term or course” effective after the last day of the enrollment period in remarks

The VCE must verify and document TIMS when remarks are unclear if the SCO is reporting the date of graduation is later than previous submitted enrollment periods or if the enrollment period has been modified.

Non-Chapter 33 benefits: The VCE will use the CADJ command to update the no-pay date on the 312 screen and enter the appropriate end reason code ‘61’ if the student is not reported as graduated or ’67 or 68’ if reported as graduated (based upon type of training completed). VCEs will release the BDN letter. This action will be completed on a supplemental end product.

c. Retroactive Updates Not Involving Changes to Enrollment Periods Issues. There are a few exceptions when VCEs should not amend enrollment periods when graduation (or completion of term) is reported.

(1) All non-chapter 33 claims where the period has moved to dropped priors and payment is not affected. Since these cannot be updated in BDN, it is not necessary for the VCE to amend the enrollment period.

(2) Chapters 35 or 32 claims where payment is not affected. Updates are dependent upon scheduling that often will delay payments to a claimant. Changes involving a different school or changes involving the type of training (IHL vs. NCD) create issues with payment certifications and add time and complexity to making proper updates. VCEs should only update claims when the retroactive award is followed by continued pursuit at the same school and under the same type of training and training codes; A, B, C, or D.

(3) Chapters 30, 1606 or 1607 claims where payment is not affected. Changes involving school and changes to type of training (IHL vs. NCD) may create issues with payment certifications and significantly increases the complexity for making the proper updates. VCEs should not update claims when the retroactive updating is followed by pursuit at more than one successive school (multiple different facility codes), or when followed by pursuit at a different school and the type of training and training changed from IHL to NCD or NCD to IHL.

NOTE: Any retroactive update involving changes to an enrollment period must be adjusted, in system when feasible or out of system with the assistance of Finance through the processing of a VA Form 22-1997.

SUBCHAPTER V.  ENTITLEMENT RESTORATION

11.20  GENERAL

a. Entitlement Restoration. VA will restore entitlement to individuals who discontinued training while in receipt of educational benefits as a result of being ordered to active duty. VA benefits include chapters 30, 32 (including Section 903), 33, and 35 of Title 38, and chapter 1606 of Title 10. Individuals receiving benefits while on active duty will also be entitled to entitlement restoration if they were ordered to new duty locations or assignments, or to perform increased amounts of work, and had to discontinue training as a result and did not receive credit for the course or courses dropped..


b. Accounts Receivable. VA will suspend collection of all Accounts Receivables (A/Rs) by individuals on active duty due to call up orders. This is a VA policy rather than a provision of law. During the time a claimant is called to active duty, the Debt Management Center (DMC) will suspend collection of all debts owed to the U.S. government. VCEs should notify DMC by their distribution list at the following address: DMCEDECManagement@va.gov when they become aware the student has been called to active duty, so that collection may be properly suspended, AND when they are aware the student has returned from active duty, so that DMC will resume collection of any A/Rs.

NOTE: Emails can now include PII when submitted to or from DMC using this process. These emails must be properly encrypted before transmission.

11.21  ENTITLEMENT RESTORATION

a. Individuals receiving chapters 30, 32, 33, 35, or 1606 benefits may be eligible for entitlement restoration for training interrupted as a result of being ordered to active duty. Individuals must have been ordered to active duty and have withdrawn from training on or after August 2, 1990, and must not have received credit or must have lost training time toward the completion of their approved educational, professional, or vocational objectives.

b. Restoration of Entitlement is authorized when a claimant had to discontinue the course or courses as a result of being ordered to:

1. Active duty service under 10 U.S. C. 688, 12301(a), 12301(d), 12301(g), 12302, or 12304; or

2. A new duty location or assignment or to perform an increased amount of work;

AND

3. Did not receive credit or lost training time for any portion of the period of enrollment in the course or courses.

NOTE: Chapter 35 also provides for those called to active duty under Title 32.

c. If a student receives an incomplete grade in a class because he/she had to drop due to military deployment, no action should be taken to terminate the enrollment prior to the date last attended, since mitigating circumstances are not an issue for persons involuntarily deployed. If, at a later date, the student is able to complete the coursework, and receive a grade the student should be paid for the entire term, regardless of the time taken to complete the work. Note since we would have restored entitlement for the term in question, when adjusting the record to reflect the completion of the course, the entitlement should now be charged for the entire term.

11.22  REVIEW PROCEDURES

a. Processing Procedures. If a withdrawal from training is reported and it is reported the student was called to active duty as the reason for withdrawal, the student may be entitled to restoration of entitlement.

b. Evidence Requirements.

(1) Evidence from the student’s unit is necessary. DD Form 214 is preferable; orders to duty are also acceptable. If the student submits his statement he was called to active duty, the VCE must validate the service period in DPRIS, VIS, etc. It is imperative the service is validated before the entitlement is restored.

(2) If a chapter 30, 33, or 32 claimant is already on active duty, withdraws from training due to being transferred, a copy of the transfer orders will be sufficient evidence to restore entitlement.

(3) If a case was previously developed for mitigating circumstances and no response was received, a copy of the orders activating the claimant or a letter from the commanding officer of the claimant's Selected Reserve unit that verifies activation is required.

NOTE: It is not necessary for individuals to actually enter active duty to be eligible for entitlement restoration. Individuals only have to be ordered to active duty and, as a result, discontinue education or training.

(4) VCEs may need to verify with the school if there is a question about whether the student did not receive credit or lost training time toward the completion of the objectives. Individuals in NCD programs who withdrew and received credit through the last date of attendance and students in standard college degree programs who withdrew from training but received passing grades are not eligible for entitlement restoration.

EXAMPLE: A student is enrolled in an NCD program at a police academy (not operating in a block or unit) from July 15, 2015 to November 15, 2015 for 40 hours per week. The course is approved for 720 hours. On September 15, 2015, the student was called to active duty. According to the school, the student accrued credit through last date attended (LDA) or September 15, 2015. The student returns from active duty on May 5, 2016. The next class for the police academy runs from June 10, 2016 to October 10, 2016. The academy is making the student attend the entire academy from June 10, 2016 to October 10, 2016.

ACTION: Although the school previously indicated the student accrued credit through LDA, they are requiring him to attend the entire next session. Therefore, since he was called to active duty and received no credit for the original training, the student is eligible for entitlement restoration.

11.24  MAXIMUM ENTITLEMENT

Normally an individual is only entitled to a maximum of 48 months of benefits if eligible under multiple education programs. However, entitlement restored under this law is not counted against the individual's maximum entitlement of 48 months. Thus, if an individual had used an aggregate of 42 months of entitlement under multiple education programs and had 6 months of entitlement restored, he or she would be considered to have 12 months of combined benefit entitlement remaining. However, entitlement can be restored only in the program in which it was originally charged.

SUBCHAPTER VI.  COMPUTER PROCESSING ISSUES


11.25  PROCESSING STOP PAYMENT AND SUSPEND ACTIONS FOR NON-CHAPTER 33 BENEFITS

NOTE: This section discusses the effect of Stop and Suspend actions on BDN cases. For chapter 33 benefits refer to Part 12 Subchapter 7, Enrollment Correct and Add Function-DGI Data Entry Rules.

a. Overview

(1) Operator Processing Vs. Computer-Generated Transactions. Stop payment and suspend payment actions may be entered by VCEs or in certain instances may be computer generated (e.g., returned check processing, delinquent certifications of attendance).

(2) Stop Payment. The purpose of a stop payment action is to establish a new ending date that is earlier than the previously scheduled termination date. It cannot be used to extend a scheduled termination date, which requires an amended award.

(3) Suspend Payment. A suspend payment action is used to suspend further payment of benefits when continued eligibility to payment is dependent upon the receipt of additional evidence, or when termination is warranted but additional information is needed to establish the correct ending date for payments. When a suspend payment action is processed, the DLP (Date Last Paid) will remain fixed, and no further payments will be released until the suspend action is cleared from the master record by an amended award or by a resume payment.

b. Processing Stop and Suspend Payment Actions

(1) Chapters 30, 1606, and 1607. Use the STOP or SUSP commands and the BDN312 screen.

(2) Chapters 32 and 35. Use the STOP or SUSP commands and the BDN 406 screen.

NOTE: For chapter 32 or 35 cases, processing a stop or suspend payment action before COB (Close of Business) on the cutoff date for recurring monthly payments will prevent issuance of the next recurring benefit payment. (See the Schedule of Operations to determine this date)

c. Processing Amended Awards to Reduce Benefit Payments

Use the CADJ command on the 312 or 412 screens as appropriate.

NOTE: For chapter 32 and 35 cases, processing an amended award before COB on the cutoff date for recurring monthly payments will prevent issuance of the next recurring benefit payment.

d. Notification to Student

(1) Reduced or Terminated Benefits. Whenever benefit payments are reduced or terminated, the student must be notified of the action and the reason(s) why, when processing the stop payment or amended award action.

(a) If the computer-generated letter will not be fully informative, send a PCGL letter.

(b) If an overpayment is created and a PCGL letter is used, ensure the letter informs the student he or she will receive a subsequent notice providing detailed information on repayment of the debt and an explanation of the student's rights to dispute the amount of the debt or to appeal the termination action.

(2) Suspended Benefits. A suspend payment action will not produce a computer-generated letter to the student. Any claim which has been suspended in BDN should always be placed in Await Status in TIMS.

(a) Always send a PCGL letter with the suspend-payment action to inform the student of the action taken and the reason(s) why.

(b) Maintain a pending issue control on any account that has been suspended so that the suspense can be cleared by a later resumption or final termination of benefit payments.

NOTE: It is important to remember a suspense action is an interim action. No final end product credit is authorized until the final action is taken.

11.26  EFFECT OF STOP AND SUSPEND ACTIONS ON VARIOUS TYPES OF TRAINING FOR BDN CASES

NOTE: This section discusses the effect on Stop and Suspend Actions on BDN cases. For chapter 33 benefits refer to Part 12 Subchapter 7, Enrollment Correct and Add Function-DGI Data Entry Rules.

a. IHL Cases

(1) Termination Date before DLP. A stop payment transaction (code 09 or 09V) processed with an effective date of termination before DLP will:

  • Terminate the account;
  • Adjust the entitlement used;
  • Remove the IHL control if set (chapters 32 and 35 only)
  • Update DLC (Date Last Certified); and
  • Establish an overpayment for the amount paid from the date of termination to DLP.

(2) Termination Date On or After DLP. A stop payment transaction processed with an effective date of termination on or after DLP will:

  • Clear the IHL control if set (chapters 32 and 35 only);
  • Update the record with the new ending date; and
  • Adjust the entitlement used.

If the effective date of the termination is on or after DLP, the stop payment action will update the DLC (chapters 32 and 35 only). A stop payment will not update the DLC for chapters 30, 1606, or 1607.

b. NCD (Non-College Degree) Cases

(1) Termination Date before DLP. A stop payment transaction processed with an effective date of termination before DLP will:

  • Terminate the account;
  • Adjust the entitlement used;
  • Remove the NCD bar (if set) (chapters 32 and 35 only);
  • Update DLC to the termination date (chapters 32 and 35 only); and
  • Establish an overpayment for the amount paid from the date of termination to DLP.

NOTE: A stop payment transaction should not be processed if certification of attendance is required at the same time. Instead, a "quit certification" (defined in Part 3, Chapter 8) should be processed.

(2) Termination Date On or After DLP. A stop payment transaction processed with an effective date on or after DLP will:

  • Update the record with the new ending date;
  • Adjust the entitlement used; and
  • Terminate the account and update the DLC to the termination date with no absences reflected for the uncertified period for chapters 32 and 35. In chapters 30, 1606, and 1607, DLC is not updated until a verification of enrollment transaction is processed.

c. Correspondence Cases (Chapter 35 Only). A stop payment transaction will properly adjust a correspondence account only under limited conditions.

NOTE: BDN currently does not process correspondence cases for benefits other than chapter 35.

d. OJT/Apprenticeship Cases (Chapter 35 Only)

(1) Termination Date On or Before DLC/DLP. A stop payment transaction processed with an effective date of termination on or before DLC/DLP will terminate the account, establish an overpayment (if appropriate), and adjust the entitlement used.

(2) Termination Date after DLC/DLP. A stop payment transaction processed with an effective date of termination after DLC/DLP will update the record with the new ending date but will not terminate the account because certification is required.

11.27  COMPUTER-GENERATED STOP AND SUSPEND PAYMENT TRANSACTIONS

a. Returned Advance Payments- Chapter 35. An advance payment, which is returned because the student is not entitled to the payment and which is properly coded under returned payment reason code "7," will cause the termination of the advance payment award. The termination date will be established as the DLC in the master record. A computer-generated termination letter is sent to the student.

NOTE: If a NOES or FOES is released, refer to the part of M22-4 which is responsible for each benefit and NOES processing.

b. Returned Payments—Regular Payments (Neither Advance Payment nor Lump-Sum)

(1) Non-entitlement

If a payment is returned for reason code (3) Deceased, (5) Non-entitlement, or (6) Non-entitlement –other, and the claimant has an existing accounts receivable in their record, the returned payment will first be applied to the accounts receivable even if the claimant has established a repayment plan. Any remaining amount of the return payments will establish proceeds in the BDN record. This applies to all education benefits.

(a) For chapter 35, an active master record will be suspended when a payment is returned because of apparent non-entitlement to the payment (returned payment reason codes other than 1, Undeliverable, or 7, Returned Advance Payment; see Part 2: Chapter 9- Code Tables, for these reason codes.)

(b) To clear the computer-generated suspense from the master record, process a resume-payment transaction (RESU command) (code 16V) or an amended award or stop payment transaction with an effective date equal to or earlier than the suspense date.

(2) Undeliverable—Active Record

(a) For chapter 35 payments returned as "undeliverable" under reason code 1 are handled as follows when a type "A" (Active) master record exists.

i. If only one payment has been returned, it will be established in the proceeds segment of the master record. This amount will be included in the next regular payment which is issued. To release the amount before the next regular payment is issued process a change of address (using CADD command) or an amended award.

ii. The master record will be suspended if more than one undeliverable payment is processed in a computer processing cycle or if the proceeds of a previously undeliverable payment exist in the master record at the time a second undeliverable payment is returned. To clear the suspense from the master record, process a change of address (CADD), a resume payment transaction (RESU), an amended award or a stop payment transaction (as appropriate).

(b) For chapter 32, a payment returned as "undeliverable" under reason code 1 is handled as follows:

i. If the address has been changed since the payment was issued and if the record is in a suspense status, the remaining amount will be released to the student at the new address. If no new address is available, a proceeds segment will be established.

ii. Active master records are suspended under reason code 21 if the address has not changed.

(3) Undeliverable-Terminated Record

(a) For chapter 32, the payment amount is applied to any existing A/R.

i. If money remains, the remainder is returned to the student.
ii. If no money remains, nothing more is done.
iii. If no A/R exists, a proceeds segment is established and NOE message 8014 is generated.

c. Identification of Computer-Generated Stop and Suspend Payment Transactions (Chapters 32 and 35)

(1) Computer-generated stop and suspend payment actions can be identified on the BDN M21 screen.

(2) By noting the data entered in the Pay Status field, Last Activity field, etc., a reliable analysis of computer-generated actions can be made even before the NOE message or award letter representing this action has been received in the field station.

(3) It is important to consider that any transactions which processed after the computer-generated action has taken place can change any or all of the data entered in these fields.
