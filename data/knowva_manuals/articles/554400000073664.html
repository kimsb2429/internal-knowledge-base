SUBCHAPTER I.  DEFINITIONS AND ELIGIBILITY

1.01 GENERAL

a. This chapter covers the unique features of chapter 35 of title 38, U.S.C. including establishing eligibility, periods of eligibility, entitlement, and rates.

b. The program of educational assistance under chapter 35 can be referred to either as Survivors' and Dependents' Educational Assistance or as DEA (Dependents' Educational Assistance.) It is designed to provide education benefits to the surviving spouse, spouse, or child of a Veteran who meets specific requirements. (See par. 1.02.)

 

1.02 ELIGIBILITY

There are several terms used within 38 CFR 21.3021 that have unique definitions.  (See (G) 2023 25 July: Procedural Advisory: Elimination of Delimiting Date for Certain Chapter 35 Beneficiaries for guidance as amended in 38 U.S.C. § 3512 by section 234 of PL 117-328)

a. Total means a disability was rated by VA as 100% (or less than 100% and unemployable). 

b. Permanent means a disability was permanent in nature; no future VA exam is required. 

c. Service-connected means a disability or death was due to military service.

d.  Eligible person means a surviving spouse, spouse, or child of a:

1) Veteran who had a service-connected death but did not have a service-connected disability rated as total and permanent with an effective date prior to death.  

The Veterans Service Center (VSC) provides a rating decision stating service-connection for the death and that DEA or chapter 35 is established. 

The VSC rating decision can be found by reviewing the following:

  • SHARE (Award/Ratings > Additional Rating Decisions) reflects that basic eligibility was established under 38 USC ch 35 on or after the date of death.  Any date of rating displayed may/may not be the actual date of rating establishing service-connection for the death; a review of Veterans Benefits Management System (VBMS) is required.  See Figure 1.03.
  • SHARE (Award/Ratings > Award Information) reflects a Dependency and Indemnity Compensation (DIC) record for a dependent.  See Figure 1.04.
  • VBMS rating that states that DEA or Chapter 35 is established on or after the date of death.  See Figure 1.05. 

NOTE 1:  If service-connection was granted for death, but the same, or an earlier, rating also granted service connection for a total and permanent disability existing prior to death, eligibility is based on the disability prior to death, see (2).

NOTE 2:  If the death was on active duty, in lieu of a rating decision, information showing a death in the line of duty will suffice.  Information showing a death in the line of duty can be found on DD Form 1300 (located in Defense Personnel Records Information Retrieval System or DPRIS) – Figure 1.01, Veterans Information Solution (VIS) – Figure 1.02, or VSC line of duty determination (located in VBMS).

NOTE 3:  If the beneficiary submits a copy of the rating or a copy is retrieved from another beneficiary’s education file, the Veterans Claims Examiner (VCE) should review VBMS to ensure the information received/retrieved is current; no later ratings revoked eligibility.

NOTE 4:  Veterans whose disability (or death) was caused by VA medical treatment or during a period of approved rehabilitation training 38 U.S.C. 1151 may receive a 100% compensation rate and dependents would receive the DIC rate.  But because the Veteran’s disability or death was not due to military service, it is not considered qualifying for chapter 35.  The VCE may notice all indicators that the disability or death was qualifying, yet DEA or chapter 35 was never established by rating.

NOTE 5:  If the death was on active duty, but no VSC rating decision or DIC record or line of duty determination is found, development to Department of Defense is required to determine if death was in the line of duty. 

If development to VSC is required, VCE should not ask beneficiaries for copies of ratings or letters notifying the Veteran of DEA eligibility.  This practice places VA in the very insensitive position of asking family members to ask other family members for sensitive information. 

Capture all supporting documentation in the beneficiary’s file.  Supporting documentation would include, but is not limited to, the following:  date of death, date of rating granting service-connection for death, DD Form 1300, or Veterans Information System (VIS).

2) Veteran evaluated with a service-connected disability that is total and permanent in nature.  If the Veteran has since passed (whether death was service-connected or non-service-connected), the total and permanent disability was rated as effective prior to death. 

VSC provides a rating decision stating that DEA or chapter 35 was established. If the Veteran has since passed, DEA was established with an effective date prior to death. If service-connection was also granted for death, this will affect a spouse’s eligibility period, as discussed in 1.05, and may affect a child’s eligibility period, as discussed in 1.04. However, eligibility is based on the disability prior to death. 

The VSC rating decision can be found by reviewing the following:

  • SHARE (Award/Ratings > Additional Rating Decisions) reflects that basic eligibility was established under 38 USC ch 35.  If the Veteran has since passed, the eligibility date is prior to death.  The date of rating displayed may/may not be the actual date of rating establishing service-connection for the death; a review of VBMS is required.  See Figure 1.03.
  • VBMS rating that states that DEA or chapter 35 is established.  If the Veteran has since passed, the eligibility date is prior to death.  See Figure 1.05. 

NOTE 6:  If the beneficiary submits a copy of the rating or a copy is retrieved from another beneficiary’s education file, the VCE should review VBMS to ensure the information received/retrieved is current; no later ratings revoked eligibility.

NOTE 7:  Include any Veteran who has a service-connected permanent and total disability; and at the time of VA’s determination is a member of the Armed Forces and is hospitalized or receiving outpatient medical care, services, or treatment; and is likely to be discharged or released from service for this service-connected disability.  The beneficiary no longer must wait until the Service member is discharged to quality for chapter 35. 

NOTE 8:  Dependents of Veterans disabled (or death was caused) by VA medical treatment or during a period of approved rehabilitation training 38 U.S.C. 1151 may receive a 100% compensation rate or DIC rate, but the Veteran’s disability or death was not due to military service and is not considered qualifying for chapter 35.  The VCE may notice all indicators show that the disability or death was qualifying, yet DEA or chapter 35 was never established by rating.

If all indicators reflect that the Veteran was evaluated with a service-connected disability (as of December 1, 2019 over $3,000 per month) that is total (100% or less than 100%, but unemployable) and permanent (no future exam), yet DEA or chapter 35 is not established, any denial letter will be edited to explain that even though the Veteran’s record indicates a service-connected disability that is total and permanent, DEA or chapter 35 was not established. 

If development to VSC is required, VCE should not ask beneficiaries for copies of ratings or letters notifying the Veteran of DEA eligibility.  This practice places VA in the very insensitive position of asking family members to ask other family members for sensitive information. 

Capture all supporting documentation in the beneficiary’s file.  Supporting documentation would include, but is not limited to, the following:  DEA effective date, date of rating that established DEA, date of notification of permanent and total disability.  If Veteran has since passed, include date of death and any date of rating granting service-connection for death

3) Service member who is on active duty as a member of the Armed Forces and who now is, and for a period of more than 90 days has been, listed by the Secretary concerned as missing in action, captured in line of duty by a hostile force, or forcibly detained or interned in line of duty by a foreign government or power.

Prisoner of war (POW) or missing in action (MIA) status may be obtained from Department of Defense (DoD) records, found in Defense Personnel Records Information Retrieval System (DPRIS).  POW information may also be obtained from the Veterans Information Solution (VIS) military history tab under Beneficiary Identification Records Locator Subsystem (BIRLS) data.

Capture all supporting documentation in the beneficiary’s file.  Supporting documentation would include, but is not limited to, the following:  DoD records, including VIS, that confirm a POW, MIA, or detention status.

e. Child means a biological, adopted, or stepchild of a Veteran as defined in 38 CFR 3.57, except as to age and marital status, per 38 CFR 3.807(d).  Therefore, the child does not have to be a member of the Veteran’s household by a certain age and VA would not terminate chapter 35 benefits due to marriage.  However, the marriage of a stepchild may terminate the relationship if the Veteran states they are no longer providing at least one-half support. 

For a biological, adopted, or stepchild, the following would establish relationship: 

  • SHARE (Award Ratings > Award Information) reflecting a DIC record (if the Veteran has passed) for the child (Figure 1.04); or
  • Child listed on Veteran’s award (SHARE > Person > All Relationships) with an award indicator of Y.  If N is shown, choose the name and then view (below) the reason the child is not on the award.  If the reason is due to school attendance or age, relationship is established (Figure 1.06); or
  • Child listed on Veteran’s award (SHARE > PreConvsn Master Rcd > Award Data (M12) showing reason 24 on the child’s 18th birthday or reason 25 on the child’s 23rd birthday (Figure 1.07); or
  • VBMS VA Form 21-8947 listing the child as added to the award (Figure 1.08); or
  • VBMS Notification letter listing the child as a dependent (Figure 1.09); or
  • VIS (C&P tab) listing the child as a dependent (Figure 1.10).
  • VIS (Education tab - Transfer of Entitlement (TOE) section) listing the biological or adopted child as a dependent (stepchild cannot be established through the TOE section).

 In addition, the following would establish relationship:

  • For a biological child, a birth certificate, displaying the Veteran as the child’s parent or a VA Form 21-686c, signed by the Veteran or surviving spouse, showing the biological relationship; or 
  • For an adopted child, the VA Form 21-686c cannot be used to establish relationship.  A copy of the final decree of adoption, interlocutory decree of adoption, or adoptive placement agreement, by the Veteran, is required.  If relationship is established based on an interlocutory decree or adoptive placement agreement and the court does not grant final approval of the adoption, 38 CFR 3.503(a)(10) requires VA to discontinue benefits.  Therefore, FLASH The Image Management System (TIMS) folder to develop, upon each enrollment received, for a final adoption decree; or
  • For a stepchild, the VA Form 21-686c, signed by the Veteran or surviving spouse, showing that the stepchild is the biological or adoptive child of the Veteran’s spouse and the date and place of the Veteran’s marriage to the stepchild’s parent, would suffice.  If the Veteran has since divorced the stepchild’s parent, the form should also show that the Veteran is providing at least half financial support.  If relationship is still not established, development to the beneficiary is required, asking for the VA Form 21-686c to be completed by the Veteran or surviving spouse.  

NOTE 9:  For a stepchild, VCE should review VBMS to ensure information is current; no later actions by VA or the Veteran revoked the relationship. 

NOTE 10:  See M21-1 Part 7 Subpart i for additional issues that may arise, but do not apply rules involving age or marital status.

If the above information does not establish relationship, development to the beneficiary is required, asking for appropriate documentation.  For a biological child, request a copy of their birth certificate or VA Form 21-686c signed by the Veteran or surviving spouse.  For an adopted child, request a copy of the adoption decree.  For a stepchild, request VA Form 21-686c, signed by the Veteran or surviving spouse.

Capture documentation in the beneficiary’s TIMS folder, supporting the relationship established. 

f. Spouse or surviving spouse means an individual as defined in 38 CFR 3.50, except as to opposite sex.  A copy of a marriage certificate in and of itself does not establish relationship, because the Veteran may no longer be married to the beneficiary.  For a surviving spouse or spouse, the following would establish relationship, whether marriage, domestic partnership, civil union, or common-law: 

  • SHARE (Award Ratings > Award Information) reflecting a DIC record (if the Veteran has passed) for the spouse (Figure 1.04); or
  • Spouse listed on Veteran’s award (SHARE > Person > All Relationships) with an award indicator of Y (Figure 1.06); or
  • VA Form 21-686c, signed by the Veteran, showing the relationship.
  • VBMS VA Form 21-8947 listing the spouse as added to the award (Figure 1.08); or
  • VBMS Notification letter listing the spouse as a dependent (Figure 1.09); or
  • VIS (C&P tab) listing the spouse as a dependent (Figure 1.10); or
  • VIS (Transfer of Entitlement) or DoD document that lists the spouse as a dependent (Figure 1.11).  This may only be used if the Service member died while on active duty, per VBA Letter 20-15-16, Administration of Same-Sex Spousal Benefits. 

NOTE 11:  The VCE should review VBMS to ensure the information is current; no later actions by VA or the Veteran revoked the relationship. 

NOTE 12:  If Veteran passes and spouse remarries, benefits are lost, with the following exceptions: 

1) Public Law 106-117 signed on November 30, 1999, reinstates benefits if the spouse terminated the remarriage due to death, divorce, or ceasing to live with another person and holding oneself out openly to the public as the person’s spouse.  No new eligibility period is granted.  Benefits may be reinstated the first of the month following termination of the remarriage.  

2) Public Law 108-183 signed on December 16, 2003, reinstates benefits if the remarriage occurred after the spouse’s 57th birthday and on or after January 1, 2004.  No new eligibility period is granted. 

NOTE 13:  See M21-1 Part 7 Subpart i for additional issues that may arise.

NOTE 14:  Same sex domestic or same sex civil unions where the Veteran has passed, refer to. M21-1MR III.iii.5.C

If the above information does not establish relationship, development to the beneficiary is required, asking for a VA Form 21-686c signed by the Veteran.

1.03 SERVICE IN ARMED FORCES - EFFECT ON ELIGIBILITY

a. No benefits under chapter 35 will be provided during any period the otherwise eligible person is on active duty with the Armed Forces, excluding any brief periods of ACDUTRA (Active Duty for Training) 38 CFR 21.3042(a).  Brief periods of ACDUTRA are periods less than 6 months for Active Duty for Training (ADT) and periods less than 3 months for Initial Active Duty for Training (IADT) 38 CFR 21.3021(f).  If the claimant is on duty; Active Duty (AD), ADT or IADT, FLASH the TIMS folder for future processors to be aware of current duty status. If claimant is currently on active duty that disqualifies payment, FLASH the TIMS folder that beneficiary cannot be paid until released from active duty. 

b. If a beneficiary has served with the Armed Forces, his or her discharge or release from each period of service must have been under conditions other than dishonorable 38 CFR 21.3042(b).  Discharges characterized as “honorable”, “under honorable conditions”, or “general” are automatically considered to be qualifying.  A discharge characterized as “dishonorable” is a bar to benefits. 

Discharges characterized as “undesirable”, “under other than honorable conditions”, or “bad conduct”, require VA to make an administrative decision to determine if service is qualifying.  When a character of discharge decision is required, Regional Processing Offices (RPOs) should consult BIRLS to see if a character of discharge decision has already been made. The character of discharge codes HVA (Honorable for VA Purposes) and DVA (Dishonorable for VA Purposes) indicate that VSC has made a character of discharge decision for the indicated period of service. 

EXAMPLE 1:  A chapter 35 applicant enlists for 3 years and serves 5 years. She receives an "other than honorable" discharge at the end of 5 years. VSC determines that this discharge is DVA. The VSC also determines that the first 3 years are HVA, applying the conditional discharge rule. The latter decision has no effect on the chapter 35 eligibility. Each period of active duty must meet the character of discharge requirement or a person cannot receive chapter 35.

If a character of discharge decision needs to be made, RPOs should request the regional office with jurisdiction over the claimant to make the necessary decision and to advise the RPO of the results. Route these requests through the RO's Education Coordinator. 

 

SUBCHAPTER II.  PERIODS OF ELIGIBILITY


1.04 PERIODS OF ELIGIBILITY – CHILDREN 38 U.S.C. 3512(a) and 38 CFR 21.3040  (See (G) 2023 25 July: Procedural Advisory: Elimination of Delimiting Date for Certain Chapter 35 Beneficiaries for guidance as amended in 38 U.S.C. § 3512 by section 234 of PL 117-328) 

a. Eligibility periods. For claims received prior to November 1, 2000, a child’s eligibility period began at age 18 (with an exception if child completed secondary schooling before age 18) and ended at age 26. 

Beginning with claims received on or after November 1, 2000, the eligibility period for a child is as follows: 

1) Veteran who died of a service-connected disability, but prior to death did not have a service-connected disability rated as total and permanent.    

a. Date of Death is before the child’s 18th birthday:  The child’s eligibility period begins at age 18 and ends at age 26. 

NOTE 15:  In a scenario where the date of death falls before age 18, but the date of rating or the date of notification falls after age 18, an election is not allowed.  The child’s eligibility period would be from age 18 to age 26.   

NOTE 16:  Eligibility may begin before age 18 if the child has completed high school, or an equivalent General Educational Development (GED).  Benefits cannot be paid prior to the Veteran’s date of death.  The ending date will remain age 26. 

NOTE 17:  Eligibility may begin before age 18 or high school/GED completion if the child is eligible for special restorative or specialized vocational training.  Benefits cannot be paid prior to the Veteran’s date of death.  The ending date will remain age 26.  See par 1.11 and 1.12. 

NOTE 18:  Eligibility may begin before age 18 or high school/GED completion if it is determined through counseling (Veteran Readiness and Employment or VR&E) that the best interests of the child would be served by beginning a program at an earlier date and the child has passed the compulsory school attendance age under state law. The compulsory school attendance age is the age that applies to students under state law.  If 18 is the compulsory school attendance age in a certain state but the child has a work permit and is 16 years of age, age 16 would be the compulsory school attendance age.  Benefits cannot be paid prior to the Veteran’s death.  The ending date will remain age 26. 

NOTE 19:  Eligibility may begin before age 18 or high school/GED completion if the child is pursuing a course designed to prepare the child for a college admissions test (preparatory tests).  The early beginning date is only for the purpose of taking preparatory tests and no other types of training.  Benefits cannot be paid prior to the Veteran’s death.  The ending date will remain age 26.   

NOTE 20:  An adopted child cannot begin benefits prior to the date that the child was adopted or, if stepchild, the date the child became a member of the Veteran’s household.  If the period of eligibility begins before the date of adoption or date the stepchild became a member of the Veteran’s household, FLASH the beneficiary’s TIMS folder to not pay before the date of adoption or date the stepchild became a member of the Veteran’s household.   

b. Date of death is between the child’s 18th and 26th birthdays: The child may choose when to start their eligibility period.  The child may choose to start their eligibility period either on the day after the date of death or on the day before the date of rating establishing service-connection for death, or any date in between.  The eligibility period will end 8 years from the date chosen.  This eligibility period may end after their 26th birthday.  There is no age limit as to the date when the eligibility period ends.  

NOTE 21:  Until the child chooses a date to start their eligibility period, VCE will choose the date of rating as the start of eligibility and allow 60 days (from the date of VA’s request for election) for the child to choose a different date.  If the child does not elect within those 60 days, the date of rating will be final.  Telephonic elections are acceptable.  The election block on the application is not used for an election, as it is for the sole purpose of having the individual elect when they would like VSC benefits to end.   

NOTE 22:  An adopted child cannot begin benefits prior to the date that the child was adopted or, if stepchild, the date the child became a member of the Veteran’s household. If the period of eligibility begins before the date of adoption or date the stepchild became a member of the Veteran’s household, FLASH the beneficiary’s TIMS folder to not pay before the date of adoption or date the stepchild became a member of the Veteran’s household.  

c. Date of death is after the child’s 26th birthday:  The child is not eligible. 

NOTE 23:  The child is not denied due to delimiting date reached, as the child has no eligibility period, to include beginning or ending dates.  The child is denied due to being overage at the time of Veteran’s eligibility. 

2) Veteran who has/had a total disability permanent in nature resulting from a service-connected disability.  If the Veteran has since passed (whether death was service-connected or non-service connected), the total and permanent disability was rated as effective prior to death. 

a. DEA effective date and date of notification of permanent and total disability are both before the child’s 18th birthday:  The child’s eligibility period begins at age 18 and ends at age 26.  

NOTE 24:  If the Veteran passed away between the child’s 18th and 26th birthdays, the child will get an automatic extension of 8 years from date of death.  A service-connected death is not required for this extension. 

NOTE 25:  Eligibility may begin before age 18 if the child has completed high school, or an equivalent GED.  Benefits cannot be paid prior to the DEA effective date.  The ending date will remain age 26. 

NOTE 26:  If there is a rating due to the hospitalization of a severely injured Service member, the claim must be expedited and there will be no date of notification.  If the DEA effective date and date of rating are both before Veteran is discharged from service and both before the child’s 18th birthday, the child’s eligibility period begins at age 18 and ends at age 26.  FLASH the beneficiary’s TIMS folder stating that no training may be paid before the enactment of Public Law 109-461, section 301, December 23, 2006.  If the date of rating is after the date the Veteran is discharged from service, the child is not eligible to receive chapter 35 under this provision   

NOTE 27:  An adopted child cannot begin benefits prior to the date that the child was adopted or, if stepchild, the date the child became a member of the Veteran’s household. If the period of eligibility begins before the date of adoption or date the stepchild became a member of the Veteran’s household, FLASH the beneficiary’s TIMS folder to not pay before the date of adoption or date the stepchild became a member of the Veteran’s household.    

NOTE 28:  Eligibility may begin before age 18 or high school/GED completion if the child is eligible for special restorative or specialized vocational training.  Benefits cannot be paid prior to the DEA effective date.  The ending date will remain age 26.  See par 1.11 and 1.12. 

NOTE 29:  Eligibility may begin before age 18 or high school/GED completion if it is determined through counseling VR&E) that the best interests of the child would be served by beginning a program at an earlier date and the child has passed the compulsory school attendance age under state law. The compulsory school attendance age is the age that applies to students under state law.  If 18 is the compulsory school attendance age in a certain state but the child has a work permit and is 16 years of age, age 16 would be the compulsory school attendance age.  Benefits cannot be paid prior to the DEA effective date.  The ending date will remain age 26. 

NOTE 30:  Eligibility may begin before age 18 or high school/GED completion if the child is pursuing a course designed to prepare the child for a college admissions test (preparatory tests).  The early beginning date is only for the purpose of taking preparatory tests and no other types of training.  Benefits cannot be paid prior to the DEA effective date.  The ending date will remain age 26. 

b. DEA effective date or date of rating establishing DEA or date of notification of permanent and total disability is between the child’s 18th and 26th birthdays:  The child may choose when to start their eligibility period.  The child may choose to start their eligibility period either on the DEA effective date (or 18th birthday, if later) or on the date of notification of permanent and total disability, or any date in between.  The eligibility period will end 8 years from the date chosen.  This eligibility period may end after their 26th birthday.  There is no age limit as to the date when the eligibility period ends.  

NOTE 31:  In a scenario where the DEA effective date falls before age 18, but the date of rating falls after age 26, an election is not allowed.  The child’s eligibility period would be from age 18 to age 26. 

NOTE 32:  Eligibility may begin before age 18 if the child has completed high school, or an equivalent GED.  Benefits cannot be paid prior to the DEA effective date.  The ending date will remain 8 years from the child’s 18th birthday. 

NOTE 33:  Until the child chooses a date to start their eligibility period, the VCE will choose the date of rating as the start of eligibility and allow 60 days (from the date of VA’s request for election) for the child to choose.  If the child does not choose within 60 days, the date of rating will be final.  Telephonic elections are acceptable. The election block on the application is not used for an election, as it is for the sole purpose of having the individual elect when they would like VSC benefits to end.

NOTE 34:  If the Veteran passed away between the child’s 18th and 26th birthdays, the child will get an automatic extension of 8 years from date of death.  A service-connected death is not required for this extension.

NOTE 35:  If there is a rating due to the hospitalization of a severely injured Service member, the claim must be expedited and there will be no date of notification.  If the DEA effective date and date of rating are both before Veteran is discharged from service and either the DEA effective date or the date of rating fall between the child’s 18th and 26th birthdays, the child may choose to start their eligibility period either on the DEA effective date or the date of rating or any date in between. VCEs must FLASH the beneficiary’s TIMS folder that no training may be paid before the enactment of Public Law 109-461, section 301, December 23, 2006.  If the date of rating is after the date the Veteran is discharged from service, the child is not eligible to receive chapter 35 under this provision.   

NOTE 36:  An adopted child cannot begin benefits prior to the date that the child was adopted or, if stepchild, the date the child became a member of the Veteran’s household. If the period of eligibility begins before the date of adoption or date the stepchild became a member of the Veteran’s household, the VCE should FLASH the beneficiary’s TIMS folder to not pay before the date of adoption or date the stepchild became a member of the Veteran’s household.     

EXAMPLE 2:  The DEA effective date is August 1, 2010, the date of rating establishing DEA is August 1, 2019, and the date of notification of permanent and total disability is August 3, 2019.  The child’s 18th birthday is on May 1, 2010, and the 26th birthday is on May 1, 2018.  Because one of the pivotal dates, in this case the DEA effective date, falls between the child’s 18th and 26th birthdays, the child may choose to start their eligibility period on August 1, 2010, or August 3, 2019, or any date in between.  If the child chooses August 3, 2019, the child’s delimiting date will be August 3, 2027, when the child is 34 years old.

 

c. DEA effective date and date of notification of permanent and total disability both fall after the child’s 26th birthday:  The child is not eligible. 

NOTE 37:  The child is not denied due to delimiting date reached, as the child has no eligibility period, to include beginning or ending dates.  The child is denied due to being overage at the time of Veteran’s eligibility. 

3) Service member for a period of more than 90 days has been listed as MIA, POW, or forcibly detained by a foreign government or power. 

a. If any of these three events occurs after the child's 18th birthday, but before the child's 26th birthday, the period of eligibility will begin on the date of the event, but not prior to age 18, and will extend 8 years, but not beyond the child's 31st birthday.

b. Delimiting Date extensions.  The below extension rules should be reviewed each time a claim is worked.  Extensions are granted, but not to exceed available entitlement.

1) Duty with the Armed Forces.  If a child serves on duty with the Armed Forces after their 18th birthday, but before their 26th birthday, see M22-4 Part 3 par 4.02 for rules and instructions. 

2) Delimiting date expires during an enrollment.  An extension may be granted through the end of the enrollment.  See M22-4 Part 3 par 4.04 for rules and instructions. 

3) Reasons beyond one’s control.  If child had to interrupt their training due to reasons beyond their control, see M22-4 Part 3 par 4.08 for rules and instructions. 

4) Call to active duty.  If child was called to active duty, see M22-4 Part 3 par 4.09 for rules and instructions. 

5) Death of Veteran.  If Veteran died after their permanent and total service-connected disability was established, see M22-4 Part 3 par 4.11 for rules and instructions. 

6) Primary Caregiver.  If child acted as the primary caregiver of a Veteran, see M22-4 Part 3 par 4.13 for rules and instructions. 

7) The Veteran is no longer rated permanently and totally disabled.  An extension will be granted to the end of the term during which the event (loss of eligibility) occurred.  See M22-4 Part 3 par 4.04 for rules and instructions. If the Veteran later re-establishes eligibility to permanent and total disability, apply a new 10-year delimiting date from the date of notification of permanent and total disability.

For information about how a Veteran could receive a reduction in their disability rating, see M21-1, Part X, Subpart ii, Chapter 4, Section A - Rating Decisions for Reduction Due to Improvement.

8) Veteran loses eligibility due to commission of fraud. Unless the dependent(s) participated in the fraud, an extension will be granted to the dependent(s) for up to 12 weeks, or to the end of the term, during which the event (loss of eligibility) occurred, whichever is less. Do not retroactively terminate nor establish overpayment against the dependent(s) when the dependent(s) was(were) not involved in the fraud.

Background: If a Veteran is determined to have obtained disability benefits through fraud, VA will terminate his or her disability compensation retroactively to the date of the fraud (which is often the date of the original award of disability compensation). In cases where the Veteran was awarded permanent and total disability (P&T) based on his or her fraud, the P&T rating will likewise be terminated retroactively. In the event that the Veteran had a dependent who qualified for DEA based on the terminated P&T rating, the dependent's eligibility to DEA is likewise terminated.

Finding that the Veteran was the only member who perpetrated the act, the dependent(s), who is(are) receiving benefits under DEA, is(are) allowed to continue training, per 38 USC § 3511(b)(1). This action will not result in any overpayments to the dependent(s).

However, when the dependent(s) participated in the fraud, terminate benefits retroactive to the date of the award. This action creates a debt for all benefits paid. (Authority: 38 USC § 5112(b)(9))

9) The service member is no longer listed as being a POW, MIA, or forcibly detained. An extension will be granted to the end of the term during which the event (loss of eligibility) occurred.  See M22-4 Part 3 par 4.04 for rules and instructions. 

10) Child ceases to be a stepchild.  An extension will be granted to the end of the term during which the event (loss of eligibility) occurred.  See M22-4 Part 3 par 4.04 for rules and instructions.

 

1.05 PERIODS OF ELIGIBILITY - SPOUSES & SURVIVING SPOUSES 38 CFR 21.3046  (See (G) 2023 25 July: Procedural Advisory: Elimination of Delimiting Date for Certain Chapter 35 Beneficiaries for guidance as amended in 38 U.S.C. § 3512 by section 234 of PL 117-328)

a. Eligibility periods. If eligibility is based on a service-connected death (Veteran did not have a service-connected disability rated as total and permanent with an effective date prior to death), and the death occurred: 

  • Before December 1, 1968, see 38 CFR 21.3046(b)(1-3). 
  • On or after December 1, 1968, and the rating established service-connection for the death before October 28, 1986, the spouse’s 10-year eligibility period began on the date of death, or the date of rating establishing service-connection for death. 
  • On or after December 1, 1968, and the rating established service-connection for the death on or after October 28, 1986, see (1) below. 

If eligibility is based on a service-connected disability, total and permanent in nature (if the Veteran passed, the Veteran had a service-connected disability rated as total and permanent with an effective date prior to death), and the DEA effective date is: 

  • Prior to October 28, 1986, the spouse’s 10-year eligibility period began on the DEA effective date or the date of notification of permanent and total disability, whichever was more advantageous. 
  • On or after October 28, 1986, see (2) below. 

1) Veteran who died of a service-connected disability but did not have a service-connected disability rated as total and permanent with an effective date prior to death.   

a. The spouse may choose when to start their eligibility period.  The spouse may choose to start their eligibility period either on the date of death or the date of rating establishing service-connection for death or any date in between.  The eligibility period will end 10 years from the date chosen.  

NOTE 38: Until the spouse responds, the VCE will choose the most advantageous date.  There is no time limit to respond, but once a date is chosen, it is final. Telephonic elections are acceptable. 

NOTE 39:  If the Veteran died on active duty, the spouse’s eligibility period begins on date of death and continues for 20 years.   

NOTE 40:  If the spouse is eligible for a 10-year eligibility period, based on date of rating and eligible for a 20-year eligibility period, based on date of death, VCE should allow what is most advantageous. 

2) Veteran evaluated with a service-connected disability that is total and permanent in nature.  If the Veteran passed away (whether death was service-connected or non-service-connected), the total and permanent disability was rated as effective prior to death. 

a. The spouse may choose when to start their eligibility period.  The spouse may choose to start their eligibility period either on the DEA effective date or the date of notification of permanent and total disability or any date in between.  The eligibility period will end 10 years from the date chosen.     

NOTE 41: Until the spouse responds, the VCE will choose the most advantageous date.  There is no time limit to respond, but once a date is chosen, it is final. Telephonic elections are acceptable. 

NOTE 42:  If the Veteran passed away, but the death was not rated as service-connected, the spouse gets an automatic 10-year extension from date of death.  If the death was rated as service-connected, the spouse may choose to start their 10-year eligibility period either on the date of death or the date of rating establishing service-connection for death, or any date in between.

NOTE 43:  If the date of marriage (even for a second spouse) falls between the DEA effective date and the date of notification of permanent and total disability, the spouse may choose between the date of marriage and the date of notification of permanent and total disability.  If the date of marriage falls after the date of notification of permanent and total disability, spouse has no election; the spouse’s eligibility period will begin on the date of marriage; the VCE should FLASH the beneficiary’s TIMS folder to not pay prior to date of marriage.   

NOTE 44:  If DEA effective date is within 3 years of Veteran’s release from active duty date, the spouse’s eligibility period will begin on the DEA effective date and continue for 20 years.  The spouse doesn’t have to be married to the Veteran for the entire time; but VA will not pay before date of marriage or date this change was effective, October 10, 2008.  VCE should FLASH the beneficiary’s TIMS folder to not pay prior to October 10, 2008. 

NOTE 45:  If there is a rating due to the hospitalization of a severely injured Service member, the claim must be expedited and there will be no date of notification.  If the DEA effective date and date of rating are both before Veteran is discharged from service, the spouse may choose to start their eligibility period either on the DEA effective date or the date of rating or any date in between.  VCEs should FLASH the beneficiary’s TIMS folder that no training may be paid before the enactment of Public Law 109-461, section 301, December 23, 2006.  If the date of rating is after the date the Veteran is discharged from service, the spouse is not eligible to receive chapter 35 under this provision.   

NOTE 46:  If the spouse is eligible for a 10-year eligibility period, based on an election period and eligible for a 20-year eligibility period, based on effective date within 3 years of Veteran’s release from active duty, VCE should allow what is most advantageous. 

EXAMPLE 3:  Date of marriage is March 1, 1995.  Veteran was released from active duty on March 1, 2014.  DEA effective date is March 1, 2015, and date of notification of permanent and total disability was March 1, 2020.  Spouse may begin their 10-year eligibility period on March 1, 2015, or March 1, 2020, or any date in between.  Since the DEA effective date was within 3 years of the Veteran’s release from active duty, the spouse is also eligible for a 20-year eligibility period from March 1, 2015.  It is more advantageous to have a 20-year eligibility period from March 1, 2015, with a delimiting date of March 1, 2035. 

EXAMPLE 4:  Date of marriage is March 1, 1995.  Veteran was released from active duty on March 1, 1994.  DEA effective date is March 1, 1995, and date of notification of permanent and total disability was March 1, 2020.  Spouse’s only enrollment (after review of the application and the TIMS folder) is for Spring 2020.  Spouse may begin their 10-year eligibility period on March 1, 1995, or March 1, 2020, or any date in between.  Since the DEA effective date was within 3 years of the Veteran’s release from active duty, the spouse is also eligible for a 20-year eligibility period from March 1, 1995, to March 1, 2015.  It is more advantageous to have a 10-year eligibility period that will cover the Spring 2020 enrollment

 

3) Service member for a period of more than 90 days has been listed as MIA, POW, or forcibly detained by a foreign government or power. 

a. The period of eligibility will begin on the date of the event and will extend 10 years.

b. Delimiting Date extensions.  The below extension rules should be reviewed each time a claim is worked.  Extensions are granted, but not to exceed available entitlement.

3) Call to active duty.  If claimant was called to active duty, see M22-4 Part 3 par 4.09 for rules and instructions. 

4) Death of Veteran.  If Veteran died after their permanent and total service-connected disability was established, see M22-4 Part 3 par 4.11 for rules and instructions. 

5) Divorce.  If the claimant divorced the Veteran, without fault on the claimant’s part, see M22-4 Part 3 par 4.04 for rules and instructions. 

6) Veteran no longer rated permanently and totally disabled.  An extension will be granted to the end of the term during which the event (loss of eligibility) occurred. See M22-4 Part 3 par 4.04 for rules and instructions. If the Veteran later re-establishes eligibility to permanent and total disability, apply a new 10-year delimiting date from the date of notification of permanent and total disability.

For information about how a Veteran could receive a reduction in their disability rating, see M21-1, Part X, Subpart ii, Chapter 4, Section A - Rating Decisions for Reduction Due to Improvement.

7) Veteran loses eligibility due to commission of fraud. Unless the dependent(s) participated in the fraud, an extension will be granted to the dependent(s) for up to 12 weeks, or to the end of the term, during which the event (loss of eligibility) occurred, whichever is less. 8) The service member is no longer listed as being a POW, MIA, or forcibly detained. An extension will be granted to the end of the term during which the event (loss of eligibility) occurred.  See M22-4 Part 3 par 4.04 for rules and instructions.

1.06 RETROACTIVE PAYMENTS (See (G) 2023 25 July: Procedural Advisory: Elimination of Delimiting Date for Certain Chapter 35 Beneficiaries for guidance as amended in 38 U.S.C. § 3512 by section 234 of PL 117-328) 

A qualifying surviving spouse, spouse, or child will be awarded an eligibility period, as described in par 1.04 and 1.05.  However, the VCE is required to alert the beneficiary, when asking for an election choice, of any commencing date rules 38 CFR 21.4131 that may affect a beneficiary’s choice. Basic commencement date rules are contained in M22-4 Part 4, par 1.12(b) and (c). 

For chapter 35, there are two rules that directly affect a beneficiary's start date election and allows the VA to pay benefits from the effective date.

        1. Effective date and the date of rating are more than one year apart.

        2. Eligible person submits their application for benefits under chapter 35 to the VA within one year of the rating decision (rating date) that establishes eligibility under chapter 35.

When a beneficiary's effective date and rating date are more than one year apart and the beneficiary has submitted their application within one year of the rating decision that establishes eligibility, the VA can pay benefits from the effective date.  In cases where one of these two rules is not met, the VA can only pay benefits from the date of the first application received minus one year.

In instances where the effective date and rating date are more than one year apart and the beneficiary submitted an application in the past before the effective date, which was denied, the VA will consider this application to have been submitted within one year of rating decision and can pay from the effective date.

Processors can use the Benefits Manager VA Eligibility Start Date Calculator Job Aid for assistance in determining the correct start date in Benefits Manager.

EXAMPLE 5:  A child’s 8-year eligibility period may begin on the DEA effective date of March 1, 2015, or date of notification of permanent and total disability of March 1, 2017, or any date in between.  The DEA effective date was more than one year prior to the date of rating (February 27, 2017); however, the child did not apply within one year of the date of rating.  The child applied on March 1, 2020.  Therefore, when requesting the child to choose their beginning date, VCE must include information stating that although they can choose a beginning date as early as March 1, 2015, VA can only pay benefits from March 1, 2019, one year retroactive from the date of application.

1.07 ENTITLEMENT

a. Months of Entitlement 38 USC 3511(a) and CFR 21.4020

(1) Each eligible beneficiary may receive a maximum of 36 months of DEA entitlement (or the equivalent in part-time training). Entitlement may not be extended.

NOTE 47:  If DEA enrollment began before August 1, 2018, the beneficiary is entitled to a maximum of 45 months of entitlement.

NOTE 48:  If beneficiary has used other benefits (chapters 30, 32, 33, 34, 1606, 1607 or Sections 901 and 903), they are entitled to a maximum of 81 months of entitlement between all benefits.  Transferred entitlement received by the beneficiary will not be included in the maximum.  Effective April 1, 2021, chapter 31 benefits used by the beneficiary will not be included in the maximum for those who have not reached their delimiting date by April 1, 2021. 

The maximum of 81 months does not apply to beneficiaries who have exhausted 48 months of benefits prior to October 1, 2013 (i.e. BDN no pay date is prior to October 2, 2013 or Long-Term Solution (LTS) entitlement exhaust date is prior to October 1, 2013).   

While applying the 81-month maximum, the beneficiary cannot exceed 48 months between non-chapter 35 sources and cannot exceed 36 months (or 45 months if training began before August 1, 2018) between chapter 35 sources. 

NOTE 49:  A beneficiary may receive an additional 5 months of full-time training for remedial, deficiency, refresher, or high school training. 

NOTE 50:  For a child, entitlement may be extended under the program of Special Restorative Training. See paragraph 1.11 regarding Special Restorative Training.

NOTE 51:  A child eligible for chapter 35 by means of more than one parent may receive concurrent chapter 35 payments.  A child could qualify for twice the full-time monthly rate but would only receive 18 months of benefits from each parent (22 ½ months from each parent, if eligible for 45 months of entitlement).  The child will elect two separate beginning dates for each parent. 

EXAMPLE 6:  Beneficiary is a Veteran, in their own right, and used 20 months of chapter 30 and 20 months of chapter 1606.  Now, he applies for DEA, with an enrollment that began Spring of 2020.  VA would award 36 months of DEA entitlement.

EXAMPLE 7:  Beneficiary is a Veteran, in their own right, and used 24 months of chapter 30 and 24 months of chapter 1606.  Dependent applies for DEA, with an enrollment that began Spring of 2020.  VA would award 33 months of DEA entitlement.

EXAMPLE 8:  Beneficiary is a Veteran, in their own right, and used 20 months of chapter 30 and 20 months of chapter 1606.  Dependent applies for DEA, with an enrollment that began Spring of 2018.  VA would award 41 months of DEA entitlement.

b. Entitlement Charges 38 CFR 21.3045.

(1) For institutional training, VA will charge a full day of entitlement for each day paid at the full-time rate. If the beneficiary enrolls at less than full time, VA will reduce the entitlement charge proportionately. If the beneficiary enrolls at less than 1/2 time, VA will charge entitlement at 1/4 the full-time rate.

(2) For apprenticeship and other on-the-job training, VA will charge a full day of entitlement for each day of training except that if the beneficiary works less than 120 hours per month, both the entitlement charge and the benefits paid will be reduced proportionately.

(3) For correspondence training, VA will charge 1 month of entitlement for a benefit amount equal to the monthly rate for full-time institutional training.

(4) For secondary level training (including noncredit refresher, deficiency, remedial, and high school training), VA will charge no entitlement for the first 5 months of full-time training. After 5 months of such training, VA will charge entitlement on the same basis as institutional training.

(5) For tutorial assistance, there is no entitlement charge. (See M22-4, Part 3, chapter 10, regarding tutorial assistance.)

(6) For certain overpayment cases where the debt is written off, VA will charge entitlement. See M22-4, Part 4, paragraph 13.05.

(7) For education loans for spouses and surviving spouses, VA will charge entitlement at the rate of one day for each day of entitlement that would have been used had the spouse or surviving spouse received educational assistance for the period for which the loan was granted. See M22-4, Part 7, chapter 7 regarding education loans.

1.09 NONDUPLICATION OF BENEFITS

b. VA Administered Education Benefits. See M22-4, Part 4, paragraph 3.02. 

Effective January 1, 2015, spouses who are eligible under the Post-9/11 GI Bill Fry Scholarship and chapter 35 based on the same Veteran’s death must make an irrevocable election.  The choice of benefit on the application is their election.
