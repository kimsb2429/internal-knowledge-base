 BDN PROCESSING PROCEDURES

2.01 GENERAL

a.  [This chapter describes certain unique features of chapter 35 BDN (Benefits Delivery Network) processing.  See part II for a description of chapter 35 processing that is similar to other systems.  See subsequent chapters in this part for specific procedures required in the adjudication of education claims. 

b.  Although no chapter 34 benefits are payable for training occurring after December 31, 1989, occasions may arise which require retroactive award actions for chapter 34 cases.  Therefore, this chapter also addresses limited chapter 34 issues, such as     reactivation of inactive M/Rs (Master Records).  (BDN has one system for both chapter 34 and chapter 35 claims.)]

2.02 311 CHAPTER 35 ELIGIBILITY SCREEN

a.  General Functions.  RPOs (Regional Processing Offices) use the 311 chapter 35 Eligibility screen to compute a beneficiaries’ eligibility, entitlement, and delimiting date for both original and supplemental claims.  RPOs also use the 311 screen to adjust a beneficiaries’ delimiting date and entitlement.  Because data from this screen goes into the beneficiaries’ master record, the 311 screen is required for original awards. 

(1)  Access.  RPOs access the 311 screen using the CDAT (Claims Data Entry) or CADJ (Claims Adjudication) BDN (Benefits Delivery Network) commands from the Ready screen or from 300 or 400 series education screens.

(2)  Completion for Data Entry Purposes.  RPOs may complete the 311 screen for either an original or supplemental claim, only when, at the same time, the VCE (Veterans Claim Examiner) is processing an award.  RPOs should use the CORR (Correction) command to make changes to the beneficiary’s record if they need to make any eligibility or entitlement data changes when no award is needed. (For CORR information, see pt. II, ch. 8.)

(3)  Worksheet.  RPOs may also use the 311 screen as a worksheet for computing eligibility or entitlement before processing an award or a disallowance.  When all the information has been entered and the eligibility or entitlement computation has been completed, the VCE may enter DEL (Delete) in NEXT SCREEN field to prevent the PIF (Pending Issue File) from storing data entered on the 311 screen.  Use of DEL will not cancel the pending issue.  DEL will only remove the data entered on the screen.

b.  Field Descriptions.  The 311 screen fields are described below.  The fields are separated into types of fields: i.e., display only, entry and display, entry only, and generated.

2.04 [BDN] SPECIAL PAYMENT PROCESSING

a.  General Functions.  A special payment may be processed on [BDN] by [the Education Division] for IHL (Institutions of Higher Learning), NCD (Non-College Degree), farm cooperative, OJT (On-the-Job Training)/apprenticeship and cooperative training as described in this paragraph.  Note that the Finance activity can make special payments using fiscal transactions, if necessary.

NOTE:  Special payments may not be processed on [BDN] for the following:  correspondence awards; payments spanning periods with training time changes; payments spanning steps in OJT/apprenticeship courses; payments exceeding $1,500; and advance pay special payments.

(1)  A special payment processed on [BDN] must meet the requirements of part IV, chapter 10.

(2) [The special payment must be processed at Hines BDC (Benefits Delivery Center) before the award is authorized.  In order to maintain this processing sequence, if BDN is used to prepare the award, the special payment must be processed on BDN.  Do not process the award on BDN without also processing the special payment on BDN].

NOTE 1:  If VA Form 22-1997 is used as an out-of-system award, special payment will be processed in accordance with part IV, paragraph 10.18.  The award must be authorized by a senior [VCE] and referred to the Finance activity for the processing of an out-of-system payment.  (See pt. IV, ch. 12.)

NOTE 2:  Although all chapter 35 transactions are processed on a cycle basis, the transactions entered during a cycle are processed in batches.  Each batch within the cycle is assigned a number according to the date the transactions were completed.  The batches are then processed in numerical order.  This means that if the operator authorizes an award and then decides to authorize a special payment the next day, both days falling within the same cycle, the award transaction will receive a lower batch number than the special payment transaction and will process before the special payment.  This can result in duplicate payments - one based on the award, the other as a result of the special payment.

(3)  When a special payment is requested and meets the criteria in part IV, chapter 10, the [BDN special payment must be processed using the HELP command and approved by a senior VCE] or higher authority via the CAUT (Claims Authorization) command.

b.  Help Command - Ready Screen

(1)  The required Ready screen entries are: 

(i)  HELP command and operator's password; 

(ii)  The claimant's file number; 

(iii)  The payee number; 

(iv)  The benefit (CH35); and 

(v)  The full name of the Veteran, or, if a master record exists, the stub name of the claimant instead of the full name of the Veteran; or 

(vi)  The claimant's stub name and the Veteran's full name if the benefit is chapter 35.

(2)  When the Ready screen edits have been passed successfully, [BDN] searches the PIF, the master record file, and BIRLS.  If a pending issue or a master record exists, [BDN] returns the operator to the M31 special payment inquiry screen with appropriate displays.

(3)  If there is no PIF or master record, [BDN] displays the BIRLS record.  The operator must verify the BIRLS information and either correct or enter the necessary information.  When the BIRLS record is completed and the Enter key is pressed, [BDN] shows the M31 screen.

(4) [If BDN cannot find a master record or a pending issue that matches all the Ready screen information, BDN shows the M31 screen with a message explaining what is wrong and with instructions to cancel the issue.  The operator must] start over.  Some of the messages in this instance are as follows:

(i)  RECORD NOT FOUND AT HINES, CANNOT ATTEMPT TO ACCESS BIRLS WITHOUT A FULL NAME.  START NEW COMMAND WITH READY KEY.  [In this instance, the operator only entered the stub name.  BDN did not find a master record for the file and payee number entered on the ready screen.]

(ii)  CANNOT EXCEED ONE SPECIAL PAYMENT PER CASE.  START NEW COMMAND WITH READY KEY.  In this instance, another special payment has been entered and is in the PIF.  Under PENDING ISSUE EP CODE, [BDN] shows the end product code (001) and the amount of the special payment.

(5)  When the special payment has been approved, [BDN] generates a print of the 602 screen with the special payment amount and the computation information. The operator should sign the print.  The action is then ready to be reviewed and authorized.

e.  Special Payment Authorization.  A special payment may be processed by [a VCE.  The special payment must be authorized by a senior VCE, unit chief, or higher designation.  The authorizer must always be a person different from the operator who processed and approved the special payment on the 602 screen.  The authorizer uses the CAUT command to access the 501 Authorization screen. Enter "N" to disapprove the action or "Y" to approve the action in AUTHORIZE.

2.05 INACTIVE SYSTEM PROCESSING FOR CHAPTER 34 ONLY

a.  General.  As explained in part II, paragraph 3.09, certain chapter 34 records are in an inactive status.  The inactive file is not accessed in the same way as the active file.  The only way to approach the inactive file is through four specific transactions.  Three of the four transactions are built from information entered into [BDN].  The fourth is created as a result of another transaction.  The inactive file transactions will generate master record write outs, set the Notice of Death Indicator, and reactivate an inactive master record.

b.  Identifying an Inactive Chapter 34 Master Record.  [When a chapter 34 master record is inactive, BDN] will display the M25 Abbreviated Education Master Record screen via a MINQ inquiry as described in part II, chapter 3.

c.  Requesting Master Record Write-outs.  Transaction 39V generates an Education master record write out.  (See par. 2.17 for 39V procedures.)  These are printed on VA Form 20-8270a, Education Master Record-Audit Write out.  With [BDN]'s immediate access to master records via the [BDN] inquiry commands, transaction 39V is not used often except in special cases.  This transaction is sometimes used because VA Form 20-8270a contains more master record information than the MINQ master record screens.

(1)  Generally, a 39V transaction is used to request an active master record.  However, if no matching active master record exists, the transaction 39V is automatically transferred to the inactive file for processing.

(2)  Within the inactive system, every master record with the same file number, as coded on the 39V transaction, will be written out under message code 001Z, REPLY TO GENERAL INQUIRY (INACT).  The 39V transaction ignores the payee number and stub name.  The master records remain intact on the inactive file.

d.  Setting the Notice of Death Indicator.  Only transaction 28 sets the Notice of Death Indicator in chapter 34 cases.

(1)  If the transaction does not match an active master record, it is sent automatically to the inactive system.  If a complete match (file number, stub name, and payee number (always "00")) is made with a record in the inactive system, the Notice of Death Indicator is set "on," thus updating the data on the inactive file.

(2)  If, however, a record is found which matches only the file and payee number, [BDN] issues both a message code 565B, DETAIL DOES NOT MATCH MASTER (INACT), and a master record write out.

e.  Reactivating an Inactive Master Record.  If it is necessary for [an RPO to recall a claims file from the RMC (Records Management Center), the RPO will initiate a request to the RMC for folder transfer using BIRLS.]  The requested folder transfer also will produce a request to reactivate the master record.  Reactivation is the removal of the master record from the inactive file and the reinsertion (transfer) of the master record to the active file.

f.  Reactivation of Matching Master Record.  When transfer of the folder is requested from the RMC through BIRLS, the folder location is updated automatically in BIRLS with the current date.  BIRLS generates a transaction 23 (change of regional office) to Hines BDC for normal cycle processing in the active master record file.  This transaction passes through the active file to the inactive file.

(1)  If an inactive master record is found with all correct matching information (file number, payee number, and stub name), the regional office number in the record will be updated and a transaction 01V will be generated to reinsert the master record into the active system.

(2)  If the inactive master record found matches on file number and payee number, but not stub name, a message code 565A, VET RETURNED T0 ACTIVE FILE--REQ STUB (XXXXXXX), will be generated, accompanied by a full master record write out.  (The message will generate the stub name from the master record and display it in the parenthesis as shown above.)  Even though the stub name does not match, the inactive file will be reinserted into the active system.

(i)  The message indicates that action should be taken to correct the stub name in the master record.

(ii)  These documents will show routing to the Correspondence activity for review and corrective action.  Follow current procedures for changing a name in a master record.

g.  Reinserting an "Inactive Master Record.  The 01V "Reinsert Master" transaction is generated by the inactive system upon successfully finding a matching master record.  The 01V transaction turns off the inactive indicator in the record and transfers the master record to the active file.  Generally, the master record is then reestablished as an active master record.  However, if another master record is found when attempting to reinsert the inactive master record into the active system, the inactive record will be rejected with message code 965, DUPLICATE M/R's--INACT M/R PURGED--REVIEW.

(1)  The message will be routed to [a VCE].  The write out should be reviewed, and any required correction should be reflected in the retained record.

(2)  If the active and inactive master records reflect records for different persons under the same file number, the active master record should be transferred to the correct file number.  Then another transaction should be processed to reactivate the inactive record.

(3)  If both master records reflect separate accounts established for the same person, no action need be taken unless there has been duplicate payment, or the inactive master record contains service, payment, entitlement, or other information critical to current benefit authorization.

(4)  In most of the cases having "critical" inactive file information, the active master record will have to be canceled (purged).  (See pt. II, ch. 8, for purge procedures.)

(i)  Action then must be taken to generate a new transaction 23 to reactivate the master record.  Use CHAIRS to generate this transaction 23.

(ii)  Any resulting A/Rs must then be established by the Finance activity within the system to create a type D (accounts receivable) master record.

(5)  If reinsertion of an inactive master record other than type E fails, the inactive record will automatically be canceled instead of being returned to the inactive file.  [BDN] issues a write-out with message code 863, DUPL CNO-REINSERT TRANS HIT ACTIVE M/R. Unless the review shows a duplicate claim situation, or information which otherwise requires correction, no action need be taken.

h.  Other Reasons for Inactive File Processing Failure

(1)  In some cases, due to erroneous master record conditions, the master record may have been removed to the inactive file even though the claims folder had never left the RPO.  Infrequently, a situation may also occur, due to a failure in transaction 23 processing, when, even though the folder was transferred from the RMC and is now in the RPO (BIRLS having been updated to reflect such location), the master record remains in the inactive file.  The record status, particularly in the latter case, may not be readily apparent from the material in the claims folder.  BIRLS may even reflect folder location as that of the RPO. Therefore, some transactions routinely processed to reactivate a claim may reject or recycle.  In such cases, corrective action must be taken immediately to generate a new transaction 23 to reactivate the master record.

(2)  Processing certain education transactions to reactivate a record which has been inactive for a long time may result in message 544, TRANSACTIONS RECYCLED--NO M/R OR WRONG TYPE M/R, issuing.  This message may be generated even though there is nothing of record to show that the claims folder was ever in the RPC, and its master record, thus, inactivated.  This situation is the result of "bad" master record conditions.  In no such case should automatic master record reactivation be assumed, or reinsertion anticipated.  Rather, immediate action must be taken to process a BUPD (BIRLS Update) transaction.

i.  Inactive Cycle Processing

(1)  All 23, 28, and 39 transactions pass through the active C&P (Compensation and Pension) system first during normal C&P cycle processing.  Then these transactions pass through the active education system for chapter 34 records and are accumulated for processing into the inactive system weekly.  (Inactive file processing is scheduled for Wednesday of each week and appears on the Education Schedule of Operations published each month by Hines BDC.  See pt. II, ch. 2.)  Therefore, a transaction 23 which passes through both the active C&P and Education system during normal cycle processing may be held for inactive processing a minimum of 1 day or a maximum of 1 week before being processed in the inactive system.  This means that while master record reactivation is automatic with permanent transfer of the claims folder from the RMC (or otherwise with change of RPO processing), the two processes are not necessarily affected simultaneously.  Sometimes, in fact, the folder will be received before reinsertion of the master record in the active file.  This is of little consequence under normal conditions since reactivation delay will be minimal, and the master record will be reestablished on the active file in ample time to accept transaction processing.  Nevertheless, certain conditions may exist for which this will not be the case (i.e., reactivation/delay may be substantial or may not take place at all).  These conditions must be clearly understood and given special consideration.

(2)  When a 544 message issues under conditions indicating a reasonable master record insertion delay, submission of a new transaction 23, generally, will not be necessary.  If, for example, the folder contains evidence of its recent (permanent) transfer from the RMC, and if, between the time of folder receipt and message issuance, the inactive system has not run, it can be assumed that the transaction 23 will process in the next scheduled inactive run.  On this basis, the processing cycle of reinsertion can be determined, as can the anticipated payment date for any recycled award transaction which will "drop in" the same cycle.  This, in turn, will determine the need for special payment action.

(3)  If authorization transactions process against the active file before reinsertion of the inactive master record, they will either recycle, issuing message 544, or reject, issuing message 566, DETAIL CANNOT BE PROCESSED AGAINST M/R.  (NOTE: A message will issue for each transaction split from the "base" transaction.  For example, if a reentrance award with change of address recycles, a separate 544 message issues for the 12C and 22V transactions respectively.)

(4)  When 544 or 566 message issuances can be attributed only to non-reactivation of an inactive master record, the likelihood of unsuccessful transaction 23 processing must first be considered.  If there is any doubt concerning effectiveness of such processing, immediate action must be taken to generate a new transaction 23.  Use CHAIRS to generate this transaction 23.

(5)  The appropriate reactivation of any record and, therefore, its correct status, depends upon proper RPO action to assign and cancel file numbers, establish records, and process authorizations with reference to the claims folder.  BIRLS data must be accurately entered and kept up to date.  If existing procedures are strictly followed, it is unlikely that many of the difficulties associated with inactive processing will occur.

j.  Reconciliation of Erroneous Conditions.  A reconciliation of erroneous conditions between the active and the inactive files will take place each January.  At this time, [RPO will send e-mail to report the improper existence of the same master record in both the active and the inactive files to the Director, Education Service.  (see pt. 1, par. 1.08 for e-mail address.)]
