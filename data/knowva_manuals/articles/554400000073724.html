SUBCHAPTER I.  312 Screen

6.01   Introduction

a. The Chapter 1607 312 screen is the primary award processing screen for Chapter 1607. It's very similar to both the Chapter 30 and Chapter 1606 systems, with a few modifications. There are only three override codes in Chapter 1607 processing: "M", "L" and "J". The dependency field is used to indicate the percentage the claimant is due, either 40%, 60% or 80%.

b. Two reason codes have been added for use on the 312 screen: 6E and 7A. 6E is used when a claimant requalifies after losing eligibility under Chapter 1607. 7A is used to reflect a "milestone", when a claimant's percentage rate increases at the one year mark and two year mark.

c. These are the primary system differences encountered when processing a 1607 claim. This subchapter will focus on the 312 screen and its function and use.

 Go to Top

6.02   Accessing the 312 Screen

a. Like the Chapter 30 or 1606 system, the 312 screen can be accessed for input through either the CADJ, STOP, SUSP or RESU commands on the Ready screen. If establishing a new end product the required Ready screen entries are:

CADJ (or the appropriate command if not CADJ)

Password

File Number

Payee Number (00)

End Product

Benefit (1607)

Stub name

b. If the 312 screen is requested under "Screen" on the Ready Screen, upon ALT/ENT the 312 screen will be displayed. If Screen is left blank on the Ready screen the 30 M (menu) will appear. The 312 screen can be accessed from there by entering 312 in Next Screen.

c. If an end product is already pending the 312 screen can be accessed from the Ready Screen by entering only 312 in Screen and the file number.

d. For inquiry the 312 screen can be accessed through SINQ or PINQ only if an issue is pending.

 Go to Top

6.03   Updating the Master Record

Any data entered on the 312 screen will update the Master Record after the award is authorized under the CAUT command. The Chapter 1607 system updates the record just as Chapters 30 and 1606 do, all three are "on-line" systems and updates to the Master Record are immediate upon authorization. Any data entered on the 312 screen will remain there for review if GAP is entered in the Next Screen field on the 312 screen.

6.05  Using the 312 Screen

a. There are 5 basic award types that are processed on the 312 screen under Chapter 1607. These are the same types of awards processed using the 312 screen under Chapters 30 and 1606.

Original Award: If the master record status is neither Running or Terminated, the award is considered to be an original award. This can also apply if benefits have previously been paid out of system, or if the award is not the original claim (COE has been issued).

Amended Award: When award lines exist in the master record and the beginning date of the award is earlier than the No Pay Date of the last master record award line, the award is considered to be an amended award.

Reentrance Award: When award lines exist in the master record and the beginning date of the award is equal to or later than the No Pay Date of the last master record award line, the award is considered to be a reentrance award.

Stop/Reentrance Award: When the award consists of only two lines, the first of which contains a No Pay Date and Reason and the second line contains Beginning Reason code "60", the award is a Stop/Reentrance award.

Back to Inception Award: This award action is a Stop or Termination to the original beginning date of the award ("00" or "75"). If new award is done following a Back to Inception award it will be processed as an original award.

b. Chapter 1607 award processing uses only 3 override codes. The override function is identical to that in Chapters 30 and 1606, it is used to specify a type of payment ("L"- lump sum), a type of claimant ("J"-incarcerated) or a rate that is different than the rate BDN calculates ("M"-manual). These three codes designate the only overrides that apply to Chapter 1607.      

c. BDN will not include a 1606 kicker in its calculations for a less than half-time enrollment or an active duty claimant due a Chapter 1606 kicker. In these situations, when charges are required on the 312 screen, the monthly rate has to be overridden to include the kicker. In these instances the M override will take precedence over the L override. Unfortunately, the claimant will not receive a lump sum payment, but he or she will get the portion of the kicker due the clamant.

 6.07  Processing Changes in Enrollment (VA Form 22-1999b)

See RPO letter 22-08-05.

In Chapter 1607 processing a VA Form 22-1999b requires two special considerations:

Use an End of the Month effective date for changes in enrollment status which occur after the enrollment has been entered in BDN, just as current processing in Chapter 35.

Adjustments made to past enrollment periods which were paid using rates generated by a separate prior active duty call-up period may require multiple awards to change the 310 screen to generate the correct rates..

For example: Joe Student is enrolled in school from Fall, 2002 through Spring, 2003. At that time he was entitled to the 40% rate. In September 2005 Joe returned to school, but active duty service in the interim earned Joe the 60% rate. If an adjustment to the Fall, 2002 term becomes necessary, the 310 must be changed to mirror the way it looked in 2002 - 2003, and then changed back to restore the 2005 enrollments. All of this is done with individual awards to reflect the changes in the process. The first change, to terminate any enrollment would be done under an EP 380. All subsequent award actions would require EPs in the 930 series.

 6.08  Paying a 1606 Kicker Under Chapter 1607

NOTE: BDN will not correctly calculate the rate in all cases for servicepersons at any training time and veterans at less than 1/'2 time if the individual has a chapter 1606 kicker.

When calculating the monthly rate due a student based on training time or duty status, first determine the Chapter 1607 statutory rate due and then add in the proportional kicker based on training time. If training time is less than 1/2 time but 1/4 time or more, pay 1/4 of the Chapter 1606 kicker. If BDN is not paying the rate correctly, override the rate on the 312 screen with an "M" override.

In all three examples below the term is 1/6/06 through 5/12/06 (5/13 no pay date), 1/4 time training (training time 0), 1607 is payable at the 60% rate, and the claimant is entitled to a $350.00 1606 kicker. For a student in the 60% category the maximum monthly rate payable for less than half-time training (effective 10/1/05) is $310.20.

Example 1: Tuition and fees are 50.00. The calculated monthly rate is $11.81 and one quarter of the 1606 kicker is $87.50. Currently BDN would pay only the $11.81, however the total award due the claimant is $99.31 (11.81 + 87.50). Use the M override on this award to pay $99.31.

Example 2: Tuition and fees are $1500.00. The calculated monthly rate is $354.33 to fully reimburse tuition and fees; BDN included a portion of the kicker ($44.13) to reach the rate necessary to reimburse the student fully. In reality, the total award due the claimant is $397.70 ($310.20 + 87.50). Use the M override on this award to pay $397.70.

Example 3: Tuition and fees are $5000.00. The calculated monthly rate is $1181.10 to fully reimburse tuition and fees, BDN will cap the rate at $310.20 and include the full $87.50 kicker in order to pay the maximum amount payable to the student $310.20 + $87.50). There is no need to use the M override in this scenario, we are paying the maximum amount payable.

In cases in which the student is due a lump sum award, and BDN is not paying the full amount due, override the monthly rate displayed on the 312 screen using the "M" override. Unfortunately, the student will not receive a lump sum award in this instance, but will receive the 1606 kicker included in his payment each month.

SUBCHAPTER II.  317 Screen

6.10   Introduction

The 317 screen is used to enter certification (or verification) of attendance for NCD and IHL training under Chapter 1607, just as it is used in Chapters 30 and 1606. Currently Chapter 1607 claimants will not have to verify their enrollments, but if awarding a retroactive award the 317 screen must be used to issue payment.

 Go to Top

6.11  Accessing the 317 Screen

For input the 317 screen can be accessed through the CERT command or CADJ command. Under the CERT command, on the Ready Screen enter the file number, payee number (always 00 for 1607), the benefit (1607) and the claimant's stub name. Under the CADJ command, enter 317 in the Next Screen field on the 312 screen.

        For inquiry, the 317 screen can be accessed through the PINQ or SINQ commands.
