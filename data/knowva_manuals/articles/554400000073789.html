Sub-Chapter 5. Distance Learning, Independent Study, and Internet Training

Sub-Chapter 5. Distance Learning, Independent Study, and Internet Training

a. The GI Bill is available for distance learning, independent study, and Internet (or online) training. In these types of training, a student and an instructor are physically separated and interact through the use of communications technology instead of in a regular classroom or lab; or, in the case of independent study, meet infrequently. Communication technology can include any of the following:

  • Telephone or e-mail
  • Audio or video-conferencing
  • Online portals
  • Other electronic means, such as one-way and two-way transmissions through open broadcast, closed circuit, cable, microwave, broadband lines, fiber optics, satellite, or wireless communication devices

Any courses that consist of interaction using communications technology and/or some standard class sessions, but do not meet the requirements of in-residence training, are considered independent study or distance learning.

b. Benefit Payment

Distance learning, independent study, and Internet training are generally offered by Institutions of Higher Learning (such as colleges and universities), and similar rules and rates apply for tuition and fees, monthly housing allowance, books and supplies stipend, and Yellow Ribbon benefits.

c. Housing Allowance

The rules for housing benefits for veterans enrolled in independent training, distance learning, or Internet training are slightly different from the rules for veterans at IHLs.

Under the Post-9/11 GI Bill, the individual must be enrolled in at least one “in-residence” course to receive the full monthly housing allowance based on the Department of Defense’s (DoD’s) Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH) rate for the school’s ZIP Code.

As of October 1, 2011, individuals are eligible to receive a monthly housing allowance based on one-half of the national average of DoD’s BAH rates if enrolled solely in distance learning, independent training, or Internet training.

Note: As for the other training programs, monthly housing allowance eligibility requires a Rate of Pursuit (RoP) of more than .50, and benefits are pro-rated based on the RoP and the individual’s benefit level.
