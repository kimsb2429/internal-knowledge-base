Sub-Chapter 6. Correspondence Training

a. Correspondence training is different from distance learning based on how the training is completed. In correspondence training, students receive lessons and submit assignments by mail. Students have a set amount of time to complete and return the lessons to earn a grade, but the training is self-paced and lacks the formal course structure of distance learning or online courses. Here are a few examples of correspondence training programs:

  • Photographer
  • Locksmith
  • Gunsmith

b. Benefit Payment Details

Under Correspondence training VA will reimburse a student for the actual net costs for in-state tuition and fees or the annual maximum for the academic year for correspondence training, whichever is less (pro-rated based on the individual’s benefit level).

Payments are made each quarter after the VA receives notice from the school that lessons have been completed.
