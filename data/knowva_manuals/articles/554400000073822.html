7.01 Correct Enrollment

a. Select the Correction button to open the Correct Undergraduate College Degree pop-up window.

The Correct Undergraduate College Degree pop-up screen displays data from the original enrollment.

During correction, edited fields are updated in the original record as well as amendments.

Correction functionality should be used in the following circumstances:

  • To correct data input errors before authorization.
  • To correct input data under a new Work Product from Long Term Solutions (LTS) user error, or because of a correction submitted by the school (such as an amended VA Form 22-1999) after the initial award has been authorized.
  • To process an increase or reduction in enrollment hours that occurred on or before the first day of the term.
  • To process Mitigating Circumstances received after Authorization of the Amended Award.

 

Click the Save button to return to the Enrollment screen.

7.02 Add Amendment

a. Selecting Add Amendment allows you to make changes to a claim when the original award (or any other amendment) has a status of either In Progress or Authorized.

The Amendment functionality should be used for any change in hours reported on a VA Form 22-1999b (Notice of Change in Student Status) except for an increase or reduction in hours that occurred on or before the first day of the term.

The left side of the window contains data entered in the original enrollment that is in read-only format.  

b. The Effective Date field is required. It is the date reported on the VA Form 22-1999b.

The Reason drop-down box is a required field. The available options are:

  • Increase
  • Prereg Never Attended
  • Reduction (Non-College Degree (NCD))
  • Reduction NP
  • Reduction Pun
  • Reduction During Drop
  • Completed NP
  • End of Term or Course
  • Graduation
  • Unsat
  • Withdraw (WD) Before Term
  • WD After Drop NP
  • WD After Drop Pun
  • WD During Drop
  • WD or Interrupt (NCD)
  • VA-ONCE Termination
  • Other

The following fields are required. Enter data as reported on the VA Form 22-1999b for the effective date of the amendment:

  • Revised Res Hours
  • Revised Dist Hours
  • Revised Tuition
  • Revised Fees
  • Revised Yellow Ribbon 

c. Mitigating Circumstances, Restoration, and No Attendance

The far-right column contains the following options:

  • Student Did Not Attend Class. Check this box for a termination, which is effective on or before the first day of the term. No other boxes need to be checked for this scenario. Remember to ensure that all credit and dollar amount entries are zeroed out.
  • Restore Entitlement for Enrollment Period. Check this box if there is a reduction or termination and Restoration of Entitlement rules apply. This will automatically prohibit the application of the Six-Credit Hour Exclusion.
  • Mitigating Circumstances are Required. Check this box if there is a reduction or termination after the school’s drop/add period, non-punitive grades are assigned, and Restoration of Entitlement does not apply (there is an unprotected change in enrollment). This will automatically allow for the application of the Six-Credit Hour Exclusion if it is available.
  • Mitigating Circumstances are Provided. Check this box if the Mitigating Circumstances are Required box is checked and mitigating circumstances are received either with the change in enrollment or after development.
  • Mitigating Circumstances are Acceptable. Check this box if the Mitigating Circumstances are Required, and the Mitigating Circumstances are Provided box have both been checked. This will tell LTS that mitigating circumstance are acceptable and apply the exclusion to the term.
  • Do Not Apply 6X. This box will be checked if the Six-Credit Hour Exclusion is available but should not be applied. The selection is not available if there is a 6X Date entered on the Claimant Biography screen.

d. The Do Not Apply 6X box should be checked in the following scenarios:

  • An Enrollment Certification is received and a VA Form 22-1999b for an unprotected reduction/termination is submitted before authorization of the Original Award. In this case, both the Original Enrollment, and Amended Enrollment should be entered under the same Work Product. The boxes for Mitigating Circumstances are Required and Do Not Apply 6X should be checked.
  • A VA Form 22-1999b is received indicating completion of the term with nonpunitive grades, and mitigating circumstances are not reported. In this case, the boxes for Mitigating Circumstances are Required and Do Not Apply 6X should be checked.
  • A VA Form 22-1999b is received, indicating completion of the term with nonpunitive grades, and mitigating circumstances are reported. In this case, the boxes for Mitigating Circumstances are Required, Do Not Apply 6X, and Mitigating Circumstances are Provided should be checked. If applicable, the Mitigating Circumstances are Acceptable box should also be checked. 
  • Mitigating circumstances are received pursuant to development where benefits were not paid or the term was completed with nonpunitive grades. In these cases, the Amended Award check boxes should be modified under the Correction functionality. The Mitigating Circumstances are Provided box should be checked. If applicable, the Mitigating Circumstances are Acceptable box should also be checked. 

Once all the appropriate boxes have been checked, click the Save button to continue processing the claim, or click the Cancel button to return to the Enrollment Screen.

7.03 VA-ONCE Enrollment

a. School information submitted on an electronic VA Form 22-1999 will be displayed with Enrollment as the Type.

b. If the Begin Date, End Date, and Facility Code of the VA-ONCE submission do not match an existing enrollment in the Enrollment Periods section (this is a new enrollment), the Decline and Add buttons will become active.

c. Selecting the Decline button will prompt the user to confirm the decision. If you choose to continue the submission, it will be permanently removed from the VA-ONCE Inbox. Otherwise, choose Cancel to return to the Enrollment screen.

d. Selecting the Add button will launch the Add Enrollment pop-up. The data entry fields will be prepopulated with the VA-ONCE data, as well as information from Web Enabled Approval Management System (WEAMS), and may be modified if necessary. If you click the Accept button, the information will be displayed in the Enrollment Periods section. Clicking the Cancel button will return you to the Enrollment screen.

e. If the Begin Date, End Date, and Facility Code of the VA-ONCE submission match an existing enrollment in the Enrollment Periods section (such as a correction to the Tuition of Fee charges), the Decline and Correct buttons will become active.

f. Selecting the Correct button will launch the Correct Enrollment pop-up window. The data entry fields will be auto-filled with the VA-ONCE data and may be modified if necessary.

g. If you click the Accept button, the information will be displayed in the Enrollment Periods section. Clicking the Cancel button will return you to the Enrollment screen.

If the school is correcting one or both of the Term Dates (the Begin Date or End Date of the VA-ONCE submission match an existing enrollment in the Enrollment Periods section), you will need to manually associate the VA-ONCE submission by clicking on the appropriate Original Enrollment in the Enrollment Periods section.

7.04 VA-ONCE Amendment

a. Amendment

School information submitted on an electronic VA-Form 22-1999b will generally be displayed with Amendment as the Type.

If there is no Amendment with the same Effective Date in the Enrollment Periods section (because this is a new amendment), the Decline and Add buttons will become active.

b. The Decline button will prompt the user to confirm the decision. If you choose to continue the submission, it will be permanently removed from the VA-ONCE Inbox. Otherwise, choose Cancel to return to the Enrollment screen.

c. The Add button will launch the Add Amendment pop-up window. The data entry fields will be auto-filled with the VA-ONCE data, including Attendance and Mitigating Circumstances information in the Change Profile section. It may be modified if necessary.

d. If you click the Accept button, the information will be displayed in the Enrollment Periods section. Clicking the Cancel button will return you to the Enrollment screen.

If there is an Amendment with the same Effective Date in the Enrollment Periods section (because this is a corrected amendment), the Decline and Correct buttons will become active.

e. The Correct button will launch the Correct Amendment pop-up window. The data entry fields will be auto-filled with the VA-ONCE data, including Attendance and Mitigating Circumstances information in the Change Profile section, and may be modified if necessary.

f. If you click the Accept button, the information will be displayed in the Enrollment Periods section.

g. Clicking the Cancel button will return you to the Enrollment screen.

7.05 VA-ONCE Orphan Amendment

a. Orphan Amendment

School information submitted on an electronic VA Form 22-1999b that does not match the Facility Code, Begin Date, and End Date of an Original Enrollment in the Enrollment Periods section will be displayed with Orphan Amendment as the Type.

You will need to manually enter and/or associate the Orphan Amendment with an Original Enrollment to activate the Add or Correct buttons. The Decline button will already be active.

7.06 VA-ONCE First Day of Term

a. First Day Change

An increase in enrollment, a reduction in enrollment, or a correction to the Res. Hrs and Dist. Hrs fields effective on the first day of the term will be displayed with Amendment—First day of term as the Type.

If you select the Amendment—First day of term, this will activate the Decline and Correct buttons.

If you select the Correct button, it will launch the Correct Enrollment pop-up window. The data fields will be auto-filled with the VA-ONCE data and may be modified if necessary.

If you click the Accept button, the information will be displayed in the Enrollment Periods section.

If you click the Cancel button, you will be returned to the Enrollment screen.

7.07 VA-ONCE Association

a. Association

Certain types of VA-ONCE submissions will not be associated with enrollments or amendments in the Enrollment Periods section. You can associate a record in the Enrollment Periods section by clicking on the row. This will highlight that enrollment or amendment and activate the Correct button.

You should process all VA-ONCE INBOX records.
