Sub-Chapter 12. Letter Information - LTS Data Entry Rules

Sub-Chapter 12. Letter Information - LTS Data Entry Rules

Now that you have completed the Enrollment screen, you will navigate to the Letter Information screen. This screen has several purposes:

  • Prompt the user for additional information needed for letter generation but not contained within the Long Term Solutions (LTS).
  • Inform the user that no additional information is required for letter generation.
  • Inform the user that the correct Work Product cannot generate a letter.  

If the claimant is being awarded education benefits for an original award, no additional information is needed to generate a letter.

This section will cover the following types of letters that are generated by the LTS:

12.01 Certificate of Eligibility (CE) (similar to the Personal Computer Generated Letter (PCGL) Award 1 Letter)

12.02 Education Award (similar to the PCGL Award 3 Letter)

12.03 General Disallowance (similar to the PCGL Disallowance 17 Letter)

12.04 Adverse Action (similar to the PCGL Adverse Action Letter)

12.05 No Letter

12.06 Letter Upload Functionality

12.01 Certificate of Eligibility (COE)

a. COE Letter

If the letter being generated is a Certificate of Eligibility (COE), you must indicate if the course is approved or not approved by choosing Yes or No by radial dial under the Is Course Approved field.  

b. If you select YES:

  • If the course is approved, no additional information is necessary, and you click the Next button to continue the claim. 

c. If you select NO:

If the course is not approved, the radial dials under Is Training/Course/Program Being Denied will become activated, and you will need to select one. You have 7 options under this section of the screen:

  • School Isn’t Approved

  • Course Isn’t Approved

  • On the Job Training (OJT) Isn’t Approved

  • Apprenticeship Program Isn’t Approved

  • Course Not Institute of Higher Learning (IHL)/No Benefits Relinquished

  • School Not IHL/No Benefits Relinquished

  • None of the above

If the course is not approved, enter the following information in the corresponding fields on the 3rd header of the Letter Information screen:

  • Course and School Name

  • Course Dates

  • Facility State

d. Course Not Approved

If the course is not approved and you select the None of the Above option under the Is Training/Course/Program Being Denied field, you will need to make a selection from either the Other Training Options field or the Federal or Flight Training field under the second header row of the Letter Information screen.

Then, enter the following information in the corresponding fields on the 3rd header of the Letter Information screen:

  • Course and School Name

  • Course Dates

  • Facility State

e. Comments

The screen includes a Comments section. This field is not required. However, if you get an error message indicating you must fill in a comment, you should type in something, even if it’s just a blank space.

Click the Next button to continue the claim.

12.03 General Disallowance

a. General Disallowance

If the letter being generated is a General Disallowance and the claimant’s Entitlement Source is not Transfer of Entitlement (ToE), no additional information is required.

b. Disallowance for TOE

If the letter being generated is a General Disallowance and the claimant’s Entitlement Source is ToE, the user must select a disallowance reason.

Click the Next button to continue the claim.

12.04 Adverse Action Letter

a. Adverse Action

If the letter being generated is an Adverse Action letter, no additional information is required. The LTS will only generate an Adverse Action letter for a protected reduction or protected termination (i.e., not involving Mitigating Circumstances or the Six-Credit Hour Exclusion).

b. Reasons Why Letter Won't Generate

The LTS will not generate an Adverse Action letter for the following circumstances:

  • A reduction effective on the first day of the term is entered into the LTS using the Correction functionality, even if it creates one or more debts.
  • A change in eligibility entitlement or active duty status creates one or more debts.
  • An unprotected change in enrollment occurs (i.e., one involving Mitigating Circumstances or the Six-Credit Hour Exclusion).
  • An amendment is entered that does not create at least one student debt.

Click the Next button to continue the claim.

12.06 Letter Upload Functionality

DGI allows manual upload of edited letters during the authorization process. This functionality also allows suppression of system-generated letters.

NOTE: Letters should always be uploaded for processing by the print vendor rather than printed locally.

a.  Work Product Summary Screen. There are three radio buttons (options) in the letters section of the Work Product Summary screen.

  • SEND SYSTEM GENERATED LETTERS. Default setting. Letters are displayed as PDF documents, and they will release when the work product is authorized.
  • SUPPRESS LETTER(S). Letters are displayed as Word documents, and no letter will be released when the work product is authorized.
  • ALLOW MANUAL LETTERS UPLOAD. Letters are displayed as Word documents. After clicking the authorize button, individual letters can be suppressed by checking the DO NOT SEND checkbox. Clicking CHOOSE FILE will allow upload of an edited letter.

b.  Letter Preparation for Upload. The VCE will perform the following.

  • Select SUPPRESS LETTER(S) option to allow editing of the system-generated letter(s) in Word.
  • Review the letter content, date, and format.
  • Do not use letter templates which rely upon an image for the letter heading.
  • Do not make alterations to the seal or address of the letter. (System validations were added to verify the document type and location of the seal and address to prevent letter upload failure.)
  • Ensure the letter is a complete document, including VA seal and all necessary enclosures.
  • If single signature authorization is warranted, continue with the steps outlined below to release the letter.
  • If dual signature authorization is required, save the edited letter(s) to a designated location as a Word document, add the name of the saved letter in the TIMS CT message, provide specific letter suppression information in the COMMENTS section of the work product, and route for authorization.

c.  Letter Upload. The claim authorizer will perform the following:

  • Review the letter content, date, and format, then save the Word document as a PDF document.

NOTE: Closely review all aspects of the letter for accuracy prior to converting to PDF. Once a letter is converted to PDF, no additional editing is normally allowed (including the date). 

  • Select ALLOW MANUAL LETTERS UPLOAD option on Work Product Summary screen.
  • Select AUTHORIZE WITH AUTOMATIC PAYMENTS option to open the upload dialogue pop-up box.
  • Select CHOOSE FILE and upload the saved PDF letter(s).
  • Check DO NOT SEND checkbox to suppress letter(s) that should not be released to vendor.

  • Select AUTHORIZE to release the uploaded and unsuppressed letter(s) to the print vendor.
  • After final authorization, confirm the status of the uploaded and unsuppressed letters and select DONE to exit the work product.
