Chapter 3. Training Programs

Chapter 3. Training Programs

The Post-9/11 GI Bill offers benefits for service members, veterans, and their dependents who are attending education and training programs at accredited colleges or universities, and through non-college degree (NCD) programs at other institutions (after October 1, 2011).

Use this table for a description of the programs covered under the Post-9/11 Education Assistance benefit and their requirements.

TRAINING PROGRAM SUMMARY OF TRAINING PROGRAM
Index of Chapter 3 of the Chapter 33 Manual

1. Institutions of Higher Learning (IHL)

Information about undergraduate and graduate college/university programs or similar institutions, including technical or business schools, offering post secondary-level academic instruction that leads to an associate’s degree or higher including non-college degrees (NCDs) at IHLs.

2. Non-College Degree (NCD) Programs

Information about non-college degree (NCD) programs at non-college degree granting institutions, that lead to a career-specific diploma or professional certification.

3. On-the-Job and Apprenticeship Training

Information about benefits for learning a trade or skill through participation in an apprenticeship or on-the-job training program.

4. Flight Training

Information about benefits for vocational flight training programs at flight schools that are not part of a degree program.

5. Distance Learning, Independent Study, and Internet Training

Information about benefits for education programs taken outside of a regular classroom setting utilizing communications technologies, such as online classes.

6. Correspondence Training

Information about benefits for training through the mail.

7. National Testing Program

Information about reimbursement for the costs of taking national tests, such as SAT, LSAT, GRE, MCAT, and others.

8. Licensing and Certification

Information about reimbursement for taking a licensing or certification test for a profession.

9. Work-study Program

Information about the VA Work-Study Program, where a student can earn money by working at a VA approved facility while going to school.

10. Tutorial Assistance Program

Information about tutorial assistance, which helps a student pay for necessary tutoring costs while attending school using education benefits.

11. Active Duty Training

Information about using education benefits to pursue a degree while on active duty.

12. Approved Courses

Information about approved programs under the Post-9/11 GI Bill.
