SUBCHAPTER I.  BDN REAP LETTERS (See System Advisory dated 3/8/07)

7.01   GENERAL

The BDN system generates letters nightly from actions that have been authorized during that day. Letters are dated with the anticipated date of delivery. Copies of these letters are sent to the RPOs. Power of Attorney copies are sent to the Regional Office with jurisdiction over the claimant's address. Letters in the following paragraphs provide the paragraphs and the criteria for using for these paragraphs for the major letters generated by award processing.

SUBCHAPTER IV.  BDN CERTIFICATION PROCESSING

7.16   CERTIFICATION PROCESSING

See RPO Letter 22-08-05.   

a. General. The Chapter 1607 system is designed for “pay by cert” payment processing.  This is the same process as found in Chapters 30 and 1606.  In that design, the payee is required to verify his or her attendance monthly before payment is issued. 

However, the regulation to require monthly verification of enrollment in Chapter 1607 was not finalized at the time the system was installed.  Therefore, an interim process was developed.  This process requires no special operator action, but will generate verification of attendance transactions of which you should be aware. 

NCD monthly certification processing in the Chapter 1607 system is the same as that in the Chapter 30 system and will not be addressed in this chapter.

b. Interim Process for Payment in the Chapter 1607 System.

Because the regulation permitting monthly verification of enrollment for Chapter 1607 is not final, the new Chapter 1607 system cannot require Chapter 1607 claimants to verify their monthly attendance.

Monthly recurring payments are generated behind the scenes through the Full File Pass process. Certification transactions (35 ??) are automatically generated each month to issue these payments. These transactions will appear on the Transaction History M22 screen.

NOTE: For awards entered after the Full File Pass process has run, claims examiners should note that an “on-line” process will automatically certify claimants through the end of the current month for any award entered after the date of the Full File Pass for that month.

For example: A claims examiner enters an award for the period 01/15 to 05/05 on the 23rd of March. The on-line system will assume that by March 23rd the Full File Pass process has run in order to pay all claimants through March 31st. Therefore, the on-line process will certify this record through March 31st without intervention by the claims examiner. A MINQ immediately following CAUT should show a DLC/DLP of April 1st.

c. Using the 317 Screen.

Although payment will be generated in the interim process without completing the 317 screen for IHL cases, it is still recommended that the Chapter 30 procedure of entering the certification when an award is done be used in Chapter 1607. For example, if an award that begins in August is entered in October, it is appropriate to enter the certification through September 30 as part of the award transaction.
