Chapter 4. Monetary Benefits

The Post-9/11 GI Bill provides financial support for education and housing to service members, veterans, and eligible dependents attending education and training programs taken at colleges or universities, and through non-college degree (NCD) programs at other institutions.

Last updated: Tuesday, October 6, 2015

1. Overview A summary of financial support for education and housing for individuals eligible for Post-9/11 GI Bill benefits

2. Tuition and Fees Information about benefits for tuition and fees, including what is covered for the different types of training programs (such as licensing and certification tests, and national tests like the SAT, LSAT, GRE, MCAT, etc.) and also includes information about grandfathered tuition rates

3. Housing Allowance Information about the monthly housing allowance

4. Books and Supplies Stipend Information about entitlement for the books and supplies stipend

5. Additional Benefits  Information about additional monetary benefits, such as the Yellow Ribbon Program and kickers

6. Reimbursements, Fees, and Overpayments  Information about refund of Montgomery GI Bill (MGIB) contributions, rural relocation benefits, reporting fees paid to schools for certifying veteran enrollment, break pay, and overpayments
