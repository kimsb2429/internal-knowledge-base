8.01  ELECTRONIC APPROVAL RECORDS

a. Overview: All ETI approval packages and associated records must be captured within the Salesforce Approval Module (Procedural Change October 2018). Approval records must contain all required documents and correspondence related to approval activities to ensure compliance with Title 38 requirements.

1. Electronic Approval records will consist of Approval Actions and VA Actions

a. Approval Actions are any approval related changes based on a Notice of Approval letter. The different types of approval actions are defined in Section 2.18.

b.  VA Actions are actions that update an approval without changing the approval conditions or contain a notice of approval letter from the SAA or when VA is acting as the SAA.  Some examples are:

i.  VA Form 22-8794 Designation of Certifying Official(s)

ii. Electronic Funds Transfer & Tax ID Updates

iii.  SCO training certificates

iv.  Advanced payment participation requests 

v.  35% Reporting Exemptions 

vi. 85/15 Rule reports and actions 

vii. VA Form 22-0831 Centrally Certified Campus/Facility Designation

viii.VA Form 22-1919 Conflicting Interests Certification for Proprietary Schools

ix. DOD Waivers

2. Uploaded Documents Name Format: Effective 10/1/2024, personnel should only provide the document name. Salesforce will automatically insert a prefix with the following format “FYXX-Fac-Code” at the beginning of each document uploaded. Documents must be uploaded by type, individually or grouped in a logical sequence to allow users to easily navigate the record.

3.  Assigned staff must review the Salesforce “Overview” tab for the data entries listed in sections a. and b. listed below for accuracy based on the current Notice of Approval Letter or VA Actions defined in paragraph a.1. above, as applicable, before completing the approval activity. If errors are found, staff must correct the entries within their permission set and use the “Chatter” function to send notification of errors they cannot correct to their supervisor. Once the notification is sent, staff may complete the approval activity and are not required to follow up on the correction request.

a. Submitted Information

    1. Facility Name
    2. Accreditation
      1. ‘Yes’ for Accredited program
      2. ‘No’ for Non-Accredited, OJT, and APP
    3. Facility Type Registration (not applicable for Initial Approvals)
    4. Type of Change (not applicable for Initial Approvals)
    5. Type of Change – Other Reason (not applicable for Initial Approvals)
    6. Effective Date of Suspense or Withdrawal (not applicable for Initial Approvals)
    7. Date of Inspection Visit
    8. Action Request Date
    9. SAA Complete Package Date
    10. Address

b. Reviewer Section

    1. Facility Type
    2. Facility Type – Secondary
    3. Other Facility Type
    4. Special Classification
    5. Centralized Cert Main Branch

4. All approval activity identified in paragraph a.1.a Approval Actions must be recorded in Salesforce. If Approval Activity documents are received through email, the following steps are required:

a. VA Actions received through email: Create an approval in Salesforce, upload all attachments received, and upload a copy of the original email.

b. VA Action documents submitted to Salesforce by SAA that are not part of an Approval Action: Download all files, create a new approval, and upload the previously downloaded documents. The original SAA submitted Approval Action must be invalidated.

5. All Salesforce users must provide evidence for the “Action Request Date” and “SAA Complete Package Date” in Salesforce.

a. To effectively measure approval processing timeliness, evidence must be provided as follows:

i. Approval Actions: Users must upload two documents to validate the following Salesforce system entries:

a. Action Request Date (ARD) – A document that identifies what triggered the action.  An uploaded document is required to support the date entered, for example the initial ETI or VA email, or ETI application.

b. SAA Complete Package Date (CPD) - A document that identifies when all required information has been received from the ETI for the SAA to review and submit the approval action. An uploaded document is required to support the date entered.

c. The ARD and CPD may be uploaded separately or combined and should be clearly identified from the documents provided in the approval action.

ii. VA Actions: The Action Request Date is the date the item was received in the ELR corporate mailbox, AVA (Ask VA) or the date the item was uploaded through the SCO File Upload Portal. The SAA Complete Package Date does not apply to VA Actions and is an optional field to complete.

b. Records of Communications. All approval activity communications will be documented within the appropriate Salesforce approval record. The preferred method for sending communications to stakeholders is through the Salesforce direct email service. If communications are sent through other email services, the email must be uploaded to Salesforce the same day it was sent to maintain and share approval progress. Minimum records of communication required in the approval record include the following:

1) Inquiries to stakeholders (as required). All initial and subsequent communications/emails used to resolve approval questions or collect additional information/documentation to complete an approval activity.

2) Approval acceptance letters with attached WEAMS 1998 Report sent to stakeholders.

8.03  FACILITIES REGISTER AND CARD FILE

a.  Numerical File.  The ELR will maintain a facilities register for all approved facilities in the state by type of facility, in numerical order according to the four character name code.  The ELR at the primary RO in states with multiple ROs will assign all facility codes for the state.  The ELR at the secondary RO will maintain a register, in numerical order, of facility codes assigned to schools and job training establishments in the RO's jurisdiction.  The suggested method is to maintain loose leaf binders with the facility names and codes listed and separated by types of facility.  The seven different types of facilities are listed in paragraph 8.04b(3).  The ELR may use index cards instead of notebooks.  If the ELR automates the records, hard copies must be maintained.

b.  Alphabetical File.  The ELR will maintain a file in alphabetical order of approved schools and job training establishments in the state, showing the facility codes assigned.  The suggested method is to maintain an index card file.  If the ELR automates the records, hard copies must be maintained.

8.04  FACILITY CODES

a.  Assignment of Facility Codes.  The ELR assigns eight-character facility codes to identify approved facilities with administrative capability.  Once the ELR has properly assigned a facility code, the ELR must not assign the code to another facility, even though at some later time the facility ceases to operate or the approval is withdrawn.

b.  Parts of Facility Codes.  The eight-character facility code has 4 parts:  profit status, type training, name, and location.

(1)  The first part is the profit status of the facility.  The following table shows the acceptable entries and their definitions.

                                                                                                                                           

 

(2)  The second part identifies the type of facility providing training.  It may be a number from 1 through 5, 9, or 0 (zero).  The ELR should assign a 1, 3, or 4 to IHLs (Institutions of Higher Learning).  For accredited hospitals, use a 2 even if the hospitals have degree granting authority.  For colleges that primarily offer teaching programs, use a 3, even if the colleges offer some programs that are not intended to prepare students for teaching.  For other IHLs, use the number that reflects the highest approved degrees, i.e., use a 1 for colleges and universities offering 4 year degrees and graduate degrees and a 4 for community or junior colleges offering 2 year degrees.  Use 5 for NCDs (Noncollege Degree Schools) and 0 (zero) for OJT (On–Job Training) and apprenticeship programs.  Use 9 for chapter 31 only facilities.

 

NOTE:  It is not necessary to change facility codes for schools that have a 6 or 8 in the second part according to the previous instructions.  The ELR should not assign new facility codes with a second part of 6 or 8.

 

(3)  The third part (characters 3 through 6) of the facility code is the name of the facility.

 

(i)  The third part may contain four numbers or a combination of letters and numbers.  The ELR should assign the third part in numerical sequence.  Since the third part is used in conjunction with the first and second parts, the ELR must establish a separate numerical sequence for each category of facilities (e.g., 11–0001–00, 11–0002–00; 21–0001–00, 21–0002–00; 31–0001–00, 31–0002–00; 12–0001–00, 12–0002–00, etc.).

 

NOTE:  The last character in the third part must not be a letter.

 

(ii)  The ELR should assign a new series of codes beginning with A000, when the numerical series for a given type of facility reaches 8999.  When A999 is reached, begin the next series with B000.  When the series A000 through Z999 have been used, the ELR should assign the next series with 0A00 through 8Z99, etc.  Do not use 8 in the third position unless schools have off campus sites with separate administrative capability or 9 unless schools offer different levels of training.  See subparagraphs c, d, and e below.

 

(4)  The fourth part (characters 7 and 8) of the facility code are the two-digit numeric identifier for the state or foreign country where the facility is located. (See M22-4, part 2, par. 9.20 for list of approved state and foreign codes.)

(i) If a US owned ETI has an approved branch campus that is located in a foreign country, the facility code, characters 7 and 8 must match the US ETI state located characters 7 & 8. Additionally, the physical address must identify the correct foreign zip code "99999" to ensure proper MHA payment. (Section 107 Colmery Act)

c.  Institutions With Separate Administrative Capability – Off Campus Locations.  Facility codes with a third part starting with an 8 identify schools that have approved off campus site(s) with separate administrative capability.  Separate administrative capability is defined as:

(1)  Maintaining sufficient records and files for VA purposes, including those necessary to conduct a compliance survey.  (Off campus sites must maintain at least copies of registration records, admission records, transcripts, etc., but do not have to have separate registrars nor admissions officials located at the off campus sites.);

(2)  Designating a certifying official located at the sites who are able to furnish all reports and certifications to VA without resorting to the parent school;

(3)  Maintaining a mailing address local to the off campus site.

EXAMPLE:  A private-nonprofit IHL in Virginia with 2 off campus sites with administrative capability and 4 off campus sites without administrative capability should have 3 facility codes.  The ELR should assign an 8000 series facility code to the main campus and to the 2 off campus sites with administrative capability.  The ELR should not assign facility codes to the 4 off campus sites without separate administrative capability.

                                                                                          

 


NOTE:  Do not use the 8000 series for apprenticeship and OJT establishments.

d.  Colleges Within Universities With Separate Administrative Capability

(1)  Universities consisting of colleges with separate administrative capability may have separate 8000 series facility codes assigned to each college with separate administrative capability, even though the colleges may be on the main campus or considered to be a part of the main campus.  This is not mandatory.  The ELR may use the 8000 series if it is convenient to the RO in scheduling compliance surveys or for other administrative purposes.

(2)  The colleges must be capable of reporting changes in student status to VA without resorting to any other part of the university.  The ELR must not assign separate facility codes unless the college has its own:

(i)  Officials, i.e., certifying official, registrar, admissions official, financial aid official;

(ii)  Catalogs;

(iii)  Record system, i.e., financial records, admission records, transcripts, drop/add records, records of certifications to VA, and all other records necessary to conduct a compliance survey.

EXAMPLE 1:  A university has a medical school with a separate registrar, admissions official, catalog, certifying official, and record system.  The ELR may assign an 8000 series facility code to the university and a second 8000 series facility code to the medical school.

EXAMPLE 2:  A university has several colleges, e.g., Business, Arts and Sciences, Graduate school, etc., with one registrar, one admissions official, and one certifying official for all students in the university.  The ELR should not assign separate facility codes even though the colleges or schools may have separate catalogs, standards of progress, and admissions requirements.

e.  IHLs Offering Different Types of Training.  A third part starting with a 9 identifies IHLs offering different types of training.  The ELR should assign two facility codes to IHLs offering different types of training.  Use the predominant type of training at the college or university to determine the second part of the facility code.  Identify the different types of training in the fourth character of the third part.  See Table 8.01.  For IHLs with approved off campus site(s) with separate administrative capability and approval to offer more than one type of training, the ELR should assign facility codes in the 8000 series to show the different locations rather than one in the 9000 series.  It is not possible to identify IHLs having both approved off campus sites(s) and different levels of training.

(1)  The second and sixth characters will be 1, 2, 3, or 4 for degree programs, graduate level certificates, medical and dental residencies, and teacher certificate programs.  Use the number that identifies the predominant type of training.  See Table 8.01.

EXAMPLE:  If a university has 2 year and 4 year degree programs and medical residencies, use 1 in the second and sixth characters for degree programs, medical and dental residencies, graduate level certificate programs, and teacher certificate programs.  Use type A or B training with this facility code, except for nonaccredited IHLs.  Use type C training for nonaccredited IHLs.

(2)  The sixth character will be a 5 for all programs not identified above.  Use type C training for programs measured in clock hours or credit hours and all programs that are not accredited.

8.08 Annual Reporting Fees (ARF) Reports

Annual Reporting Fees reports are generated by the Office of Financial Management and the Hines Benefit Delivery Center during the second quarter of the fiscal year. Reports are sent to facilities and AC&L. The following information provides details on the reports, see Part 9, Chapter 9 and Part 10, Chapter 2  for general ARF information:

  1. Annual Reporting Fee Listing. Each facility receiving an ARF payment will also receive a pay list that specifies all students who were in receipt of benefits the previous calendar year, January through December. The pay list is arranged by student name (stub name), VA file number (last four digits of the student's social security number or VA file number), benefit (e.g. CH30, CH31, CH33, CH35, 1606), station (e.g. 51 for Muskogee, 07 for Buffalo), and rate (amount of ARF payment). The total number of students and a total dollar amount paid is provided at the end of the list. The pay list is mailed to the facility with a letter addressed to the School Certifying Official (SCO). The letter explains the Annual Reporting Fee, how to read the enclosed list, and how to report discrepancies in the list to the Education Liaison Representative (ELR) of Jurisdiction.

           

Figure 8.18. Annual Reporting Fee Listing

 

  1. Annual Reporting Fee Reports Provided to AC&L. To assist AC&L with pay list preliminary procedures to reduce errors, ARF inquiries, and SCO reported discrepancies, the following reports are provided:
  1. Preliminary Student Paid List. Includes: file number, payee number, stub name, station, benefit, state code, and rate.
  2. Preliminary Reject List. Includes: file number, payee number, state code, facility code, stub name, station, benefit, rate, and reason rejected. 
  3. Final ARF Paid List. Includes: facility code, state, school name, station, count of advance pay students, count of regular students, total count of students, and total paid.

8.12 VA-ONCE (VA Online Certification of Enrollment) Reports

AC&L users have the same access to reports as SCO users. The following information provides details on the reports. SeePart 10, Chapter 2 for additional information regarding use of VA-ONCE during compliance survey preliminary procedures.

  1. Weekly Certs Report. The Weekly Certs Report gives users the option to print certifications for an entire week without having to go to each individual student.
  2. Weekly Detail Certs Report. The Weekly Detail Certs Report allows users to view the details of all of the enrollments submitted in any given week. It includes more details about each certification than the Weekly Certs Report, to include the number of hours submitted and tuition and fees.

8.13 Two Certification Report

Effective August 1, 2021, Public Law (P.L.) 116-315, Section 1010 added enrollment verification requirements for schools and beneficiaries using the Post-9/11 GI Bill (chapter 33). Schools are required to submit a second verification of enrollment following the period during which the beneficiary is able to withdraw from courses without penalty. See Part 12, Chapter 11 for general information on enrollment verification requirements.

AC&L staff use the Two Certification Report to identify Institutions of Higher Learning (IHLs) and Non-College Degree (NCD) facilities that fail to submit a subsequent enrollment certification for each enrollment period. The report can be used to identify trends indicating a need for additional training and communications to schools. The Two Certification Report is generated and distributed to AC&L on the first of each month and/or on demand and is composed of the following two sections:

  1. Two Certification Summary Report. Includes:

    1. State of Facility’s Physical Address

    2. Facility Name

    3. Facility Code

    4. Number of Enrollment Periods Where Begin Date Falls with the specified date range

    5. Number of Enrollment periods that are missing a second certification

    6. Proportion of enrollments lacking a second certification

  1. Two Certification Detail Report. Includes:
    1. State of Facility’s Physical Address
    2. Facility Name
    3. Facility Code
    4. Claimant File Number
    5. Term ID for Claimant’s Enrollment Period (when number of students requiring second certification exceeds 0)
    6. Category
      1. No amendment
      2. Late amendment
      3. Late Enrollment

8.14 WEAMS (Web Enabled Approval Management System) VA Form 22-1998 Report

WEAMS is a web-based application used to store approval information. Approval information is primarily received from the State Approving Agency (SAA) and processed/entered in WEAMS by ELRs and VACO personnel. The approval information is used by Education Service (EDU) and Veterans’ Readiness and Employment (VR&E) personnel to determine claimant eligibility to benefits for training offered by organizations.

WEAMS users can generate the VA Form 22-1998 Report on demand. The VA Form 22-1998 Report includes the facility approval details specified in WEAMS. Generally, ELRs will provide an updated 22-1998 to facilities with a copy to the SAA when taking approval action. For additional information and procedures on dissemination of approval information, see Part 9, Chapter 2.
