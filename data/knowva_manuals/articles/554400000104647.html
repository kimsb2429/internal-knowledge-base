SUBCHAPTER I. GENERAL

13.01 INTRODUCTION

a. Purpose. This chapter provides Regional Processing Offices (RPOs) with guidance regarding Check and Electronic Fund Transfer (EFT) Tracer requests.

b. Background: Previously, each RPO was responsible for resolving education tracer requests. Education Service has consolidated all Check and EFT Tracer activities to the Muskogee RPO where they will maintain a SharePoint site will be used to track and monitor check tracers.

13.02 POINT OF CONTACT DESIGNATION

Each RPO is required to designate one employee from the local Regional Office’s (RO) Support Services Division (SSD) and at minimum one employee from the Education Services Division. The RPO will determine the appropriate person(s) and number of POCs based on their available staffing resources. Once designated, the RPO POCs will be given access to the SharePoint site which will be used to track and monitor check tracers maintained by the Muskogee RPO.

 

13.03 WHAT TO DO WHEN A TRACER REQUEST IS RECEIVED

When a claimant or School Certifying Official (SCO) reports a missing payment either issued by EFT or by check through the Education Call Center (ECC), written correspondence, and/or Right Now Web (RNW), a report of contact VA Form 0820d (See Figure 13.01) will be prepared and an encrypted e-mail sent to the designated RPO POC. The RPO POC will upload the tracer request to the Muskogee RPO SharePoint to manage the request. In addition, the individual who received the initial request will capture a copy of the report of contact in the claimant’s TIMS folder.

Actions taken when Request for Tracer Received. When a missing payment is initially reported, the RPO:

1. Validate appropriate timeframe has elapsed.  The appropriate timeframe is 10 business days from date of issue of paper check or 5 business days from date of issue of EFT. In order to confirm the issue date, a review of SHARE is required. Capture the SHARE screen showing the issue date to the TIMS folder.

2. Ensure the payment has not been returned in SHARE. If the payment is shown in SHARE as having been returned, follow RPO processing guidelines for reissuing payment.

3. If it is a paper check that was reported missing, validate the claimant’s mailing address. If the payment was issued by direct deposit, verify that all fields of the EFT are correct. If any of the information is incorrect, make the necessary corrections.

4. If the report indicates the payment was lost (lost means a paper check was received and subsequently lost) or stolen, send an email to the RPO POC immediately. The encrypted email should include the following information:

  1. RPO of Jurisdiction
  2. File or Claim Number
  3. Payee #
  4. Last Name, First Name, Middle Name or Initial
  5. Claimant’s Address
  6. Claimant’s Telephone Number
  7. Date of Contact
  8. Name of Person contacting VA (if different than claimant)
  9. VA Employee Contacted
  10. Date and Amount of missing payment or payments
  11. Payment issued by: [BDN]
  12. Payment Method: Paper Check or Direct Deposit
  13. Type of Payment: Regular, Retro, Irregular?
  14. Was check stolen and/or endorsed?
  15. Was check lost and/or endorsed?
  16. Was address or banking information changed at this date?
  17. Comments:

 

Actions to be taken by RPO POC. The RPO POC must review any check tracer email messages daily.

Once a request has been received, the POC should:

1. On the Muskogee SharePoint site select, “Support Services” found at the top middle of the page.

2. On the left-hand side of the page select, “Check Tracer Request”.

3. Open and complete the Check Tracer Form.

4. A list of previously entered check tracer requests will appear. At the bottom of this list select, “Add New Item”. This will open the Check Tracer Form.

5. Complete the Check Tracer Form and submit.

6. Verify the request was submitted, click the Issue ID of the file and capture the form to the TIMS folder.

Status for Tracer Requests. If the claimant or SCO contacts the VA again, ensure 10 business days have passed since the request was uploaded to SharePoint and check TIMS for updated status. Muskogee SSD will update TIMS once per week on each open tracer request.

Once the tracer request is completed with the Treasury Department, Muskogee SSD will place a note in the claimant’s TIMS file.  

Note: If status has not been updated, the POC should email the VAVBAMUS/RO/SSD box requesting a “tracer status update”. Be sure to include the claimant’s name and SharePoint ticket number in the encrypted email message.
