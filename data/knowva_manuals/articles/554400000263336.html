II.2.B.1.a

General

Prior to the transition from the Benefits Delivery Network (BDN) to the Enterprise Management of Payment Workload and Reporting (eMPWR) that occurred between June 28, 2024, and July 8, 2024, all Chapter 33 payment and receivable actions were transmitted through BDN to the Department of Treasury for payments and/or the Debt Management Center (DMC) for debt management.  With the conversion to eMPWR, the activities once performed by BDN have been integrated with eMPWR or rendered obsolete.

 

 

 

 

 

 

II.2.B.1.b

BDN Master Record

Before the transition to eMPWR, a Chapter 33 master record was established in BDN in order to facilitate payments and debts for Chapter 33.  This master record was established using either the Claims Establishment (CEST) command or the Claims Adjudication (CADJ) command.  The master record contained the basic eligibility information for Chapter 33 beneficiaries, including the date last paid (DLP), if eligible, for the beneficiary, and the relevant payment address to which payments were sent, whether it was direct deposit or a physical address for paper checks.  The DLP indicated the day after the actual last day of the last payable period.

From the master record, processors can review the Chapter 33 transaction and payment history for the beneficiary for actions taken before June 28, 2024.

For claims processed authorized after July 8, 2024, no CEST or CADJ actions are needed or possible. 

With the conversion to eMPWR, Veterans Claims Examiners (VCEs) will still be able to review existing Chapter 33 master records in BDN using the master record Inquiry (MINQ) command.  Review of the BDN master record may still be required to perform audit and review actions of claims that were authorized on or before June 28, 2024.

Reference: See M22-4, Part XII, 9.01 - 9.07 for details on the Chapter 33 Master Record screen (M21) and the transaction and payment history screens (M22, M23, M24, and M01).

 

 

 

 

 

 

II.2.B.1.c

DGI to BDN Actions

The integration of BDN and DGI resulted in several actions which potentially impacted the file in BDN. 

The most basic of these actions was the authorize functionality located on the DGI Work Product Summary (WPS) screen which allowed VCEs to submit a work product and potentially take the additional actions discussed in the following section. 

Other actions included:

  • refreshing the Chapter 33 master record on the WPS screen,
  • retrying failed payments on the Payment Instructions (PI) screen, and
  • manually entering failed payments on the PI screen.

In the case of the failed payments actions, no additional work products could be created for the beneficiary until the failed payments were resolved.

Payments sent via DGI to BDN were scheduled and submitted every night after close of business as part of routine behind the scenes processing.

II.2.B.1.d

DGI-BDN Validations

The Chapter 33 master record in BDN was integrated with the beneficiary’s file in DGI which allowed numerous validations to occur between the systems upon the attempted authorization of a work product in DGI. 

If the validation checks were successful, then the authorization was allowed to complete. 

If one of the validation checks failed, then a red error banner was returned on the DGI WPS screen, and the authorization did not process.  In order to authorize the work product, the VCE needed to resolve the error and authorize again.  Each of the following validations have been discontinued, being no longer relevant once DGI integrates with eMPWR: 

  • a suspended Chapter 33 master record,
  • a mismatched stub name between the master record and DGI,
  • a mismatched DLP between the master record and DGI,
  • a master record not found in BDN,
  • a mismatched housing payment between BDN and DGI,
  • proceeds existing on the master record,
  • an only debts master record in BDN,
  • an invalid master record status type, and,
  • a duplicate, invalid, or masked Social Security Number (SSN) or File Number.

With the conversion to eMPWR, no new master records are needed for Chapter 33 claims.  Existing master records cannot be updated.

·Topic 14: Work Product Summary Screen for information on the WPS screen of M22-4, Part II, 2.D.14

II.2.B.1.e

STEM and VET TEC actions

While the Edith Nourse Rogers Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) Scholarship and the Veteran Employment Through Technology Education Courses (VET TEC) programs have never involved BTs being submitted via BDN, processing actions for both depended on the existence of a master record in BDN to be successful in a similar manner to the integrations described for Chapter 33.

With the conversion to eMPWR, the dependency on master records in BDN has been removed.  No new master records are created for STEM or VET TEC beneficiaries.

References: See (H) 2020 04 August: Procedural Advisory: STEM Scholarship Processing in LTS – Buffalo STEM Team for details on STEM processing.  See VET TEC Claims Processing Guides for details on VET TEC Payment Instructions.

 

 

 

 

II.2.B.1.f

Controlling Work in BDN

BDN was an integral part of controlling and managing Chapter 33 processing work.  Pending End Products (EPs) were used in managing and tracking pending work.  Cleared EPs were used to track completed work and to calculate individual and RPO productivity.  Diaries (EPs with future dates) were used to track and manage future processing work.

For the Chapter 33 claims the DGI system attempted to automate, BDN created EPs for beneficiaries whose work products offramped and did not successfully automate.

With the conversion to eMPWR, most of this functionality was turned off in BDN to prevent erroneous release of payments.

For information on interim and future work credit systems, see M22-4, Part II, 6.

References: See M22-3, Chapter 5. QUANTITATIVE MEASUREMENT for additional details on EPs.
