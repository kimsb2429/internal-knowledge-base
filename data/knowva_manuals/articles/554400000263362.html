Topic 1: Handling Authorization Errors in eMPWR

 

 

 

Introduction

This section covers how to handle payment errors that occur after June 26, 2024.

 

 

Change Date

June 24, 2025

 

 

II.6.A.1

General

Various technical errors may occur during routine processing.  This section discusses known possible errors and provides instructions for resolving them.

a.  Pre-Authorization/Authorization Failure

If DGI rules cannot generate the fields required for successful transmission of payments to the Enterprise Management of Payment Workload and Reporting (eMPWR) or invalid values are passed, the impacted work product will be unable to be authorized until the issue is rectified.

The Veteran Claims Examiner (VCE) will be notified of an error by a red authorization error banner as described in M22-4, Part II, 2.D.14.b.  Note: This list may not cover every possible error message.

• Payee code is not available for payment. Please try again or contact your coach if error persists.
• Veteran information unavailable/cannot be retrieved from VADIR. Please contact your coach.
• Veteran information cannot be established in DGI. Please contact your coach.
• Participant id is not available for payment. Try again or contact your coach.
• MPI Sync found multiple claimants match. Contact your coach.
• VAProfile id is not available for payment. Try again or contact your coach.
• Exception while adding Benefit Claim to BGS. Try again or contact your coach.
• CorpDB Sync Failure occurred.
• MPI response does not match with search criteria or low confidence score on claimant.

  Figure 7.1: Authorization error message example

For each of the above error messages, the VCE should take the following steps.

(1)   Select on the Work Product Summary tab on the left side of the DGI screen to refresh the record.

(2)   Attempt to authorize the work product a second time.

(3)   If the error indicates a “BGS” or an address, phone, or email issue, take the following steps.

(a)   Review TIMS and DGI to identify the most recent mailing address.

(b)   Check the format of the most recent address against the United States Postal Service (USPS) address validator.  Enter the address into the validator and click “Find.” 

(c)   Correct the address in DGI to the exact address provided by USPS.

(4)   If the error indicates that a Fry claim cannot be created, take the following steps.

(a)   Review the TIMS and Share files to locate the SSN of the Fry veteran.

(b)   Enter the Fry veteran SSN on the BIO screen.

(5)   If this fails, consult with a Senior VCE or Lead Senior VCE for review and an additional attempt to authorize the work product on their own.  Follow local procedures as appropriate.

(6)   If the work product continues to fail, follow local procedures to request review by the local data remediation point of contact (POC) who will follow their procedures to fix the problem or submit a Defect in Functionality (DIF) report for database correction, as appropriate.

(7)   Follow local procedures to suspend the token in The Image Management System (TIMS) until data remediation and/or DIF resolution.

(8)   Upon communication that the remediation has been completed, the VCE will continue normal processing.

b.  Data Remediation

Due to data inconsistencies across VA systems, some first post-eMPWR work products may require data remediation in order to proceed with establishing an Education claim in the Corporate Database (CorpDB).  Each Regional Processing Office (RPO) will establish local procedures for VCEs to request POC intervention to resolve any data mismatches.

Data remediation POCs need access to the following systems:

• Master Person Index (MPI) Toolkit with access to the “Search Experian” button
• SHARE application with access to the “Corporate Inquires,”, “Corporate Update,” and “BIRLS Update” (BUPD) commands
• eFolder application with SSA Inquiry access to view documents
• TIMS application with Folder Processing access to view Education Service (EDU) documents

(1)   For errors that involve a missing ID or an MPI issue, data remediation POCs will complete the following:

(a)   Compare identity information in the MPI Toolkit with the information in Share, TIMS, and eFolder to determine the correct Participant ID (PID).

(b)   Upon identifying the correct PID, the data remediation POC follows local procedures to submit a Service Now (SNOW) ticket to the VBA EDU Identity Tiger Team for correction.

(c)   For any systems that need data corrected, take the POC will take the necessary corrective actions:

i.   If there is an incorrect file number/SSN in eFolder, submit a SNOW ticket to the VBA CORP OBI Data Quality team along with any supporting documentation to correct VBMS.  This ticket must be resolved before moving onto any additional corrections.

ii.   If the information in BIRLS is found to be incorrect, use the BUPD button in Share to update that information, as appropriate.  Before making any BIRLS updates, run a “Search All in List” action and compare the name and date of birth to ensure the BIRLS and CorpDB records match.

iii.   If the information in CorpDB is found to be incorrect, use the Corporate Update button in Share to update that information, as appropriate.

iv.   If the information in MPI is found to be incorrect, submit a Request in the MPI Toolkit.

v.   If the information in DGI is found to be incorrect, update the BIO screen as appropriate.  If the SSN in DGI needs to be updated, submit a SNOW ticket for DGI updates using established SSN correction procedures.

vi.   If the information of the TIMS folder is found to be incorrect, follow established procedures to have corrections made to the TIMS folder.

(d)   Once all SNOW tickets have been resolved and all corrections have been made, the remediation POC will return the token to the VCE to complete normal processing.

(2)   For errors that involve a missing date of birth (DOB), data remediation POCs will complete the following:

(a)   Review the DGI file to locate the beneficiary’s DOB.

(b)   Perform a BUPD in Share to update the DOB.

(c)   If the DOB in DGI was adjusted as part of the existing Fry work around, re-associate the file to VADIR and return the token to the VCE with instructions to enter the appropriate graduate date on the Enrollments screen and complete normal processing.

See (F) 2024 28 June: Procedural Advisory: Chapter 33 eMPWR Conversion Identity Mismatch Procedures for additional information. 

c.   Business Transaction (BT) Order Failures

When a work product includes both payments and receivables, then upon authorization, overpayments are transmitted first to (eMPWR, followed by payments, to allow eMPWR’s Participant Systematic Review (PSR) to properly review and code items for collection, affording due process rights when needed.

If any of the receivable or associated payment BTs fail or are rescheduled, then the entire set of BTs will fail.  The VCE will know this occurred through an error showing on the Payment Instructions (PI) screen of DGI.

 

  Figure 7.2: BT Failure example

Example: If a work product generates a housing debt AND a currently payable housing payment for a beneficiary, and either the housing debt or the housing payment fail at the time of authorization, THEN both the debt and payment will fail.

Note: This failure will not automatically cause the failures of other payment or debt types, such as books and supplies, tuition and fees, or Yellow Ribbon.

To resolve this issue, the VCE should take the following steps.

(1)   Suspend the token in TIMS until the next business day.

(2)   After the suspension time has elapsed, review the authorized work product in DGI to see if the error is still displaying.  This will give the system the opportunity to run its automatic retries.

(3)   If the error persists, contact your Coach to follow local procedures to submit a DIF report for database correction.

(4)   Follow local procedures to suspend the token in TIMS until DIF resolution.

(5)   Upon receiving notification of resolution, continue to work the claim follow standard procedures.

Note: Until the issue is resolved, no new work products can be created for the beneficiary.

Figure 7.3: Unable to create work product error example

d.   Payment Error Resolution Attempts (Automated)

When a processor (whether VCE or automation) attempts to authorize a claim within DGI and there is a failure to successfully transmit to eMPWR, the system will do the following.

(1)   The unsuccessful BTs will show with a status of Rescheduled on the PI screen.

 Figure 7.4: BT Rescheduled example

 

(2)   The system will automatically attempt to resubmit the transaction up to five (5) times.

(a).   If one of retries is successful,

                            i.  Then there is no further action needed, and

                           ii.  The BT will display with a status of Successful on the PI screen.

                          iii.  The failed payment attempt would not be reported to the RPO of jurisdiction.

(b)   If, after five (5) attempts, the BT is still not unsuccessful,

                            i.  Then the BT will display with a status of Failed on the PI screen

                           ii.  The errored BT will display on for the Topic 4: Payment Action Report along with a reason for review and corrective action.

(3)   Once a BT displays on the Payment Action Report, the Production Operations (ProdOps) team from Accenture Federal Services (AFS) will work directly with the local Support Services Division (SSD) to have payments manually issued through eMPWR and to make any corrections necessary to continue normal processing.

Note: VCEs will not usually see this activity, since it happens in the background.

(4)   If a VCE comes across a beneficiary with failed payments that have not been resolved, take the following steps:

(a)   Suspend the token in TIMS until the next business day.

(b)   After the suspension time has elapsed, review the authorized work product in DGI to see if the error is still displaying.

(c)   If the error persists, contact your Coach to follow local procedures to coordinate with SSD to manually issue the BTs and to submit a DIF report to AFS ProdOps for database correction.

(d)   Follow local procedures to suspend the token in TIMS until DIF resolution.

(e)   Upon receiving notification of resolution, continue to work the claim follow standard procedures.

 

e.  Work Product Creation Failure

If there is an error or a rescheduled BT which impedes the creation of a new work product, then paused payments are rolled back to their previous state to prevent payments from being unnecessarily delayed.

 

f.   Retry Failed Payments Button

Before the integration with eMPWR, DGI displayed a Retry Failed Payments button.  As of July 8, 2024, this button no longer is an option.  To retry failed payment, VCEs must identify the relevant scenario and follow the procedures indicated.

 

g.   Manually Enter Payments Button

Before the integration with eMPWR, DGI displayed a Retry Failed Payments button.  As of July 8, 2024, this button no longer is an option.

Education service will no longer have the ability to issue these payments on its own due to the Office of Financial Management’s (OFM) need to maintain fiscal separation of duties.

h.   Payment Error (DGI unavailable)

 If DGI goes offline and fails to receive a response from eMPWR regarding the status of a BT, then,

(1)   DGI will resend the BT to eMPWR,

(2)   eMPWR will cross-reference the previously received BT and re-send the response, and

(3)   eMPWR will NOT reprocess the duplicate BT.

 No manual action is necessary.

 

i.   eMPWR System Outage

If eMPWR is down for an outage (planned or otherwise), the monitoring team will notify the RPOs of the outage.  No BTs will be sent to eMPWR during the outage.

Introduction

Change Date

II.6.A.4

General

Once a business transaction (BT) displays on the Payment Action Report, the Production Operations (ProdOps) team from Accenture Federal Services (AFS) will work directly with the local Support Services Division (SSD) to have payments manually issued through eMPWR and to make any corrections necessary to continue normal processing. For awareness purposes, the Payment Action Report will be created daily for transmission to VA Central Office (VACO) and RPO staff for review.  No active local review process is necessary.

If a Veterans Claims Examiner (VCE) finds a beneficiary with failed BTs during normal processing, see Topic 1: Handling Authorization Errors in eMPWR for procedures.

 

The report includes all BTs which failed due to system/record issues. This report includes: 

•   Masked SSN
•   Claimant ID (same/future term for Product Key)
•   Work Product Key
•   Date Authorized (if so)
•   # of Errors
•   Reason of Error
•   Total Errors
•   Total Claims

Topic 5: Debt Transfers

 

Introduction

This section contains information on debt transfers between different benefit chapters.

Change Date

July 31, 2025

II.6.A.5

Debt Transfers

Before the conversion to eMPWR, receivable balances associated with a Chapter 30, 1606, or 1607 master record could be transferred to a Chapter 33 master record for collection via processing of a BT 04E 90C, 96C, and 97C deduction in BDN. Prior to Release 8, 61C and 63C deductions were required to offset receivables established for benefit chapters other than the currently active chapter. As of eMPWR Release 19, a C-type deduction is no longer required to offset receivables established for benefit chapters other than the currently active chapter.

In eMPWR, all chapter 33 participant payables with the exception of BT 06I Housing/Kick are considered ‘Retro.’ All non-chapter 33 payables with the exception of BT 35, 35CO, 35FCO, and 35SRT are considered retro.

Chapter 33 participant BT 06I Housing/Kicker awards can be considered either ‘Retro’ or ‘Recurring’. For chapter 33 purposes, retro is defined as any 06I where the payable period is for a prior month. Recurring is defined as any 06I where the payable is for the current month. DGI processes distinct 06Is that separate award periods so that no 06I contains an award period that is both Retro and Recurring. If a payable 06I is received by eMPWR in the last two business days of the month, regardless of the payable effective date, the award will be considered retroactive and will be processed on the first working day of the following month.

Non-chapter 33 BTs 35, 35CO, 35FCO, and 35SRT can be considered either 'Retro' or 'Recurring'. For non-chapter 33 purposes, retro is defined as any of the previously mentioned BTs, where the payable period is earlier than the immediately preceding month. Recurring is defined as any of the previously mentioned BTs, where the payable period is for the immediately preceding month or current month. Benefits Manager processes distinct BTs that separate award periods so that no BT contains an award period that is both Retro and Recurring. If a payable BT is received by eMPWR in the last two business days of the month, and the award is for the immediately preceding month, the award will be considered retroactive and will be processed on the first working day of the following month.

eMPWR employs a Participant Systematic Review (PSR) to automatically determine if there are existing proceeds, receivables, or offsets which can be applied to a payable resulting from a BT. Listed below are general rules which the PSR follows:

1. Recurring payments are protected from offset against collectible receivables, even if the receivable has congruent dates

2. Retro payments will be offset by collectible receivables

3. Retro payments will be offset by receivables that are in a "Due Process" collection status when the dates of the receivable are congruent with the dates of the award

4. When a receivable has an active Benefit Repayment plan attached to it, the receivable is protected from any retro payment offset, so long as the receivable and award do not have congruent dates

5. When a receivable has an active Benefit Repayment plan attached to it, only Recurring payment will have deductions made

6. PSR is driven by the RECIP_ID of the receivable and award, so when an award and receivable have different file numbers (i.e. chapter 35 award and chapter 33 Self receivable), but the RECIP_ID is the same, offset between programs will occur

Topic 6: Requesting Manual Payment Processing

Introduction

This section discusses submitting a request for manual payment processing to the Support Services Division (SSD).

Change Date

June 24, 2025

II.6.A.6.a

Manual Payments

Business Transaction (BT). 

If the Veterans Claims Examiner (VCE) finds that business transactions (BTs) need out of system action,

(1)   Complete the attached Fiscal Action PDF and follow local procedures to send form to SSD Finance for manual transactions to be completed. 

(a)   Include screen captures of Payment Instructions screen, audit, or other evidence, as appropriate. 

(b)   If the BT is from a TOE or Fry Scholarship claim, include the file number of the source veteran in the “TOE Claim” or “FRY Scholarship Claim” field, as appropriate.

(2)   If the manual payments are required for specialized processing, such as incarcerated claimant claims or retroactive inductions, the work product must be authorized before being sent to SSD.

(3)   If the payment is to resolve a failed payment from a previous work product, SSD must release the payment and EDU will follow local procedures to submit a Defect in Functionality (DIF) ticket to AFS ProdOps to adjust the payment status in the database.  No new work product can be created until both actions have been completed.

Work Study

If Chapter 33 eligibility was established in DGI before June 26, 2024, and no subsequent work product was authorized, a new DGI work product needs to be created and authorized before requests for manual payment can be sent to SSD.

II.6.A.6.b

Manual Payments Without a Corporate Record

If no eMPWR conversion work product exists AND no work product authorized on or after July 8, 2024, exists, then no manual financial action can be taken by either SSD or the Debt Management Center (DMC) on the beneficiary’s Chapter 33 record.  This situation most commonly can occur in the processing of Work Study claims and DMC actions to collect a debt.

The Regional Processing Offices (RPOs) need to assign Points of Contact (POCs) for SSD and DMC to contact when no Education record exists in CorpDB and must be created to process a fiscal action.  Upon receiving that contact, the POC needs to create and authorized a work product in DGI to create the corporate record.  Once the work product is authorized, the POC will respond to SSD or DMC as appropriate to allow them to continue their work.
