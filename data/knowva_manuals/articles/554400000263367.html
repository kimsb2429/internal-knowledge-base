Topic 1: RPO Processor Production Reporting

 

 

Introduction

This section discusses the measurement of production levels for Chapter 33, Edith Nourse Rogers Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) Scholarship, and the Veteran Employment Through Technology Education Courses (VET TEC) processing after the switch to the Enterprise Management of Payment Workload and Reporting (eMPWR).

Note: This functionality is temporary.  The initial release of Workload Manager (WM), scheduled for Spring 2025 will replace all functionality discussed below.

 

Change Date

June 24, 2025

 

II.6.D.1

General

The switch from the Benefits Delivery Network (BDN) to eMPWR will not have a direct impact on how individual and office productivity measures are gathered for Chapter 33, STEM, and VET TEC processing.  Processors will continue to clear end products (EPs) in BDN and track other activities on their Distribution of Operational Resources (DOOR) report as has been done in the past.

There are, however, some differences from past procedures that will be noticed.

a.    No pending EPs can be created either automatically or manually.

b.    All Chapter 33 clearing actions (PCLRs) will follow the steps as if clearing an EP 330 or EP 400 for individual credit.

c.    No EPs will have the name of the beneficiary, address, or other information captured in a pending EP (i.e., a pending EP 340 or EP 350).

d.    Capturing requirements for EPs remain unchanged.

Topic 2: Education Service Workload Reporting

 

 

Introduction

This section discusses the processes used to control pending work after the conversion from BDN to eMPWR.

 

Change Date

June 24, 2025

 

II.6.D.2.a

General

BDN EPs are no longer used to track pending work and to calculate timeliness considerations.  Rather, work products in DGI will be used to track Chapter 33 inventory.  This functionality already exists to track automated Chapter 33 claims which have not created EPs since March 2023.

 

II.6.D.2.b

Tracking Adjusted Suspense Date

With pending EPs no longer existing for Chapter 33, Education Service is no longer able to adjust the suspense date of a pending EP to identify claims that may age more than usual due to activities such as development.

 As such, the following steps must be taken by Veteran Claims Examiners (VCEs) to control these claims.

(1)   Take standard development procedures.

(2)   Create or edit a pending work product in DGI for the claim with a description of “SD - <30 days from action> - ” See M22-4, Part II, 2.D.6.

Example: “SD – 01/31/24 – DOD”

(3)   Suspend the token in The Image Management System (TIMS) with a facility code of “SD MM/DD.”

Example: “SD 01/31”

(4)   Follow standard procedures for Failure to Prosecute (FTP) or when the requested information is received.

Topic 3: Maintaining Diaries for Future Work

 

 

Introduction

This section discusses the processes used to create and manage diaries after the BDN to eMPWR transition.

 

Change Date

June 24, 2025

 

II.6.D.3.a

General

BDN is no longer able to support diaries for Chapter 33, STEM, and VET TEC to track future work for those benefit types.  Rather, a SharePoint list will be used to track diaries before the initial release of WM is able to handle diary functionality.

 

II.6.D.3.b

Creating a Diary

When a diary is needed, VCEs access SharePoint and enter the following information:

(1)   SSN

(2)   Benefit Type

•  Chapter 33
•  STEM
•  VET 

(3)   Maturation Date

(4)   Disposition Reason

•  Chapter 30 refund due
•  Term starting more than 180 days in the future.
•  Future RAD
•  Incomplete grade
•  Pending delimiting date extension decision
•  Pending equitable relief decision
•  Reconsideration supplemental claim (RSC) 

(5)   Completed checkbox – leave blank at the time of diary creation

 

II.6.D.3.c

Matured Diaries

When a diary matures, a system-generated email is be sent to the creating VCE (with the VCE’s Coach/Lead SVCE copied) directing them to work the diary.  Upon completion of work, VCE will select a link back to the SharePoint diary to select the completed box.

 

II.6.D.3.d

Diary Reports

Depending on permissions, Education users can see the following:

•  My Pending Diaries (for the logged in user)
•  All Pending Diaries
•  Overdue Diaries (Diaries past the Maturity Date that are not completed)
•  All Items
