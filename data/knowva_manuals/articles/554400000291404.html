Topic 1: Overview

Introduction

 This topic provides a general overview of the DGIB Managed Service. 

Change Date

April 11, 2025

II.1.A.1

General

The DGIB Managed Service makes it easier to integrate applications, manage them, and increase efficiencies. Previously, each application had a unique purpose and provided a specific service. Most applications were unable to work together and share data. During the development of the Managed Service, many existing legacy applications were replaced and updated with new applications to create the more integrated DGIB Managed Service platform.

Topic 2: Claimant data information

Introduction

 This topic discusses the various EDU and VBA systems that manage identity, contact, and VBA benefits data. 

Change Date

April 11, 2025

II.1.A.2.a

My Education Benefits (MEB)

 The MyEducationBenefits (MEB) application allows Veterans and other beneficiaries to apply for and manage their education benefits.  MEB uses information from other systems integrated with the DGIB Managed Service to present the applicant with the information EDU has on file for them and allows the applicant to make updates as necessary.

II.1.A.2.b

Claimant database

 The Claimant database stores information on a beneficiary’s education history.  After a claimant record has been created in either BM or the Chapter 33 Processing System (DGI), searching the Claimant database will retrieve the beneficiary’s EDU information.

II.1.A.2.c

Master Person Index

 The Master Person Index (MPI) is the Veterans Benefits Administration’s (VBA) record of a beneficiary’s biographical information including their social security number (SSN), name, date of birth (DOB), and date of death (DOD).  Updates to biographical information from other divisions of VBA will update the information in BM and DGI, if a corresponding record exists.

II.1.A.2.d

VA Profile

 The VA Profile is the VA’s record of a beneficiary’s contact information including mailing address, telephone number(s), and email address.  Updates to biographical information from other divisions will update the information in BM and DGI, if a corresponding record exists.

II.1.A.2.e

Corporate Database

 The Corporate Database (CorpDB) is the VBA’s record of a beneficiary’s payment address information including direct deposit and Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT) data.  Updates to payment address information from other divisions will update the information in BM and DGI, if a corresponding record exists.

II.1.A.2.f

VADIR

The VA/DoD Identity Repository (VADIR) stores a beneficiary’s military and education information.  As the primary source for this information on a beneficiary’s file, it often contains all the service-related information required to adjudicate a claim. 

If a user has reason to believe the information in VADIR is incomplete or inaccurate, additional information must be obtained through the development process to adjudicate the claim.

II.1.A.2.g

Topic 3: Payments and the DGIB Managed Service

Introduction

 This topic discusses the how payments and debts are issued within the DGI Managed Service. 

Change Date

April 11, 2025

II.1.A.3.a

BT generation

 BM and DGI are programmed with rules that are used to determine and populate the necessary information to create both payments and receivables sent to the enterprise Management of Payment Workload and Reporting (eMPWR).  These rules consider a wide variety of information including eligibility determinations, and enrollments, amendments, or manual awards.  Only some of these calculated values are displayed in the claim.  The full calculated details for a given business transaction (BT) can be viewed in the eMPWR system.

II.1.A.3.b

eMPWR

The eMPWR system is a payment processing system operated by the Office of Financial Management (OFM).  BTs authorized within either BM or DGI are transmitted to eMPWR via a payment microservice that ensures all required data is sent to eMPWR to generate accurate and successful payments.

“Business transaction” is the general term for any payment-related actions sent from the DGI Managed Service to eMPWR, including any payments or receivables (also known as debts) created during processing.  The payment microservice holds all BTs until they reach their maturation date.  BTs are not sent to eMPWR until maturation is reached.

  • Starting on July 8, 2024, all chapter 33 BTs are transmitted to eMPWR.
  • Starting on December 7, 2024, all non-chapter 33 BTs processed in BM are transmitted to eMPWR.
  • Payments from claims processed in the Benefits Delivery Network (BDN) are still transmitted through BDN.  (All BDN claims processing is scheduled to end in summer 2025 with release 8).
  • STEM and VET TEC BTs are still manually entered into eMPWR using existing processes.  (STEM and VET TEC claims will be integrated with eMPWR at a future date).

Negative BTs (receivables) will generally be transmitted to eMPWR immediately upon authorization.  Positive BTs (payments) will generally be transmitted to eMPWR immediately upon enrollment verification.  Any transactions submitted after 5:59:59 PM CT will be processed by eMPWR on the following business day.  Matured BTs are visible in eMPWR nearly immediately upon authorization, though payment details take one business day.

II.1.A.3.c

Debts generated in the Managed Service

If a receivable exists or a new receivable is generated upon the authorization of a claim, BM or DGI will send a notification of all scheduled housing payments to the Debt Management Center (DMC). The notification(s) will provide the appropriate data needed for DMC collections.

Some payments can be used to offset existing debts during the debt collection process.  Automatic debt collection abides by the following rules.

  1. “Recurring” beneficiary payables are protected from offset.
  2. “Retro” basic payables can be directly offset against basic receivables under specific circumstances.

No VCE action is needed regarding either issue.

II.1.A.3.d

BDN to eMPWR conversion

As part of the transition to the DGIB Managed Service, BDN is being incrementally retired. 

  • On July 8, 2024, all scheduled chapter 33 BTs were converted from BDN to eMPWR.
  • With release 8, scheduled for summer 2025, all beneficiaries previously established in BDN who are receiving chapter 30, chapter 1606, or chapter 35 benefits will have their BTs converted from BDN to eMPWR.
