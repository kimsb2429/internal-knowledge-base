Topic 1: Claim-0

Introduction

This topic provides information about the Claim-0 functionality in BM.

Change Date

June 27, 2025

II.3.I.1.a

General

The Claim-0 functionality will enable continued payment of running Education awards adjudicated prior to Release 8 in BDN, without the need for additional field input.

A Claim-0 will be created through an automated process for beneficiaries with awards adjudicated in BDN, who at the time of Release 8, have not yet been paid in full for their BDN awards.

II.3.I.1.b

Criteria for conversion

Beneficiaries must meet the following criteria as of the Release 8 go-live date to be eligible for a Claim-0:

  1. Receiving benefits under chapter 1606, 30, or 35 in BDN
  2. Enrolled in a training program which is one of the following:
    1. Undergraduate;
    2. Graduate or advanced professional;
    3. Non-college degree; or
    4. High school
  3. BDN master record status is Active
  4. BDN master record suspense reason is null
  5. Has a Social Security Number on their BDN master record
  6. Not receiving concurrent benefits

Beneficiaries who do not meet the listed criteria will not have a Claim-0 created, and will instead require a Claim-R. See Topic 2 of this section for more information on Claim-R.

II.3.I.1.c

Claim overview

Claim-0 will be created by an automated process during the initial Release 8 deployment. An authenticated user is not able to create a Claim-0 manually.

Authorization will be attempted by automation in all instances of a Claim-0. There are instances where authorization may be unsuccessful, resulting in an off-ramped Claim-0; see Claim-0 exception processing for more information Claim-0 exceptions.

Figure: Example of fully authorized claim-0 in BM claim history tab

Within the Claim-0, minimal information regarding the beneficiary’s eligibility and entitlement will be present. The following sections of the Claim summary will be populated:

  1. Relationship information (chapter 35 only)
  2. Net awards
  3. Verified through date (the day prior to the BDN date last paid)

The user is able to interact with the Net awards table in the same manner as a normal claim, with each award being expandable to show additional award information.

II.3.I.1.d

Awards generated by Claim-0

Net awards will be generated for each BDN rate line on or after the BDN date last paid. The following is an example of an adjudicated award in BDN, and the corresponding net awards generated by a Claim-0:

Figure: Example of adjudicated award in BDN

Figure: Example of net awards generated by claim-0

In this example, net awards have been generated for each rate line on or after the BDN date last paid (June 1, 2025). A distinct net award is generated for each rate line in BDN and is further segmented if the rate line crosses the end of a month. The value in the "Amount" column represents the total amount payable for each segment.

II.3.I.1.e

Letters generated by Claim-0

Letters will not be generated in a Claim-0. The beneficiary has already been notified of the details of the awards being converted in the Claim-0 by virtue of their BDN award letter.

Manual letter upload functionality is unavailable for off-ramped Claim-0 claims. No letter is required in this instance.

II.3.I.1.f

Enrollment verification requirements

The requirement for monthly verification of enrollment for awards generated by a Claim-0 will be the same as the requirement in BDN for the benefit and award type, with one exception.

  • Chapter 1606 and 30 beneficiaries will be required to submit monthly verifications in order to receive payment for awards generated by a Claim-0.
  • Chapter 35 beneficiaries enrolled in undergraduate or graduate training at an IHL will not be required to submit monthly verifications in order to receive payment for awards generated in a Claim-0. Awards will mature automatically at the end of each award period, unless superseded by a Claim-R.
  • Exception: Chapter 35 beneficiaries enrolled in non-college degree training will not be required to submit monthly verifications in order to receive payment for awards generated in a Claim-0. This requirement has been temporarily waived as part of the Claim-0 process. Awards will mature automatically at the end of each award period, unless superseded by a Claim-R.

II.3.I.1.g

Claim-0 exception processing

Authorization of a Claim-0 may be unsuccessful and result in the claim being off-ramped for review. Reasons for unsuccessful authorization may include, but are not limited to:

  • Temporary lapses in availability of integrated systems, such as eMPWR, MPI, VADIR, VA Profile, etc.;
  • Exceptions during the claimant sync process with the Corporate Database, MPI, and/or VA Profile

If a Claim-0 authorization is unsuccessful, a user should open the in-progress Claim-0 and attempt authorization. If authorization continues to be unsuccessful, refer the case to the Technical Response Unit (TRU) for further triage. If the Claim-0 is unable to be authorized after triage by TRU, the Claim-0 should be deleted and a Claim-R initiated. See Topic 2 of this section for more information on Claim-R.

Topic 2: Claim-R

Introduction

This topic provides information about the Claim-R functionality in BM.

Change Date

June 27, 2025

II.3.I.2.a

General

The Claim-R functionality will establish values baseline eligibility and entitlement for beneficiaries previously paid in BDN, to enable processing of claims in Benefits Manager point forward.

A Claim-R is conceptually similar to a reconciliation Work Product in the chapter 33 Processing System for a beneficiary who was paid in the Front-End Tool (FET) prior to deployment of the Long-Term Solution (LTS, now known as DGI).

No awards generated in a Claim-R will be sent to the payment service or released to eMPWR.

To create a Claim-R, select the appropriate Reconciliation claim type for the benefit from the “Benefit” dropdown in the Create a claim modal.

II.3.I.2.b

Claim-R requirement

A Claim-R will be created manually by a user prior to adjudication of a claim and will be required on a per-benefit basis. If a BDN master record exists for a specific benefit type, Benefits Manager will enforce creation and subsequent authorization of a Claim-R for that benefit type, prior to creation and adjudication of a supplemental claim for that benefit type.

II.3.I.2.c

Claim overview

Claim-R will be created manually by a user on an ad-hoc basis. Instances which may cause a user to create a Claim-R include but are not limited to:

  • Receipt of a supplemental claim
  • Receipt of a new enrollment
  • Receipt of an amendment to a previously processed enrollment
  • Unsuccessful authorization of Claim-0

A Claim-R can be initiated on the Claim history tab by selecting the “Create a claim” button, then choosing the appropriate Claim-R type from the “Benefit” dropdown. Initial options will include:

  1. Chapter 1606 (MGIB-SR) Reconciliation
  2. Chapter 30 (MGIB-AD) Reconciliation
  3. Chapter 35 (DEA) Reconciliation

Upon successful authorization of a Claim-R for a benefit, additional options will appear in the “Benefit” dropdown.
 

Figure: Example of fully authorized claim-R on the claim history tab

Within the Claim-R, the claim workflow will be largely identical to the workflow of an original or supplemental claim for the benefit. Exceptions will be noted in subsequent sections of this topic.

II.3.I.2.d

Eligibility section

The Claim-R uses the same Eligibility workflow as a normal claim. For more information on the Eligibility section of a claim, see M22-4, Part II, 3.D.

In general, eligibility data will need to be reviewed (if automatically synced from VADIR) or input in the same manner as an original claim.

A deviation from the routine original processing flow will be the requirement to adjust the VA eligibility start date. By default, the system will set the VA eligibility start date to one year prior to the date of the Claim-R. The TIMS folder and the BDN Historical Report must be reviewed to determine the appropriate start date, based on the earliest application for benefits or first award date, and adjusted appropriately in the “Start dates” section.

An additional deviation from routine processing flow will be the requirement to input delimiting date extensions as appropriate. If the delimiting date generated by the system is not correct based on eligibility inputs, the TIMS folder and BDN Historical Report should be reviewed to determine the correct delimiting date. The “Delimiting date” section should be used to input the appropriate extension(s).

II.3.I.2.e

Entitlement and kickers section

The Claim-R uses the same Entitlement and kickers workflow as a normal claim. For more information on the Entitlement and kickers section of a claim, see M22-4, Part II, 3.E.

In general, entitlement and kicker data will need to be reviewed or input in the same manner as an original claim.

A deviation from routine processing flow will be the presence of a system-generated entitlement charge in the “Manual entitlement adjustments” section. The system rules will attempt to reconcile the used entitlement amount in BDN at time of conversion with the enrollment submitted for the beneficiary in Enrollment Manager.

If the used entitlement value from BDN exceeds the amount calculated by the system rules, a system-generated charge will be present. The entitlement amount of the charge will be equal to the difference between the used entitlement value from BDN and the used entitlement amount calculated by the system rules. This may be caused by enrollments and/or adjustments not submitted in VA-Once or Enrollment Manager, entitlement charges not related to enrollments, etc.

If the used entitlement amount calculated by the system rules exceeds the BDN value, a system-generated adjustment will not be present. Instead, a warning message will appear on the Claim summary page. This may be caused by enrollments and/or amendments submitted in VA-Once or Enrollment Manager which were not processed in BDN, etc.

The converted entitlement values from BDN should be accepted as correct unless there is evidence to the contrary. Adjustments to system-generated entitlement adjustment may be necessary to bring the entitlement usage in line with BDN; see Enrollment reconciliation for more information on these adjustments.

II.3.I.2.f

Enrollments section

The Claim-R uses an Enrollments workflow which has reduced functionality in comparison to a normal claim. For more information on the normal functionality of the Enrollments section of a claim, see M22-4, Part II, 3.F.

The Enrollments workflow in a Claim-R will only allow for deletion of enrollments and/or amendments submitted in VA-Once and Enrollment Manager.


Deletion of submitted enrollments may be necessary if the enrollment was not processed in BDN and causes calculated entitlement to be overstated, or if the submitted enrollment contains erroneous data which leads to processing errors, such as invalid objective or data which causes an amendment to be considered orphaned.

To delete an enrollment, the user will click the trashcan icon present on the row associated with the enrollment.

II.3.I.2.g

Claim summary section

The Claim-R uses the same Claim summary workflow as a normal claim. For more information on the Claim summary section of a claim, see M22-4, Part II, 3.G.

In general, the Claim summary will display the same information as an original or supplemental claim.

A deviation from routine processing flow will be the presence of a Payment action status of “No Payment” on all Net awards generated in the Claim-R. As the Claim-R is a baseline, no payments are sent to eMPWR. If awards exist for training beyond the Verified thru date, a subsequent supplemental claim must be created and authorized after successful Claim-R authorization.

An additional deviation from routine processing flow will be the lack of a system-generated letter. Because the purpose of the Claim-R is to establish a baseline for processing in Benefits Manager, there is no beneficiary notification required.

A user may submit the Claim-R for authorization by using the “Submit for authorization” button, or may authorize the claim by using the “Authorize” button. Signature requirements for Claim-R's will be dictated by RPO management.

II.3.I.2.h

Letters generated by Claim-R

Letters will not be generated in a Claim-R. There is no requirement for beneficiary notification when a Claim-R is authorized, as there should be no net change to eligibility or entitlement.

II.3.I.2.i

Entitlement reconciliation

As discussed in Entitlement and kickers section, the Claim-R will receive the BDN entitlement value as inputs and attempt to reconcile these values by calculating entitlement usage according to system rules, using enrollments submitted through VA-Once and/or Enrollment Manager. In some cases, a system-generated entitlement charge will exist in the “Manual entitlement adjustments” section of the Entitlement and kickers page.

If a user needs to charge entitlement used beyond the value displayed in the Claim-R, the user will select the “Adjust manual entitlement” button on the Entitlement and kickers page, then charge an entitlement amount equal to the system-generated value, plus the additional amount being charged.

Example: The BDN used entitlement amount is 10-21 and a system-generated charge of 07-05 exists. The user wants the used entitlement to be 11-00, which is 9 days greater than the converted amount. The user should enter a manual entitlement charge of 07-14 (07-05 plus 00-09 additional entitlement).

If a user needs to restore entitlement, the process is identical to restoration of entitlement in an original or supplemental claim flow. For more information on restoring entitlement, see M22-4, Part II, 3.D.2.b.

Topic 3: Manual Transaction Reconciliation

Introduction

This topic provides information about the Manual Transaction Reconciliation functionality in BM.

Change Date

June 27, 2025

II.3.I.3.a

General

The Manual Transaction Reconciliation functionality will allow for a re-baseline of eligibility and entitlement for beneficiaries while suppressing awards generated for retroactive changes.

A Manual Transaction Reconciliation is conceptually similar to authorizing a Work Product with no payment in the chapter 33 Processing System.

To create a Manual Transaction Reconciliation claim, select the appropriate Manual Transaction Reconciliation claim type for the benefit from the “Benefit” dropdown in the Create a claim modal.

II.3.I.3.b

Claim workflow

The Manual Transaction Reconciliation follows the claim workflow detailed in M22-4, Part II,  3.C. Functionality remains identical, with two exceptions:

  1. No awards generated for adjustments prior the Verified thru date in a Manual Transaction Reconciliation will be sent to the payment service or released to eMPWR. Awards which supersede previously scheduled awards or new awards for periods after the Verified thru date will be scheduled and released by Payment Service upon maturation or verification of enrollment, whichever is applicable.
  2. Two signatures are required for all Manual Transaction Reconciliations.

II.3.I.3.c

Letters generated by a Manual Transaction Reconciliation claim

Letters will not be generated in a Manual Transaction Reconciliation. Beneficiary notification may be necessary if there are material changes to eligibility, entitlement, or changes to awards during the Manual Transaction Reconciliation. If notification is necessary, the user must compose an appropriately informative letter outside of the system. Letters may be uploaded as detailed in M22-4, Part II, 3.G.1.c.
