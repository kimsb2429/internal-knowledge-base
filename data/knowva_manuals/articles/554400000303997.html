48 MONTH RULE (ENTITLEMENT LIMITATION)

Most individuals eligible for benefits under two or more VA education programs may not exceed 48 months of entitlement.

6-CREDIT HOUR EXCLUSION

VA automatically grants MITC mitigating circumstances for up to 6 credits the first time a student reduces or terminates, and mitigating circumstances must be considered. This automatic grant is called the 6-Credit Hour Exclusion. The exclusion is a one-time grant made the first-time mitigating circumstances must be considered for the student. Up to 6 credits can be excluded if the student has been awarded benefits for the credit. The 6-Credit Hour Exclusion cannot be granted if the student completes the term and receives non-punitive grades.

70/80 PERCENT RULE (PGIB)

If the aggregate service including training is at least 24 months (730-909 days) but less than 30 months (910-1094 days) AND the aggregate service excluding training is at least 18 months (545-729 days) but less than 24 months, the individual will be deemed eligible at the 70% benefit level under PGIB.

81 MONTH RULE

Effective October 1, 2013, Public Law 112-154 “Honoring America’s Veterans and Caring for Camp Lejeune Families Act of 2012”, authorized VA to award up to 81 months of entitlement to individuals who use the Survivors’ and Dependent’s Educational Assistance (DEA) program (chapter 35) combined with entitlement from the other VA education programs.

85/15 PERCENT RULE

VA will not approve a new enrollment or a re-enrollment in any program for an otherwise eligible student under this rule. VA will stop the enrollment-approval process when more than 85% of the students enrolled in an approved program have all or part of their tuition, fees, or other charges paid for by the educational institution or VA.

90/10 RULE

Department of Education (ED) implemented changes to the 90/10 rule included in the American Recovery Plan Act which was signed into law in 2021. These changes now require for-profit institutions of higher education participating in federal financial student aid (FSA) to include funds provided for the purpose of paying tuition from all federal educational assistance programs, including VA educational assistance, into their calculation of amounts received from federal funding sources.

Under ED rules effective January 1, 2023, covered schools must include the amount of VA educational assistance funds paid directly to the school (tuition payments under PGIB), as well as, monthly stipends paid directly to students under MGIB, MGIB-SR, DEA, and VR&E. Covered schools do not need to include PGIB monthly housing allowances (MHA) paid directly to students because those funds are not for the purpose of paying tuition.

ACADEMIC YEAR

Period of time beginning August 1st of each calendar year and ending July 31st of the subsequent calendar year under 38 U.S.C. 3034(a), 3323(a), and 3680(a)).

ACCELERATED PAYMENT

Offered under MGIB for high tech courses leading to a job in a high-tech industry as defined by the National Science Foundation (NSF).

Lump sum payment of a maximum of 60 percent of the established tuition and fees for a student’s enrollment for a term, quarter, or semester in an approved program of education leading to employment in a high technology industry.  In the case of a program of education not offered on a term, quarter, or semester basis, the accelerated payment is a lump sum payment of a maximum of 60 percent of the established tuition and fees for the entire program. (See 38 C.F.R. 21.7020(b)(51)).

ACCREDITING AGENCY

Nationally recognized agency or association which the Secretary of Education determines as a reliable authority on the quality of training offered by an educational institution. The Secretary publishes a list of the agencies and associations State Approving Agencies (SAA) use for establishing what courses may be approved under the provisions of 38 U.S.C. 3675.

ACCRUED BENEFITS

Benefits payable to next of kin or other eligible beneficiaries of a deceased Veteran/service member; payments represent amounts due but unpaid at time of death.

ACTIVE DUTY (ACDU)

Full-time duty in the Armed Forces of the United States; under a call or order to active duty under specific statues under title 10 and title 32; and full-time duty performed by commissioned officers of the Public Health Service (PHS) and National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA). (See 38 U.S.C. 101(21).) 

ACTIVE DUTY FOR TRAINING (ACDUTRA)

Full-time duty in the Armed Forces performed by Reserves for training purposes and, in the case of members of the Army National Guard or Air National Guard, full-time duty under specific sections of title 32. (See 38 U.S.C. 101(22).)

ACTIVE GUARD RESERVE (AGR)

For Reserve, active duty for purposes other than training under title 10, U.S. Code.

  • Naval Reserve equivalent is TAR (The Active Reserves)
  • Marine Corps Reserve equivalent is FTS (Full-Time Service)
  • Coast Guard Reserve does not have AGR service.

For National Guard, active duty for the purpose of administering, instructing, organizing, recruiting, or training members under 32 U.S.C. 502(f).

ADMINISTRATIVE ERROR

Error solely created by a VA employee when processing VA benefits that results in an erroneous payment or award. Occur when VA pays benefits to a beneficiary in an amount due to actions on the part of VA usually, but not always, due to a misapplication of the law. Administrative errors include errors in judgment on the part of VA employees.

ADVANCE PAYMENT

Monetary allowance which may be payable to a student for the first fractional (or full) month and the following full month of enrollment. These payments are issued to schools before the beginning of classes for release to students who have completed registration.

(See 38 U.S.C. 3034(a), 3323(a), 3680(d).)

ADVERSE ACTION

Any action that reduces, denies, or terminates benefits or services.

AFFIRMATION OF ENROLLMENT (VA FORM 22-1999c)

Signed by the student affirming an enrollment contract for a correspondence course. The date of affirmation must be 10 days after the student has signed the enrollment contract. Used only for those pursuing correspondence courses.

AGGREGATE SERVICE

Sum of all individual qualifying service periods after September 10, 2001.

AMOUNT ACTUALLY PAID

Amount paid by check or EFT after any offsets or deductions. This amount is not used in any calculations for awards or authorizations.

APPLICATION

Used synonymously with a computer program or software used in benefit processing. Both Microsoft Word and Benefits Manager, as examples, are applications.

APPRENTICESHIP

On-the-job training (OJT) which has been established as an apprentice course by a training establishment and has been approved as an apprentice course by the SAA. This includes courses under the supervision of a college or university, any state department of education, state apprenticeship agency, state board of vocational education, joint apprenticeship committee, or Bureau of Apprenticeship and Training.

Apprenticeship training differs from OJT in that it combines OJT with classroom instruction to prepare workers for a job. Students often receive a Certificate of Apprenticeship after completing an apprenticeship. Examples of apprenticeship training include machinist, land surveyor, or baker.

ARMED FORCES

United States Army, Navy, Marine Corps, Air Force, Space Force, and Coast Guard, including their respective Selected Reserve components. (See 38 C.F.R. 3.1.)

AskVA (AVA)

Secure electronic system allows customers to submit questions, concerns, recommendations, and compliments. Messages can be tracked from submission to resolution, and past messages and responses are viewable.

ASSIGNMENT OF BENEFITS

Any arrangement by a school which denies the student the opportunity to control the proceeds of a check. Payments of benefits due under the laws administered by VA shall not be assigned except as specifically authorized by law. (See 38 U.S.C. 5301(a).)

There are no laws that authorize assignment of VA educational checks or direct deposit/EFT (Electronic Fund Transfer).

ATTENDANCE

Presence of a student

  • in the class where the approved course is being taught in which they are enrolled.
  • at a training establishment; or
  • any other place of instruction, training or study designated by the educational institution or training establishment where the student is enrolled and is pursuing a program of education.

(See 38 C.F.R. 21.4200(q).)

AUDITED COURSE

Any credit course that a student attends as a listener only with a prior understanding between school officials and the student that such attendance will not result in credit being granted toward graduation.

AUTHORIZED PAYMENT

Amount authorized for payment prior to the application of any offsets for previously established debts or other deductions.

AUTOMATION

Use of technology to perform tasks where human input is minimized.

AWARD AMOUNT

Dollar amount of benefit calculated and awarded to a specific student for a claim based on their entitlement, eligibility, enrollment and other information, typically broken down by benefit type.

BASIC EDUCATIONAL ASSISTANCE Monetary benefit payable to beneficiaries who meet the basic requirements under MGIB for pursuit of a program of education. (See 38 C.F.R. 21.7020(b)(4)).

BASIC ELIGIBILITY

Eligibility for educational assistance established under PGIB based on active duty service after September 10, 2001.

BENEFICIARY

Individual who has filed a claim for educational assistance from VA. Also referred to as claimant, applicant, student, or customer.

BENEFICIARY IDENTIFICATION AND RECORDS LOCATOR SUBSYSTEM (BIRLS)

Computerized record system containing basic identifying information for VA beneficiaries, including current location of their folder and service information.

BENEFITS DELIVERY NETWORK (BDN)

Formerly referred to as the Target system, BDN is the main computer system for all claims processing activities, including development, award, disallowance and related actions. BDN generates the payment information that is sent to the Department of the Treasury for producing, changing, or holding benefit checks. BDN also contains the "master records" of award, disability, and payment information for beneficiaries. (See BDN COMMANDS AND ACRONYMS for more information.)

BDN Shell is a program that facilitates BDN access by reducing repetitive typing and providing easy access to BDN commands and screens.

BOARD OF VETERANS APPEALS (BVA)

Delegated the authority to render final decisions on appeals of VA benefit decisions for the Secretary and is directly responsible to the Secretary. The Board is bound in its decisions by VA regulations, instructions of the Secretary, and precedent opinions of the VA chief legal officer.

Each decision includes a written statement of the findings and conclusions, reasons for the findings and conclusions, and order granting appropriate relief or denying relief. (See 38 U.S.C. 71.)

BRANCH CAMPUS

A location of an educational institution that

  • is geographically apart from and operationally independent of the main campus of the educational institution,
  • has its own faculty, administration, and supervisory organization; and
  • offers courses in education programs leading to a degree, certificate, or other recognized education credential.

BUSINESS TRANSACTION (BT)

All transactions processed through DGI pertaining to the creation, resolution, or removal of payables or receivables for beneficiaries and/or vendors.

BUY-UP

Service members who are eligible for benefits under MGIB Category I could contribute an additional $600 and receive as much as an additional $150 per month in benefits. Payment must be made while on active duty.

CENTRALIZED COMMITTEE SYSTEM 

System in which authority to hear all cases pertaining to a particular type of debt is centralized at one location or set of locations rather than housing a committee at each RO.  This system is used to increase the efficiency of the COWC process and bring uniformity to decisions across VBA.  VBA has centralized the COWC function at four (Buffalo, Milwaukee, St. Paul and Manila) ROs for certain types of debts.

CENTRALIZED MAIL

The Centralized Mail (CM) Program transitioned VBA mail operations from the former decentralized mail processing to a centralized receipt and virtual analysis concept by using the United States Postal Service (USPS), contractor-operated scanning, and automated work routing processes to add VA beneficiary correspondence received via mail directly to the TIMS folder. CM is a central component in the VBA claims transformation effort to address incoming paper and electronic mail, as well as outgoing paper and electronic mail.

CERTIFICATE OF ELIGIBILITY (COE)

Letter issued to a beneficiary showing, at a minimum, the approval to pursue a program of education at an approved training institution, the remaining entitlement, and ending date of eligibility. PGIB letters will also include benefit level and service information.

CERTIFICATE OF RELEASE OR DISCHARGE FROM ACTIVE DUTY (DD FORM 214)

Prepared at the time a Service member completes a period of active duty in the Armed Forces, or at least 90 consecutive days of active duty training. Used to verify military service for benefits, retirement, employment, and membership in veteran organizations.

NOTE:  DD Form 215 is a corrected or amended version of the DD Form 214.

Former members of PHS and NOAA do not receive a DD Form 214 but they will receive a comparable document which verifies their service information.

CERTIFICATION OF DELIVERY (COD)

VA Form 22-1999V containing preprinted information furnished to schools in a separate envelope when an advance payment check is issued for one of their students. Must be completed by the certifying official and immediately returned to VA when the advance payment check is given to the student. Failure to do so will result in the student’s VA education benefits being suspended.

CERTIFYING OFFICIAL

Individual(s) designated to sign enrollment certifications and other documents relating to VA benefits. The designation is made on VA Form 22-8794, Designation of Certifying Official(s). (See also SCHOOL CERTIFYING OFFICIAL.)

CHANGE OF PROGRAM

Change in a student’s program and curriculum. This includes any change that results in a loss of credit or lengthens the time necessary to complete their program. A change of program is generally charged when there is a loss of 12 credits. A change of program is not charged if the student completes one program and then begins a totally different program. (See 38 C.F.R. 21.4234(a)).

CHARACTER OF DISCHARGE/SERVICE Characterization of a Veteran’s period of service as determined by their service branch. To be considered for VA benefits, a Veteran must have been discharged under conditions other than dishonorable.

CHIEF EDUCATION LIAISON OFFICER (CELO)

Oversees Regional Processing Office (RPO) Education Services Units (ESU). Supervises ESU’s assigned Education Liaison Representatives (ELRs) and Education Compliance Survey Specialists (ECSSs).

CLAIM

May also be referred to as an application. It refers to a formal or informal written communication requesting a determination of entitlement or potential entitlement to Chapter 31 benefits. The original claim refers to the initial claim submitted by the beneficiary.  Subsequent claims may be made for benefits. (See 38 C.F.R. 21.30.)

CLAIMANT

Individual who has filed a claim for VA benefits. This term is used at any stage in the process of resolving a claim, including those potentially eligible for benefits, those receiving benefits, and those whose claims have been disallowed. (See also BENEFICIARY.)

CLEAR AND UNMISTAKABLE ERROR

A very specific and rare kind of error, of fact or law, that when called to the attention of later reviewers compels the conclusion, to which reasonable minds could not differ, that the result would have been manifestly different but for the error.  If it is not absolutely clear that a different result would have ensued, the error complained of cannot be clear and unmistakable.  Generally, either the correct facts, as they were known at the time, were not before VA, or the statutory and regulatory provisions extant at the time were incorrectly applied. (See 38 C.F.R. 3.105(i).)

CLOCK HOUR

A full 60-minute period of classroom, laboratory, or shop instruction for non-college degree programs. This time does not include any scheduled break (vacation) period.

CODE OF FEDERAL REGULATIONS (C.F.R.)

Codification of the general and permanent rules published in the Federal Register by the Executive departments and agencies of the Federal Government Code of Federal Regulations

Rules and regulations of the Secretary of Veterans Affairs are contained in Title 38 of the C.F.R. (38 C.F.R.). They are empowered to prescribe all rules and regulations, consistent with existing law, necessary or appropriate to carry out the laws administered by the Department (Section 501, Title 38 U.S.C.).

COLLEGE FUND

Also referred to as a “kicker”. Offered as an enlistment or reenlistment incentive under MGIB, VEAP, and MGIB-SR. An additional amount of money that increases the basic monthly benefit and is included in beneficiary’s VA payment. MGIB and MGIB-SR kickers can also be paid under PGIB in certain circumstances.

COMMISSION (ADMINISTRATIVE ERROR)

Occurs when the beneficiary knowingly takes a wrongful action. An example includes a MGIB beneficiary who verifies their attendance monthly as 12 hours when only enrolled for 6 hours.

COMMITTEE ON WAIVERS AND COMPROMISES (COWC)

Makes decisions on requests to waive debts caused by overpayments or to make a compromise on the collection of a debt.

  • Chairperson. Manages work of the Committee and assigns cases to COWC members, prepares quality and timeliness reviews, and trains COWC members.
  • Reporting Member. Member designated by the chairperson to be the presiding officer over a panel.
  • Member. Individual who participates on the COWC panel to review waiver requests and compromise offers. The individual has special competence and familiarity with one or more of the debt claim areas (i.e., compensation, pension, education, insurance, loan guaranty, etc.).
  • Liaison. Person designated at each RO whose job it is to communicate with DMC and PMC, coordinate needed actions, process administrative work, and coordinate/arrange hearings (by phone or video-conferencing – COWC member must take part in the hearing).

COMMON SECURITY SERVICES (CSS)

Collection of applications supporting connectivity and security between services used by VBA. CSS was established to provide adequate security controls and reduce the need for multiple applications to sufficiently monitor access to Veterans records.

COMPLIANCE SURVEY

An onsite review conducted at education and training institutions pursuant to 38 U.S.C. §3693. The survey is conducted to ensure the institutions and their approved programs follow all applicable provisions of the laws and regulations administered by VA.

A compliance survey has five principal objectives:

1. Verify the propriety of educational benefits paid to, or on behalf of, eligible individuals under the provisions of the laws administered by VA.

2. Confirm continued compliance with all applicable approval, recordkeeping and reporting requirements.

3. Assist school or training officials and eligible individuals to better understand their responsibilities and procedural requirements.

4. Determine, based on facts found during document reviews and personal visits, whether there are deviations from the established responsibilities and requirements by eligible individuals, schools or training establishments; and

5. Assure action is promptly taken through appropriate channels for the successful remediation of discrepancies found, or for the discontinuance of benefits when correction is not accomplished or when the findings of non- compliance are egregious.

COMPROMISE

Governed by 31 U.S.C. § 3711, a compromise is an offer and acceptance of a partial payment in settlement and full satisfaction of the offeror’s indebtedness as it exists at the time the offer is made. It is a final settlement, binding on the parties to the compromise, unless procured by fraud, misrepresentation of a material fact or mutual mistake of fact.

CONCURRENT ENROLLMENT

Student pursuing a degree at a school may take a course at another school because it’s at a more convenient time; it’s less expensive, etc. The school that will grant the degree is the student’s parent school. All other schools are guest schools. If the parent school will accept the guest school’s course as a transfer credit that applies towards completion of the student’s degree, then both the parent school and the guest school can certify the student to VA. Students may pursue courses at a guest school at the same time, concurrent, or at a different time, supplemental.

CONCURRENT PAYMENT

Concurrent receipt of education benefits under more than one law administered by VA, education benefits under one law administered by VA from multiple sources, or education benefits and certain other federal benefit programs.

CONDITION INTERFERED WITH DUTY (CIWD)

Separation from active duty based on a health condition not constituting a physical disability.

CONFIRMED ENROLLMENT

Enrollment certification which is dated and signed by the school's certifying official on or after the first day of a certified enrollment period. An Enrollment Certification may be signed and submitted to the RPO prior to the beginning of the enrollment period being certified if the student is pre-registered.

NOTE:  Schools might submit changes to the enrollment period before the Enrollment Certification is processed.

CONTINUOUS ACTIVE DUTY

Serving on active duty continuously without interruption.

CONVENIENCE OF GOVERNMENT (COG)

Separation from active duty reason which allows discharge before completion of the obligated service period. If released for COG, Veterans must have served a minimum of 20 months of a two-year enlistment or 30 months of a three-year enlistment to retain eligibility under MGIB.

COOPERATIVE TRAINING

Training that allows a student to attend school and gain work experience at the same time. Some schools partner with employers, allowing the student to attend classes in their desired field.

CORPORATE DATABASE (CorpDB)

Single repository of all data pertaining to a Veteran, including extensive demographic data, dependency information, all development actions, record of contact, and all rating data.

CORRESPONDENCE TRAINING

Lessons are received in the mail/via e-mail and students have a certain amount of time to complete and return the lessons for a grade. This is different from distance learning, which is completed on a strict schedule. Examples include training to become a photographer, locksmith, or gunsmith.

COST OF COURSE

Total cost of tuition and fees for a course that an educational institution charges to everyone who attends their institution.

COST OF LIVING ALLOWANCE (COLA)

Salary adjustment due to changes in the cost-of-living index based on the student’s geographic location.

COURSE

Unit of instruction required for an approved program of education that provides an individual with the knowledge and skills necessary to meet the requirements of the selected educational, professional, or vocational objective.

COURT OF APPEALS FOR VETERANS CLAIMS (CAVC)

Federal appellate court having exclusive jurisdiction and initial appellate authority over decisions made or affirmed by BVA.

Provides veterans an impartial judicial forum for review of administrative decisions by BVA that are adverse to the veteran-appellant's claim of entitlement to benefits for service-connected disabilities, survivor benefits and other benefits such as education payments and waiver of indebtedness. (See CAVC home page for more information.)

COVERED EDUCATION

Course of education at an institution of higher education and paid for with educational assistance furnished under a law administered by the Secretary.

COVERED MEMBER

Member of the Armed Forces (including the reserve components) enrolled in covered education.

COVERED SERVICE

Active service or inactive-duty training, as such terms are defined in section 101 of title 10, or State active duty, as defined in section 4303 of title 38.

CREDIT HOUR

Basic unit of measure for college credit. Credit hour is a time-based reference for measuring educational credit based on the number of classroom hours a week throughout a semester, trimester, or quarter term.

CREDITABLE SERVICE

Active duty service used in the calculation to determine the benefit level payable under PGIB.

DAILY AND OVERTIME TRACKER (DOT)

Employee tool used to account for production, scheduled time off, specialized processing time, meeting and training time, overtime, production data, and individual statistics.

DATABASE

Set of data elements that have specifically defined lengths, formats, and meanings and are related to each other by a common usage or need.  After the data elements have been defined, they can be used to store the same information for multiple entities.

DATA ELEMENT

Also called a database field. For example, Date of Birth, would be a data element that is used to define a person. The element would be defined by its length (DGI uses a standard date length of 8 characters) and format (a defined date format, such as MM/DD/YYYY). Databases use data elements for sorting data and allows processors to enter specific element(s) to see data that pertains to a beneficiary or training facility. Data entered on the screen is pulled from the database and formatted on the screen.

DATE OF CLAIM

Earliest date received for a document or set of documents upon which action is required to be taken by VA.

DATE OF LAST CERTIFICATION (DLC)

Last date through which a student’s training has been verified. DLC field on BDN shows the day following the actual date of last certification. For example, if the Veteran's training has been verified through June 30, DLC on BDN will show July 1.

DATE OF LAST PAYMENT (DLP)

Last date through which payment has been issued based on a student's enrollment. DLP field on BDN shows the day following the actual date of last payment. For example, if the Veteran has been paid through April 30, DLP on BDN will show May 1.

DATE RECEIVED

Date on which a document was received, by electronic means or otherwise, by the VA or a U.S. embassy or consulate.

DEBT

Money or property owed to VA.

DEBT MANAGEMENT CENTER (DMC)

Located in St. Paul, MN. DMC is responsible for generating reports concerning overpayments created and recovered as a result of award or stop actions processed by processing offices. Upon adjudication of an overpayment of VA benefits, DMC sends a computer generated letter notifying the beneficiary of their right to request a waiver of recovery of the overpayment.

DEFECTIVE ENLISTMENT

A voided agreement for enlistment into military service for one of the following reasons:

  • individual enlisted was a minor for purposes of service in the Armed Forces.
  • the enlistment or induction was erroneous; or
  • the enlistment agreement was defective.

NOTE:  Defective enlistment is a factor in MGIB and PGIB eligibility.

DEFENSE MANPOWER DATA CENTER (DMDC)

DMDC record contains information from the service member’s military records. VA accesses the DoD manpower database through computer interface between DMDC, Monterey, CA, and the BDN system. This data is used primarily for MGIB and MGIB-SR.

DEFENSE PERSONNEL RECORDS INFORMATION RETRIEVAL SYSTEM (DPRIS)

Web-based system which allows the user to access to Veteran’s official military personnel record.

DEFICIENCY COURSE

Any secondary level course or subject not previously completed satisfactorily but specifically required for pursuit of a post-secondary program of education. (See 38 C.F.R. 21.4200(s)).

DEGREE-GRANTING (INSTITUTION)

Public or nonpublic postsecondary institution that grants associate degrees, baccalaureate level degrees, and/or offers degrees at the graduate level including master's, doctorate, or first professional degrees.

DEGREE-SEEKING (STUDENT)

Student who has been formally admitted (matriculated) to a degree program with the intention of pursuing a degree.

DELAYED ENLISTMENT PROGRAM (DEP)

Form of delayed entry which allows individuals to contract to enter on active duty in the Armed Forces at a later date. Periods of delayed entry vary according to the specific program involved. For example, under DEP, the delayed entry onto active duty varies up to 270 days. In other programs, such as ROTC (Reserve Officers' Training Corps), the delay may be several years.

DELIMITING DATE

The date on which the Veteran or Service member's eligibility for benefits ends. This date will generally be set based upon the release from active duty (RAD) date of the most recent service period that contains sufficient qualifying days. Benefit types that do not expire will not have a delimiting date.

DEPARTMENT OF DEFENSE (DoD)

An executive branch department of the federal government charged with coordinating and supervising all agencies and functions of the U.S. government directly related to national security and the U.S. Armed Forces.

DEPARTMENT OF HOMELAND SECURITY (DHS)

Cabinet level agency which supplies service data, kicker information and transfer of entitlement approval information for members of the Coast Guard and Coast Guard Reserve.

DEPARTMENT OF VETERANS AFFAIRS (VA)

Executive department established under title 38, U.S. Code, to administer Veterans benefits.

NOTE:  The correct abbreviation for “Department of Veterans Affairs” remains “VA”, not “DVA” as is often seen.

  • 1930 – Established as the Veterans Administration, when Congress authorized the President to "consolidate and coordinate government activities affecting war veterans."
  • 1946 – Department of Medicine and Surgery was established.
  • 1953 – Department of Veterans Benefits (DVB), the predecessor of the current Veterans Benefits Administration (VBA), was created to administer the GI Bill and VA's huge compensation and pension programs.
  • 1963 – VA insurance program became part of DVB.
  • 1973 – National Cemetery System was transferred to the VA from the Department of the Army.
  • 1989 –Veterans Administration became the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA), the 14th department in the President's Cabinet.

VA Central Office (VACO) is in Washington, DC. The main VA office is located at 810 Vermont Ave, and the VBA office is located at 1800 G. Street NW.

DEPENDENT

Veteran or service member’s spouse, biological child, stepchild, adopted child, ward, or foster child. In rare circumstances, a parent may be considered a dependent if dependent upon the Veteran for financial support. (See subparagraphs (A), (D), and (I) of 10 U.S.C. 1072(2) and 10 U.S.C. 1072(6).)

DEPENDENTS' EDUCATIONAL ASSISTANCE PROGRAM (DEA or CHAPTER 35)

Benefits payable to eligible dependents and survivors of Veterans under 38 U.S.C. 35.

DEVELOPMENT (or CLAIMS DEVELOPMENT)

In the context of processing claims for VA benefits, "development" is the collection of evidence needed to resolve a claim.  Processors request additional information or documentation from sources such as the beneficiary, Department of Defense, training institution, or another third-party.

DGI/PGIB PROCESSING SYSTEM

Information technology application for the automated processing of claims for PGIB, STEM, and VET TEC benefits.

DGIB MANAGED SERVICE

Collection of Web-based applications developed for Education Service as part of the Digital GI Bill modernization efforts.

DIARY

Workload management tool to track future benefit/award action(s) needed on a claim.

DISABLING EFFECTS OF CHRONIC ALCOHOLISM

Alcohol-induced physical and/or mental disorders (such as habitual intoxication, withdrawal, delirium, amnesia, dementia, and other like manifestations of chronic alcoholism) which

        • Have been medically diagnosed as manifestations of alcohol dependency or chronic alcohol abuse, and
        • Are determined to have prevented commencement or completion of the affected individual's chosen program of education.

(See 38 C.F.R. 21.7020(b)(38).)

DISALLOWANCE

VA must provide beneficiaries and their representatives notice of all decisions VA makes about a claim for benefits.  Each notice of a disallowance must include an explanation of the procedure for getting a review of the decision, a statement of the reasons for the decision, and a summary of the evidence that led to the decision.

  • Disallowance Date:  Date on which a beneficiary is denied benefits.
  • Disallowance Reason:  Eligibility requirements that were not met by the beneficiary.

DISCHARGE or RELEASE

Dismissal or release from a period of service. (See 38 U.S.C. 101(18) for special circumstances which may be considered a discharge.)

DISTANCE LEARNING

Formalized teaching system that consists of interaction between the student and the instructor while physically separated using communications technology instead of regularly scheduled, conventional classroom or laboratory sessions. Communications technology includes mail, telephone, audio or videoconferencing, computer technology (online Internet courses or e-mail), or other electronic means (such as one-way and two-way transmissions through open broadcast, closed circuit, cable, microwave, broadband lines, fiber optics, satellite, or wireless communications devices).

DIVISIONS OF A SCHOOL YEAR

Ordinary school year is generally a period of two semesters or three quarters which is not less than 30 or more than 39 weeks in total length. Other divisions of the school year are terms, quarters, semesters, summer terms, and summer sessions. (See 38 C.F.R. 21.4200(b).)

DROP PERIOD

Period defined by the school or institution in which there is no penalty to a student to reduce the number of credits they are pursuing during the same term. Varies from institution to institution. For VA purposes, however, the drop period can never be greater than 30 days from the first day of the term.

DROPPED PRIORS

Award lines covering dates earlier than the current award period which have been dropped from the primary award screen due to lack of storage space.

REMEDIAL or DEFICIENCY COURSES

Noncredit training at the secondary level that is required for entrance to or preparatory to the successful pursuit of a postsecondary educational program. If secondary level training is needed for the student to reach a certain mastery level necessary for pursuit of a postsecondary program of education, the courses must be listed separately on the Enrollment Certification.

Upon entrance each student's academic background is evaluated and as warranted, a required noncredit program established. These secondary level courses may include, for example, basic English skills, fundamental math, reading, or other special academic assistance necessary for the student to qualify for admission. These courses may also include noncredit secondary level training to overcome a handicap such as in speech and may be offered at a high school, college, or other educational institution. It is the institution’s responsibility to determine if remedial/deficiency courses are necessary.

Remedial/deficiency courses are courses designed to correct deficiencies in basic mathematics, English, and reading at the elementary or secondary level. These courses can be certified as part of an approved program, but only for students for whom a verifiable need has been established. Keep test results and other documents in the student’s file that support the student’s need for remedial and deficiency courses.

Remedial/deficiency credit is used to correctly charge entitlement for DEA students.

  • MGIB, PGIB, MGIB-SR:  Entitlement is charged for remedial/deficiency training.
  • DEA:  Entitlement is not charged up to 5 months of full-time remedial and deficiency training. If a DEA student is certified for 6 standard credits and 6 remedial credits the student is paid at the full-time rate, but entitlement is charged at the ½-time rate.

Remedial/deficiency courses offered in an online or hybrid format are not approved for VA benefits. (See 38 C.F.R. 7020(b)(26).)
