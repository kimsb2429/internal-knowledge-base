**1.01 OVERVIEW OF BENEFITS**  
(Updated: February 26, 2020)

---

This manual provides implementing instructions for the following education benefit programs assigned to the Education Service within the Veterans Benefits Administration (VBA):

a. [Chapter 30, title 38, U. S. Code](https://uscode.house.gov/browse/prelim@title38/part3/chapter30&edition=prelim), All-Volunteer Force Educational Assistance Program (also known as the Montgomery GI Bill - Active Duty or MGIB).

b. [Chapter 32, title 38, U. S. Code](https://uscode.house.gov/browse/prelim@title38/part3/chapter32&edition=prelim), Post-Vietnam Era Veterans' Educational Assistance (also known as VEAP).

c. [Chapter 33, title 38, U. S. Code](https://uscode.house.gov/browse/prelim@title38/part3/chapter33&edition=prelim), Post 9/11 GI Bill Program (also known as Chapter 33).

d. [Chapter 35, title 38, U. S. Code](https://uscode.house.gov/browse/prelim@title38/part3/chapter35&edition=prelim), Survivors' and Dependents' Educational Assistance (also known as Dependents’ Education Assistance or DEA).

e. [Chapter 1606, title 10, U. S. Code,](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=(title:10%20chapter:1606%20edition:prelim)%20OR%20(granuleid:USC-prelim-title10-chapter1606)&f=treesort&num=0&edition=prelim) Educational Assistance for Members of the Selected Reserve (also known as the Montgomery GI Bill - Selected Reserve or MGIB - SR).

f. Section 901, Public Law 96-342, Educational Assistance Test Program ([chapter 106A, title 10, U. S. Code](https://uscode.house.gov/browse/prelim@title10/subtitleA/part3&edition=prelim)).

g. Section 903, Public Law 96-342, Educational Assistance Pilot Program [(in the amendments section of chapter 106A, section 2141, title 10, U.S. Code](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title10-section2141&num=0&edition=prelim)).

h. The Antiterrorism Act of 1986, Educational Assistance for Members Held as Captives and Their Dependents ([chapter 110, title 10, U.S. Code](https://uscode.house.gov/browse/prelim@title10/subtitleA/part3&edition=prelim)).

i. National Call to Service (NCS) ([chapter 31, section 510, title 10, U.S. Code](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title10-section510&num=0&edition=prelim)).

**1.02 PURPOSE OF M22-4**  
(Updated: February 26, 2020)

---

The purpose of M22-4 is to provide detailed claims processing procedures and course approval procedures for the Regional Processing Offices (RPOs). The RPOs are the Education Divisions located in Buffalo, and Muskogee. M22-4 also provides background material for personnel who answer inquiries or perform other functions in support of education programs..

**1.03 OTHER DIRECTIVES**  
(Updated: February 26, 2020)

---

M22-4 addresses the most frequent issues involved in the administration of the education programs listed above. In rare situations, the VCE may be directed to other manuals or related documents for specific issues.

**a.** M21-1MR, Adjudication Procedures Manual Rewrite, is the manual for the compensation and pension benefit programs and for related benefits. Some procedures in this manual apply to education benefit processing. Examples include attorney fee cases.

***NOTE:*** *When non-education directives conflict with education directives, the education directives take precedence.*

**b**. [M22-3,](http://www.warms.vba.va.gov/M22_3.html) Manpower Control and Utilization in Education Activities, provides procedures for managing education claims processing at RPOs. This manual contains a complete definition of each end product code used for work measurement.

**c**. [M27-1,](http://www.warms.vba.va.gov/M27_1.html) General Operations, is a manual for VA employees dealing with the public. Many of the subjects in this manual are being incorporated into M21-1MR.

**d.** [M28-1,](http://www.warms.vba.va.gov/M28_1.html) Veteran Readiness and Employment Service Procedures under 38 U.S.C. Chapter 31, gives procedures for providing counseling services required by law. This manual is directed to Veteran Readiness and Employment Service (VR&E) Divisions.

**e.** [M23-1, Part I](http://www.warms.vba.va.gov/M23_1.html#a), Administrative Operations, provides certain clerical procedures. Different activities within a field station may perform these procedures.

**f.** [MP-4, Part IV](http://www.va.gov/publ/direc/finance/finance.htm), Benefit Payment and Support Activities, provides finance procedures related to education claims.

**g.** [Circulars,](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/22/ref/circulars/index.html) [Letters](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/22/ref/letters/index.html) and [Policy Advisories.](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/22/ref/chronandpa.html) M22-4 is supplemented by these other documents. VA personnel may go to the [Knowledge Management Portal](https://vaww.vrm.km.va.gov/system/web/view/platform/common/login/top.jsp?partitionId=1&isAspectUser=false&locale=&localLogin=false&mainConsole=1), which is an internal website containing a complete list of all documents pertaining to education benefits.

 

**1.04 EDUCATION BENEFITS NOT COVERED IN M22-4**  
(Updated: February 26, 2020)

---

M22-4 does not cover all of VA's education related benefit programs.

**a. Current Programs Not Covered in M22-4**

(1) Compensation and Pension benefits based on school attendance. See [M21-1 MR.](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/21/publicat/Manuals/Part4/414.htm)

(2) Restored Entitlement Program for Survivors (REPS). See [M21-1 MR.](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/21/publicat/Manuals/Part4/414.htm)

(3) Chapter 31 and chapter 15, section 524, of title 38, U.S. Code. See [M28-1](https://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/21/publicat/Manuals/Part4/414.htm) for VR&E Division procedures.

**b. Expired Programs.** The following programs have expired, and M22-4 does not include full implementing procedures:

(1) The Hostage Relief Act of 1980, Public Law 96-449. See [chapter 3](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/22/ref/m22-4/Part%20I/ch03.htm#303d) for a summary of this program that expired on January 31, 1991.

(2) [Chapter 34, title 38 U. S. Code.](https://uscode.house.gov/browse/prelim@title38/part3/chapter34&edition=prelim) This was the Vietnam Era GI Bill. Although this program expired on December 31, 1989, M22-4 retains sufficient instructions for RPOs to make necessary chapter 34 eligibility determinations required for certain chapter 30 claims.

(3) Section 207, Public Law 101-366. Individuals had until January 1, 1992, to file applications under this program, described in [chapter 3.](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/22/ref/m22-4/Part%20I/ch03.htm#303f) See [Circular 22-90-5, Appendix B](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/22/ref/circulars/22circulars/229005.htm#B) for full instructions.

(4) Service Members Occupational Conversion and Training Act (SMOCTA) Procedures. See [part I, paragraph 3.03g](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/22/ref/m22-4/Part%20I/ch03.htm#303g) for a summary of this program that expired on January 31, 2000.

(5) Veterans Retraining Assistance Program (VRAP). On November 21, 2011, the President signed into law the “[VOW to Hire Heroes Act” of 2011](https://www.congress.gov/112/plaws/publ56/PLAW-112publ56.pdf).” That law established a retraining assistance program ([VRAP](https://www.congress.gov/112/plaws/publ56/PLAW-112publ56.pdf)) for individuals who are at least 35 but no more than 60 years of age, have another than dishonorable discharge, are unemployed, have no eligibility for any VA educational benefits, and are not in receipt of VA compensation due to unemployability. [VRAP](https://www.congress.gov/112/plaws/publ56/PLAW-112publ56.pdf) benefits started on July 1, 2012.

(6)  [Chapter 1607, title 10, U.S. Code](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid%3AUSC-prelim-title10-chapter1607&edition=prelim), Education Assistance for Reserve Component Members Supporting Contingency Operations and Certain Other Operations, (also known as the Reserve Educational Assistance Program or REAP). This benefit was discontinued November 25, 2015.  All remaining active participants were switched to Post 9/11 GI Bill by November 26, 2019.  
  
 

**1.05 ORGANIZATION**  
(Updated: February 26, 2020)

---

M22-4 has separate parts for each of the major benefit programs (chs. 30, 32, 33, 35, and 1606) as well as separate parts for instructions that apply to more than one benefit program.  

**a.** There are 12 parts in all.

(1) [Part I, General,](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/22/ref/m22-4/Part%20I/index.html) contains topics that are common to multiple education benefit programs such as definitions, legislative history, work-study and direct deposit.

(2) [Part II, Computer Processing and Inquiry,](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/22/ref/m22-4/Part%20II/index.html) describes the Benefits Delivery Network (BDN) and other computer processing that is common to more than one education benefit program.

(3) [Part III, Claims Processing and Miscellaneous Benefits](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/22/ref/m22-4/Part%20III/index.html), covers claims issues that are not directly related to award processing such as application processing, extension of delimiting dates, counseling, and programs of education. It also covers miscellaneous benefits such as tutorial assistance, section 901, and Anti-terrorism, National Call to Service, licensing and certification, and national tests.

(4) [Part IV, Claims Processing - Award Issues](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/22/ref/m22-4/Part%20IV/index.html), addresses claims processing topics directly related to award processing such as enrollment certifications, duplication of benefits, changes of program, measurement of courses, and interval payment..  
  
(5) [Part V, Chapter 30 Procedures](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/22/ref/m22-4/Part%20V/index.html), addresses issues that are unique to chapter 30.

(6) [Part VI, Chapter 32 Procedures](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/22/ref/m22-4/Part%20VI/index.html), addresses issues that are unique to chapter 32.

(7) [Part VII, Chapter 35 Procedures](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/22/ref/m22-4/Part%20VII/index.html), addresses issues that are unique to chapter 35.

(8) [Part VIII, Chapter 1606 Procedures](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/22/ref/m22-4/Part%20VIII/index.html), addresses issues that are unique to chapter 1606.

(9) [Part IX, Liaison and Approval Activities](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/22/ref/m22-4/Part%20IX/index.html), deals with course approval issues and the responsibilities of the ELR and the State approving agencies.

(10) [Part X, Compliance Survey](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/22/ref/m22-4/Part%20X/index.html), deals with compliance survey procedures.

(11) [Part XI, Chapter 1607](http://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/22/ref/m22-4/Part%20XI/index.html), addresses issues that are unique to chapter 1607. (now archived)

(12) [Part XII, Chapter 33](https://vaww.vrm.km.va.gov/system/templates/selfservice/va_kanew/help/agent/locale/en-US/portal/554400000001048), addresses issues which are unique to chapter 33.

**b.** Changes will be issued electronically and posted in the Knowledge Management portal.  Electronic notices to internal Education Service employees is sent via Gov Delivery advising of the updated information.

**1.07 RELATIONSHIP OF M22-4 TO 38 U.S.C AND 38 CFR**  
(Updated: February 26, 2020)

---

The laws passed by Congress are found in the United States Code (U.S.C.). The benefits described in this manual are found in title 38, U.S.C., or title 10, U.S.C.  The Code of Federal Regulations (CFRs) contains detailed rules and regulations governing agency implementation of the statutes in the U.S. Code. The federal regulations for the benefit programs described in this manual are found in title [38 CFR, part 21](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/text-idx?SID=89d2207034df135bca768ebfac6f957b&mc=true&node=pt38.2.21&rgn=div5). (The Antiterrorism Act does not have any regulations in the 38 CFR. There are regulations for the Antiterrorism program in the [22 CFR, part 192, subpart E](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/text-idx?SID=9e3a41fa5f0d2b920b2ead3e622282e7&mc=true&node=pt22.1.192&rgn=div5#sp22.1.192.e), which is under the jurisdiction of the State Department. The NCS s will be codified in 38 CFR at a future date.)

**a. Cross-References.** M22-4 in many instances provides cross-references to the related regulation. Book G is organized as thus:

(1) [Subpart B](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=&SID=271bcdb5128b7896814a60294d2a349c&mc=true&n=pt38.2.21&r=PART&ty=HTML#sp38.2.21.b) - Claims and Applications for Educational Assistance. (*NOTE: Subpart B applies to multiple education programs.)*

(2) [Subpart C](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=&SID=271bcdb5128b7896814a60294d2a349c&mc=true&n=pt38.2.21&r=PART&ty=HTML#sp38.2.21.c) - Chapter 35.

(3) [Subpart D](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=&SID=271bcdb5128b7896814a60294d2a349c&mc=true&n=pt38.2.21&r=PART&ty=HTML#sp38.2.21.d) - Administration of Educational Assistance Programs. (*NOTE: Subpart D applies to multiple education programs.)*

(4) [Subpart G](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=&SID=271bcdb5128b7896814a60294d2a349c&mc=true&n=pt38.2.21&r=PART&ty=HTML#sp38.2.21.g) - Chapter 32 (and Sec. 903 from [38 CFR 21.5290](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/text-idx?SID=271bcdb5128b7896814a60294d2a349c&mc=true&node=sg38.2.21_15270.sg63&rgn=div7) through [21.5294](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/text-idx?SID=271bcdb5128b7896814a60294d2a349c&mc=true&node=sg38.2.21_15270.sg63&rgn=div7)).

(5) [Subpart H](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=&SID=271bcdb5128b7896814a60294d2a349c&mc=true&n=pt38.2.21&r=PART&ty=HTML#sp38.2.21.h) - Section 901.

(6) [Subpart K](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=&SID=271bcdb5128b7896814a60294d2a349c&mc=true&n=pt38.2.21&r=PART&ty=HTML#sp38.2.21.k) - Chapter 30.

(7) [Subpart L](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=&SID=271bcdb5128b7896814a60294d2a349c&mc=true&n=pt38.2.21&r=PART&ty=HTML#sp38.2.21.l) - Chapter 1606.

(8) [Subpart O](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=&SID=271bcdb5128b7896814a60294d2a349c&mc=true&n=pt38.2.21&r=PART&ty=HTML#sp38.2.21.o) - National Call to Service (future).

(9) [Subpart P](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=&SID=271bcdb5128b7896814a60294d2a349c&mc=true&n=pt38.2.21&r=PART&ty=HTML#sp38.2.21.p) – Chapter 33

**b. Precedence.** Any rule published in the CFR takes precedence over M22-4 if there is a conflict. However, it typically takes several years to publish changes to the CFR. Therefore, M22-4 takes precedence when the issue is the proper implementation of a public law for which no CFR change has been issued or in cases where the M22-4 specifically states to override the CFR.
