**Introduction**

---

This chapter explains how to determine the training time (full time, 3/4 time, 1/2 time, etc.) for students enrolled in degree courses at IHLs (Institutions of Higher Learning).  See [Part 4: Chapter 7 - Other Measurement Issues](https://eg5813aprl-int-nlb.egainstz.cloud/system/ws/v11/ss/article/554400000073560?portalId=554400000001018&usertype=customer&\$lang=en-us&) chapter 7 for measurement of non-degree courses.  
  
*NOTE:  This chapter explains the rules that apply to training that occurs on or after July 30, 1993.  For the prior rules, consult the September 1992 version of this chapter.  If the earlier version is not available, contact the Education Service at (202) 273-7181 or by E-mail to [VAVBAWAS/CO/225A.](mailto:VAVBAWAS/CO/225A.)*

| d.  Procedure | Use the following table to determine training time if 12 credit hours is full time. |

 

| **If the student is enrolled in:** | **The Training Time is:** |
| --- | --- |
| 12 or more credit hours | Full time |
| 9 through 11 credit hours | 3/4 time |
| 6 through 8 credit hours | 1/2 time |
| 4 through 5 credit hours | \*Less than 1/2 time; more than 1/4             time |
| 1 through 3 credit hours | \*1/4 time or less |

\*For chapter 32 and section 903, these two training time categories are replaced by a "1/4 time" category.  For chapter 1606 these two training time categories are replaced by a "less than 1/2 time" category.

 

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| e.  Example 1 | A school reports 11 credit hours for a veteran enrolled in a standard length semester.  Training time:  3/4 time |
| f.  Example 2 | A school reports 4 credit hours for a chapter 32 veteran who is enrolled in a standard length semester.  Training time:  1/4 time |
| g.  Example 3 | A school reports 5 credit hours for a chapter 35 child who is enrolled in a standard term.  Training time:  less than 1/2 time; more than 1/4 time |

**6.06 How to Measure Nonstandard Terms**

---

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| a.  Definitions | Nonstandard Term.  A division of a school year that is longer or shorter than a quarter or semester.  (See definitions of "quarter" and "semester" in [par. 6.01b](#601b).)  Equivalent Credit Hours.  The number of credit hours in a standard term comparable to the number of hours in a term that is not a standard quarter or semester.  *NOTE:  VA treats "equivalent credit hours" as credit hours for measurement purposes.* |
| b.  Principle | If a term is shorter or longer than a standard quarter or semester (as defined in [par. 6.01b](#601b)), the credit hours must be adjusted to "equivalent credit hours" before determining training time.  *Reference:* *[38 CFR 21.4272(g)](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=1&SID=5f6d7adbb6a68e4712625f2c90e8610b&ty=HTML&h=L&mc=true&r=SECTION&n=se38.2.21_14272)* |
| c.  Procedure | Follow these steps to measure nonstandard terms. |

 

| **Step** | **Action** |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Determine the number of whole weeks in the nonstandard term:       Determine the number of days from the beginning to the end of the term as certified by the school, subtracting any vacation period of 7 days or more.       Divide the above result by 7.       Consider a remainder of 4 days or more to be a whole week and disregard a remainder of 3 days or less.  EXAMPLE:  A term extends from July 13 to August 18.  This term is 37 days long or 5 weeks and 2 days.  Disregard the remainder of 2 days.  The term is considered 5 weeks for the purpose of computing "equivalent credit hours."  *NOTE:  In the above calculation, always use the beginning date of enrollment certified by the school as the starting date for computing the length of a nonstandard term.  If a school certifies the registration date as the beginning date as permitted under [38 CFR 21.4131(b)(4)](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=1&SID=5f6d7adbb6a68e4712625f2c90e8610b&ty=HTML&h=L&mc=true&r=SECTION&n=se38.2.21_14131), use this date to compute the length of the term regardless of the fact that class instruction may actually begin a few days after the registration date.* |
| 2 | Compute "equivalent credit hours":    Multiply the credits to be earned in the term by 18 if credit is granted in semester hours, or by 12 if credit is granted in quarter hours.    Divide the product by the number of whole weeks (see Step 1 above) in the term.  *NOTE: Drop any fractional unit that may result from this calculation.  However, if the student is taking less than one equivalent credit hour always round up to one equivalent credit hour.*  Example 1:  A student is taking a 4 semester-hour course that lasts 8 weeks.  Twelve credit hours is full time at this school.  Compute "equivalent credit hours" as follows:  (4 x 18)/8 = 9  Authorize:  3/4-time training (based on 9 "equivalent credit hours")  Example 2:  Same facts as example 1 above except the school is on a quarter hour basis  (4 x 12)/8 = 6  Authorize:  1/2-time rate (based on 6 "equivalent credit hours") |

*NOTE:  An alternative to the above procedure is to use the Training Time Equivalency tables shown in paragraph 14.02.*

**6.07 How to Measure Independent Study Courses**

---

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| a.  Definition | Independent study is an accredited course or subject leading to a standard college degree and consisting of a prescribed program of study with no provision for regularly scheduled, conventional classroom instruction.  (See [38 CFR 21.4267(b)](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=1&SID=5f6d7adbb6a68e4712625f2c90e8610b&ty=HTML&h=L&mc=true&r=SECTION&n=se38.2.21_14267) for the full definition.) |
| b.  School certification | Schools should include independent study hours on the enrollment certification.  They must list specific independent study courses by course number in Remarks on the back of the form.  See sample enrollment certification in [paragraph 6.01e.](#601e)  Schools should certify the maximum allowable period for completion of the independent study course or courses (par. 1.16b(2)).   Schools may certify independent study courses after the end of a term, quarter, or semester under certain conditions (par. 1.27b). |
| c.  Approval information | The OLAF E02 screen (see sample in [par. 6.02d](#602d)) indicates if a school has any approved independent study courses.  The OLAF E04 screen (see sample in [par. 6.02g](#602g)) shows specific approved independent study courses.  *NOTE:  Nonaccredited independent study courses are not permitted after*  *October 29, 1992, except as described in [38 CFR 21.4252(g)](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=1&SID=5f6d7adbb6a68e4712625f2c90e8610b&ty=HTML&h=L&mc=true&r=SECTION&n=se38.2.21_14252).* |
| d.  Principle 1 | (See Policy Advisory dated 2/13/07) Independent study courses are measured in the same manner as other credit-hour courses taken in residence.  *Reference:  Public Law 102-568* |
| e.  Principle 2 | There are special restrictions on taking independent study in chapter 1606:       Not all reservists under chapter 1606 are eligible to take independent study without taking courses in residence at the same time.         The reservists affected by this provision first became eligible to chapter 1606 before October 1, 1990.  See part VIII, paragraph 3.04d(2), for the evidence required to establish eligibility to take independent study alone in these cases. |
| f.  Procedure | Apply the following procedure if an enrollment certification includes independent study. |

 

| **Step** | **Action** |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Determine if Principle 2, which applies to certain chapter 1606 cases, bars payment of independent study.  If yes, deny payment for independent study courses.  (Pay for home courses if appropriate.)  If no, continue to step 2. |
| 2 | Determine if the school is on the IHls Exempt from Review listing.  If yes, go to step 5 unless there is some reason to question whether the independent study courses are approved.  Go to Step 4 if there is reason to question.  If no, go to Step 3. |
| 3 | Determine if the OLAF E02 screen shows that independent study courses are approved at that school.  If yes,  go to Step 4.  If no, ask the ELR to double check the approval before denying payment for the independent study course or courses. |
| 4 | Review the OLAF E04 screen.  Are the specific independent study courses listed on the enrollment certification listed on this screen (or on NotePad)?  If yes, go Step 5.  If no, ask the ELR to double check the approval before denying payment for the independent study course or courses*.* |
| 5 | Add the independent study credit hours to all other credit hours to determine measurement or, if this is a graduate student taking independent study, follow the provisions of [paragraph 6.13.](#613)  *NOTE:  If the independent study is certified for a period that is not a standard quarter or semester, apply [paragraph 6.06](#606) to determine "equivalent credit hours."* |

 

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| g.  Example | The enrollment certification for a chapter 30 student shows 9 credits, and the Remarks section shows a 3-credit independent study course: History 101.  The school is not on the IHLs Exempt from Review listing.  If the OLAF E04 screen lists History 101 as an approved independent study course, count all 9 credits when determining training time.  Otherwise count only 6 credits.  *NOTE: If History 101 is not listed in OLAF as an approved independent study course, check with the ELR before denying benefits for this course.* |

 

**6.08  How To Measure Noncredit Courses**

---

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| a.  Background | Many institutions permit students to take noncredit remedial, refresher, or deficiency courses.  These courses do not count toward graduation.  *NOTE 1:  See* [PART 02, COMPUTER PROCESSING. CHAPTER 2 - TRANSACTION CODES](https://eg5813aprl-int-nlb.egainstz.cloud/system/ws/v11/ss/article/554400000076474?portalId=554400000001018&usertype=customer&\$lang=en-us&)*, paragraph 2.02, for the formal definitions of these terms.*  *NOTE 2:  Chapter 35 permits 5 months of entitlement free training for noncredit courses.  See* [Part 7: Chapter 4 - Award Processing and Other Authorization Issues](https://eg5813aprl-int-nlb.egainstz.cloud/system/ws/v11/ss/article/554400000073737?portalId=554400000001018&usertype=customer&\$lang=en-us&)*, paragraph 4.12.* |
| b.  School certification | Schools must report noncredit refresher, deficiency, or remedial on the enrollment certification. See [subparagraph 6.01e](#601e) for a sample.  Schools must list specific noncredit courses the student is taking in the Remarks section of the form. |
| c.  Approval information | The OLAF E02 screen (see [par. 6.02d](#602d)) has a Remedial/Deficiency Approved field.  If a "Y" appears in this field, it means that some or all of the noncredit refresher, remedial, and deficiency courses offered at the school are approved.  To determine if specific courses are approved, review the OLAF E04 screen (see [par. 6.02f](#602f)) or contact the ELR. |
| d.  Principle 1 | The credit hour equivalent for noncredit refresher, remedial, and deficiency courses will be added to regular credit hours to determine the student's training time.  *Reference:  [38 CFR 21.4272(k)](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=1&SID=5f6d7adbb6a68e4712625f2c90e8610b&ty=HTML&h=L&mc=true&r=SECTION&n=se38.2.21_14272)* |
| e.  Principle 2 | Schools may include noncredit hours on enrollment certifications only if the student needs to take these courses and only if an authorized official has certified as to the need.  (The certification of need rules are found in [Part 3: Chapter 7 - Restrictions on Training](https://eg5813aprl-int-nlb.egainstz.cloud/system/ws/v11/ss/article/554400000075993?portalId=554400000001018&usertype=customer&\$lang=en-us&), par. 7.16.)  *NOTE:  Schools make the certification of need when they sign the enrollment certification.  Adjudicators should not routinely question the need for noncredit courses.* |
| f.  Procedures | Follow these steps if noncredit courses are certified in Item 11B. |

 

| **Step** | **Action** |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Determine if specific courses are listed in the Remarks section of the enrollment certification.  If yes, go to Step 2.  If no, do not pay for the noncredit courses.  Set up a control and ask the school for a listing of the specific course or courses. |
| 2 | Determine if the school is on the IHLs Exempt from Review listing.  If yes, assume the courses are approved and pay for them unless there is some reason to question them.  Go to Step 3 if there is reason to question.  If no, go to Step 3. |
| 3 | Review the OLAF E04 screen.  Are the specific noncredit courses shown on the Enrollment Certification listed?   If yes, do pay for the noncredit courses.  If no, ask the ELR to double check the approval. |

*NOTE:  If noncredit courses are certified in clock hours and there is a need to combine credit hours and clock hours, see the examples in [paragraph 6.12](#612).*

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| g.  Example | An enrollment certification shows 6 regular credit hours and 3 noncredit hours.  The Remarks section lists a remedial English course.  The school is not on the IHLs Exempt from Review listing but the OLAF E04 screen indicates that this noncredit course is approved.  To determine training time, combine the noncredit hours with the regular credit hours  (6 + 3 = 9) and measure the course based on 9 credit hours. |

 

**6.09  How to Measure Open Circuit Television Courses**

---

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| a.  Background | Open circuit television is television available to everyone in the community.  Special restrictions applied to training received in this manner before October 9, 1996, as described below.  Closed circuit television is television with limited availability (for example just on a college campus).  Instruction offered through closed circuit television that requires regular classroom attendance is recognized to the same extent as regular classroom or laboratory training.  [(38 CFR 21.4233(c)(2)](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=1&SID=5f6d7adbb6a68e4712625f2c90e8610b&ty=HTML&h=L&mc=true&r=SECTION&n=se38.2.21_14233) |
| b.  School certification | Schools must report open circuit TV courses on the enrollment certification.  See sample enrollment certification in [paragraph 6.01e.](#601e) |
| c.  Principles | Before October 9, 1996.  For training that occurred before October 9, 1996, a student could be paid for open circuit television courses only if he or she was also enrolled in a course or courses taken through residence training during the same term, semester, or quarter.    On or After October 9, 1996.  For training that occurred on or after  October 9, 1996, VA considers open circuit TV to be a form of independent study and the procedures in [paragraph 6.07](#607) must be applied. |
| d.  Example 1 | A student's enrollment for the winter 1996 semester is for 6 credits through open circuit TV and 6 credits through residence training.  The enrollment should be measured based on 12 credit hours. |
| e.  Example 2 | A veteran enrolls in 6 credits of open circuit  TV courses (with no concurrent resident courses) from September 16, 1996, to December 20, 1996.  Deny benefits from September 16 through October 8.  Pay benefits for 6 credit hours from October 9, 1996, through December 20, 1996. |

 

**6.10 How to Measure Practice Teaching**

---

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| a.  Background | Practice teaching (or student teaching) often requires a full day in the classroom, 5 days a week.   However, the number of semester or quarter hours of credit granted for this training varies and is sometimes not commensurate with the amount of time actually expended. |
| b.  School certification | The enrollment certification for students enrolled in practice teaching should indicate that they are pursuing practice-teaching.  Schools may report both credit hours and clock hours on the enrollment certification. |
| c.  Principle | The training time for students in practice-teaching will be measured on either a clock-hour or a credit-hour basis, whichever will provide the greater benefit.  *NOTE: Eighteen clock hours per week will be considered full time for an accredited course in practice teaching when the course is measured on a clock-hour basis.  See chapter 7 for clock-hour measurement tables.* |
| d.  Example | A student is enrolled in practice teaching for 18 clock hours and 9 credit hours.  Eighteen clock hours is full time whereas 9 credit hours is 3/4 time.  Measure this training on a clock-hour basis since it will provide the greater benefit. |

 

**6.11 How to Measure Overlapping Courses Offered in Credit Hours**

---

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| a.  Background | In some cases a student enrolls in courses with overlapping enrollment periods.  This can happen with enrollment at two schools or at one school.  *NOTE:  Schools will certify the commencing date of courses in accordance with [38 CFR 21.4131(b)](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=1&SID=5f6d7adbb6a68e4712625f2c90e8610b&ty=HTML&h=L&mc=true&r=SECTION&n=se38.2.21_14131).  Applying this regulation can result in the certification of overlapping courses at the same school.* |
| b.  Principle 1 | If the student is enrolled in overlapping enrollment periods, compute the training time separately for the enrollment periods before, during, and after the overlapping period.  Retain fractional credit hours when adding the credits for overlapping periods, but drop any fraction from the final result. |
| c.  Example 1 | A student is enrolled as follows in a school in which 12 semester hours is considered full time:  Term 1:  10/1/96 through 12/20/96 (12 weeks)                             4 semester hours  Term 2:  10/15/96 through 12/6/96 (8 weeks)                               4 semester hours  Equivalent Credit Hours  Term 1: (4 x 18)/12 = 6  Term 2: (4 x 18)/8   = 9  Authorize:  1/2 time from 10/1/96 (based on 6 equivalent credit hours)  Full time from 10/15/96 (based on 15 equivalent credit hours)  1/2 time from 12/7/96 through 12/20/96 (based on 6 equivalent credit hours) |
| d.  Principle 2 | If one or more overlapping terms is of nonstandard length, compute equivalent credit hours for the nonstandard term per [paragraph 6.06.](#606) Retain fractional credit hours when adding the credits for overlapping periods, but drop any fraction from the final result. |
| e.  Example 2 | Same facts as above example except claimant is a serviceperson under chapter 30 with a full-time rate of $427.87 and the tuition and fees as indicated.  Term 1: $1500.00 tuition and fees ($562.50 prorated monthly)  Term 2:   $145.00 tuition and fees ($83.65 prorated monthly)  Authorize:  $213.94 from 10/1/96 (This is the maximum 1/2-time rate; the prorated rate of $562.50 exceeds the maximum.)  $427.87 from 10/15/96 (This is the maximum full-time rate; the combined prorated amounts, $562.50 + $83.65 = $646.15, exceeds the maximum.)  $213.94 from 12/7/96 through 12/20/96 (This is the maximum 1/2-time rate; the prorated rate of $562.50 exceeds the maximum.) |

**6.12 How to Measure Overlapping Courses Offered in Both Credit and Clock Hours**  
    (See Policy Advisory dated February 7, 2007)

---

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| a. Background | In some cases, a student is enrolled in both credit hour and clock hour courses that are overlapping.  *Reference:  [38 CFR 21.7172](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=1&SID=5f6d7adbb6a68e4712625f2c90e8610b&ty=HTML&h=L&mc=true&r=SECTION&n=se38.2.21_17172)* |
| b.  Principle 1 | If a clock-hour  course overlaps with a course taken at the primary school that is measured on a semester- or quarter-hour basis, convert the clock hours to equivalent semester or quarter hours.  (See par. 1.09a to determine which school is the primary school.)  To make this conversion:    Divide the number of semester or quarter hours required for full-time attendance at the primary school by the number of clock hours required for full-time attendance at the secondary school.     Multiply the result by the number of clock hours in which the student is enrolled.    Drop any fraction that my result. |
| c.  Example 1 | A student is taking 9 semester hours concurrently with 6 clock hours.  Twelve semester hours is full time at the primary school and 25 clock hours is full time at the secondary school.  Computation:  12 credit hours/25 clock hours  = .48;                         6 clock hours X .48 = 2.88 equivalent semester hours.                          Then 9 + 2.88 = 11 equivalent semester hours.  (Fractions at the final step only are dropped.)  Authorize 3/4 time benefits based on 11 equivalent semester hours. |
| d.  Principle 2 | If a semester- or quarter-hour course overlaps with a course taken at the primary school measured on a clock-hour basis, convert the semester or quarter hours to equivalent clock hours.  If the semester or quarter hours are for nonstandard terms, first convert credit hours to equivalent credit hours as described in [paragraph 6.06](#606).  To convert semester or quarter hours to equivalent credit hours:    Divide the number of clock hours required for full-time attendance at the primary school by the number of semester or quarters hours required for full time at the secondary school.    Multiply the result by the number of semester or quarter hours in which the student is enrolled.    Drop any fraction that may result. |
| e.  Example 2 | A student is taking 9 clock hours and 3 semester hours in a regular semester.  (18 clock hours is considered full time and 12 semester hours is full time.)  Computation:  18 clock hours/12 semester hours  = 1.5.                         3 semester hours X 1.5 = 4.5 clock hours.                         Then, 9 + 4.5 = 13 clock hours.  (Fractions at the final step only are dropped.)  Authorize benefits based on 13 clock hours. |

 

**6.13 How to Measure Graduate or Advanced Professional Training**

---

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| a.  Background | Graduate and advanced professional training at IHLs have special measurement procedures described in this paragraph.  *NOTE:  Different measurement procedures apply to nonaccredited professional training and to professional training that is not "advanced professional training."  See chapter 7 for these rules.* |
| b.  Basic principles | Schools can qualify to make their own determination of training time for graduate and advanced professional courses.   If they do not qualify to make this determination, these courses are measured on the same basis as undergraduate courses.  *NOTE:  If a school qualifies to determine training time for graduate courses, it also qualifies to determine training time for any undergraduate courses the graduate student may be enrolled in.*  *Reference:  [38 CFR 21.4273(a)](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=1&SID=5f6d7adbb6a68e4712625f2c90e8610b&ty=HTML&h=L&mc=true&r=SECTION&n=se38.2.21_14273)).* |
| c.  School certification | Schools will mark Type of Training (Item 7 on Enrollment Certifications) as Graduate or Advanced Professional when a student is enrolled in this type of training. |
| d.  Approval information | The OLAF E01 screen FULL TIME MEASUREMENT: Graduate field will indicate AS CERTIFIED when the school is authorized to determine training time for graduate courses.  If this field shows "12," "13," or "14," measurement will be the same as for undergraduate training.  See [paragraphs 6.04](#604) and [6.05.](#605) |
| e.  Additional principles | Terms of nonstandard length:       Accept the school's certification of the training time or the training time computed for undergraduate students (see [pars. 6.04](#604) and [6.05](#605)) whichever is the greater.  Independent study and open circuit TV:       Apply the same principles given for undergraduate courses.  See [paragraphs 6.07](#607) and [6.09.](#609) |
| f.  Procedure | Apply the following procedure to determine training time for graduate and professional training. |

 

| **Step** | **Action** |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Consult the FULL TIME MEASUREMENT: GRADUATE field on the OLAF EOI screen. |
| 2 | If the entry is "AS CERTIFIED," accept the school's certification as to the training time. Go to step 3 if the term is of nonstandard length.   If the entry is a number such as "12", "13", or "14", use the measurement tables for undergraduate training described in paragraph [6.04](#604) and [6.05](#605). Go to step 3 if the term is of nonstandard length. |
| 3 | If the term is of nonstandard length, compute "equivalent credit hours" as for undergraduate courses (see [par. 6.06](#606)).  Pay based on whichever method gives the higher training time:  the school's certification of training time or the measurement that applies to undergraduates.  *NOTE:  Apply the same principle if  a student is pursuing undergraduate courses as part of a graduate program.  Pay based on whichever method gives the higher training time:  the school's certification of training time or the measurement that would apply to undergraduates under [paragraphs 6.04](#604) and [6.05.](#605)* |

 

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| g.  Example | A graduate student is enrolled for 6 credit hours (3 are for an undergraduate course) which the school certifies as 3/4 time.  The E01 screen shows the school is approved to determine training time for graduate students.  The adjudicator should accept 3/4 time as the correct training time. |
