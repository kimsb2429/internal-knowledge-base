**SUBCHAPTER I.  310 SCREEN**

**5.01   Introduction**

---

The BDN Chapter 1607 system is similar to the Chapters 30 and 1606 systems in many ways. The same screens are used, however the layout of the screens and the information input by the VCE can vary slightly between these programs. There is nothing different in processing a Certificate of Eligibility, for example, however some new fields have been added to the 310 screen for chapter 1607 processing.

This chapter will focus on the 310, 411 and COE screens and instruction for processing a REAP claim utilizing these screens.      

  **5.02  Purpose of the 310 Screen**

---

**a**. The 310 screen is used for two major purposes:

(1) First, the 310 screen must be completed in order to record initial eligibility (or ineligibility) for Chapter 1607.

(2) Second, if eligibility is established, the computation of original entitlement and delimiting date (if applicable) is done on the 310 screen. Original entitlement adjustments are made on the 310 screen as well.

**b.** The 310 screen is similar to the 310 screen in the Chapters 30 or 1606 system. Generally, eligibility data is contained on the top portion of the screen, delimiting date (if applicable) and entitlement computation fields are on the bottom portion of the screen. The eligibility data is different in detail due to differences between these programs. Delimiting Date and entitlement fields are essentially the same, with the exception of some Prior VA Training codes.       

 **5.03   Accessing the 310 Screen**

---

For input: The 310 screen is most commonly accessed through the CADJ command, from either the Ready Screen or from the 30M, 312, 317, and 411 screens.

For inquiry: The 310 screen can be accessed for inquiry with the PINQ command; it can be accessed through the Ready Screen or as a Next Screen entry on any of the PINQ accessible screens.       

 **5.04   Updating the Master Record.**

---

In order for an award or disallowance to be processed, eligibility and entitlement must be determined. These computations are done on the 310 screen. When you leave the 310 screen, the eligibility and entitlement information is stored in the Pending Issue file. If the Pending Issue file is eventually authorized as an award, disallowance, or Certificate of Eligibility, the eligibility and entitlement information is stored in the database and becomes part of the claimant's master record.

**5.06   Using the 310 Screen**

---

**a**. If the claimant doesn't have a VA REAP eligibility record you cannot establish eligibility. In that instance, do not complete the 310 screen. Instead go straight to the 411 screen and check the appropriate disallowance reason (01A).

**b**. If the claimant has a REAP record, but it indicates not eligible (and there is no conflicting evidence submitted by the claimant), place an "N" in the ELIG field, complete all fields as necessary, ALT/ENT and capture this screen into his or her TIMS file. The applicable disallowance code will be checked on the 411 screen.

**c**. If the claimant is eligible, DoD will provide all necessary data on the VA REAP eligibility web site. This will include the date of activation (EOD date) which qualifies the member for chapter 1607 benefits. The DOE will be 90 days from the EOD, unless the member was separated due to a service connected disability prior to completing 90 days of service. In most cases the DOE cannot be prior to 12/9/01.

**d.** If the member has recently been released from active duty, DoD may not have that information, or may not have provided it to VA. Accept the member's DD 214 (if submitted) as confirmation of the clamant's duty status and date of release from active duty (the RAD date). The RPO's DoD Coordinator should inform the service department point of contact of the DD 214 and fax a copy to them if requested. If there is a conflict concerning the release from active duty date between the VA REAP Eligibility web site and the DD 214, use the dates on the DD 214 and contact DoD regarding the discrepancy. Do not delay processing the claim unless the dates are so confusing that the DoD Coordinator must call the point of contact to clarify the conflict.

**e**. If the claimant has more than one period of qualifying active duty service, see [paragraph 5.09](#509) for specific processing instructions.

**f.** If the claimant is entitled to a Chapter 1606 kicker, it is also payable under Chapter 1607. Enter the kicker code ("L", "M", or "H") in the 1606 Kicker Code field on the 310 screen. There isn't an override on the 312 for the kicker, once entered on the 310, BDN will include the kicker amount in the payment calculations on the 312 screen.

**g.** Only those members who separate from the reserves due to a service-connected disability will have a Service Connected Date of Eligibility (S/C DOE). This date is the original DOE entered on the 310 screen unless the member left the reserves and had to reestablish his or her eligibility (and therefore has a different DOE now than initially). The delimiting date will be 10 years from the S/C DOE. Don't confuse the period of eligibility with a period of active duty service. A member could have several periods of active duty during the same period of eligibility. A member should only have additional DOEs if they left the reserves, reentered and were called up again and served a minimum of 90 days of active duty to requalify for Chapter 1607.

**h.** The member's reserve service information is entered on the 310 screen as well. The Entered Selected Reserve (ESR) date will be taken from the members 1606 DOD screen. If there isn't a 1606 DOD screen for that member, use the member's EOD date as the ESR date. The Released from Selected Reserve (RSR) date and reason will be provided by DOD on the member's VA REAP record. Only those members who separate for a service connected disability will retain chapter 1607 eligibility.

**i.** If the member previously used any other VA education benefit program, including Chapter 31, the amount of time training under the other program(s) must be entered on the 310 screen by the VCE. If more than 12 months have been used elsewhere, the amount of 1607 original entitlement will be adjusted by BDN. As with all VA education benefits, a member cannot use more than 48 months of combined entitlement under two or more programs. Prior VA Training types are found in [paragraph 5.05](#505).

**j.** The delimiting date, if applicable, will be calculated as 10 years after the S/C DOE entered by the VCE. The delimiting date may be extended if the claimant was prevented from training due to his or her disability. He or she must request an extension within one year of the delimiting date or within one year of the last day he or she was prevented from training, whichever is later. If the claimant is in school when the delimiting date is reached, benefits may be extended until the end of the term.

**k**. In no circumstance can a claimant use more than 36 months of entitlement under the chapter 1607. Entitlement is not extended to the end of the term in Chapter 1607. Original entitlement is 36 months under Chapter 1607 unless more than 12 months were used in another program.

 **5.08   Processing Changes in Eligibility Status**

---

There are no limits on the number of times a person can lose and reestablish eligibility under Chapter 1607. A member will lose their eligibility if they do not return to the component from which they were called to active duty. If called up from the SR, they must return to the SR. If called up from the IRR, they must return to the IRR, or they can retain eligibility by joining the SR, but if they leave the SR and join the IRR eligibility is terminated. The members do not have one year in which to reaffiliate to retain eligibility under Chapter 1607, however a member can be out for 90 days or less and still remain eligible under Chapter 1607. Bear in mind that DoD will determine and inform VA of any changes in eligibility, this is simply background information.

If a claimant leaves the reserves and DoD determines eligibility has been lost:

**a.** If the award is terminated but there isn't an enrollment to retroactively terminate, under the CORR command annotate the M21 screen message field to show the date of termination (RSR date). Document the TIMS file with a FLASH as well. Send PCGL Letter DIS 25.

**b**. If the award is terminated and there is an enrollment to retroactively terminate, use the STOP command and terminate the enrollment with reason code 99. BDN will generate the correct letter. The 310 is not accessible under the STOP command; the RSR date should be added to the message field on the 312 screen.

**c**. If the award is running and must be terminated, use the STOP command and reason code 99. Add the RSR date to the message field on the 312 screen. BDN will generate the correct letter.

**d**. If the claimant subsequently rejoins the reserves, is called up and serves a minimum of 90 days of continuous active duty service (or is released due to a service connected disability prior to that), therefore reestablishing eligibility under Chapter 1607, the 310 screen must be changed to show new eligibility (first capture the original 310 screen into the TIMS file for record purposes). Change the original EOD date to the new EOD date, change the RAD date if one exists, and change the DOE to the newer date of eligibility. Finally, remove the RSR date and reason. Upon ALT/ENT the 310 should now reflect an eligible status.

 **5.09  Processing Multiple Active Duty Call Ups**

---

If a claimant is in training during or after different call-up periods, multiple 310 screens may be necessary with a change to the 310 screen for each in order to generate correct rates on the 312 screen. Each change will require a separate award, each one authorized before the next change may be entered. Take one EP and all other actions should be done under a 930 series EP.

Generally, the 310 screen must be changed when it becomes necessary to show a change in duty status, a change in reserve components, or when a subsequent period qualifies the claimant for a higher rate.

**a.** If the claimant is off active duty and nothing else has changed due to a subsequent period of service (the claimant doesn't qualify for a higher percentage based on this additional period of active duty, for example, or the active duty status has not changed), do not change the 310 except to indicate the number of Additional Mobilization Periods (AMP). Process an enrollment if a VA Form 22-1999 is on file, or issue a COE (See [Example 1](#E1)).

**b.** If the claimant is back on duty with a subsequent call-up and is still due the same percentage, the 310 must be changed so BDN will accept charges and pay as in service. Change the EOD date to the newer period, remove a RAD date if one exists and change the IN SERVICE field to "Y". The TIMS file should be annotated with the periods of active duty as well. Process a VA Form 22-1999 is one is on file or issue a COE (See [Example 2](#E2)).

**c**. If the claimant's subsequent call-up is longer than the earlier one, qualifying the student for a higher rate of pay, the 310 screen must be changed to show the newer, longer period of active duty. Change the EOD and RAD (if applicable) to the subsequent call-up dates of active duty. Any other changes to the 310 screen that are necessary should be done as well. Process a VA Form 22-1999 if one is on file, or issue a COE (See [Example 3](#E3)).

**d.** If the reverse is true, the student qualified for 60% originally, but was called back up for a shorter period of active duty, and the student is on active duty, the 310 must be changed so that BDN will accept charges. Change the EOD date to reflect the new EOD so that it does not appear that she has been on duty continuously, remove the RAD date and indicate ON DUTY with a "Y". Since BDN will now interpret the active duty period to be a shorter period, the rates must be overridden with an M override to pay at the 60% rate, not the 40% rate.(See [Example 4](#E4)).

Example 1: A member served on active duty for 9 months. Once off duty she attends school and receives REAP benefits. Subsequently she is called up again by her unit and serves another 6 months. She goes back to school again once she is released from active duty. There is no need to change the 310 screen to reflect this second period of active duty **if she is off duty** when she attends school

Example 2: Same student, but she is on duty and in school during her subsequent call-up. The percentage due has not been affected. Change the 310 screen to reflect the newer EOD date, remove the RAD date and add a "Y" to the IN SERVICE field. The only reason to change the 310 in this instance is because she is on duty and must be paid tuition and fees, BDN must accept charges.

Example 3: Same student, but on the second call-up she serves for 14 months, now qualifying for 60%. The 310 must be changed. Change the original EOD date to the subsequent EOD date. If she is off duty add the new RAD date; if she is still on duty place a "Y" in the IN SERVICE field on the 310 screen. Now BDN will calculate her new rates correctly at 60%.

Example 4: Same student, but she was originally at 60% and the second active duty call-up only lasted 102 days, which would have qualified her for the 40% rate. If she is off duty there is no need to change the 310 screen to show the lesser period of service. If she is on active duty, remove the RAD date, add the "Y" in the IN SERVICE field and change the EOD date to reflect the new one so that BDN will not try to pay 80%, thinking she was on duty continuously from the original EOD. The rates must be overridden with the M override on the 312 screen to pay the correct rate of 60%, not 40%.

If the claimant established eligibility and is receiving benefits when he or she qualifies for a higher rate, BDN will automatically make those adjustments using reason code 7A.

 **SUBCHAPTER II.  411 SCREEN**

**5.10   Introduction**

---

The 411 screen for Chapter 1607 claims processing is basically similar to the 411 screen used in chapters 30 and 1606, many of the disallowance reasons are common to all three. All 01 disallowance codes are generated from the 310 screen, and are also operator enterable. Just as with 30 and 1606, disallowance reasons 16, 21, 22 and 29 do not generate letters. Disallowance reason 99 ("Left the Ready Reserves") can only be used as a STOP reason and is not available on the 411 screen.

Valid Next Screen selections are DEL, END, GAD, GAP, M01, M21 through M24, 310, 312, 317, 501, 601 S99 and RES. An entry of DEL will delete all entered disallowance reasons; an entry of RES will display the screen as it appeared before the last entry.      

**5.11   Accessing the 411 Screen**

---

For input: The 411 screen may be accessed directly from the Ready Screen or from the following screens while using the CADJ command: 30M, M01, M20, through M 23, 101, 310, 312, and 317.

For inquiry: The 411 screen is only available for inquiry purposes while an award action is pending authorization by using the PINQ or SINQ commands.      

**5.12   Updating the Master Record**

---

Disallowance reasons generated from the 310 screen will be displayed as "X" next to the appropriate reason. Entered items can be done using either "X" or "Y". Once authorized, the data will be stored in the chapter 1607 database.

 **5.14   USING the 411 Screen Display**

---

**a**. When an "N" is placed on the claimant's 310 screen in the field following "ELIG" a message will display "NO DOD ELIGIBILITY-GAP OR HIT ENTER TO DISALLOW". The next screen available is the 411 with Reason 01B ("NO DOD ELIGIBILITY") generated by BDN.

**b.** If the claimant is disallowed for any other reason, indicate "N" in the ELIG field and change the disallowance reason to the correct one on the 411 screen. For example, if the claimant was released due to a disability that DoD coded as not being service-connected, delete the "X" in front of reason 01B and check reason 06 "DISABILITY NOT DUE TO SERVICE". This is important because the BDN letters are generated based on the reason code selected by the VCE. It is possible to select more than one disallowance reason, however only the reason code with the lowest number will be stored in the system and will determine the letter generated by BDN. The use of multiple disallowance reasons has value only if the screen is captured and retained for record purposes.

**c**. If it is necessary to advise the claimant of more than one reason for his or her disallowance, suppress the system generated letter by entering a "Y" in the SUPPRESS LETTER" field and send a PCGL disallowance letter amended to address all issues.

**SUBCHAPTER III.  COE SCREEN**

 

**5.16   Introduction**

---

VCEs can process Certificates of Eligibility for Chapter 1607 claims by using the BDN COE screen. The Chapter 1607 COE screen is identical to the COE screen for Chapter 30.     

**5.17   Accessing the COE Screen**

---

For input:   The COE screen is accessed through the CADJ command from the READY Screen or by entering COE in the next screen field from the 310 screen.

For inquiry: The COE screen is accessed for inquiry from the READY screen under either SINQ or PINQ commands.
