**3.01 Transfer of Entitlement**

---

(Updated October 25, 2021)

**a.  Number of months transferable.** A service member (active duty or Selected Reserve, officer or enlisted) can transfer up to 36 months of his or her Post-9/11 GI Bill (chapter 33) entitlement to their eligible dependent(s). If a service member has used any benefits, he or she can transfer the remaining unused entitlement to their dependent(s).

**NOTE:** The Department of Defense (DoD) or Department of Homeland Security (DHS) can limit the number of months an individual may transfer.

**b. Rules of Eligibility as Determined by DoD.**

**NOTE 1:** This information is only for historical purposes.  Veterans Claims Examiners (VCEs) should NOT routinely review to ensure these rules are met. If in Veterans Information Solution (VIS), DoD has made the decision.

To transfer entitlement, the service member must meet one of the following criteria:

1. Have at least 6 years of service in the Armed Forces (active duty and/or Selected Reserve) and agree to serve 4 additional years in the Armed Forces.

2. Have at least 10 years of service in the Armed Forces (active duty and/or Selected Reserve), and

* Not be barred from committing to an additional 4 years, due to policy or by statute (law) and
* Agree to serve for the maximum amount of time allowed by such policy or statute

3.   Be or become eligible to retire between August 1, 2009, and August 1, 2012

* For those individuals eligible for retirement on August 1, 2009, no additional service is required
* For those individuals who had an approved retirement date after August 1, 2009, and before July 1, 2010, no additional service is required
* For those individuals eligible for retirement after August 1, 2009, and before August 1, 2010, 1 year of additional service after approval of transfer is required
* For those individuals eligible for retirement on or after August 1, 2010, and before August 1, 2011, 2 years of additional service after approval of transfer are required
* For those individuals eligible for retirement on or after August 1, 2011, and before August 1, 2012, 3 years of additional service after approval of transfer are required
* For those individuals eligible for retirement after August 1, 2012, 4 years of additional service after approval of transfer are required

4. Be a Purple Heart recipient. The issuance of the Purple Heart accounts for eligibility.  There is NO length of service requirement.

**c. Failure to Complete Required Service Obligation**. Dependents are not eligible for transferred entitlement if the service member fails to complete their obligated service in order to participate in the transferability program, unless the separation reason is due to a death, preexisting medical condition (EPTS), hardship (HDSP), physical or mental condition interfering with performance of duty (CIWD), disability (DIS), or reduction in force (RIF). (38 U.S.C. 3034(a), 3311(c)(4), 3319).  As stated in 3.01b Purple Heart is also an exception. DoD notified us that the “Sponsor’s Transfer Obligated Projected End Date” displayed in the Veterans Information System may be up to 7 days after the release from active duty (RAD) date. As such, Regional Processing Offices (RPO) should NOT deny or remove Transfer Of Entitlement (TOE) benefits for a dependent(s) when the “Sponsor’s Transfer obligated Projected End Date” is no more than 7 days after the RAD.

It is imperative VCEs confirm the service member has completed at least 10 years of service time (active/drilling) prior to the child’s first enrollment period.

**NOTE:** VA will treat all payments of educational assistance to dependents as overpayments if the service member does not complete the required service. Effective March 5, 2021, the amount of any transferred entitlement that has been used as of the date of such failure shall be treated as an overpayment of education assistance charged to the **transferor** and is subject to collection by VA.

**d. Statute to Transfer Entitlement.**  The statute creating the Post-9/11 GI Bill mandates requests to transfer entitlement to spouses or dependents must be made while the individual is in the Armed Forces. The decision to approve or deny such requests is solely within the purview of the Secretary of Defense.  VA determines eligibility for transferred Post-9/11 GI Bill benefits based on information provided by the DoD.  As of August 1, 2018, under the Harry W. Colmery Veterans Educational Assistance Act, if a designated dependent dies  the veteran may designate a new dependent, notwithstanding the fact that the veteran is no longer a member of the Armed Forces.  Additionally, if the service member or Veteran dies, a designated dependent may transfer any unused transferred benefits to another designated dependent of the deceased (the “another designated dependent” must have already been designated by the deceased – the transferring designated dependent does not have the authority to add a new dependent).

**NOTE:** DoD will no longer implement the 12 January 2020 Transfer of Education Benefits (TEB) 6 to 16 Year TEB policy (DoD Instruction (Dodi) 1341.3, published on 12 Jul 2018) because of a law change contained in the Fiscal Year 2020 (FY 20) National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA). Specifically, Section 578 of the FY 20 NDAA restricts the Secretary of Defense from prescribing any regulation that would "provide for a limitation on eligibility to transfer unused" Post 9/11 GI Bill Education Benefits "to family members based on a maximum number of years of service in the Armed Forces".

**e. Processing Issues.** VCEs should always review VIS as the source for TOE claims.  If it appears the obligation has not been met, development may be required to the DoD Point of Contact (POC).  VCEs should follow local RPO procedures to develop to DoD POCs for any family member's claim for transferability if the information is not in VIS, unless there is no way the individual would be eligible for transferability.

Capturing VIS to support the decision is required.

 Examples include, but are not limited to:

      1) Service member has less than five years’ service in Armed Forces (active duty and/or Selected Reserve) OR

2) Service member separated from Armed Forces (active duty and/or Selected Reserve) before August 1, 2009, and not since served as a member of the Armed Forces (active duty and/or Selected Reserve)

3) If the applicant requests consideration under TOE (submits VA Form 22-1990e), and is not listed as a dependent in VIS, the VCE can immediately deny the claim for the appropriate disallowance reason.

**3.02 Approved Transfer of Entitlement**

---

**a.** If the service member is approved to transfer unused education benefits under the ToE program, benefits can be transferred to:

* A spouse
* One or more children or
* Any combination

**b.** Children are defined as natural children, stepchildren (the children of your current spouse), and pre-adoptive and adopted children. Legal wards and foster children are not eligible to receive transferred benefits.

**c.** Service members must transfer entitlement to an eligible dependent before they leave the Armed Forces. Dependents must be enrolled in the Defense Eligibility Enrollment Reporting System (DEERS) and be eligible for benefits at the time of transfer to receive educational benefits.

**3.03 Elections and Transfer of Entitlement**

---

**a.** By regulation ([38 C.F.R. § 21.9520(c)(2)](https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=1&SID=5f6d7adbb6a68e4712625f2c90e8610b&ty=HTML&h=L&mc=true&r=SECTION&n=se38.2.21_19520)), a request for approval to transfer entitlement by a Servicemember constitutes an irrevocable election to receive benefits under the Post-9/11 GI Bill. The transfer request date constitutes the effective date of the election. Although the Servicemember must acknowledge that an election is being made, the Transfers of Education (TEB) website does not allow the Servicemember to specify the benefit being relinquished; therefore, in cases where there is eligibility for multiple benefits, further development is needed in order to determine which benefit the Servicemember wishes to relinquish.

**b.** Claims examiners must review transfer approval information displayed in the Veterans Information Solution (VIS) when determining the eligibility of the Servicemember or Veteran for benefits under the Post-9/11 GI Bill *or under one of the relinquishable programs (i.e. MGIB – AD, MGIB – SR, or REAP)*.

**c.** When an irrevocable election is submitted, the effective date of the election will be the earlier of either an application for Chapter 33 that was previously received and submitted by the Transferor or the “Transfer Request Date as displayed in VIS.

**3.04 Development**

---

**a.** Because the TEB website does not allow a Servicemember to choose the benefit he/she is relinquishing, you must develop for the specific benefit relinquished if the Servicemember is eligible for 2 (or all 3) of the relinquishable programs. If the claimant is only eligible for one such program, then no additional development is required. If the Transferor does not relinquish a benefit in a timely manner (30 days or less) you may proceed with a Failure-to-Prosecute and deny benefits until a proper election is received.

**b.** However, if a Transferor elects to relinquish a benefit they are **not** eligible for, send a new development letter that specifies benefits they are eligible for and continue suspending within the 30-day window. If no response is received, proceed with a Failure-to-Prosecute letter.

**3.05 Rights and Revocations**

---

**a.** Service members or veterans may revoke or modify ToE at any time.

* If a child is designated as a “transferee” and he or she later gets married, the marriage will not affect eligibility to receive the educational benefit
* If a spouse is designated as a “transferee” but then gets divorced, the divorce will not affect eligibility to receive educational benefits unless the transferor revokes the designation

**b.** Inquiries from Veterans who believe they were not properly informed of the opportunity to transfer entitlement while still in service should be told that they can request a correction of military records to allow for transferability by submitting a DD Form 149, “Application for Correction of Military Record Under the Provisions of [Title 10, U.S.C, § 1552](http://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=(title:10 section:1552 edition:prelim) OR (granuleid:USC-prelim-title10-section1552)&f=treesort&edition=prelim&num=0&jumpTo=true)” to their service component’s Board for Correction of Military Records. The Boards for Correction of Military Records have the mission to correct errors in, or remove injustices from, military records.

Determinations on the correction of military records to allow transfer of entitlement will be made on a case-by-case basis. The DD Form 149, complete with instructions and mailing addresses for each military branch, is available at [www.dtic.mil/whs/directives/infomgt/forms/eforms/dd0149.pdf](http://www.dtic.mil/whs/directives/infomgt/forms/eforms/dd0149.pdf "blocked::http:").

 **3.06 Dependents**

---

**(Updated October 25, 2021)**

To receive transferred entitlement, a dependent (i.e. transferee) must be enrolled in DEERS and the transferor must be eligible for benefits at the time of transfer. VCEs should always review the Veterans Information Solution (VIS) as the source for TOE claims. As indicated in 3.01 of this sub-chapter, recipients of a Purple Heart award do not need to meet the time in service requirements. Dependents may start to use the benefit immediately.

**a. Spouse**

* May start to use the benefit only after the individual making the transfer has completed 6 years of service in the Armed Forces
* May start immediately if service member is a Purple Heart recipient.
* May use the benefit while the service member remains in the Armed Forces, or after the service member’s separation from active duty
* Effective October 1, 2011 is eligible for books and supplies stipend while the service member is serving on active duty
* Is not eligible for the monthly housing allowance while the service member is serving on active duty
* Period of eligibility is same as service member, unless an earlier or revoke date is provided on VIS

**b. Child**

* May start to use the benefit only after the individual making the transfer has completed at least 10 years of service in the Armed Forces unless separated early for a qualifying reason (Refer to 3.01 of this part)
* May start immediately if the service member is a Purple Heart recipient
* May use the benefit while the service member remains in the Armed Forces, or after the service member’s separation from active duty
* May not use the benefit until he or she has earned a secondary school diploma (or equivalency certificate) or has reached 18 years of age
* Is entitled to the monthly housing allowance as well as the books and supplies stipend, even if the eligible individual is on active duty
* Period of eligibility is limited to age 26, unless an earlier end date or revoke date is provided on VIS

**3.07 Delimiting Date Extension**

---

**a.** Benefits are not payable on or after the delimiting date. [Public Law 111-377](https://www.congress.gov/111/plaws/publ377/PLAW-111publ377.pdf) allows an extension to a delimiting date for individuals who acted as the primary caregiver for a veteran or member of the Armed Forces and who, as a result, are prevented from pursuing a program of education on or after August 1, 2011.  

**b. Primary Care Providers of Seriously Injured Veterans**

To qualify for an extension, an individual must be:

* Eligible under the Montgomery GI Bill (chapter 30)
* An eligible child under the Survivors’ and Dependents’ Educational Assistance program (chapter 35) or
* An eligible child receiving Transfer of Entitlement (ToE) benefits under the Post-9/11 GI Bill (chapter 33)

Additionally, the individual must:

* Have a delimiting date on or after August 1, 2011
* Be currently serving or have served as a primary caregiver under section 1720G(a) of title 38 during the eligibility period (before delimiting date) and
* Have been prevented from training or suspended pursuit of a program of education on or after August 1, 2011

**Scenario**

Susan was enrolled in an educational institution and was receiving benefits under chapter 33 ToE. Her father was a disabled Veteran and was in need of personal care. The Veterans Health Administration (VHA) designated her as a primary caregiver. She terminated her attendance to care for her father; however, her delimiting date is approaching. She will need to apply for an extension in order to maintain her eligibility.

**3.08 Fry Scholarship**

---

**a. Overview.** The Post-9/11 GI Bill was amended to include the Marine Gunnery Sergeant John David Fry Scholarship (Fry Scholarship). The Fry Scholarship was signed into law on June 24, 2009, under the provisions of P.L. 111-32 and provides Post-9/11 GI Billbenefits to the children and surviving spouses of service members who died in the line of duty (LOD) after September 10, 2001. Eligible beneficiaries attending school under this provision may receive up to 36 months of benefits at the 100% benefit level. Public Law  116-315 Section 1002 expanded eligibility criteria effective for training beginning on or after August 1, 2021. Section 1002 expands the eligibility for the Fry Scholarship by adding coverage for a child or spouse of either:

* An individual who, on or after September 11, 2001, dies in line of duty while serving on duty other than active duty as a member of the Armed Forces.
* A member of the Selected Reserve who dies on or after September 11, 2001, while a member of the Selected Reserve from a service-connected disability.

Fry Scholarship beneficiaries may receive the following benefit payments:

* Full tuition and fees paid directly to the school for all public school in-state students. For those students attending private or foreign schools, tuition and fees are capped at a statutory maximum amount per academic year.
* A monthly housing allowance.
* A books and supplies stipend.
* Yellow Ribbon program which become effective August 1, 2018.

**b.  Eligibility**

1) Children of active duty members of the Armed Forces who died in the line of duty on or after September 11, 2001, are eligible for this benefit.

* + If the child became eligible before January 1, 2013, eligibility ends on the child’s 33rd birthday.
  + There is no time limit if the child became eligible on or after January 1, 2013.
  + A child may begin an approved program of education before the age of 18 if they graduated high school.
  + A child’s marital status has no effect on eligibility.

2) Surviving spouses of active duty members of the Armed Forces who died in the line of duty on or after September 11, 2001, are eligible for this benefit.

* A surviving spouse can receive benefits for terms beginning on or after   January 1, 2015.
* A surviving spouse has no time limitation to use benefits.

**NOTES:**

* Eligibility under the Fry Scholarship provision of the Post-9/11 GI Bill (chapter 33) is based solely on the Veteran’s service. The dependent’s character of discharge from their own service will not affect their eligibility under the Fry Scholarship.
* Dependents will not receive the monthly housing allowance while on active duty.
* Dependents are not limited to 36 months under chapter 33 if eligible under the Fry Scholarship provision and chapter 33 based on their own service; they may use (not concurrently) up to 72 months of benefits, as long as no non-chapter 33 benefits are used. If non-chapter 33 benefits are used, their entitlement under chapter 33 would be limited by the 48-month rule (refer to Carr v. Wilkie) or 81-month rule, as applicable.
* Dependents are not limited to 36 months under the Fry Scholarship provision due to multiple events (e.g. death of two parents or death of one spouse and one parent). They may use (not concurrently) up to 36 months of benefits under each event, as long as no non-chapter 33 benefits are used.  If non-chapter 33 benefits are used, their entitlement from each event is limited by the 48-month rule (refer to Carr v. Wilkie) or 81-month rule, as applicable.
* Dependents may receive additional entitlement under the Transfer of Entitlement (TOE) provision of chapter 33. TOE entitlement is not impacted by the 48-month rule (refer to Carr v. Wilkie) or 81-month rule, as applicable.
* Dependents who are eligible for a kicker under Montgomery GI Bill Active Duty (MGIB-AD) or Montgomery GI Bill Selected Reserve (MGIB-SR) must relinquish eligibility under the respective program to receive kicker benefits under the Fry Scholarship provision. The amount of kicker entitlement under the Fry Scholarship is limited to (1) the amount of unused kicker entitlement or, (2) if MGIB is relinquished, the amount of MGIB entitlement remaining.
* If entitlement exhausts under the Fry Scholarship during a term, VA is authorized to extend benefits.
  + Semester/Quarter Hour-based Program.  May extend benefits to end of term if operated on a standard basis. If operated on a non-standard basis, benefits may be extended to the end of the term or maximum length of a standard semester or quarter (as applicable), whichever is less.
  + Clock Hour-based Program.  May extend benefits up to 12 weeks or to the end of the term, whichever occurs first, provided majority of course has been completed.
* As of January 2, 2025, a surviving spouse does not lose eligibility upon remarriage. Denials or discontinuance of benefits solely due to remarriage should not be issued on or after this date.

**Bar to Duplication of Benefits:** A child entitled to both the Fry Scholarship and chapter 35 must choose between the two. Similarly, spouses may not receive assistance under the Fry Scholarship and chapter 35 but must make an irrevocable election to relinquish one of those benefits in order to establish eligibility under the elected benefit.  
**Exception:** Section 3322(h) barring entitlement to both the Fry Scholarship and chapter 35 based on the same event or period of service was added by section 111 of PL 111-377 with an effective date of August 1, 2011.  VA has always interpreted this provision and its effective date to mean the bar to duplication is not applicable to deaths occurring prior to August 1, 2011 and will consistently apply this rule to those now eligible based upon section 1002 of PL 116-315.  
If the beneficiary has already used chapter 35 benefits based on the death but the death did not qualify for Fry Scholarship prior to section 1002, then the beneficiary has not yet made an election to apply that same event or period of service to either Fry scholarship or chapter 35 because, prior to section 1002, that election was not applicable.  In these situations, VA will not bar the person, under 3322(h), from now using the death to qualify for Fry scholarship (essentially converting from chapter 35 to Fry Scholarship).  The beneficiary will now need to elect Fry scholarship in lieu of chapter 35 and, from that point forward, the 3322(h) bar to duplication will apply.  The beneficiary’s entitlement to Fry scholarship benefits will be limited by the limitation on aggregate benefits in 38 U.S.C. 3695 (81-month/48-month rule).

1) Eligibility Review.

a. Ensure chapter 33 Fry Scholarship or chapter 35 Survivors' and Dependents' Education Assistance (DEA) election was completed on VA Form 22-5490. (See Bar to Duplication of Benefits Section above)

b. Qualifying service period status includes a death on or after September 11, 2001 which occurred in one of three ways:

1. 1. 1. **In the line of duty (LOD) while on active duty.**

1) Verify LOD on active duty in the “Line of Duty Death” section in the Veterans Information System (VIS) on the “Education” tab (“Active Duty Service Periods” section).

a) Department of Defense (DoD) reports death on active duty to VA which displays under this section.

**NOTE**: VA presumes death on active duty is LOD unless other evidence exists indicating the death was not LOD.

 

1. 1. 2. **In the LOD while serving on duty other than active duty as a member of the Armed Forces.** (May be *paid only for terms beginning August 1, 2021 or later*.)

1. Accept official documentation from military service departments that clearly state the member **died in the line of duty** OR

2. When service documentation is not available, the VCE must review VIS to determine if the Veteran died during a period while in an active or reserve component of the Armed Forces.

a) If the death occurred while not in service, deny the claim.

b) If the death occurred while in service, develop to the service component to verify if the death was in the line of duty either on active duty or on duty other than active duty.

Once response received, either process as eligible or issue disallowance. 

1. 1. 3. **Due to a service-connected disability while a member of the Selected Reserve.** (May be *paid only for terms beginning August 1, 2021 or later*.)

1. Review VIS – “Military History” tab – Reserve Component Codes (RCC) column (“National Guard & Reserve Service Periods” section) to determine if death occurred during a period while in the Selected Reserves. If not, VCE should develop with service component when service information is unclear.

      a) Death determined as a result of a service-connected disability.

i. Review Veterans Service Center (VSC) records for rating. See M22-4 [Part 7: Chapter 1](https://vaww.vrm.km.va.gov/system/templates/selfservice/va_kanew/help/agent/locale/en-US/portal/554400000001048/content/554400000073664/Part-7-Chapter-1-General) and [Part 7: Chapter 3](https://vaww.vrm.km.va.gov/system/templates/selfservice/va_kanew/help/agent/locale/en-US/portal/554400000001048/content/554400000073695/Part-7-Chapter-3-Eligibility-Issues) for in-depth guidance for verifying and developing for a service-connected death.

ii. Accept rating when provided by the beneficiary.

2. Deny claim if the death did not occur while the Veteran was in the Selected Reserves or if the Veteran’s death was not rated service-connected.

 

2) Process the claim in DGI by selecting “FRY” as the entitlement source when creating the work product. See [M22-4, Part 12, Chapter 8](https://vaww.vrm.km.va.gov/system/templates/selfservice/va_kanew/help/agent/locale/en-US/portal/554400000001048/content/554400000078109/8-Fry-Scholarship-Claim) for step-by-step directions on how to complete a claim in DGI for a child/spouse applying for the Fry Scholarship.  

a. VCEs should deny claims when:

1. 1. 1. 1. 1. 1. The beneficiary is not a child or spouse of a deceased Veteran.
               2. The Veteran is not deceased.
               3. Death occurred prior to September 11, 2001.
               4. The Veteran’s death did not occur during a qualifying service period status.

* Veterans Information Screen (VIS) showing Veteran died in LOD

2) Dependency evidence. VCEs must ensure the proof of dependency to the surviving spouse or child of the service member is captured into the TIMS folder.  Examples of acceptable evidence are:

* Existing Benefits Delivery Network (BDN) M21 screen from Dependents Education Assistance (DEA or chapter 35) master record.  NOTE: This screen must show the established dependency.

* Birth certificate confirming relationship to service member.
* Marriage certificate confirming relationship to service member.
* Dependent listed in SHARE DIC record in their name or is shown as a dependent listed in Veteran’s VIS record, under Transfer of Entitlement (TOE) information (foster/ward status is not acceptable for Fry purposes), or VA Form 21-686c, Application Request to Add and/or Remove Dependents

For a child, develop for a birth certificate or a VA Form 21-686c, signed by the surviving spouse. For a spouse, develop for a VA Form 21-686c self-certifying married to Veteran up until time of death. 

Allow 30 days for development, if unable to establish dependency within the development period, the VCE may disallow the claim due to dependency not established. VCE should ensure proper disallowance letter is sent with appellate rights.

**d.  Procedures for Fry Scholarship claims involving Survivors’ and Dependents’ Educational Assistance (DEA or chapter 35)**

1) Applications.  A claim must be established using VA Form 22-5490. VCEs must determine whether the beneficiary is applying for Fry Scholarship benefits of a service member who died in LOD and if dependency can be established. If information on the VA Form 22-5490 is unclear or incomplete, VCEs may need to develop for additional information for an election. 

2) Elections.

A. Child. If the service member’s death occurred on or after August 1, 2011, the VCE must verify the child has made an irrevocable election between DEA and the Fry Scholarship using VA Form 22-5490.

As stated in [chapter 3, 3.10,](https://vaww.vrm.km.va.gov/system/templates/selfservice/va_kanew/help/agent/locale/en-US/portal/554400000001048/content/554400000073583/Part%203:%20Chapter%203%20-%20Processing%20Applications%20for%20Benefits#310), **Point of Irrevocability** **,** the issuance of the first payment makes this election irrevocable. A claimant may make a change in the election of benefits after a Certification of Eligibility (COE) has been issued provided NO payment has been released.

         B. Spouses. Eligibility for surviving spouses under the Fry Scholarship has been modified four times**.**

(1) On August 7, 2014, The Veterans Access, Choice, and Accountability Act of 2014 (Choice Act) was signed into law. Section 701 of the Choice Act established eligibility for surviving spouses under the Fry Scholarship provisions of the Post-9/11 GI Bill.  However, because the Choice Act provisions did not go in effect until January 1, 2015, some surviving spouses reached their delimiting date (last day of eligibility), as early as 2016. Prior to the Choice Act, eligibility for the Fry Scholarship was previously limited to the children of active duty service members who died in LOD. 

(2) On December 16, 2016, the Veterans Health Care and Benefits Improvement Act of 2016 (P.L. 114-315, section 401) amended Fry Scholarship for surviving spouses. This provision of the Fry Scholarship provided a delimiting date extension for beneficiaries utilizing the benefit of service members who died in the LOD between September 11, 2001, and December 31, 2005. This provision offered eligible spouses these options to help complete their educational goals: 

i. Up to an additional four years of eligibility under the Fry Scholarship.               

ii. The option to transfer from DEA to the Fry Scholarship.     

(3) On August 16, 2017, the passage of the Harry W. Colmery Veterans Educational Assistance Act of 2017 allowed time limit restrictions to be removed under section 112 for all spouse Fry Scholarship recipients.  This change effectively nullified delimiting date extensions for those impacted by section 401 of P.L. 114-315 for benefit usage on or after August 16, 2017. 

(4) On January 2, 2025, section 202 of the Senator Elizabeth Dole 21st Century Veterans Healthcare and Benefits Improvement Act removed the statutory language relating to delimiting dates for Fry spouses, including the discontinuance of benefits as of the date of a surviving spouse’s remarriage. Section 112 of the Colmery Act previously removed delimiting dates for all surviving spouses which had not re-married, by virtue of all Fry Spouses first becoming eligible for benefits on or after January 1, 2013.                                                                                                              

C. Election Issues. 

(1) A surviving spouse who is eligible for benefits under both DEA and Fry Scholarship must make an irrevocable election in writing as to which benefit, he or she wishes to receive, for any and all quarters, semesters, or terms, beginning on or after January 1, 2015. The eligibility for DEA and the Fry Scholarship need not be based on the same event (i.e. death of a spouse in LOD). Fry Scholarship benefits for a spouse will not be paid for terms that begin prior to, or span, January 1, 2015.

As a reminder, 38 United States Code (U.S.C.) 3311(f)(3) is not a bar to future DEA eligibility when a surviving spouse who elected eligibility for Fry Scholarship benefits (over DEA), loses Fry Scholarship eligibility (due to remarriage), and then regains eligibility for DEA based on a subsequent marriage. Effective April 17, 2019, any applications for DEA made by a spouse who had previously utilized Fry Scholarship benefits, but has remarried another qualifying Veteran, should be considered potentially approvable.  A spouse may regain eligibility for DEA based on a second distinct qualifying event.

(2) A child whose parent died prior to August 1, 2011, may still be eligible for both benefits, but he/she may only use one program at a time, and combined benefits are capped at a total of 81 months of full-time training.

**NOTE:** If a beneficiary has not made an election when required, the VCE must develop for an election using the modified DEV1 letter in the Personal Computer-Generated Letters (PCGL). ## e. The 81-Month Rule. Beneficiaries who have used chapter 35 prior to electing Fry may be entitled up to 81 months of benefits combined from more than one benefit [38 USC 3695](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=(title:38%20section:3695%20edition:prelim)%20OR%20(granuleid:USC-prelim-title38-section3695)&f=treesort&edition=prelim&num=0&jumpTo=true)().  Individuals who use only non-chapter 35 benefits cannot exceed 48 months of entitlement combined when using more than one benefit. The 81-month provision does not apply to individuals who have exhausted 48 months of benefits prior to October 1, 2013.

The chapter 33 Processing System (DGI) contains functionality for this.  When establishing eligibility, the VCE should enter all prior used entitlement into DGI.  When entering chapter 35 entitlement, VCEs should check the 81-month rule eligible checkbox to enable the system functionality when appropriate to properly determine entitlement.

**f.  Children in receipt of DIC.** P.L. 111-377, section 111(b), specifically bars duplicate receipt of Fry Scholarship and DIC benefits effective August 1, 2011 for a child over the age of 18 years old.  Similar to DEA, the VCE must notify the Pension Management Center (PMC) when a child in receipt of DIC has been issued a new award under the Fry Scholarship.  

Regional Processing Offices (RPOs) must inform the PMCs when a Fry Scholarship award has been created AND a DIC award is active, indicating the same child is receiving both benefits. The VCE should complete and send the [Notice of Removal of Dependent Due to Education Award](https://dvagov.sharepoint.com/:w:/r/sites/VBAEDUSvc/225/225A/Presentations/Dependency%20Update%20Form%20(Fillable).docx?d=w09c3978d968d4486bc5c20f72b26b24e&csf=1&web=1&e=aaqFTZ)  template to the Centralized Mail Portal so the appropriate action can be taken on the DIC claim. 

**NOTE:** The election of DEA or Fry Scholarship does not affect eligibility for DIC benefits for spouses 

**g.  Awarding Benefits for Fry Scholarship Recipients Under 18 Years Old.** The VCE must review TIMS and VA Form 22-5490 for information reported about high school completion when a school certifies enrollments which begin prior to the child’s 18th birthday as DGI will not automatically generate payments. If a high school graduation date is on file, the date should be entered in the “High School Graduation or Equivalent” field in the “Miscellaneous” section on the Enrollment screen.   

**NOTE 1:** **Prior to DGI 6.0, a dummied date of birth was required to pay Fry children prior to their 18th birthday. This workaround is no longer required as of DGI 6.0. Records which previously had this workaround applied should have the date of birth corrected in DGI and the high school graduation date added in a new Work Product. If automation was previously stopped solely due to this workaround, the Stop Automation flag should be removed after correction and the record should be re-associated with VADIR.**

**NOTE 2:** If a child applies for Fry Scholarship before age 18 AND has not graduated from high school, they are not eligible for payment due to the lack of high school graduation.  In those unique situations, allow DGI to issue the Certificate of Eligibility (COE) letter but edit it to state “Benefits may not be paid prior to age 18 unless the child has completed high school or the equivalent.” Since the child is under age 18, be sure to address the letter to Guardian of the child.
