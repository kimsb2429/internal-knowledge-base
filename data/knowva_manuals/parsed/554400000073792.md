# Sub-Chapter 7. National Testing Program

**Sub-Chapter 7. National Testing Program**

Service members, veterans, and their dependents may be reimbursed for national tests through the Post-9/11 GI Bill.

**a. Benefit Payment Details**

VA will reimburse a student for required test fees. But VA can’t reimburse a person for any optional costs related to the testing process.

VA may reimburse for the following test fees:

* Registration fees
* Fees for specialized tests
* Administrative fees

VA *cannot*reimburse:

* Fees to take pre-tests
* Fees to receive scores quickly
* Other costs or fees for optional items not required to take an approved test

**b. Reimbursement**

To be reimbursed for a national test, an individual must the following information to VA after the test has been taken:

* A copy of the individual’s test results
* A receipt for proof of payment
* A completed VA Form 22-0810 providing:
  + - Name of the test
    - Name of the organization offering the test
    - Date the test was taken
    - Cost of test

**c. Approved Tests**

Examples of approved tests include the following: 

* SAT (SAT Reasoning Test)
* LSAT (Law School Admission Test)
* GRE (Graduate Record Exam)
* GMAT (Graduate Management Admission Test)
* AP (Advanced Placement Exams)
* CLEP (College-Level Examination Program)
* ACT (American College Testing program)
* DAT (Dental Admission Test)
* MAT (Miller Analogies Test)
* MCAT (Medical College Admission Test)
* OAT (Optometry Admission Test)
* PCAT (Pharmacy College Admission Test)
* TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language)
* DSST (DANTES Subject Standardized Tests)
* ECE (Excelsior College Examinations)

A comprehensive list of all approved National Tests can be found in WEAMS.

 **d. Entitlement Charge**

Under the Post-9/11 GI Bill, entitlement is deducted based on the cost of the exam divided by a statutorily mandated amount for the academic year in which the test is taken. The statutorily mandated amount for the academic year can be found on the [Post-9/11 GI Bill (Chapter 33) rates page](https://www.va.gov/education/benefit-rates/post-9-11-gi-bill-rates/).
