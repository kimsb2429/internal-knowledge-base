# Sub-Chapter 10. Tutorial Assistance Program

**Sub-Chapter 10. Tutorial Assistance Program**

An individual who is eligible to receive benefits under chapter 33 may receive additional assistance for tutorial services. VA will pay for this assistance if tutoring is necessary for the student to successfully complete his or her program of education.  Tutorial assistance helps students pay for necessary tutoring and is a supplement to the student’s regular education benefit.

**a. Benefit Payment Details**

The monthly rate of tutorial assistance is the lesser of the cost of tutoring or $100. The maximum amount payable for tutorial assistance is $1,200.

**b. Requirements**

All of the following criteria must be met for a student to be eligible for tutorial assistance.

* The student must be in a post secondary program ½-time or more. For Chapter 33, the Rate of Pursuit must be at least .50.

* The student must have a deficiency in a course that is part of his or her approved program.

* The student must be enrolled in the course during the quarter, semester, or term in which the tutoring is received for the course. Tutoring may not occur between quarters or semesters.

**c. Application**

The VA student, tutor, School Certifying Official (SCO), and professor/instructor [when tutorial assistance under the Post-9/11 GI Bill] must complete a [VA Form 22-1990t, *Application and Enrollment Certification for Individualized Tutorial Assistance*](https://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/20/cio/20s5/forms/VBA-22-1990t-ARE.pdf).

**d. Certifications**

The table below identifies each individual and information certified to VA using the [VAF 22-1990t](https://vbaw.vba.va.gov/bl/20/cio/20s5/forms/VBA-22-1990t-ARE.pdf).

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Who is Responsible?** | **Certifies That** |
| VA student [applicant] | General information is completed by the applicant. |
| Tutor | Gave the applicant individualized tutorial assistance as shown on the VAF 22-1990t. |
| The charges to the applicant shown on the VAF 22-1990t are correct. |
| Tutor and applicant are not close relatives (e.g., spouse, parent, child, brother, sister). |
| SCO | The individualized tutorial assistance for the unit subject or subjects shown was required for the satisfactory pursuit of the student's approved program. |
| The tutor is qualified to conduct individualized tutorial assistance. |
| The charges do not exceed the customary charges for other students who receive the same tutorial assistance. |
| Professor or instructor of the course for which tutoring was necessary [additional certification required to receive tutorial assistance under the Post-9/11 GI Bill (chapter 33)] | Tutorial assistance benefits are essential to correct a deficiency of this student in the course. |
| That the course is required as part of, or is prerequisite or indispensable to the satisfactory pursuit of, an approved program of education. |
