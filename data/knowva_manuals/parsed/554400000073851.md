**9.01 General**

The Chapter 33 Processing System (formerly known as the Long Term Solution, now referred to as DGI) automatically generates letters when possible. The Letter Information Screen will indicate which letter(s) was/were able to generate as a result of the data entered into the current work product. This screen may also have prompts for the user to enter additional information, based on the letter that is being generated. A Comments section displays on the screen.  Entering a comment is not required, but anything entered will display in the Comments section of the Work Product Summary Screen (see [Sub-Chapter 10. Work Product Summary LTS Screen](https://eg5813aprl-int-nlb.egainstz.cloud/system/ws/v11/ss/article/554400000207638?portalId=554400000001018&usertype=customer&\$lang=en-us&)) after the user clicks the “Next” button.

If a Power of Attorney (POA) is listed on the Claimant BIO screen, letters produced for that beneficiary will generate with a carbon copy (cc) line for the POA. A copy of the letter will also be generated. When letters are automatically sent to the print vendor for mailing, the POA copy of the letter will be shipped to the authoring Regional Processing Office (RPO) for distribution.

The “Station Number” indicated on the Claimant BIO Screen will determine the return address displayed on the top of system generated letters. If the beneficiary is under 18 years of age, their letters will generate using the information entered in the “Parent/Guardian Information” section of the Claimant BIO Screen.

DGI cannot generate letters for the Veterans Technology Education Courses (VET TEC) benefit, and no letters should be produced when VET TEC is the benefit source. System-generated letters are also filtered out of the following work product types: FETImport, WorkProductZero, Reconciliation.

The Work Product Summary screen allows the user to suppress system-generated letters and upload modified letters. More information can be found in [Sub-Chapter 10. Work Product Summary LTS Screen](https://eg5813aprl-int-nlb.egainstz.cloud/system/ws/v11/ss/article/554400000207638?portalId=554400000001018&usertype=customer&\$lang=en-us&)[.](https://vaww.vrm.km.va.gov/system/templates/selfservice/va_kanew/help/agent/locale/en-US/portal/554400000001048/content/554400000207638/Sub-Chapter-10-Work-Product-Summary-LTS-Screen)

**9.02 Certificate of Eligibility (COE)**

Generally, a COE will generate when the beneficiary is eligible and no award actions, payments and/or debts, result in the current work product. This may include scenarios where an enrollment or amendment has been added to DGI without a payment or debt being scheduled or created.  Example: Flight or Correspondence course was entered without any certs. When this occurs, the COE letter will contain a paragraph explaining that the program is approved, but no payments have been made because nothing is due. An example of the system-generated COE is attached below.

When the alternative election indicator is checked on the Entitlement and Kickers Screen, the system-generated COE letter will include a paragraph which explains that an alternative election was made. This paragraph will only show in the letter for the work product when the checkbox has been checked.

If a beneficiary has transferred entitlement to their dependent(s) under the Transfer of Entitlement (TOE) program, the COE letter will include a paragraph explaining that the remaining entitlement reflected in the letter has been affected by the transfer. Unless there have been manual entitlement redistributions, the system-generated COE letter should be used as notification to a service member or veteran when their dependent applies for TOE benefits. The letter should not be suppressed unless other modifications are necessary.

When a COE generates, the Letters Information Screen will display “AWARD 1 LETTER – COE AND/OR BAD COURSE.” The COE letter’s content changes based on the entries into the screen. If the training is not approved, the letter will give the student information about the approval process for their school or program and how to pick a different program.

Is Course Approved:** This section contains two radio buttons for “Yes” or “No.” The default selection is “Yes”, so when the school and course is approved, no additional information is necessary. **c.** The system uses the selected option from the previous section when determining which type of approval information to display in the letter. If the course dates are known, selecting “Yes” in this field will allow the user to enter the dates in the **Course Dates** section. Entering these dates will display them in the letter.

**f. SAA State:** This field is mandatory.

**9.03 Denial**

Generally, a denial letter will generate when the beneficiary is not eligible for benefits. An example of the system-generated denial letter is attached below.

A denial letter will generate when working an original claim and the beneficiary is beyond their delimiting date or has previously used more than 48 months of benefits under other education programs.

The denial letter may not generate when the beneficiary is no longer eligible because they are past their delimiting date or have no remaining entitlement. When processing this type of claim, the user should follow the guidance provided in [Part 2: Chapter 7- Education Letters](https://eg5813aprl-int-nlb.egainstz.cloud/system/ws/v11/ss/article/554400000072899?portalId=554400000001018&usertype=customer&\$lang=en-us&).

When a denial generates, the Letters Information Screen will display “DISALLOWANCE 17 LETTER – BENEFIT NOT INDICATED.” No entry can be made to this screen, so any changes to the denial letter must be done manually.

**9.04 Award 3**

Generally, an Award 3 letter generates when positive net awards (payments) are created during the current work product. It will continue to appear as long as there are payments scheduled. The letter explains the payments being issued, including those being sent directly to the school. An example of the system-generated Award 3 letter is attached below.

The paragraphs in the Award 3 letter change based on the type of training that is being paid. It will not generate or contain information about manual awards, the Montgomery GI Bill – Active Duty (Chapter 30) refund, rural relocation benefits, or tutorial assistance. During the annual batch update work product, used to pay the new rates for the academic year, the Award 3 letter will have an alternative introduction paragraph explaining that the beneficiary's rates are being updated.

When an Award 3 generates, the Letters Information Screen will display “AWARD 3 LETTER – POST-9/11 AWARD.” No entry can be made to this screen, so any changes to the letter must be done manually.

![Award 3 Screen Example](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/Award%203%20screen.png)

**9.05 Adverse Action**

The Adverse Action letter is a general-purpose debt letter. It generates when overpayments are created during the current work product for books and supplies stipend, monthly housing allowance, and/or kicker unless these overpayments are caused by amendments that require mitigating circumstances. An example of the system-generated Adverse Action letter is attached below.

The paragraphs in the Adverse Action letter change based on the types of overpayments created. The adverse action letter will not generate for negative manual awards or miscellaneous payment changes. The adverse action letter will include:

* Name of institution
* Dates of enrollment
* Number of credits prior to change and after change
* Date and type of change that created overpayment
* Type and amount of overpayment due
* Contact information for the Debt Management Center

**NOTE:** After January 5, 2021, adverse action letters no longer include school debts for tuition and fees or Yellow Ribbon, which are indicated on a separate School Debt letter, see [section 9.10](#9.10 School Debt).

An Adverse action letter will generate for reductions and terminations prior to the beginning of the term, when no payments have been made, and will indicate to the beneficiary that payments previously scheduled will not be made.

Both an Adverse Action letter and an Award 3 letter will generate when a books and supplies payment is created to immediately offset a books and supplies debt. No additional intervention is needed by the user.  The student is entitled to notification of both the books and supplies debt and of the payment.

When an Adverse Action Letter generates, the Letters Information Screen will display “ADVERSE ACTION LETTER.” No entry can be made to this screen, so any changes to the letter must be done manually. When the debts are caused by changes to eligibility, this letter does not contain enough information about the reason for the debt, and it must be edited.

**9.06 Mitigating Circumstances Requested (MITC 1)**

The MITC1 letter is used to request mitigating circumstances from the beneficiary. Mitigating Circumstances are defined in [Part 4: Chapter 11 - Enrollment Adjustments](https://eg5813aprl-int-nlb.egainstz.cloud/system/ws/v11/ss/article/554400000073644?portalId=554400000001018&usertype=customer&\$lang=en-us&), and they allow the beneficiary to be paid until the end of the month or term (whichever is earlier) for reductions or until the last day of attendance for withdrawals. An example of the system-generated MITC1 letter is attached below.

A MITC 1 letter is generated when an enrollment adjustment contains the following:

·         Mitigating Circumstance are Required checkbox is selected AND

·         Mitigating Circumstances are Provided checkbox is NOT selected AND

·         Mitigating Circumstances are Acceptable checkbox is NOT selected AND

·         Restore Entitlement for Entitlement Period is NOT selected AND

If applying the Six-Credit Hour Exclusion (see[Part 4: Chapter 11 - Enrollment Adjustments](https://eg5813aprl-int-nlb.egainstz.cloud/system/ws/v11/ss/article/554400000073644?portalId=554400000001018&usertype=customer&\$lang=en-us&)) to a termination or reduction, a MITC1 letter will generate only if the reduction was greater than 6 credit hours. When this occurs, the letter will contain the six-credit hour exclusion information. If the reduction was fewer than 6 credit hours, a MITC4 letter generate as described in [section 9.09](#9.09 MITC4).

When a MITC1 Letter generates, the Letters Information Screen will display “MITIGATING CIRCUMSTANCES 1: MITIGATING CIRCUMSTANCES REQUESTED.” No entry can be made to this screen, so any changes to the letter must be done manually. This letter does not generally require additional editing unless a complex or non-typical situation occurred during processing that requires a unique explanation.

**9.07 Mitigating Circumstances Received and Not Acceptable (MITC 2)**

The MITC2 letter is used to inform beneficiaries that mitigating circumstances submitted for their withdrawal were not accepted. The letter states that the overpayment will be from the first day of enrollment. If the mitigating circumstances are received but not accepted and the Six-Credit Hour Exclusion is granted for the same amendment, the MITC2 letter will contain information about the Six-Credit Hour Exclusion. An example of the system-generated MITC2 letter is attached below.

A MITC2 letter is generated under the following circumstances:

·         Mitigating Circumstances are Required checkbox is selected AND

·         Mitigating Circumstances are Provided checkbox is selected AND

·         Mitigating Circumstances are Acceptable checkbox is NOT selected AND

·         Restore Entitlement for Entitlement Period is NOT selected.

The letter will generate during the work product when the “Mitigating Circumstances are Provided” box is checked. This may be in a separate work product from the one containing the amendment, if mitigating circumstances are received at a later date.

When a MITC2 Letter generates, the Letters Information Screen will display “MITIGATING CIRCUMSTANCES 2: MITIGATING CIRCUMSTANCES NOT ACCEPTED.” This screen allows the user to select a reason(s) that the mitigating circumstances were not accepted, and the selected reason(s) will display in the letter. If the user selects the Free Text option, they must enter the reason in the text field. This information will display in the letter exactly as it is typed, so the user should proofread the input. This letter does not generally require additional editing unless a complex or non-typical situation occurred during processing that requires a unique explanation.

![MITC2 Screen Example](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/MITC2%20screen.png)

**9.08 Mitigating Circumstance Acceptable (MITC3)**

The MITC3 letter is used to inform beneficiaries that mitigating circumstances submitted supporting their withdrawal were accepted. The letter states that they will be paid for the portion of the term they attended. If the mitigating circumstances are accepted and the Six-Credit Hour Exclusion is granted for the same amendment, the MITC3 letter will contain information about the Six-Credit Hour Exclusion. An example of the system-generated MITC3 letter is attached below.

A MITC3 letter is generated under the following circumstances:

·         Mitigating Circumstances are Required checkbox is selected AND

·         Mitigating Circumstances are Provided checkbox is selected AND

·         Mitigating Circumstances are Acceptable checkbox is selected AND

·         Restore Entitlement for Entitlement Period is NOT selected.

The letter will generate during the work product when the “Mitigating Circumstances are Acceptable” box is checked. This may be in a separate work product from the one containing the amendment.

When a MITC3 Letter generates, the Letters Information Screen will display “MITIGATING CIRCUMSTANCES 3: MITIGATING CIRCUMSTANCES ACCEPTED.” This screen allows the user to select the reason that the mitigating circumstances were not accepted, and the selected reason will display in the letter. The user can only select one option on this screen. If the user selects the Free Text option, they must enter the reason in the text field. This information will display in the letter exactly as it is typed, so the user should proofread the input. This letter does not generally require additional editing unless a complex or non-typical situation occurred during processing that requires a unique explanation.

![MITC3 Screen Example](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/MITC3%20screen.png)

**9.09 Six-Credit Hour Exclusion (MITC4)**

The MITC4 letter is used to explain the Six-Credit Hour Exclusion when the withdrawal was for 6 credit hours or less. This means that all hours were covered by the exclusion, so mitigating circumstances are not required. There may still be an overpayment for the portion of the term that the beneficiary did not attend. An example of the system-generated MITC4 letter is attached below.

A MITC4 letter is generated under the following circumstances:

* Mitigating Circumstance are Required checkbox is selected AND
* Mitigating Circumstance are Provided checkbox is NOT selected AND
* Mitigating Circumstance are Acceptable checkbox is NOT selected AND
* Six-Credit Hour Exclusion is AVAILABLE before the amendment is entered AND
* The Six-Credit Hour Exclusion **must be APPLIED** during the current work product AND
* The amendment that used the withdrawal as for 6 credit hours or less.

When a MITC4 Letter generates, the Letters Information Screen will display “MITIGATING CIRCUMSTANCES 4: SIX-CREDIT HOUR EXCLUSION.” No entry can be made to this screen, so any changes to the letter must be done manually. This letter does not generally require additional editing unless a complex or non-typical situation occurred during processing that requires a unique explanation.

![MITC4 Screen Example](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/MITC4%20screen.png)

Figure 9.09 [Letters Screen Example – MITC4]

**9.10 School Debt & Student Copy**

A School Debt letter generates when overpayments for tuition and fees and/or Yellow Ribbon are created during the current work product. The school debt letter is addressed to the mailing address for the institution incurring the debt and addressed to the school certifying official. A student copy of the School Debt letter is also generated with a cover letter that is addressed to the beneficiary. An example of the system-generated School Debt letter with student copy is attached below.

The School debt letter includes:

* Name of impacted student
* Name of institution
* Impacted enrollment period
* Total tuition and fees and/or Yellow Ribbon paid to institution for that period
* Amount and type of overpayment

When a School Debt Letter generates, the Letters Information Screen will display both a “SCHOOL DEBT STUDENT LETTER” and a “SCHOOL DEBT LETTER.” No entry can be made to this screen, so any changes to the letter must be done manually. This letter does not generally require additional editing unless a complex or non-typical situation occurred during processing that requires a unique explanation.

![School Debt Screen Example](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/School%20Debt%20screen.png)

**9.11 STEM Acknowledgement**

A STEM Acknowledgement letter is produced when a STEM application is processed on the Entitlement and Kickers Screen with no reason for denial.  This letter advises the beneficiary that their STEM Application has been processed but that they have not been selected for the program. They will receive an additional letter if they are selected to participate. There are no appeal rights granted with this letter. An example of the system-generated STEM Acknowledgement letter is attached below.

When a STEM Acknowledgement letter generates, the Letters Information Screen will display “STEM ACKNOWLEDGEMENT LETTER.” No entry can be made to this screen, so any changes to the letter must be done manually.

![STEM Acknowledgement Screen Example](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/STEM%20Acknoledgement%20screen.png)

**9.12 STEM Selection**

A STEM Selection letter is produced when a DGI super user processes a STEM Scholarship Selection. This must be done after an application is authorized with no reason for denial, and a STEM Acknowledgement letter is sent.  Typically, when the super user processes the selection, a work product is automatically authorized. However, if the work product does not automate, the user must authorize the work product to complete the transaction. This letter is a positive decision letter for the STEM program, and acts as a COE. An example of the system-generated STEM Selection letter is attached below.

When a STEM Selection letter generates, the Letters Information Screen will display “STEM Selection Letter.” No entry can be made to this screen, so any changes to the letter must be done manually.

![STEM Selection Screen Example](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/STEM%20Selection%20screen.png)

**9.13 STEM Denial**

A STEM Denial letter is produced when one or more STEM disallowance reasons is generated, based on the STEM application information entered on the Entitlement and Kickers Screen.  An example of the system-generated STEM Denial letter is attached below. The following denial reasons must be selected as the “program type” when the application is entered in order to generate a denial letter:

* Program of Study not STEM Eligible
* Program of Study not approved
* Not earned a prior STEM undergraduate or graduate degree
* In a clinical training program, but has not earned a prior STEM undergraduate or graduate degree

When a STEM Denial letter generates, the Letters Information Screen will display “STEM DENIAL LETTER.” This screen shows all system-generated denial reasons as checked boxes. It also allows the user to select some additional applicable denial reasons, and the selected reason(s) will display in the letter. If the user selects the Free Text option, they must enter the reason in the text field. This information will display in the letter exactly as it is typed, so the user should proofread the input.

Figure 9.13 [Letters Screen Example – STEM Denial Example]
