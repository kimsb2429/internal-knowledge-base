**SUBCHAPTER I.  OVERVIEW OF MASTER RECORD INQUIRY**

**3.01  GENERAL**

---

**a.  [Organization]** 

(1)  [Subchapter 1](#SI) provides general information on how to obtain the master records for the various education [benefit programs, including chapters 30 and 1606.]  

(2)  [Subchapter 2](#SII) describes the master record screens for chapters 32, 34, 35 and section 903.  The screens for these benefits are nearly identical.

(3)  [Subchapter 3](#SIII) describes the master record screen for the section 901 program and the common screen which displays accounts receivable data for various programs.

**b.  Master Record Inquiry Commands**

(1)  The [BDN (Benefits Delivery Network) MINQ (Master Record Inquiry) command gives direct access to the master record for the benefit specified.  (See [pars. 3.03](#303) and [3.04](#304) for instructions on using the MINQ command.)  One drawback to MINQ is that it does not alert the operator to the existence of multiple education master records. 

(2)  The BDN PINQ (Pending Issue Inquiry) command displays all pending issues under one file number at a particular BDNRS (Benefits Delivery Network Regional System).  A person can only review those screens with pending information using the PINQ command.  Like the MINQ command, PINQ does not provide a list of all existing master records.  (See [par. 3.02.](#302))  Once the pending issues are identified, the complete master record or records may be reviewed with the MINQ command.

(3)  The BDN] SINQ (Status Record Inquiry) command is by far the most powerful inquiry command.  It will display a list of all education master records provided there are no pending issues at the same BDNRS.  (See [par. 3.02](#302))

**3.02.  PINQ AND SINQ COMMANDS**

---

**a.  PINQ**.  The PINQ command accesses the pending issue file located at the BDNRS.  The [PIF (Pending Issue File) contains data entered by the operator, data from BIRLS (Beneficiary Identification & Records Locator Subsystem) and data from the claimant's master record if one exists.  The P01 screen (Pending Issue File Claim Status) displays the status of the claim under CLAIM HISTORY.  The pending issue remains accessible until it is resolved and the EPC (End Product Code)] taken.  The S02 (Payee Selection) screen will display all pending issues if more than one EPC is pending.  The P01 and S02 screens are described further in [Part 02, Computer Processing. Chapter 6 - BDN Processing, General (Subchapter 2)](https://eg5813aprl-int-nlb.egainstz.cloud/system/ws/v11/ss/article/554400000077928?portalId=554400000001018&usertype=customer&\$lang=en-us&)

(1)  Make the following entries on the ready screen to obtain a PINQ screen (P01):

(a)  The command PINQ.

(b)  Operator's password.

(c)  File number.

(2)  The P01 shows the status of the claim under CLAIM HISTORY.  The CLAIM LOCATION field shows location (e.g. ADJUDICATION) and action (e.g., GAP-DATA ENTRY).  Messages at the bottom of the screen give additional information (e.g., PENDING ISSUE CLEARED,  "12B" PENDING ACCEPTANCE).  Access is limited to screens in the 300 or 400 series and related to that pending issue.  "SCREEN INAPPROPRIATE FOR ISSUE" is displayed when entry of a screen that could not be used for that claim is made. 

*NOTE:  If there is no pending issue, the ready screen will be redisplayed with an error message.  Use the MINQ command to determine the master record status.*

**b.  SINQ**.  The SINQ command is a useful command as it affords access to the PIF, master record files and BIRLS. 

(1)  Make the following entries on the ready screen for a SINQ command:

(a)  Command (i. e., SINQ);

(b)  Operator's password;

(c)  File number.

(2)  [BDN] shows the claim status, on the PO1 screen, if a claim is pending.  If more than one pending issue is found, the system displays an S02 selection screen.  If no pending issue exists, the system next searches for a master record.  If a master record exists, the first screen of the master record is displayed.  An S02 screen is shown if two or more master records exist.  If no master record exists, the system displays the BIRLS VID (Veteran Identification Data) screen with the correct file number.  "NO RECORD" is shown on the ready Screen if no BIRLS record is found.

**3.03.  EDUCATION DATA BASES**

---

**a.  General**.  Use the MINQ command to obtain education master record data.  It is important to know the separate education data bases.  See paragraph 3.04 for ready screen entries. 

(1)  Chapter 34 and 35 records as well as C&P (Compensation and Pension) records are on one data base.

(2)  Chapter 32 and Section 903 and 901 records are on one data base.

(3)  Chapters 30 and [1606 each have separate data bases for their benefit records.

(4)  Chapters 30 and 1606 have separate data bases for their DOD (Department of Defense) records.]

**b.  Chapter 30**.  See chapter 4 for master record screens.

**c.  Chapter 32**.  There are eight master record screens. 

(1)  M21- Education Master Record Status (Current).

(2)  M22- Education Master Record Award Status  (Prior, Current, and Future).

(3)  M23- Education Master Record Eligibility and Entitlement Data.

(4)  M24- Education Master Record Miscellaneous Data.

(5)  M26- CH32 Participant Account Summary.

(6)  M27- CH32 Participant Contribution History.

(7)  M28- CH32 DOD Contribution and Miscellaneous Data.

(8)  M29- CH32 Benefit History.

*NOTE:  Only the M26 through M29 screens, sometimes referred to as the Banking Record, are available if benefits have not been paid even though contributions have been made.  [The Hines PPU (Payment Processing Unit) previously processed chapter 32 benefit payments for certain types of awards, such as bank problem cases and correspondence awards.  BDN showed the message "CHAPTER 32 BENEFITS HAVE BEEN PAID" at the bottom of the M26 and M27 screens for these cases.  There are no M21 through M24 screens for these cases.  Do not use the M26 through M29 screens to determine if any entitlement has been used.  PPU no longer processes chapter 32 benefit payments.  However, if PPU previously paid benefits and the RPO (Regional Processing Office) needs to resolve inquiries on these cases, send [e-mail](mailto:VAVBAHINES/FIN/RECRT?subject=Part%202%3A%20Chapter%203%20-%20Master%20Record%20Inquiry) to PPU at "VAVBAHINES/FIN/RECRT".]*

**d.  Chapters 34 and 35**.  The education master record inquiry screens are as follows:

(1)  M21- Education Master Record Status (Current).

(2)  M22- Education Master Record Award Status  (Prior, Current, and Future).

(3)  M23 - Education Master Record Eligibility and Entitlement Data.

(4)  M24- Education Master Record Miscellaneous Data.

(5)  M25- Abbreviated Education Master Record (chapter 34 only).

**e.  Chapter [1606]**.  See [TG (Training Guide) 22-99-5, the Chapter 1606 User Guide.]

**f.  Section 901**.  Only eligibility information is available for Section 901 via the MINQ command.  The "M30-Educational Assistance Test Program Benefit" is the only inquiry screen available for display of section 901 data.   [BDN does not have any benefit history record.  Contact the Waco RO (Regional Office)] for information on specific cases. 

**g.  Section 903**.  Section 903 banking and benefit screens are the same as for chapter 32 as shown in [subparagraph c](#303c) above.

**h.  Accounts Receivable**.  There is one additional screen that may be  available when reviewing education master records.  This is the M01 screen (Deduction/Receivable Balance Data).  [  ]

**3.04  MINQ PROCEDURES**

---

**a.  Ready Screen Entries**.  To obtain a master record screen, make the following entries on the ready screen:

(1)  The command MINQ.

(2)  Operator's password.

(3)  Screen number.  [When this field has a screen number (M22, M23, etc) in this field, BDN displays that screen initially instead of the M21 screen. 

(4)  File number.

(5)  Benefit Field. 

(a)  Entries for this field are CH30, CH32, CH34, CH35, [1606], 901, 903, DOD and 30D. 

(b)  This field may also be left blank, but entry is required to search the chapter 30, 32/901/903, [1606], and DOD data bases. 

*NOTE 1:  When both the screen number field and the benefit field are left blank and the Chapter 34/35 system and the C&P system have two or more master records, BDN shows a S02 screen.  The operator can select the desired record from this screen.*

*NOTE 2:  When both the screen number field and the benefit field are left blank and the Chapter 34/35 system and the C&P system has only one master record, BDN shows that master record.  If that master record is a chapter 34 or 35 record, BDN shows the M21 screen.  If that master record is a C&P record, BDN shows the M11 C&P Master Record Status Screen.*

(6)  Press the enter key.

**b.  No Record Messages**.  If no record is found for a particular benefit, the following messages are shown:

(1)  The ready screen shows ["CHAP 30 RECORD NOT FOUND"] when the chapter 30 data base is searched. 

(2)  The ready screen shows "MASTER DOES NOT EXIST AT HINES" when the chapter 32/901/903 data base is searched.

(3)  The ready screen shows "NO RECORD; C&P CH34/35" when the chapter 34/35 data base is searched.

(4)  The ready screen shows ["1606 RECORD NOT FOUND" when the chapter 1606] data base is searched.

(5)  The ready screen shows "NO DOD RECORD FOUND" when the DOD database is searched. 

**c.  Data Base Search Limitations**.  A search under one data base will not show the presence of a record in a different data base, even if the record exists and could be accessed in that separate data base.  Therefore, it is sometimes necessary to perform multiple MINQ commands to rule out the possible existence of specific education records.

(1)  Chapter 34/35 data base.  A search for a master record is restricted to the [C&P/CH34/35 data base unless operators make a ready screen entry of M26, M27, M28, M29. or M30 in the SCREEN NUMBER field, or an entry of CH30, CH32, 1606], 901, 903, DOD, or 30D in the BENEFIT field.

(2)  Chapter 32, 901 and 903 data base:

(a)  The M26 screen is shown if a chapter 32 benefit screen (M21-M24) is requested and no benefit account exists.  Conversely, the M21 is shown if a bank screen (M26, M27, M28, or M29) is requested and no bank account exists.  NOTE:  This is possible only for disallowed "J" type master records. 

(b)  If the master record which [BDN finds does not match the entered benefit type or screen number, the master record found will be shown.  If no record is found, a] message will be shown at the bottom of the screen to indicate that the record requested was not found.

(c)  A modified CH32/903 disallowed master record (M21) screen is shown if an M26-M29 screen is requested for a CH32/903 record and only a J-type (disallowed) master record exists.  The disallowance reason is shown.

(d)  A participant can contribute to the chapter 32 fund from two different service branches [(e.g., Navy and Marines, Army and Navy)].  Two separate records are established for the participant because each branch of service submits separate information to the VA bank file. 

(e)  "DUPLICATE CHAPTER 32 RECORD -- CALL HINES FINANCE OFFICE" is shown on the S02 screen when a MINQ is done and more than one chapter 32 record is found with the requested file number.  PPU must combine these duplicate records before award processing can take place.  Take the following steps:

(1)  [Notify PPU at "VAVBAHINES/FIN/RECRT" of the situation.  PPU will consolidate the two files and notify the RPO after the records are consolidated.]

(2)  Make a special payment, and control the case with a local diary.  Process the [BDN] award when notified by the PPU.

**d.  MINQ Screen Printouts**.  MINQ screen printouts will be requested only if:

(1)  A complex claim is being processed and master record data must be thoroughly analyzed, or

(2)  A copy of the MINQ screen data is needed in the claimant's folder for historical or record purposes. 

*[NOTE: RPOs should scan these screens directly into the claimant's electronic folder.  If RPOs print these screens or receive VA Form 20-8270a, dispose of this paper, when no longer required, in accordance with Records Control Schedule VB-1, Part I, item Nos. 13-052.400 and 08-041.000.]*

**c.  Additional Futures--Additional Priors**

(1)  Each column of dates is considered a "prior," "current," or a "future" payment period.  The column specifies a period of payment at a specific monthly rate.
