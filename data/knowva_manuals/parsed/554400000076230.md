**2.02   BACKGROUND**

---

**a**.  Only the RPOs (Regional Processing Offices) can process [MGIB] awards and disallowances.  Currently the RPOs are the Buffalo and Muskogee regional offices.

**b**.  All ROs (Regional Offices) can use the MINQ commands to review [MGIB] master records of claims processed on [the BDN].  (See [Part 2: Chapter 4 - Master Record Inquiry (Chapter 30)](https://eg5813aprl-int-nlb.egainstz.cloud/system/ws/v11/ss/article/554400000077340?portalId=554400000001018&usertype=customer&\$lang=en-us&) )  ROs can use the CERT command to input verification of enrollment information to [BDN] master records for [MGIB] IHL (Institution of Higher Learning) and NCD (Non–College Degree) students who report non receipt or loss of VA Form 22-8979, Student Verification of Enrollment, and report no change in their enrollment.  (See [Part 5: Chapter 6 - Certifications](https://eg5813aprl-int-nlb.egainstz.cloud/system/ws/v11/ss/article/554400000077042?portalId=554400000001018&usertype=customer&\$lang=en-us&),  par. 6.06.)

**c**.  [MGIB] IHL and NCD awards are processed on [the BDN].  Apprenticeship, correspondence, flight, and on-the-job training awards are processed on the [MGIB] PC (Personal Computer) system.

**2.03  MAJOR DIFFERENCES IN THE [MGIB] SYSTEM**

---

The following are the primary differences between [MGIB] claims processing and [VEAP and DEA] claims processing:

**a.  Eligibility Determinations.**  For an individual in category I or subcategories IIIA or IIIB, [the BDN] indicates if he or she has basic eligibility based on the data displayed on the 310 screen, Chapter 30 Eligibility and Entitlement.  For an individual in category II or IIIC, the operator may override the status code which shows that the individual is ineligible if the individual is determined to be eligible for [MGIB] benefits.  (See [par. 2.06](#206).)

**b.  Interaction With DOD Records.**  During initial claims processing, data from an individual's DOD record and [VEA (Vietnam Era Educational Assistance)] master record, if category II, is displayed on the 310 screen.  (See [par. 2.06a](#206a).)  Also, if DOD data or the amount of [VEA] remaining entitlement changes, a NOE (Notice of Exception) message will be issued and, under certain conditions, [MGIB] benefits will be suspended automatically.  (See  [Part 5: Chapter 5 - Notice of Exception Processing](https://eg5813aprl-int-nlb.egainstz.cloud/system/ws/v11/ss/article/554400000076311?portalId=554400000001018&usertype=customer&\$lang=en-us&).)

**c.  On-Line Processing.**  Unlike the [VEAP and DEA] batch processing systems, the [MGIB] system is an on-line system.  Once an award or other transaction is authorized, the [MGIB] master record is updated.  Therefore, sequential transactions may be processed on the same day on an individual's master record as long as all preceding transactions have been authorized.  (See pt. II on [BDN] System processing.)  The only exception is certification processing under the CERT command.  Only one certification transaction may be processed per day.

*NOTE:  Some transactions do not require authorization, such as certification processing while under the CERT command.*

***d.  Certifications.**  An individual receiving [MGIB] benefits including ones pursuing standard college degrees at IHLs will be paid after a periodic certification is processed according to the Schedule of Operations. (See*[Part 2 Chapter 1 - Computer Processing Concepts](https://eg5813aprl-int-nlb.egainstz.cloud/system/ws/v11/ss/article/554400000078763?portalId=554400000001018&usertype=customer&\$lang=en-us&)*, par. 1.13b.)  There is no recurring payment cycle for [MGIB] similar to [VEAP] BIG PAY or the [DEA] cycle 0.*

**2.04  APPRENTICESHIP, CORRESPONDENCE, FLIGHT, AND ON-THE-JOB TRAINING**

---

The [MGIB] PC payment system for apprenticeship, correspondence, flight, and other on-the-job training described in  [Part 4: Chapter 12 - Out-of-System Award Processing](https://eg5813aprl-int-nlb.egainstz.cloud/system/ws/v11/ss/article/554400000073667?portalId=554400000001018&usertype=customer&\$lang=en-us&) , will continue until [the BDN] is modified to accept awards for these types of training.  However, [the BDN] will be used to establish eligibility even though the awards cannot be processed.  (See [par. 2.08b](#208b).)  In these cases, master records will be established and a legend that out-of-system payments are being made will be displayed.

**SUBCHAPTER II.  ELIGIBILITY PROCESSING**

**2.06  ELIGIBILITY DETERMINATIONS—OVERVIEW**

---

[The BDN] displays data on the 310 screen which indicates if an individual is eligible for [MGIB].  [Figure 2.01](#F201) shows and [paragraph 2.07](#207) describes the display and entry fields on the 310 screen.

![This is a screenshot of the BDN Chapter 30 Eligibility and Entitlement screen.](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/Fig2.01.jpg)  
**Figure 2.01 Display and Entry Fields on 310 Screen**

**a.  Interaction With DOD Records.**   When a [MGIB] claim is established, [BDN] uses the SSN (Social Security Number) in the PIF (Pending Issue File) to search for a matching DOD record.

(1)  If [ ] a matching DOD record is found (i.e., the SSN in the [MGIB] PIF matches the SSN in an existing DOD record), certain data from the DOD record is displayed on the 310 screen upon initial display.

(i)  The data displayed on the 310 screen is the same as the data displayed on the 30D screen, Chapter 30 DOD Data Record.  (See pt. II, par. 4.10, for a description of the screen.)

(ii)  The underlined entries in [figure 2.02](#F202) show a sample of DOD data on the 310 screen.

![This is a screenshot of the BDN Chapter 30 Eligibility and Entitlement screen.](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/Fig2.02.jpg)  
**Figure 2.02 DOD Data Displayed on 310 Screen (DOD data is underlined.)**

(2)  If there is no matching DOD record (i.e., the SSN in the [MGIB] PIF does not match the SSN of any existing DOD records), [the BDN] will prompt a disallowance of the claim (see [par. 2.15](#215)).

(i)  A 310 screen with entries in FILE NUMBER, END PRODUCT, NAME, SSN, and DATE OF BIRTH fields and the message DOD RECORD DOES NOT EXIST - ENTER CH34 INDICATOR AND CH34 O/R IF APPLICABLE will be displayed.

(ii)  When processing a disallowance for a category II individual without a DOD record, the operator must enter an "X" in the CH 34 INDICATOR field.  This will insure that the correct disallowance paragraph is generated.\

(iii)  To process a disallowance for a category I individual, press ENTER or request the 411 screen, Chapter 30/106 Disallowance, in the NEXT SCREEN field.  [The BDN] will automatically generate the appropriate paragraph.

(iv)  After pressing ENTER, the 411 screen will be displayed with disallowance reason code 01 (no military/reserve service) checked.

(3)  It is not necessary to complete the 310 screen during supplemental award processing after eligibility has been initially established.

(i)  If a change in the DOD record occurs which may affect basic eligibility or entitlement, [the BDN] will require completion of a new 310 screen.  The 310 screen will be displayed when GAD (Generate and Display) or GAP (Generate and Print) is attempted.  The 310 screen will display data from the DOD record including the recent changes.

(ii)  During supplemental claims processing, the DOD record on the 310 screen can be requested by typing "310" in the SCREEN NUMBER field on the Ready screen or in the NEXT SCREEN field on the 101 screen, Pending Issue Control Establish.  (If a 310 screen is requested from the 312 Education Award or 317 General Certification screens, the 310 screen will display master record data only and will not access the DOD record.)

**b.  Completing the 310 Screen**

(1)  After reviewing the DOD data displayed on the 310 screen, correct the data displayed, as necessary, and complete the required fields.  The entries for this screen are described in [paragraph 2.07c](#207c).

(2)  If no 30D screen exists, but a category II applicant is entitled to [MGIB], create a 310 screen by entering an "X" in the CHAPTER 34 INDICATOR and the CH 34 0/R (Override) fields and completing all other required entries on the screen.  If a 30D screen exists, use the CH 34 O/R field to override an ineligible status code of 30, 32, 33, 34, or 35 only.

**c.  Generating Eligibility Messages.**  After completing the 310 screen, press ENTER.  [The BDN] will indicate whether or not the individual should be eligible based on the displayed and entered data on that screen.

(1)  If the individual is eligible for [MGIB], entitlement and the delimiting date will be computed and the message ELIGIBILITY ESTABLISHED - HIT ENTER TO STORE will be displayed on the 310 screen.  [Figure 2.03](#F203) shows entitlement, the delimiting date, and the message that eligibility is established.

 

![This is a sceenshot of the BDN Chapter 30 Eligibility and Entitlement screen.](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/Fig2.03.jpg)  
**Figure 2.03  Completed 310 Screen Indicating Eligibility Established**

(2)  If an individual is not eligible, [the BDN] will display the message NO CH30 ELIGIBILITY - GAP OR HIT ENTER TO DISALLOW (or similar disallowance message).  Press ENTER or enter GAD in the NEXT SCREEN field to display the 411 screen with the corresponding disallowance reason code checked.  If GAP is entered in the NEXT SCREEN field, the disallowance reason(s) based on the information entered on the 310 screen will be stored and VA Form 22-8945, Education Award, or a 500 screen, Education Award (for the Buffalo regional office only) will be generated without displaying the 411 screen.  (To add or change the disallowance reasons on the 411 screen, enter 411 in the NEXT SCREEN field.)  [Figure 2.04](#F204) shows eligibility not established.  Note that in this figure, the STATUS field shows INELIGIBLE.

**2.08  SPECIAL ELIGIBILITY SITUATIONS**

---

The following are various situations involving chapter 30 eligibility that will be encountered when processing chapter 30 claims:

**a.  Multiple DOD Records.**  If an individual has more than one 30D screen, more than one 310 screen that will be displayed during processing the claim.  If there is more than one 30D screen, the message X ADD'L DOD RECORDS - HIT ENTER TO SEE NEXT DOD RECORD will be displayed.  ("X" is the number of 310 screens that have not been viewed.)  A category II individual will have only one 30D screen unless he or she is also eligible under category III. 

(1)  Do not make an entry on any 310 screen until after reviewing all the 310 screens.

(2)  When the last 310 screen is displayed, the message LAST DOD RECORD - HIT ENTER TO SEE FIRST DOD will be displayed.

(3)  Continue through the 310 screens again by entering "Y" in the NEXT DOD screen field until the screen which is to be used for determining eligibility for the claim is displayed.  After reaching the desired screen, do not enter "Y" in the NEXT DOD field.  Make the necessary entries on that screen for an eligibility determination.

**b.  Eligibility Established – No Award**

(1)  If eligibility and entitlement are established in an original claim but no award is to be processed or can be entered on the Target System, complete the 310 screen as follows:

(i)  If eligibility is established and a certificate of eligibility is to be issued, enter COE in the NEXT SCREEN field of the 310 screen.  (See [par. 2.09](#209).)

(ii)  If eligibility is established and the claimant is pursuing apprenticeship or on-the-job training, correspondence training, or flight training, enter APP (Apprenticeship), OJT (On-the-Job), COR (Correspondence), or FLT (Flight Training) as appropriate, in the NEXT SCREEN field of the 310 screen.  The legend APPRENTICESHIP (or OJT or CORRESPONDENCE or FLIGHT) - OUT OF SYSTEM PAYMENT will be displayed on the Target M21 screen, Chapter 30/106 Status and Award History.

(2)  These entries in the NEXT SCREEN field will cause a type I master record to be established so that a NOE message can be issued if the DOD record for the individual is changed.

**c.  Change in Status in Selected Reserve.** If a veteran's status in the Selected Reserve changes, make the appropriate entries on the 310 screen as described below.  Then process an award as described in [paragraph 2.12b](#212b).  The entries on the 310 screen alone will not generate an amended award.

(1)  If a veteran becomes obligated to serve in the Selected Reserve for at least 4 years, enter the effective date of the Selected Reserve service in the ESR field.  This entry will cause the generated fields described in [paragraph 2.07d](#207d) to be recalculated.

(2)  If a veteran is released from the Selected Reserve, enter the date of release from Selected Reserve in the RSR field and the reason for release in the REASON field.  This entry will cause the generated fields REMAIN ENTITLEMENT (CH30) and DELIMIT/TERM DATE (CH30) to be recalculated, if necessary.

(3)  Note that changes in status in the Selected Reserve have completely different effects on two groups of individuals.

(i)  If a veteran with an initial obligation of 2 years who is already eligible for chapter 30 benefits based on active duty service incurs an obligation to serve at least 4 years in the Selected Reserve, his or her monthly rate will increase.  Likewise, if such a veteran is released from the Selected Reserve for an unacceptable reason, the monthly rate, and possibly entitlement, will be reduced.

(ii)  If a veteran is not eligible for chapter 30 benefits based solely on active duty service, he or she will have no chapter 30 eligibility before an ESR date is entered.  Likewise, if such an individual leaves the Selected Reserve for an unacceptable reason, chapter 30 eligibility ends.  A delimiting date is generated effective 1 day after the RSR date that is entered.

**2.11  ENTRIES ON THE 312 SCREEN**

---

The entries on the 312 screen for the Chapter 30 system are quite similar to those for the existing Chapters 32 and 35 systems, as described in part VI, chapter 2, and part VII, chapter 2.  The primary differences are as follows:

**a.  Apprenticeship/OJT Fields.**  The APP/OJT: STEP/MOS/DAYS and ST fields are protected from entry until award processing for apprenticeship/on-the-job training is installed on Target.

**b.  Program Change Indicator.**  Entries of 1 through 6 are allowed in the field PROG CHG.

**c.  Beginning Date.**  The earliest beginning date of a Chapter 30 award for a category I individual is July 1, 1985.  The earliest beginning date of a Chapter 30 award for a category II individual is January 1, 1990.  The earliest beginning date of a Chapter 30 award for a category IIIA individual is October 23, 1992.  The earliest beginning date of a Chapter 30 award for a category IIIB individual is October 23, 1992, the individual's RAD date, or the beginning date of training, whichever is later.  The earliest beginning date of Chapter 30 award for a category IIIC individual is October 23, 1992, or the beginning date of training (if all necessary evidence is received before October 23, 1993) whichever is later.

**d.  Beginning Reasons 00, 0X, 60 and 6X.**  Use beginning reason 00 and 60 for an original and reentrance award without an advance pay request if the training period begins 120 days or less from the date the award is input.  The award will not process as an advance pay.  If advance pay is requested, use beginning reason 0X or 6X.  Do not use beginning reasons 0X or 6X on a lump-sum award.

**e.  Beginning Reason 71.**  Beginning reason code 71 is generated under the following conditions during GAD/GAP processing:

(1)  There is a change in active duty status during the award period.  (See [par. 2.12a](#212a).)

(2)  There is a change in status in the Selected Reserve during the award period.  (See [par. 2.12b](#212b).)

(3)  The kicker amount changes during the award period to cause a change in the monthly rate.

(4)  Entitlement to kicker, but not basic educational assistance, is exhausted during the award period.

**f.  Beginning Reason 72**

(1)  Use beginning reason code 72, not code 29 or 70, in an award line if there is a change in the number of hours but not in the training time.  If reason code 70 is entered in such a situation, the message CHANGE RSN REQUIRES TRNG TIME CHANGE - USE RSN 72 will be displayed.

(2)  Minimize the use of reason code 29 as it results in an excessive number of master record award lines.  The Chapter 30 Target record stores only 16 master record award lines.  After 16 lines have been stored, the earliest award lines are dropped to make room for more current lines.

**g.  Beginning Reason 30.**  Beginning reason code 30 is a Target-generated reason code for a category II individual only.  Reason 30 is generated during GAD/GAP processing when the remaining chapter 34 entitlement transferred to Chapter 30 has been exhausted during an award period.  An individual is entitled to the basic Chapter 30 rate after his or her Chapter 34 entitlement is exhausted.  Reason 30 is not for operator entry.

**h.  Dependency Beginning Reasons.**  The following beginning reason codes, which apply to changes in dependency, are acceptable for category II cases which have remaining Chapter 34 entitlement available.  The codes are not valid during the period after the Chapter 34 transferred entitlement has been exhausted.  They are not acceptable entries in category I or III awards.

 

| **CODE** | **REASON** |
| --- | --- |
| 15 | Dependent child marries |
| 22 | Death of dependent |
| 23 | Child over 18 enters school |
| 24 | Child reaches 18 |
| 25 | Child over 18 terminates school or reaches 23 |
| 26 | Dependent added |
| 53 | Helpless child enters school |
| 54 | Child no longer helpless |
| 55 | Child status changed from school to helpless |

**i.  No Pay Reason 74.**  Use no pay (ending) reason code 74 to extend the ending date of an existing award period.

**j.  Number of Dependents.**  The DEP (Number of Dependents) field is a required entry on all category II awards.  It is protected from entry on all category I and III awards.  Acceptable dependency codes are 00, 10-79, and 81-89.  (See pt. II, par. 9.04, for the codes.)  Entries in the first input award line will roll down to subsequent award lines.

**k.  Override Indicator.**  Acceptable entries are I, J, L, and M.  Use override indicator I for independent study, J for an incarcerated individual, and L for lump-sum awards.  (See subch. IV for lump-sum award procedures.)  Use override indicator M if the Target-generated monthly rate would be incorrect.  For example, an individual is due an additional amount for a kicker but Target does not reflect the kicker.  Compute the monthly rates manually and enter them on the award lines with the M override.  This bypasses Target's rate edits.  These awards require two signatures.  (See ch. 4 for further restrictions.)

**l.  Message Field.**  An unedited message of up to 55 characters in the MSG (Message) field at the bottom of the 312 screen may be entered.  This message will be redisplayed on the M21 and 501, Authorization, screens.

**m.  NEXT SCREEN Field.**  In addition to existing entries in the NEXT SCREEN field, master record screens may be accessed directly from the 312 screen by entering M01, M21, M22, M23, or M24 in the NEXT SCREEN field.

**SUBCHAPTER IV.  LUMP-SUM AWARD PROCESSING**

**2.18  GENERAL**

---

**a.  Overview**.  This subchapter explains how to process Chapter 30 lump-sum awards using Target.  See chapter 1, paragraph 1.26c, for an explanation of what individuals are eligible for lump-sum payments.

**b.  Retroactive Awards**.  If an entire award period is retroactive, process the award and certification of pursuit or attendance together for the entire enrollment period.  A lump-sum payment will result.

**c.  Award Types**.  Lump-sum award processing will allow original, reentrance, and certain amended and combination stop/reentrance awards.

**d.  Stop/Suspend/Resume**.  Enter stop, suspend, and resume transactions in the normal manner

**e.  Minimal-input Awards**.  Lump-sum award processing will not allow minimal-input awards when adding dependents to a category II master record.  Use full-input procedures to add or delete dependents.

**f.  Override (OR) Indicator**.  Lump-sum award processing uses OR indicator "L" to direct Target to process the award as a lump-sum award.  It also triggers all the edits for lump-sum award processing.  The indicator directs Target to advance the DLC (Date Last Certified) and DLP to the end of the lump-sum award period.

**2.19  312 SCREEN ENTRIES**

---

**a.  Beginning Date**

(1)  The beginning date of a lump-sum award cannot be a future date.  A lump-sum award must be based upon a confirmed enrollment, i.e., the enrollment certification must be signed after the beginning date of the enrollment period.  If a future beginning date is entered, the message BEGINNING DATE CANNOT BE IN FUTURE will be displayed.

(2)  The L override indicator will block an amended award with a beginning date earlier than the last no pay date under the following conditions.  If either condition exists, the message AMEND INVALID FOR LUMP SUM will be displayed.

(i)  The last master record award period has an L override indicator and the no pay date is in the future (after the current date); or

(ii)  Any award line on the 312 screen has an L override indicator.

**b.  Beginning Reason**.  Target will not accept beginning reason codes 0X or 6X on a lump-sum award.  If one of these codes is entered, the message INVALID REASON CODE will be displayed.

**c.  No Pay Date**

(1)  A lump-sum award cannot exceed 9 months in length.  If the no pay date is later than the beginning date plus 9 months, the message LUMP SUM EXCEEDS 9 MONTHS will be displayed.

(2)  If the no pay date is before the current date and an L override indicator is entered, the message RETIRE AWARD-LUMP SUM INVALID will be displayed.  (See [par. 2.18b](#218b) above for the procedure.)

(3)  The L override indicator will not allow entry of more than one no pay date.  If more than one no pay date is entered, the message ONLY ONE NO PAY DATE ALLOWED will be displayed.

(4)  The L override indicator will block a combination stop/reentrance award under the following conditions.  If either condition exists, the message STOP/REENTRANCE INVALID FOR LUMP SUM will be displayed.

(i)  The last master record award period has an L override indicator and the no pay date is in the future (after the current date); or

(ii)  Any award line on the 312 screen has an L override indicator.

**d.  OR Indicator**

(1)  Enter the L override indicator on all lump-sum awards.

(2)  If there is more than one award line on the 312 screen, enter the L override indicator on each award line.  If the L override indicator is not entered, the message ENTRY REQUIRED will be displayed.

(3)  If the L override indicator is entered, the training time must be "0" or "1".  If this condition does not exist, the message LUMP SUM ONLY VALID IF TRNG TIME = 0/1 will be displayed.

(4)  If the L override indicator is entered and the master record is in a running status, the message M/R STATUS INVALID FOR LUMP SUM will be displayed. A lump-sum award can only be processed against a terminated master record.  Review the previous award and take the necessary action to stop the award.

(5)  The L override indicator cannot be entered in combination with any other valid override indicators.  If this condition exists, the message OVERRIDE INCONSISTENT WITH LUMP SUM will be displayed.

**2.20  GAD/GAP Processing**

---

**a**.  It is not necessary to complete the 317 screen for lump-sum awards.  If the 317 screen is completed, the message 317 INVALID WITH LUMP SUM will be displayed.

**b**.  When the L override indicator is used, the 312 screen will display the informational message, REVIEW LUMP SUM AWARD under GAD.  After GAP processing, the message will be printed on VAF 22-8945 and included on the 500 screen for the Buffalo regional office.

**c.** If GAD/GAP processing adjusts the no pay date because an individual is no longer eligible for benefits, the message ELIGIBILITY TERMINATED XX-XX-XX - REVIEW CHARGES.  After GAP processing, the message will be printed on VAF 22-8945 and included on the 500 screen for the Buffalo regional office.

(1)  XX-XX-XX equals the Target-generated ending date.  If Target generates this message, adjust the total charges to reflect the shortened award period.

(2)  GAD/GAP processing will adjust the no pay date before Target generates the monthly rate based upon the total charges.  If the charges are not adjusted, Target will generate an incorrect rate for the shortened period.

**d**.  If a veteran's delimiting date falls during a lump-sum award, Target will adjust the no pay date and the message AWARD END DATE EXCEEDS DELIMIT DATE - REVIEW CHARGES will be displayed.  After GAP processing, the message will be printed on VAF 22-8945 and included on the 500 screen for the Buffalo regional office.  Use the procedure in paragraph 4.14 to determine the adjusted charges for the shortened enrollment period.

**e**.  If an individual exhausts his or her entitlement during a lump-sum award, Target will adjust the no pay date and the message ENT EXHAUSTED XX-XX-XX - REVIEW CHARGES will be displayed.  After GAP processing, the message will be printed on VAF 22-8945 and included on the 500 screen for the Buffalo regional office.

(1)  XX-XX-XX is the actual exhaust date.  The adjusted no pay date will be after the exhaust date.

(2)  When an individual exhausts his or her entitlement during an award period, Target will use the earlier of the following dates as the adjusted no pay date:

(i)  If type hours is "S", the extended exhaust date is the earlier of:

A.  The entitlement exhaust date plus 136 days or

(ii)  The no pay date on the 312 screen.

A.  If type hours is "Q", the extended exhaust date is the earlier of:

B. The entitlement exhaust date plus 94 days or

C.  The no pay date on the 312 screen.

(iii)  If type hours is "C", "P", or "R", the extended exhaust date is the earlier of:

A.  The entitlement exhaust date plus 84 days or

B.  The no pay date on the 312 screen.

(3)  In most instances, the adjusted no pay date will be the no pay date on the 312 screen.  It will be necessary to adjust the charges only if the adjusted no pay date is earlier than the no pay date on the 312 screen.  Use the procedure in paragraph 4.14 to determine the adjusted charges for the shortened enrollment period.

**2.22  CNUM (CHANGE OF CLAIM NUMBER) PROCEDURES**

---

Changing a claim number for a Chapter 30 record is similar to the chapter 35 system.  See  [Part 2: Chapter 8 - Miscellaneous Target Processing](https://eg5813aprl-int-nlb.egainstz.cloud/system/ws/v11/ss/article/554400000072991?portalId=554400000001018&usertype=customer&\$lang=en-us&), paragraph 8.08, for a general discussion of CNUM and DUPC commands.  CNUM for Chapter 30 records is restricted to the RPOs.  Before starting the process, do BINQ, MINQ, and PINQ inquiries under both the old and the new numbers.  The system accesses BIRLS, DOD (30D), benefit master record, and PIF records.

**a.  Access.**  The entries on the Ready screen are the command, password, old claim number, payee number, benefit type, and stub name.  If no Chapter 30 master record is found, the message NO CHAPTER 30/106 MASTER EXISTS is displayed.  If a record is found, the M21 screen, Chapter 30/106 Status and Award History, is displayed from the Chapter 30 master record.

**b.  Entry.**  The only possible entry is in the FILE NUMBER field.  If a 9-digit number is entered in the FILE NUMBER field, the SOCIAL SECURITY NUMBER field will be changed automatically.

**c.  BIRLS Processing.**  The new claim number and the stub name are compared with BIRLS.  If an exact match is not found, processing is terminated and the message NO RECORD will be displayed.  If the stub name does not match, processing will stop and the message BELONGS TO A A AAAAA is displayed. (A A AAAAA is the stub name in BIRLS.)  If a match is found, processing continues.

**d.  Chapter 30 Master Record Processing.**  The new claim number is compared with the chapter 30 master records.

(1)  If a record exists under the new claim number, processing is terminated and the message RECORD EXISTS FOR CHANGED FILE NUMBER will be displayed.

(2)  If a record is not found under the new claim number, the old record will be changed and the message CNUM PROCESSED - VERIFY CONTINUED ELIG/ENT is displayed on the Ready screen.  The next time the record is accessed under CDAT or CADJ, the message CHANGE IN ELIG - REVIEW 310 will be displayed on the 310 screen.

**e.  DOD Processing.**  The social security number is compared with all DOD records.  The processing will continue even if a matching DOD record is not found.

**f.  Pending Issue File Processing.**  The old claim number is checked against the pending issue file.  If a pending issue is found under the old claim number and the issue has not been authorized, PCAN'd, or PCLR'd, the message ENTRY PROHIBITED - ACTIVE PIF EXISTS will be displayed.  Cancel the pending issue before resuming CNUM processing.

**2.23  DUPC (DUPLICATE RECORD CONSOLIDATION) PROCEDURES**

---

Consolidating duplicate Chapter 30 records is similar to the chapter 35 system.  See [Part 2: Chapter 8 - Miscellaneous Target Processing](https://eg5813aprl-int-nlb.egainstz.cloud/system/ws/v11/ss/article/554400000072991?portalId=554400000001018&usertype=customer&\$lang=en-us&), paragraph 8.09, for a general discussion of CNUM and DUPC commands.  Since its original installation, DUPC processing has been modified to handle cases involving duplicate chapter 30 master records.  Before starting the process, do BINQ, MINQ, and PINQ inquiries under both the retained and canceled numbers.

*NOTE:  All field stations can process DUPC actions affecting chapter 30 records.  If a field station other than an RPO processes a DUPC affecting a Chapter 30 record, send a message through Wang Office to the RPO with the retained and canceled claim numbers.  If a field station receives 903 message – 26 TR PROCESSED AGAINST POSSIBLE CH34/30 M/R – REVIEW FILE NUMBER CHANGE, review the CH30 master record to be sure the change hit.  If not, refer it to the RPO for a CNUM transaction.*

**a.  Access.**  The entries on the Ready screen are the command and password.  Target displays a CON (Record Consolidation) screen.

**b.  Entries.**  The entries on the CON screen are the claim number and name for the retained record and the claim number and name for the canceled record.  It is necessary to enter the name for the canceled record if the name is the same in both records.

**c.  BIRLS Processing.**  If one BIRLS record shows a Chapter 30 folder, the information will be moved to the retained record.  If both BIRLS records show Chapter 30 folders, the information in the retained record will remain.  The information in the consolidated record can be corrected prior to entering UPD (Update) in the NEXT SCREEN field.

**d.  Chapter 30 Master Record Processing.**  The new claim number is compared with all Chapter 30 master records.

(1)  If only one Chapter 30 master record is found, further processing will depend upon whether the master record was previously under the retained or canceled claim number.

(i)  If the Chapter 30 master record was previously under the retained claim number, no further action is necessary.

(ii)  If the Chapter 30 master record was previously under the canceled number, the master record is changed to the retained claim number and the message CHANGE IN ELIG – REVIEW 310 is displayed on the Ready screen.  The next time the record is accessed under CDAT or CADJ, the message CHANGE IN ELIG – REVIEW 310 is displayed on the 310 screen.

(2)  If duplicate Chapter 30 master records are found, Target will not consolidate the records.

(i)  The Chapter 30 master record under the retained claim number will remain and the message CHANGE IN ELIG – REVIEW 310 is displayed on the Ready screen.  The next time the record is accessed under CDAT or CADJ, the message CHANGE IN ELIG – REVIEW 310 is displayed on the 310 screen.

(ii)  The Chapter 30 master record under the canceled claim number will remain, but further processing is prohibited.  The LAST ACTIVITY field will show "26", the date of last activity will be the date the DUPC action was processed, and the M/R STATUS field will show P-PURGED.  If an operator attempts to access the canceled claim number under CADJ, CDAT, CERT, CEST, or PEST, the message CANCELED FILE NUMBER – PROCESSING PROHIBITED - XXXXXXXXX RETAINED is displayed on the M21 screen.  (XXXXXXXXX is the new claim number.)  The record can be reviewed under MINQ or SINQ.

**e.  Pending Issue File Processing.**  The system will check the pending issue file for issues under the canceled claim number.  The issue will be changed to the retained claim number unless an identical pending issue is found under the retained claim number.  If an identical pending issue is found under the retained claim number, the issue under the canceled claim number will be deleted.  Because a pending issue waiting authorization will be reset, the award must be reGAP'd before it can be authorized.

**f.  Full File Pass.**  During the next Full File Pass following the DUPC transaction, NOE messages 6042 and 6043 will be generated under the retained claim number.  If an accounts receivable or proceeds existed in the canceled Chapter 30 master record, the appropriate NOE message 6044, 6045, or 6046 will be generated under the retained claim number.  See  [Part 5: Chapter 5 - Notice of Exception Processing](https://eg5813aprl-int-nlb.egainstz.cloud/system/ws/v11/ss/article/554400000076311?portalId=554400000001018&usertype=customer&\$lang=en-us&) for further information on the NOE messages.

**2.24  CORR (MASTER RECORD CORRECTION) PROCEDURES**

The Chapter 30 benefit master record consists of various screens on the Target system.  See part II, chapter 4, for descriptions of the screens.  The Chapter 30 RPOs use the CORR command to correct certain fields on the Deduction/Receivable/Balance Data (M01) screen, the Education Master Record Status (M21) screen, the Chapter 30/106 Eligibility and Entitlement (M23) screen, and the General Certification (M24) screen.  Corrections to fields other than those shown below require amended award action.

**a. Ready Screen Entries.**  Ready screen entries for Chapter 30 master record correction are the same as for other benefits.  However, when attempting to access a Chapter 30 master record, the operator must enter CH30 in BENEFIT (type of benefit).  If the operator requests an invalid screen or the screen is not acceptable based on the command, an appropriate message will be displayed on the Chapter 30 Menu screen (30M).  Valid screen entries under the CORR Command are M01, M21, M23, and M24.  If a particular master record screen is not requested on the Ready screen, the M21 screen will be displayed.

**2.25  500 AND 50B SCREENS – BUFFALO REGIONAL OFFICE**

---

In order to accommodate the needs of the optical disk system used by the Buffalo RPO to process Chapter 30 claims, the Education Service developed the 500 and 50B screens to be used in lieu of VA Form 22-8945.  The screens are use in place of the printed award forms and added to the optical disk record upon completion of processing.  All of the fields on VA Form 22-8945 are contained on the screens.  (See pt. II, ch. 6, for description of the fields.)  [Figures 2.21](#F221) and [2.22](#F222) show all the fields on the 500 and 50B screens.
