**2.01 PURPOSE**

---

This chapter outlines those computer processing functions that are unique to chapter 32 and section 903. See part II, chapter 1, for general background on the education systems.  See part II, chapter 3, for a description of chapter 32 master record inquiry screens and part II, chapter 6, for BDN (Benefit Delivery Network) processing which chapter 32 has in common with chapter 35. 

**2.02 GENERAL**

---

The differences found in chapter 32 processing are partly due to the contributory nature of the program and the resulting banking file.  A detailed explanation of the banking system appears in MP-6, part IV, supplement 7-1.  This chapter covers three major areas of chapter 32 processing:  disallowance processing; eligibility screen processing; and certain unique award screen processing.  Chapter 32 uses the 310 Chapter 31/32/34 Eligibility screen for eligibility determinations.  This chapter also includes information on using this screen for computing chapter 34 eligibility.  Computing chapter 34 eligibility is still necessary for certain chapter 30 cases.  (See pt. V, ch. 3, for a discussion of such cases.)

**2.03  DISALLOWANCE PROCESSING**

---

**a.  The 411 Education Disallowance Screen.**  Each type of disallowance is listed by code and a descriptive phrase.  Select the applicable code or codes by entering "X" to the left of the code.  (See [fig. 2.01.](#f201))  (See RPO Letter 22-03-08).

(1)  Chapter 32 Disallowance Reason Codes.  In chapter 32 processing, the following reason codes either are not valid and will not be accepted or must be handled specially:

| 14 - Relationship Not Established | 84 - Education Plan Not Completed |
| 14 - Non-Continuous Cohabitation | 86 - Denied for Counseling Reasons |
| 15 - Widow Remarried (Inference) | 89 - Basic Elig Not Estab |

(ii)  Chapter 32 Only Codes.  Reason code 95 (Contributions Refunded) is the proper reason for a disallowance only if there has been a refund (see the DISENROLLMENT REFUND history segment on the M27 CH 32/903 Participant Contribution History screen) and no subsequent participation is of record (the M27 STATUS field will show the legend TERMINATED).  Select reason code 97 (No Contributions) only if a bank record does not exist (there are no M26-29 screens), or a bank record exists but has no allotment information (the STATUS field on the M26 Ch 32/903 Participant Account Summary screen or M27 screen will show the legend STATISTICAL).

![411 Disallowance Screen](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/m224_VIch32_201.jpg)  
**Figure 2.01.  411 Disallowance Screen**

(2)  Section 903 Disallowance Reason Codes

(i)  Disallowance processing for section 903 benefits is almost identical to chapter 32 disallowance processing.  However, reason codes 01, 95, and 97 allowed under chapter 32 are not acceptable for section 903 cases.

(ii)  If a disallowance is based on the service department's eligibility requirement  (i.e., no record is found, or the ELIGIBILITY STATUS field shows ELIGIBILITY NOT ESTABLISHED or ELIGIBILITY TERMINATED), use reason code 29 (Other).

(3)  Other Limitations

(i)  For both chapter 32 and section 903, multiple reasons may be entered, if in order.  If two or more reasons are entered, BDN prints only one reason code and description in the Disallowance block on VA Form 22-8945, Education Award.  BDN prints the information for the lowest numbered reason code entered.  The authorizer can review the other reasons entered by using the PINQ (Pending Issue Inquiry) command.  The letter that BDN generates will include paragraphs for all codes selected if such a paragraph exists.

(ii)  Do not combine reason code 22 (Death of Claimant) or reason code 29 with any other code.

**b.  GAD/GAP Edits.**  When the 411 screen is completed, the disallowance is ready to be generated and printed.  BDN prints the disallowance on VA Form 22-8945 and moves the claim automatically to the Authorization stage (if the operator enters GAP (Generate and Print) in the NEXT SCREEN field).  BDN shows the 501 Authorization screen for entry if the operator enters 501 in the NEXT SCREEN field.  (See pt II, ch. 6, for GAD (Generate and Display), GAP (Generate and Print), and 501 procedures.)  During GAD/GAP, BDN performs edits to ensure the accuracy of the disallowance action.  If BDN finds inconsistencies in the entered data, the 411 screen is shown with a message at the bottom.  The message may show information or describe an error.  (See [fig. 2.02](#f202).)

![411 Screen With Message]( https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/m224_VIch32_202.jpg)  
**Figure 2.02.  411 Screen With Message**

(1)  An informational message alerts the adjudicator to a specific condition.  Although an informational message allows processing to continue, the condition brought to the adjudicator's attention should be carefully reviewed and any necessary action taken before completing the current action.  Examples appear below:

(i)  DELIMITING DATE IN FUTURE-REVIEW-HIT ENTER TO CONTINUE.  This message results if reason code 66 (Beyond Delimiting Date) is entered and the claimant's delimiting date has not yet arrived.

(ii)  M/R AWARD ADJUSTMENT MAY BE NEEDED-HIT ENTER TO CONTINUE.  This message is generated when reason code 03 (Character of Discharge) is entered and a type A (active) or E (inactive) master record already exists.

(iii)  LETTER CANNOT BE GENERATED-NO DICTATED LETTER REQUIRED.  Reason code 16 (Failure to Prosecute) and 21 (Whereabouts Unknown) will generate this message during GAD and print a similar message on VA Form 22-8945.  A dictated letter is required for reason code 16 (Failure to Prosecute).  (See pt. III, ch. 9, regarding disallowances.)

(iv)  LETTER CANNOT BE GENERATED-DICTATED LETTER REQUIRED.  This message appears during GAD processing and on VA Form 22-8945 with the selection of reason codes 04 (Entitlement Forfeited), 22, 29, 79 (Course/School Does Not Meet Reqmts), and 80 (Course Not Approved).

(2)  An error message is one which requires resolution of conflicting information, either in the PIF (Pending Issue File), the master record, or both, before processing can continue.  Examples of this type of error are discussed below:

(i)  DISALLOWANCE RSN INCONSISTENT WITH M/R.  This message appears when a reason code does not agree with information in the master record.  For example, reason code 65 (Entitlement Exhausted) was selected when the master record reflects remaining entitlement.

(ii)  DISALLOWANCE RSN INCONSISTENT WITH PIF.  This message appears when information stored in the PIF conflicts with a disallowance reason.  For example, reason code 02 (No/Insufficient Qualifying Service) was selected when service dates stored in the PIF clearly reflect qualifying service.

(iii)  NO DELIMIT DATE FOUND.  This message appears when reason code 66 has been entered, but the 310 screen has not been completed and stored in the PIF for original claims.  This message also appears when reason code 66 has been entered, but the 310 screen has not been completed and stored in the PIF for reopened claims with a type J (disallowed) master record.

(iv)  RSN INVALID FOR THIS BENEFIT OR PAYEE.  This message is generated by the selection of a reason code which is not applicable for whatever chapter is being processed.

(v)  INCOMPATIBLE ENTRIES-REVIEW.  Entry of two or more conflicting reason codes will result in the generation of this message.

(3)  When the disallowance has been GAP'd or printed, sign VA Form 22-8945 and file it on the left flap of the claims folder.

**c.  Claims Authorization**.  The CAUT (Claims Authorization) command will be used to authorize chapter 32 and section 903 disallowances.  (See GAD, GAP, and 501 procedures in pt. II, ch. 6.)

**d.  Letter Generation**.  Chapter 32 and section 903 disallowance processing in most cases will generate a letter to the claimant with an explanation of the reason(s) for disallowance.  (See ch. 7 for letter paragraphs.) 

**e.  Transaction Codes.**  The disallowance action itself will determine which transaction codes will be sent to TCS (BDN Central System).  There are three possible disallowance transaction codes for chapter 32.  Each is discussed below:

(1)  Transaction Code 03D.  When no benefit account or master record exists or when only a type D (accounts receivable) master record exists, TCS prepares a transaction 03D.  When processed, this transaction will either establish a type J master record or convert a type D to a type J master record.  A letter to the claimant, unless otherwise directed, will be issued.  Disallowance codes 01, 02, 03, 65, and 66 will be sent as 03D transactions for processing and generation of an appropriate disallowance letter.

(2)  Transaction Code 03E.  When a benefit account exists (master record types A, E, or J), a disallowance action will result in the generation of a transaction code 03E if a letter is to be issued.  A transaction 03E will not affect any master record field although the transaction is sent for letter generation.

(3)  Transaction Code 03F.  Whenever a disallowance is processed against a benefit account and no letter is to be issued, BDN generates a transaction code 03F.  While transactions 03D and 03E actually transmit information to TCS, 03F transactions are not sent at all and will not alter any master record field.  There is also an instance when a transaction 03E will become a transaction 03F.  When a disallowance is processed against a benefit account and the disallowance reason would normally issue a letter, VA Form 22-8945 for the GAP'd award and the 501 screen shows transaction code 03E.  If the letter is suppressed during the authorization stage, the 03E converts to a transaction 03F.  See paragraph 4.02 for procedures.

**2.04  310 CHAPTER 31/32/34 ELIGIBILITY SCREEN**

---

**a.  General Functions.**  The 310 screen is used primarily to determine eligibility and entitlement to education benefits for chapters 32 and 34 original claims.  (It is not used for chapter 31 claims.)  The 310 screen may also be used to enter additional or corrected data for recomputing eligibility or entitlement.  Example: a claim, previously denied because of an other than honorable discharge, is reopened because of a change in the type of discharge.

(1)  Access.  The 310 screen is accessed via the BDN commands CDAT (Claims Data Entry) and CADJ (Claims Adjudication) from the Ready screen or from any 300 or 400 series screen.

(2)  Application.  Complete the 310 screen for all original chapter 32 or 34 awards.  Also, use the 310 screen for adding, changing, or correcting data related to eligibility or entitlement when an award is being processed at the same time.  If no award screen is being processed at the same time, any changes to eligibility or entitlement data must be made via CORR (Correction) to the veteran's master record. 

(3)  Worksheet.  The completed 310 screen may also be used as a worksheet for computing eligibility and entitlement in cases where an award will not be immediately processed.  After the operator has entered all the information and BDN has computed eligibility and entitlement, press the PRINT key to generate a demand print.  (If the operator enters DEL (Delete) in NEXT SCREEN, the data will not be stored in the PIF and a demand print will not automatically be generated.  Use of DEL will not cancel the pending issue from the PIF; DEL only removes the data entered on the screen.) 

**b.  Field Descriptions.**  Specific fields are described in the following subparagraphs.  These fields are separated into the following types: display, entry, and generated.

**g.  NEXT SCREEN Entries.**  If an operator enters DEL in NEXT SCREEN, BDN deletes the data entered or shown (on the 310 screen) from the PIF.  (Use of DEL will not delete the pending issue from the PIF.)  Enter RES (Restore) in NEXT SCREEN to remove the data entered, but to keep the data shown originally.  (RES restores the screen to its original displayed condition.)  Enter a 300 or 400 series screen number in NEXT SCREEN to continue processing.

 

**2.05  UNIQUE GAD/GAP PROCESSING**

---

Chapter 32 GAD/GAP processing differs slightly from chapter 35 processing described in part II, chapter 6.  These differences are described below:

**a.  Additional Award Line Generation.**  Under certain circumstances, GAD/GAP processing generates additional award lines.

(1)  Receipt of a DOD contribution may cause generation of a single award line.  When DOD submits their special DOD contributions (also known as "kickers"), DOD also gives an entitlement effective date (month and year).  If the designated effective date for a DOD contribution falls in an award period, BDN increases the claimant's pay rate on the first day of the following month, generating an award line reflecting the change with an effective reason code of 71.

**EXAMPLE**:      A chapter 32 claimant started contributing but stopped and then resumed contributions.  He has contributed $1,000 to the chapter 32 fund over a 20 month period.  The claimant's contribution allotments were $50 per month, and he is no longer contributing.  DOD authorized a separate contribution (or "kicker") of $2,000 with an effective date (month and year) of June 1984.  An operator had entered an award on the 312 or 412 General Education Award screens as shown below:

**b.  Computer Generated Monthly Rate Changes.**  A chapter 32 claimant's pay rate may be subject to monthly changes.  BDN calculates chapter 32 pay rates on a month-by-month basis using the exact entitlement months and dollars available for each calculation.  If a participant enters training while contributing to the chapter 32 fund, his or her pay rate fluctuates under the following conditions:

(1)  If the claimant has made more than 36 monthly allotments, or

(2)  The claimant changed his or her monthly allotment (e.g., from $50 to $60).  Under the above conditions, BDN calculates the proper rates during GAD/GAP processing and shows the generated lines on the award screen or print.  The rate will change slightly on the first day of each month, and the EFFECTIVE RSN for each generated line will be 71.  BDN projects continuing allotments (on an active allotment case) at the rate of the last allotment when projecting future monthly rates, up to the maximum of $2700 in contributions.

        **c.  Allotment Outside Service Period.**  In GAD/GAP processing, BDN compares a claimant's service periods against the allotments in the Contributions Account.  If the account has any allotments which are not within the claimant's service periods, BDN shows  "ALLOTMENT NOT IN SVC PRD: (YR-MO)."  The year and month shown in this message will be the first allotment month found outside the service periods.  For example, if a veteran's RAD date was June 15, 1984, and his or her Contributions Account shows allotments through September 1984, then the error message would be generated showing 84-07.  See paragraph 4.03 for procedures.
