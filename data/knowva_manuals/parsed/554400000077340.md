**4.02  READY SCREEN ENTRIES**

---

**a.**  Make the following entries on the Ready Screen:

(1)  Command MINQ.

(2)  Operator's password.

(3)  Screen number (optional).

(4)  File number.

(5)  BENEFIT Field.  Enter "CH30".

6)  Press the ENTER key.

**b.** BDN (Benefits Delivery Network, previously called Target) shows the M21 screen if a particular screen is not requested.  The ready screen shows "CH 30 RECORD NOT FOUND" when a benefit record is not located.

**c.**  PINQ (Pending Inquiry) and SINQ (Status Inquiry) processing remain unchanged for chapter 30.  (See ch. 3.)  There are five BDNRSs (Benefits Delivery Network Regional Systems) and four RPOs (Regional Processing Offices).  PINQ and SINQ commands only show issues pending within a given region.  Benefits Delivery Network (BDN) shows the S02 Selection screen if more than one record exists.  Select the appropriate record from the S02 screen.

**SUBCHAPTER II.  CHAPTER 30 DOD MASTER RECORD INQUIRY**

**4.10  CHAPTER 30 DOD DATA RECORD SCREEN (30D)**

---

**a.  General**.  There are two very distinct Chapter 30 DOD Data Record screens as described below.

(1)  ["Regular" 30D Screens.  These "regular" 30D screens are for all persons who meet the different chapter 30 category criteria defined in part V.  ([Figure 4.15](#f415) shows a 30D screen for a category I claimant.  This could be a regular 30D screen for any eligibility category.)  If any fields on the 30D screen are blank, it means DOD does not have the data in its chapter 30 database (the data may exist elsewhere in DOD).

(2)  "Special" 30D Screens.  These "special" 30D screens are limited to those individuals who were eligible to receive chapter 34 benefits, the Vietnam Era GI Bill, on December 31, 1989.  These "special" 30D screens for category II cases were created by VA (NOT DOD) based on data present during 1989.  These "special" 30D screens can not show any data other than that data present when these screens were created during 1989.  See [figure 4.16](#f416) for a category II 30D screen.

*NOTE:  There is also a Chapter 1606] DOD Data Record, commonly referred to as the "DOD" screen.  See Training Guide 22-99-5, The Chapter 1606 User Guide, part II, chapter 1 for details.]*

**b.  Inquiry Processing**

(1)  Restrictions.  The 30D screen is intended for use by all [ROs in resolving inquiries.  It is for use by RPOs in making eligibility determinations.  The 30D screen is NOT a final determination of eligibility.  ONLY THE RPOs may make an eligibility determination.  No VA personnel should make definitive statements as to eligibility, rates, or monthly kickers unless the RPO has actually processed a claim and BDN shows a master record (i.e., M21, M22 and M23 screens).  Inquiries that cannot be answered with the use of BDN] must continue to be referred to the RPO.

(2)  Ready Screen entries.  Make the following entries on the Ready screen to obtain a 30D screen:

(a)  Command MINQ.

(b)  Operator's password.

(c)  Screen number.  Enter 30D.

*NOTE:  "30D" in the screen number limits the BDN System search to the chapter 30 DOD database.*

(d)  File number.

(e)  BENEFIT Field.  Optional Entry of "CH30".

*NOTE:  This is true only if the operator is reviewing an "old" DOD screen.  The old "DOD" screen is the DOD screen in effect before the publication of TG 22-99-5.*

(4)  BDN shows "CH 106 RECORD (DOD) EXISTS" if the chapter 30 30D screen is being viewed and a chapter 1606 eligibility record exists.  Enter DOD in NEXT SCREEN field to review the chapter 1606 eligibility record.

*NOTE:  This information refers to an "old" DOD screen.  To obtain a current DOD screen, use the MINQ command with "DOD in the Screen Number field and"1606" in the Benefit field.]*

**e.  Service Data**.  [Regular 30D screens can show service data (Branch, EOD, RAD, Separation Reason, Character of Service, Enlistment Term, and Education Level) in two columns.  Special 30D screens can show service data in four columns.

(1)  Regular 30D screens show the service data currently in DOD's chapter 30 database.

(2)  Special 30D screens DO NOT SHOW current service information.  The first three columns show BIRLS data as of February 4, 1989.  The fourth column shows DOD service data as of November 7, 1989.  The fourth column EOD is a BASD (Basic Active Service Date).  This is an adjustable date and does not always reflect the date entered on duty.]
