# Chapter 33 Sub-Chapter 4.4 Books and Supplies Stipend

**Sub-Chapter 4. Books and Supplies Stipend**

Individuals pursuing an approved program of education may be eligible to receive benefits for books, supplies, equipment, and other educational costs, up to $1,000 for each academic year (prorated based on benefit level).

**4.01 IHLs**

---

**a. General**

Individuals training in a program offered at an Institution of Higher Learning (IHL) receive a Books and Supplies Stipend of up to $1,000 per academic year (pro-rated based on the individual’s benefit level).  Payment amounts are calculated based on the number of credits in which the individual is enrolled for each term.

**4.02 NCD Schools**

---

**a. NCD Books and Supplies**

Individuals training in a Non-College Degree NCD program at a non-IHL school will receive a lump-sum amount for books, supplies, equipment, and other educational costs in an amount equal to $83 for each month (pro-rated based on the individual’s benefit level).

**4.03 OJT/Apprenticeship Programs**

---

**a. OJT/APP Books and Supplies**

Individuals pursuing an on-the-job or apprenticeship training (OJT) program will receive a lump sum payment for the books and supplies stipend in an amount equal to $83/month for each month of the program (prorated for benefit level, partial months, and/or less than 120 hours per month).

**4.04 Active Duty**

---

**a. Active Duty Books and Supplies**

Effective October 1, 2011, individuals pursing a program of education while on active duty (or their spouses using transferred entitlement) are eligible for the books and supplies stipend. Benefits are awarded based on the IHL and NCD School rules, the same as for veterans.  Individuals on active duty (and their spouses using transferred entitlement) don’t qualify for benefits for an OJT or apprenticeship training program.
