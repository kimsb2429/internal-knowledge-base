# Chapter 33 Sub-Chapter 4.3 Housing Allowance

## Sub-Chapter 3. Housing Allowance

Individuals pursing a program of education at an Institution of Higher Learning (IHL) or a Non-College Degree (NCD) program at a non-degree granting school may be eligible to receive a Monthly Housing Allowance (MHA). The MHA benefit is based on the military’s Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH) rate for an E-5 with dependents. BAH rates, and the corresponding MHA payments, are subject to change with each academic year (effective August 1) based on the changes to DoD’s BAH rates the preceding January 1. The amount is prorated if eligibility is at less than the 100% benefit level and/or if the student is training at less than full time.

To qualify for MHA benefits, an individual must be:

* A Veteran,
* The spouse of a veteran using transferred entitlement,
* A child using transferred entitlement (regardless of the transferor’s duty status), or
* A Fry Scholarship recipient, and
* Enrolled at more than one-half time (i.e. Rate of Pursuit greater than .50).

### 3.01 Housing Benefit for In-Residence Study at U.S. Schools

---

**a. General**

Individuals enrolled at a U.S. school (even if the campus is outside the U.S.) that meet the basic MHA eligibility requirements and are attending at least one in-residence course will be paid a MHA based on the BAH rate for the ZIP Code of the school the claimant is attending in-residence.

### 3.02 Housing Benefit for In-Residence Study at Foreign Schools

---

**a. General**

Individuals enrolled at a foreign school (i.e. a school without a main campus in the U.S.) that meet the basic MHA eligibility requirements and are attending at least one in-residence course will be paid a MHA based on the national average of the BAH rates payable in the U.S.

### 3.03 Housing Benefit for Distance Learners

---

**a. Distance Learning**

Effective October 1, 2011, individuals that meet the basic MHA eligibility requirements and are taking *only* distance learning courses will be paid a MHA based on 50% of the national average of the BAH rates payable in the U.S.

### 3.04 Housing Benefit for Part-time Students

---

**a. Pro-Rated Housing**

Effective October 1, 2011, individuals enrolled less than full time receive a prorated amount of the applicable BAH rate. MHA is calculated by multiplying the BAH rate by the rate of pursuit and rounding to the nearest multiple of 10.

Below is a chart showing how a student’s enrollment would affect MHA payments in a term where 12 credits is full time.

 

| **Credits** | **Rate of Pursuit** | **% of BAH** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 12 | 1.00 | 100% |
| 11 | 0.92 | 90% |
| 10 | 0.83 | 80% |
| 9 | 0.75 | 80% |
| 8 | 0.67 | 70% |
| 7 | 0.58 | 60% |
| 6 | 0.50 | Not eligible |

### 3.05 Housing Benefit for OJT/Apprenticeship

---

**a. OJT/APP Housing**

Individuals pursuing an approved OJT or apprenticeship program are paid a monthly allowance (similar to the MHA paid for institutional training) as follows:

* **First 6 months**: A monthly amount equal to the BAH rate for an E-5 with dependents where the employer is located (prorated for benefit level and/or less than 120 hours per month)
* **Second 6 months:** A monthly amount equal to 80% of the BAH rate for an E-5 with dependents where the employer is located (prorated for benefit level and/or less than 120 hours per month)
* **Third 6 months:** A monthly amount equal to 60% of the BAH rate for an E-5 with dependents where the employer is located (prorated for benefit level and/or less than 120 hours per month)
* **Fourth 6 months:** A monthly amount equal to 40% of the BAH rate for an E-5 with dependents where the employer is located (prorated for benefit level and/or less than 120 hours per month)
* **Over 24 months:** A monthly amount equal to 20% of the BAH rate for an E-5 with dependents where the employer is located (prorated for benefit level and/or less than 120 hours per month)

### 3.06 Housing Allowance in Lieu of the Chapter 31 Subsistence Allowance

---

**a.** **Chapter 31 Housing**

Effective August 1, 2011 an individual eligible for Chapter 33 who is receiving benefits under Chapter 31 may elect to receive the applicable Chapter 33 MHA in lieu of the subsistence allowance.

Procedural Note: Vocational Rehabilitation Alternative Subsistence Allowance benefits are awarded based on vocational rehabilitation regulations and procedures, which are not identical to those used for awarding Chapter 33 MHA.
