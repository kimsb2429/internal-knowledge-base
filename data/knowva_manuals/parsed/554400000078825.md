**SUBCHAPTER I. OVERVIEW**

**3.01  INTRODUCTION**

---

**a. General.** Chapter 1607 has a separate BDN system which is similar to the separate chapter 30, 32, 35, and 1606 BDN systems.

(1) Key Terms. See part II, paragraph 1.01a.

(2) BDN Components. See part II, paragraph 1.02.

(3) BDN Payment Systems. See part II, paragraph 1.03.

4) BDN Award Types. See part II, paragraph 1.04.

(5) BDN Data Entry. See part II, paragraph 1.05.

(6) BDN Master Records--General. See part II, paragraph 1.08.

**b. Functions Missing from Chapter 1607 BDN**

(1) There is initially no advance payment function. This function will be added at a later time.

(2) DMDC (Defense Manpower Data Center) data cannot be accessed through BDN. DMDC does not exchange data with BDN. (Users can access DMDC data from a website as described in paragraph 2.05.) Implications: DMDC data is not available on the 310 screen as in chapters 30 and 1606. NOE (Notice of Exception) messages based on changes in DMDC data are not available.

(3) There is no monthly certification of attendance required of the claimant. Instead, the system automatically certifies the individual at the end of each month. See paragraph 7.16 for more details on this "auto cert" process.

**c. Functions Somewhat Different Than Other BDN Systems**

(1) 310 Screen. The 310 screen entries are done entirely by the operator. The 310 screen calculates entitlement and delimiting date (when applicable) but does not calculate eligibility. The operator must determine eligibility by reviewing the REAP Website date (see par. 2.05). See chapter 5 for a full discussion of the 310 screen.

(2) 312 Screen. There are some minor differences with other systems. See chapter 6.

**SUBCHAPTER III. MISCELLANEOUS PROCESSING**

**3.08  INTRODUCTION**

---

Miscellaneous processing refers to all of the screens and processes that are shared by, or are common to more than one system.  The most obvious of these is the READY screen which is used to access all BDN screens and functions, regardless of benefit type.

**a**.  In general, miscellaneous screens and processes do not change between systems.  The data entered may be program specific, but the entry is the same.  For example, on the Ready Screen, BENEFIT is a required entry for some programs.  For chapter 30, CH30 is entered; for chapter 1607, "1607" is entered.  But the screen itself remains the same.

**b**.  Miscellaneous processing and associated screens should not be confused with screens that are found in more than one system.  The 312 (Award) Screen and all of the Inquiry screens are found in chapter 30, chapter 32, and now chapter 1607.  These screens are not included in Miscellaneous processing because the screens are unique to each program, in that field names and data elements are tailored to the specific program.

**c**.  There are some miscellaneous processes not covered below but which are covered in chapter 4. These are the Ready screen, which is basic to all BDN processing, the M01 screen and PINQ and SINQ processing.

**3.09  FREQUENTLY USED MISCELLANEOUS SCREENS**

---

Following is a discussion of the most frequently used screens that are common to multiple benefit systems.  Unless noted all processing is the same as in the chapter 30 system, except “1607” is entered on the Ready Screen to access them.

**101 Screen**

The 101, Pending Issue Control Establishment, screen is used to enter a claimant’s name and address, Power of Attorney, and Fiduciary information.  It also is used to establish a Pending Issue.  It is accessed with either the CEST or CADJ commands.  For more see part II, paragraph 6.04d. 

![BDN 101 Pending Issue Control Establishment](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/M22-4_Part_11_Chapter_3_101.jpg)

 **S02 Screen**

The S02, Payee Selection screen is a menu screen that is generated by the system when more than one pending issue exists for a File number or when more than one type of benefit Master Record exists for a claimant. 

![BDN S02 Payee Selection Menu](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/M22-4_Part_11_Chapter_3_S02.jpg)

**601 Screen**

The 601, Change of Name and Address screen is used to enter changes of name and address, as well as EFT (Electronic Funds Transfer) information.  For more on this screen, go to m21-1, part V, chapter 13. For EFT instructions, go to M22-4, part I, chapter 10.

![BDN 601 Change of Name and Address Screen](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/M22-4_Part_11_Chapter_3_601.jpg)

**S99 Screen**

The S99, Disposition, screen is used to change the disposition of a pending issue.

![BDN S99 Disposition Screen](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/M22-4_Part_11_Chapter_3_S99.jpg)

**30M Screen**

The 30M, Screen Selection, screen displays all available screens in the chapter 1607 system.  It is similar to the 30M screen in the chapter 30 system.  Not all screens are available through all commands.  This screen is displayed during CADJ processing when no NEXT SCREEN entry is made on any screen.  It is displayed during inquiry processing when a NEXT SCREEN is entered that is unavailable through the command being used.  A sample 30M screen is shown below with screens available through CADJ processing in bold text.

![BDN 30M Screen Selection Screen](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/M22-4_Part_11_Chapter_3_30M.jpg)

**3.10 DEVELOPMENT SCREENS**

---

The following development screens are available in the new 1607 system.

* 203 Screen, General Claimant Data (CDEV and CADJ)
* 205 Screen, Education Development Letters (CDEV and CADJ)
* 207 Screen, Vocational Rehabilitation and Counseling (CDEV and CADJ)
* 209 Screen, Miscellaneous Letters (CDEV and CADJ)
* NDT Screen, Notice of Disagreement Tracking (CADJ only with 270 series end product)

   
**Accessing Development Screens**

These screens can be accessed through the CDEV command or during CADJ processing.  See chapter 5 for a full discussion of the 310 screen.

The 201 and 202 screens, used for requesting military data, are accessible through CADJ processing but are currently not functional.  If you access one of those screens, you will receive a message instructing you to use the PIES system.

The 204 Screen, Dependency Data, is not currently accessible, but was left in the system in case of future legislative changes.

**3.12 ADDITIONAL FUNCTIONS**

---

The following section discusses three important functions that are shared by multiple systems and that are now available in the chapter 1607 system.

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **EFT** | WIPP is used to access reports for Work in Progress.  All WIPP functions are available in the new system.  Unlike chapter 30, however, in order to isolate chapter 1607 issues, it is necessary to request chapter 1607 specific end product codes. See chapter 5 for a full discussion of the 310 screen. |
| **TINQ** | TINQ stands for Treasury Inquiry.  It provides check information on payments that have been authorized.  The appearance of check information on the TINQ screen does not, however, guarantee that the check or payment was actually issued.  The TINQ data is internal to VA for all programs, it is not updated by the Treasury Department.  The TINQ information is updated at the same time the payment voucher is released to Treasury.  If, for some reason, the voucher is lost or Treasury does not process it, the payments will not actually be issued even though TINQ will suggest that they were.  Failure to process a voucher is a rare occurrence, but it has happened.  If you notice a number of non-receipt inquiries even though TINQ indicates that payment has been made, notify Central Office. |

**Schedule of Operations**

The chapter 1607 Schedule of Operations will be modified somewhat and will follow essentially the same schedule as chapter 30 and 1606. BDN will update immediately upon authorization of an award and payments will be issued based upon Hine's Schedule of Operations. The Schedule of Operations is a valuable tool, particularly in inquiry processing, since it provides the scheduled date.
