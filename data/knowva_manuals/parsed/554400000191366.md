**11.01 General**

Effective August 1, 2021, [Public Law (P.L.) 116-315, Section 1010](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/PLAW-116publ315/pdf/PLAW-116publ315.pdf) added enrollment verification requirements for schools and beneficiaries using the Post-9/11 GI Bill (chapter 33). Schools are required to submit a second verification of enrollment following the period during which the beneficiary is able to withdraw from courses without penalty. Additionally, all Chapter 33 beneficiaries enrolled at IHLs and NCDs, including the Edith Nourse Rogers STEM Scholarship and VET TEC, must verify their enrollment status each month to continue receiving their monthly housing allowance (MHA) and/or kicker payments. The monthly enrollment verification does **NOT** apply to OJT/Apprenticeship, Flight (at a non-IHL facility) or correspondence programs.

Due to system limitations, the implementation of the monthly enrollment verification by beneficiaries was completed in phases.  Phase 1 was deployed August 1, 2021 for Non-College Degree (NCD) beneficiaries attending enrollments beginning on or after August 1, 2021.

Phase 2 was implemented for Institution of Higher Learning (IHL) beneficiaries attending enrollments beginning on or after December 17, 2021.

**SUBCHAPTER I.  TWO CERTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS**

**11.02 Overview of the Two Certification Requirement**

The two certification requirement is the responsibility of the school. In addition to submitting an initial enrollment certification for each beneficiary, Institutions of Higher Learning (IHLs) and Non-College Degree (NCD) facilities, are required to submit a subsequent enrollment certification for each enrollment period. With one exception, this requirement applies to all chapter 33 enrollments at IHLs and NCDs, including the Edith Nourse Rogers STEM Scholarship. The one exception is for schools that use a flat tuition and fee structure and have been granted a waiver. 

**11.03 Timing for Submission of Certifications**

**a. Initial Enrollment Certification.** For prompt reporting, the initial certification needs to be submitted between 180 calendar days (for chapter 33 benefits) before and 30 calendar days after the first day of the enrollment period.  

**b. Second Enrollment Certification (Verification).** Schools must submit an additional enrollment certification (verification) after the last day on which a beneficiary can withdraw from the course or program of education without penalty, known as the drop-add period. For this purpose, "without penalty” has been defined as either an academic penalty or a financial penalty, whichever occurs first.

1. **Schools with a drop-add period.** The second verification should be received no later than 30 calendar days after the last day of the school’s drop-add period or 60 calendar days from the first day of the enrollment period, whichever occurs first.
2. **Schools that do not have a drop-add period.** The second verification must be received no sooner than 30 calendar days after the first day of the enrollment period, but within 60 calendar days from the first day of the enrollment period.

**11.04 Second Submissions that Meet Verification Requirements**

Submission of only an initial enrollment certification, no matter the timing, does not meet the verification requirements (e.g., it is not acceptable for a school to just submit one enrollment after the drop-add period). In certain situations, notification of graduation or program completion information can be accepted in lieu of a second enrollment certification.

1. **Dual Certifications**. Many schools use a practice of submitting an initial enrollment certification with $0.00 in the tuition and fees field(s). After the drop-add period, the tuition and fees are certified by amending the term to add the tuition and fees amount. This is called **dual certification**. If the amended (tuition and fees only) certification is submitted within the time limits explained above, this will meet the two certification requirement.
2. **Enrollment Changes**. If a beneficiary makes changes to their enrollment during the drop-add period or within the first 30 calendar days of the term, the school should follow current guidance to report changes to VA.  If reported after the drop-add period, the submission of a change to an enrollment period will suffice as the second certification and meet the two certification requirement when received within the established time limit.
3. **Unchanged Certifications**. If the school has no changes to make to the enrollment or tuition and fees for a term, a second enrollment certification is still mandatory. In VA-ONCE, this should be done by amending the enrollment, making no changes, and submitting the certified term. Comments or remarks should be avoided (unless necessary) on these “unchanged” amendments to allow the greatest likelihood of automation by VA.
4. **Retroactive Certifications.**When schools are submitting enrollment certifications for terms that have already been completed, the two certification requirement remains in effect. An amended term should be submitted on the next day to maximize automation, as described for “unchanged” certifications above.
5. **Paper Certifications.**If a school is unable to certify enrollments using VA-ONCE, the SCO should submit two enrollment certifications using a VA Form 22-1999.  The first should be sent to VA when the beneficiary enrolls. If there are no changes after the drop-add period, schools should submit a duplicate enrollment certification with remarks stating: “No Change/Verification Only” within the time limits previously described. If there are changes to the original enrollment, a Notice of Change in Student Status (VA Form 22-1999b) should be reported after the drop-add period, which will suffice for the second certification.
6. **Report of Graduation or Program Completion.** If the enrollment period is less than 30 calendar days, the second enrollment certification is still required. If the program is less than 30 calendar days, and the beneficiary graduates or completes the program, the school’s Notice of Change in Student Status reporting “Graduation” (degree program) or "End of Term or Course" (non-college degree program) can serve as the second certification.

**11.05 Effects on Payment (Two Certification Requirement)**

The two certification requirement has no bearing on VA payments made to schools or beneficiaries, and payments will continue as usual. Accurate and timely submission of the second enrollment certification will be reviewed during compliance surveys.

**SUBCHAPTER II.  ENROLLMENT VERIFICATION AND EDUCATION CALL CENTER IMPACT**

**11.06 Overview of Enrollment Verification**

Monthly enrollment verification is the responsibility of the beneficiary, no one other than the beneficiary can do the monthly enrollment verification . Chapter 33 beneficiaries must verify their enrollment status each month to continue to receive their monthly housing allowance (MHA) and/or kicker payments. Enrollment verification can be done on the last day of each month. If a beneficiary fails to verify for two consecutive months, VA will withhold any additional MHA and/or kicker payments until verified by the beneficiary. This requirement applies to all chapter 33 beneficiaries enrolled at IHLs and NCDs, including the Edith Nourse Rogers STEM Scholarship and VET TEC. The monthly enrollment verification does **NOT** apply to OJT/Apprenticeship, Flight (at a non-IHL facility) or correspondence programs.

**NOTE:**If your term **officially ends** earlier than the end of the month, (example: on May 20, 2022), you are able to verify your final enrollment status on the **last day of your term** (May 20, 2022). However, payments will be sent out at the end of the month as they do normally.

**11.07 Methods Used for Beneficiary Verification of Enrollment**

**a. Text messaging.** The primary system for monthly verification is the text messaging system. Beneficiaries with a U.S. mobile phone number (including Puerto Rico) can use text messaging to verify enrollment. Beneficiaries who provide a valid mobile phone number will receive a text message inviting them to “opt-in” to text message verification. Those who reply “Yes” will then be able to verify their monthly enrollment via text messaging. Those who reply “No” will be defaulted to email, as long as a valid email address is in the DGI - Chapter 33 Processing System. The link within that invitation text message will expire in 14 calendar days. 

1. **Opted-in to Text**. Beneficiaries will receive the first text verification message on the last day of the month.
   2. If the beneficiary selects “Yes”, their enrollment will be verified and payment released automatically by the Chapter 33 Processing System, and an ATTENDY (see Table 11.01) document will go to TIMS. Once verified a confirmation text will be sent to the beneficiary.
   3. If the beneficiary selects “No”, their payments will go on hold, and an ATTENDN (see Table 11.01) document will go to TIMS, creating an active token for VCE review.

A follow-up text message will be sent after three calendar days and the link to verify enrollment will remain open for three additional calendar days. After seven calendar days the conversation closes, and the system is updated with “no response” to the verification message.

2. **Opted-out of Text**. Beneficiaries will be auto enrolled in email verification and will receive an email message as outlined in the subsequent section.

**b. Email.** As an alternative, beneficiaries have the option to verify their enrollment information each month through an automated email system. If a beneficiary opts-out of text messages, they will be automatically enrolled into the email system, so long as a valid email address is in the Chapter 33 Processing System. 

1. The TIMS Doc Types (see Table 11.01) for the email verification are the same as Text message Doc Types.
2. Beneficiaries will be sent an automated email on the last day of the month that needs to be verified. The email has two links. One for “Yes, my enrollment is the same” and one for “No, my enrollment has changed.” The beneficiary will be sent a second email, serving as a reminder, after seven calendar days without a response. The conversation will close after 14 calendar days without a response.

3. If the beneficiary selects “Yes”, their enrollment will be verified automatically by the Chapter 33 Processing System, and an ATTENDY (see Table 11.01) document will go to TIMS. The beneficiary will be sent to a landing page that is housed on VA.gov. This page will display a confirmation that we received their verification.

4. If the beneficiary selects “No”, their payments will go on hold, and an ATTENDN (see Table 11.01) document will go to TIMS, creating an active token. The beneficiary will be sent to a landing page on VA.gov that confirms their response. It will also ask them to contact their School Certifying Official to adjust their enrollment if necessary.
5. If the beneficiary does not respond to a second consecutive month’s verification email, their future 06I payments will be held, and an ATTENDNR (see Table 11.01) document will go to TIMS, creating an active token. Their future housing payments will be held until the beneficiary manually verifies their enrollment by calling the ECC.

6. If the beneficiary clicks an expired link, they will be sent to a landing page on VA.gov.  This page tells them that no action was taken because the link has expired and directs them to contact the ECC.

**c. Telephone**. The beneficiary may also call the ECC to verify their enrollment status. 

ECC staff can verify the enrollment within the Chapter 33 Processing System by performing the following steps:

1. Verify the beneficiary’s identity using standard protocol.
2. If the beneficiary verifies that their enrollment is the same (i.e., “yes”), verification may occur (see section 11.10).
3. If the beneficiary states that their enrollment has changed (i.e., “no”), additional action is needed:  
   * Create a Report of Contact (119) describing any changes to the enrollment. The 119 must be captured to TIMS using one of the document types specified in Table 11.01.
   * The ECC agent should advise the beneficiary to contact their School Certifying Official (SCO) to notifiy of the enrollment status change if a 1999b has not been received.
   * Route claim, following local guidance, to the appropriate chapter 33 supplemental queue for processing.

**d. Other**. An email from the claimant received via Ask VA (AVA) or through any VA e-mail system may be used to verify the enrollment status.

**11.08 Manual Enrollment Verification Opt-In Methods**

**Opt-in Method (Manual Updates).** Education Call Center Technicians (ECCT) will assist with updates to a beneficiary’s preference of their enrollment verification messaging method when assistance is requested by the caller by performing the following steps:

1. Verify the beneficiary’s identity using standard protocol.
2. Ensure the mobile phone number field on the BIO screen reflects a mobile number (required in order to properly receive the text messages),
3. Verify and update when necessary the beneficiary’s email address on the BIO screen (required for email communications)
4. Review the current Opt-In Method on the BIO screen which may have been updated automatically. If the current setting reflects the preference of the beneficiary, no additional action is required.
5. When the current Opt-In Method requires updating document the opt-in or opt-out request in The Image Management System (TIMS) with a TIMS Note or a Report of Contact form (119). 
   1. If there is no open claim token when adding the 119 to TIMS, use the document type "COPYA".
   2. After capture of the 119 is complete, change the document type to "119", this will prevent opening a claim token in error.
6. There are three manual update setting which address any scenario. Apply one of these three Opt-In Method settings below:
   1. **Opt-in Text:** If the beneficiary calls the ECC to opt in to receive text messages, the ECCT will navigate to the Claimant BIO screen and manually change the **Opt****In****Method** field to “Text” and **Opt****In****Status** field to “Yes.” This will allow the beneficiary to receive enrollment verification text messages each month.

**11.09 Effects on Payment**

Beneficiary enrollment verification affects VA payments (i.e., monthly housing allowance (MHA) and/or kicker payments) made to beneficiaries.

**a. Standard Enrollment Verification Requirements.** As a result of the enrollment verification requirements, DGI creates a separate lump sum 06I payment for each month where a housing and/or kicker payment is due. These monthly 06I payments are released automatically by DGI for the first two months and subsequently thereafter if the beneficiary supplies a positive (i.e., “Yes, my enrollment is the same”) monthly verification response. If the beneficiary responds “No, my enrollment has changed” or fails to verify for two consecutive months, MHA and/or kicker payments will be held and VCE/ECC staff will need to take appropriate action (see section 11.09). A hold can be removed by either a positive verification response or a termination of the term with no other future awards scheduled.

If the beneficiary responds “No, my enrollment has changed” or fails to verify for two consecutive months, MHA and/or kicker payments will be held and VCE will need to take appropriate action ("No" response see section 11.10/ fails to verify see section 11.11).

**b. Enrollment Verification Requirements for Retroactive Certifications.** Beneficiaries are not required to verify their enrollment status when their school submits enrollment certifications for fully completed terms. Since the term was completed prior to processing in DGI, the Work Product Summary page will display an 06I payment for all Housing/Kickers benefits due for the term. If the term was in progress and partially completed when the enrollment certification was processed, the Work Product Summary page will display an 06I payment for Housing/Kickers benefits due for the months completed prior to processing. Standard enrollment verification requirements will apply for months not fully completed.

**c. Verification of Enrollment Payment Status.**

The possible payment statuses which will be listed on the “Verification of Enrollment” screen include:

* Successful Automatic Payment – The payment was automatically transmitted and accepted by BDN.
* Scheduled Automatic Payment – The payment is scheduled to be sent to BDN on a future date.
* Paused – The payment has been scheduled; however, a work product is pending in LTS. The work product must be completed before the payment is sent to BDN.
* Hold – All payments are held due to the beneficiary’s failure to verify enrollment for two (2) or more consecutive months.

**NOTE:** Enrollment verification is required by the beneficiary for all periods listed under “Unverified Periods” to prevent future payments from being placed in a “Hold” status.

**SUBCHAPTER III.  ENROLLMENT VERIFICATION**

**11.10** **Processing Monthly Verifications**

**a. Claims Processing Impact/Responsibilities.**

1. The new requirement for schools to submit multiple certifications to comply with new verification requirements will increase workload. Veterans Claims Examiners (VCEs) should be made aware of this change and follow the guidance for processing multiple enrollment certifications. VCEs should continue to process enrollment certifications (VA Form 22-1999) and notices of change in student status (VA Form 22-1999b) submitted using current guidance.  If an “unchanged” amendment is received, it should be accepted from VA-ONCE or manually entered into DGI, the Chapter 33 Processing System. The DGI work product should be authorized with payments, letter(s) should be released, and an appropriate supplemental end product should be cleared.

2. The Image Management System (TIMS) has been updated to reflect new documents for chapter 33 verification (see Table 11.01). If a phone call is received, the contact form should be captured as one of the following document types to ensure proper routing through TIMS.  These TIMS documents will route to the typical chapter 33 supplemental queues for routine processing.

**NOTE:** Text Doc Types will be used for either text or email verification messaging with the method indicated.

3. DGI has been updated to automatically accept text messages which state “YES”. These affirmative chapter 33 text messages will NOT be sent to TIMS.  DGI will automatically schedule the 06I housing payments to be released 3 business days prior to the end of each month. **NOTE:** STEM “ATTENDY” messages will continue to be sent to TIMS as they require manual fiscal payment processing.

4. If the beneficiary responds “NO” to the text message, DGI will automatically hold all MHA and/or kicker payments. A TIMS document (ATTENDN) will be created as an active claim token and routed to the chapter 33 supplemental queue(s) for routine processing. When the VCE receives the ATTENDN document, the following actions will be performed:

1. 1. Review the TIMS folder for any reported changes from the school.
   2. Process any pending enrollment certifications or adjustments found as usual.
   3. If there is no evidence of a change submitted by the school in the TIMS folder, the VCE must contact the school to request any updated enrollment information.
   4. Update TIMS with a Report of Contact (119) describing any changes or confirmation that the beneficiary remains certified correctly.
   5. Process reported changes documented in TIMS.
   6. Ensure the DGI system generated letter is not suppressed.
   7. After authorization, or if no changes reported the enrollment verification screen must be updated.
   8. Remember the “ATTENDN” represents the beneficiary stating that the enrollment is not verified as certified.

**NOTE:** If the school confirms over the phone that a change in enrollment was made, the VCE can process the change based on the telephone contact but must ensure the Report of Contact memorializing the action is captured in the TIMS folder. (The VCE should remind the school that a proper submission of enrollment or change in enrollment is required for compliance purposes.)

**11.11 Delinquent Verification Processing**

**a. Beneficiary Fails to Verify Enrollment for Two Consecutive Months**

As previously stated, once the work product has been authorized the Enrollment Verification screen must be accessed to release the hold on the MHA and/or kicker payments.

When a beneficiary does not respondafter two consecutive months**,** the Chapter 33 Processing System will automatically hold any future housing and/or kicker payments. A TIMS document (ATTENDNR) will be created and sent to TIMS as an active claim token.

1. 1. The VCE will open the TIMS document and review if the text was for a chapter 33 claim.
2. 2.  If for STEM, route to the appropriate STEM queue for action by the Buffalo STEM Team. **NOTE:** Due to system limitations, STEM requires monthly verifications and manual processing.
3. 3.  Otherwise, create, send, and capture into TIMS a properly formatted letter to the beneficiary using the attached “**We Need You to Verify Your Enrollment Letter**” template attached at the bottom of this chapter.
4. 4.  Finish the TIMS claim token.

**11.12 TXTERROR (Foreign or Invalid Phone and Invalid or Missing Email) Processing**

**a. TXTERROR TIMS Document Type.**When the system is unable to send a text message for any of the above reasons, an active “TXTERROR” TIMS document is created. Expanded functionality of email verification requires no action when the BIO screen Opt In Method has been automatically set to “Email” with Status “Yes” indicating the beneficiary will be receiving email enrollment verification messages.

**NOTE:** Due to system limitations, phone numbers that originate either from a foreign country or U.S. territory (excluding Puerto Rico) are displayed as invalid and will not allow text message use for enrollment verification purposes.

1. The VCE will open the TIMS document and verify if the text was for a chapter 33 claim.
2. If for STEM, route to the appropriate STEM queue for action by the Buffalo STEM Team
3. Otherwise, review the TIMS folder for a new mobile phone number or email,
   1. If no new information is of record no action is necessary in the Chapter 33 Processing System. **DO NOT** change Opt-In method to “Text” and Status to “No.” This will cause the beneficiary to contact the Education Call Center to verify each month.

1. 2. If the BIO screen Opt-In Method is set to “None” or “Not Selected” or Opt-In Status setting indicates “No” or “Invalid” additional processing is required.
      1. Review TIMS. If a new mobile phone number or email is on record in the file **and** the file does not indicate the beneficiary opted out from both text and email messages, update the BIO screen accordingly and change their Opt-In Method to “Text” or “Email” with Status of “Yes” whichever is appropriate.
      2. If the Opt-In Method could not be updated, create, send, and capture into TIMS a properly formatted letter to the beneficiary using the attached “**How to Verify Your Enrollment Letter**” template at the bottom of this chapter.
      3. Additionally if the record could not be updated, email the "**Pre-formatted email message to the School Certifying Official**" language attached at the bottom of this chapter.
      4. Finish the TIMS claim token.

**11.13 Letters**

Changes have been made to certain VA letters in order to comply with Section 1010. These changes include:

**a. Certificate of Eligibility (COE) Letter**

A section has been added to the letter regarding the requirement for the beneficiary to both verify their enrollment information and keep their contact information up to date.

**b. Payment (Award 3) Letter**

The letter has been rearranged and updated with new language, bringing it into alignment with Section 1010 requirements. Specific sections affected included housing payments, kickers, and the addition of a new section discussing the requirement to verify enrollment information.
