**SUBCHAPTER I. DGIB MANAGED SERVICE**

**5.01 Managed Service**

1. **General.** Digital GI Bill (DGIB) is a Managed Service. A Managed Service makes it easier to integrate applications, manage them, and increase efficiencies. Previously, each application had a unique purpose and provided a specific service. Most applications were siloed and often unable to work together and share data. In order to build a Managed Service, many of the existing applications were replaced and updated to properly integrate with each other. The new applications (DGIB microservices) make up the DGIB Managed Service.
2. **Enrollment Manager.** Enrollment Manager (EM) is one of the applications that comprise the Digital GI Bill, a new digital solution that modernizes VA systems and allows users direct access to a single platform with real-time communications and integrated access to records. Enrollment Manager is a School Certifying Official (SCO) facing platform that streamlines claims processing functionality by helping Certifying Officials add/remove GI Bill students at schools, submit/manage enrollments for students, and view/update student information. Enrollment Manager replaced VA Online Certification of Enrollment (VA-ONCE) and is VA’s system for Certifying Officials to certify student enrollments and changes in enrollment. Certifications submitted through Enrollment Manager are the electronic versions of VA Forms 22-1999 *Enrollment Certification*, 22-1999b *Notice of Change in Student Status*, 22-6553c *Monthly Certification of Flight Training*, and 22-6553d-1 *Monthly Certification of On-The-Job and Apprenticeship Training*.
3. **The Chapter 33 Processing System (DGI) and Enrollment Manager Integration.** When the Long Term Solution (LTS) claims processing system transitioned to the Managed Service, the application became known as the Chapter 33 Processing System (DGI). Transition to the Managed Service resulted in improved claims processing with increased automation.
4. **Benefits Manager and Enrollment Manager Integration.** Benefits Manager (BM) is the Digital GI Bill application for Veterans Claims Examiners (VCEs) to process awards. Initially, Benefits Manager will be used to adjudicate eligibility, entitlement, and awards for chapters 30,1606, and 35 beneficiaries. In the future, chapter 33 will also be processed using Benefits Manager. Until then, chapter 33 will continue to be processed through the Chapter 33 Processing System. Benefits Manager coordinates directly with Enrollment Manager and payment services.
5. **Approval Manager and Enrollment Manager Integration.** Approval Manager (AM) is another Digital GI Bill application. Approval Manager is a future platform that will replace the Web Enabled Approval Management System (WEAMS) as the Approval Management System of Record. Until Approval Manager goes live and is the sole storage site of approval, information from WEAMS will be analyzed by the Chapter 33 Processing System.
6. **VA Education Platform Portal and Enrollment Manager.** VA Education Platform Portal, also referred to as User Access Manager (UAM) to internal users, is a centralized service used to manage all user roles for DGIB systems. Assigned Oversight and Accountability Division staff are able to approve or deny Certifying Official access requests to Enrollment Manager. In addition, Certifying Officials utilize the VA Education Platform Portal to authorize access for Assistants and Read-Only Certifying Officials associated with their facility.
7. **Claimant Service and Enrollment Manager Integration****.** When logged into Enrollment Manager, Certifying Officials have the ability to search for students with existing accounts in VA systems via the Claimant Service. If a student is not found, Certifying Officials have the ability to add a new student to Enrollment Manager and Claimant Service. The Certifying Official will receive alert messages in Enrollment Manager to assist them with tracking the student’s enrollment(s) and checking their eligibility through the system.

**Note 1**: Unlike VA-ONCE, a student’s address data is not stored in Enrollment Manager, it is stored in Claimant Service and displayed in Enrollment Manager. A change to a student’s address in an Education (EDU) system is always a change in Claimant Service. There are also triggers to check for a change of address within VA Profile/Corp DB, which will allow Claimant Service to update if a newer address is submitted by the student online or elsewhere in VA.

**Note 2**: Initial release of Enrollment Manager allows Certifying Officials to edit the address of a student whose record exists in Claimant Service. When a Certifying Official edits the information in Enrollment Manager, it will immediately update the information in the Claimant Service. The ability for a Certifying Official to edit this information will be restricted in a future release to read-only. The future release will allow Certifying Officials to add an address for a student only if they do not have an address on record with VA.

8. **CWINRS and Enrollment Manager Integration.** Corporate WINRS (CWINRS) is a Case Management System that allows Veteran Readiness and Employment (VR&E) staff to move their cases through the Vocational Rehabilitation process. Using Enrollment Manager, Certifying Officials have the ability to submit/manage enrollments for chapter 31 students to VR&E for processing in CWINRS.

**Note:** Due to CWINRS limitations, paper OJT/APP and flight certification forms must be submitted to VR&E. Functionality to submit those certifications electronically has been disabled in Enrollment Manager for chapter 31.

1. **ECAP and Enrollment Manager Integration.** The Education Certification Automated Processing (ECAP) sends a manual/automated acknowledgement of each enrollment to Enrollment Manager. This allows Certifying Officials to check the status and verify whether the enrollment was automated or manual.

10. **Iterative Release.** The first version of Enrollment Manager is known as IOC (Initial Operation Capability). IOC is the state achieved when capability is available in its minimally useful deployable form. Improvements and enhanced functionality are in development and will be incorporated in future releases. When development is complete, FOC (Full Operation Capability) will be achieved.

**SUBCHAPTER II. ACCESSING ENROLLMENT MANAGER VIA VA EDUCATION PLATFORM PORTAL**

**5.02 VA Education Platform Portal**

User access controls and permissions are managed for both internal users (e.g., VA employees, State Approving Agency, contractors) and external users (e.g., Certifying Officials, Read-Only Certifying Officials, Assistants) in the VA Education Platform Portal. Identity verification and authentication are required and methods vary for internal and external users.

**5.03 External Users**

1. **Identity Verification Using ID.me or Login.gov**. External users will navigate to the VA Education Platform, logon using Single Sign On External (SSOe), select ID.me or Login.gov, and follow the steps to log in using their account. Many Certifying Officials already use an ID.me or Login.gov account for “Ask VA” inquiries. If an external user needs to create an account, they must first verify their identity and will need their Social Security number, driver’s license or passport, and mobile phone or email address. Because the account is tied to the individual, and not the employer, Certifying Officials are instructed to use their personal email address vs. school email address when creating an ID.me or Login.gov account.

![ID.me or Login.gov Sign In](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/V210.01 ID.me or Login.gov Sign In.png)

**Figure 5.01** **ID.me or Login.gov Sign In**

2. **International Identity Verification.** Foreign Certifying Officials with an address outside the U.S. can use ID.me to verify their identity. They are required to show their identity documents on a video call with an ID.me representative. Even though individual users can verify their identity if they live outside the U.S., identity cannot be validated if they are not able to produce the required primary and secondary identification documents. For instance, if an individual does not have a U.S. Social Security number; they need to show a valid U.S. or International Passport as one of their identity documents. Certifying Officials experiencing problems with the verification process need to work directly with ID.me or Login.gov to resolve their issue.

**Note 1:** Due to a wider range of acceptable primary and secondary documentation, ID.me is preferable to Login.gov for foreign identity verification.

**Note 2:** “Ask VA” also requires multifactor authentication and is integrated to ID.me/Login.gov; therefore, Foreign Certifying Officials from countries that cannot utilize ID.me/Login.gov or who cannot successfully validate their identity, are permitted to use the existing certification submission method of postal mail. See [Procedural Advisory: Foreign School Certification Submission (va.gov)](https://vaww.vrm.km.va.gov/system/templates/selfservice/va_kanew/help/agent/locale/en-US/portal/554400000001048/content/554400000220192/(A)-2023-19-January:-Procedural-Advisory:-Foreign-School-Certification-Submission).

3. **Subsequent Multifactor Authentication:** Multifactor authentication, also known as 2-factor or dual authentication, is an extra layer of protection on an individual’s account. Individuals who are able to successfully validate their identity, will use multifactor identification each time they sign into a VA website or application. Certifying Officials experiencing problems with authentication need to work directly with ID.me or Login.gov to resolve their issue.

![ID.me Multifactor Authentication](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/V210.02 ID.me Multifactor Authentication.png)

**Figure 5.02** **ID.me Multifactor Authentication**

**5.04 Internal Users**

**Identity Verification Using Personal Identity Verification (PIV).** Internal users with PIV credential cards logon using Single Sign On Internal (SSOi). If an internal user is unable to logon successfully, they should work within their supervisory chain. 

**5.05** ****Access** Requests from External Users**

 

1. **Landing Page.** Once internal or external users have successfully logged in, they are navigated to the **Landing Page**. External users initiate access requests to Enrollment Manager by selecting **Request Access** or **Legacy VA-ONCE Users.**

![VA Education Platform Landing Page](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.03 VA Education Platform Landing Page.png)

**Figure 5.03 VA Education Platform Landing Page**

2. **Request Access.**  New Certifying Officials initiate Enrollment Manager access by selecting **Request Access**on the **Landing Page**. A pop-up modal displays fields requesting **First Name**, **Last Name**, **Email**, **Role**, **State**, and includes checkboxes for acknowledgement of the submission of the VA Form 22-8794, *Designation of Certifying Official(s)*, and Section 305 training certificate(s). Once access is requested, a notification email is sent to the Education Liaison Representative (ELR) of jurisdiction mailbox, while a confirmation email is sent to the requestor.
3. **Facility Code**. Reserved for future use. Enhanced capability to include a field for the facility code on requests is expected in a future release.

 

![Request Access](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/V210.04 Request Access.png)

**Figure 5.04 Request Access**

**5.06** **Access Request Approval**

1. **Assigned Oversight and Accountability Division Staff Approval**. After a request has been submitted by a Certifying Official, it proceeds to the assigned Oversight and Accountability Division staff of jurisdiction for approval. From the **Landing Page**, assigned Oversight and Accountability Division staff will select the **Location** (e.g., state, region) from a drop-down located at the top-right of the page and then select **SCO Access Manager** located at the top-left of the page.

![Figure Request Access ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.05 Location - Access Request Approval.png)

**Figure 5.05 Location – Access Request Approval**

2. The **SCO Access Manager** page displays a list of pending requests for the selected location. Sorting and filters allow assigned Oversight and Accountability Division staff to locate Certify Officials for initial (e.g., **Approve**, **Deny**, **Mng Facility**) and subsequent (e.g., **Mng Facility**, **Revoke**) action. Each field (e.g., **Firstname**, **Lastname**, **Email**, **Status**) has filter functionality. When filtering by **Status**, **Pending** and **Approved**, Certifying Officials will be displayed. Once **Revoked**, Certifying Officials will not be listed on the **SCO Access Manager** page.
3. In conjunction with normal 22-8794 processing procedures, assigned Oversight and Accountability Division staff will act to either **Approve** or **Deny** Enrollment Manager access requests submitted by Certifying Officials. An automatic notification email will be sent to the requestor once the request is approved or denied. If denied, assigned Oversight and Accountability Division staff have the option to add a reason that will display in the email.

![SCO Access Manager Page ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/V210.06 SCO Access Manager Page.png)

**Figure 5.06 SCO Access Manager Page**

4. Once approved, action buttons display **Mng Facility** and **Revoke.** Assigned Oversight and Accountability Division staff will need to select **Mng Facility** to **Assign** the facility code(s) for main and branch campuses. Extension campuses, indicated with an “X” as the third character of the facility code, are associated by checking the **Include Extensions** box.

![Associate Facility Code(s)](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/V210.07 Associate Facility Code(s).png)

**Figure 5.07** **Associate Facility Code(s)**

**Note**: Until the VA Education Platform Portal has the functionality to automatically revoke Certifying Officials who have been withdrawn in Approval Manager (WEAMS in the interim), assigned Oversight and Accountability Division staff will need to complete this action manually by selecting **Revoke**.

5. Once a Certifying Official has been approved, the **Open Enrollment Manager** button will become enabled on the **Landing Page**. Certifying Officials can access Enrollment Manager via the **Open Enrollment Manager** button and subsequently bookmark the Enrollment Manager login page for direct access.

**5.07  Access Requests from Legacy VA-ONCE Users**

1. **Legacy VA-ONCE Users****.** From the **Landing Page**, existing Certifying Officials with VA-ONCE access will initiate Enrollment Manager access by selecting **Legacy VA-ONCE Users**. A pop-up modal displays fields requesting their existing VA-ONCE credentials: **User Name**, **User Password**, and **State**.

![Legacy VA-ONCE Users ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/V210.08 Legacy VA-ONCE Users.png)

**Figure 5.08** **Legacy VA-ONCE Users**

2. Enrollment Manager access requests submitted by Certifying Officials via the **Legacy VA-ONCE Users** option bypass assigned Oversight and Accountability Division staff review/approval. When a request is submitted, the Certifying Official’s information is cross-referenced with the VA-ONCE user data base. Immediately after submission, a pop-up modal informs legacy VA-ONCE users that access to Enrollment Manager has been granted. Once access has been granted, the **Open Enrollment Manager** button will become enabled on the **Landing Page**; however, the user may have to log back in for the change to take effect.

![ Access Granted - Legacy VA-ONCE Users ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.09 Access Granted - Legacy VA-ONCE Users.png)

**Figure 5.09 Access Granted – Legacy VA-ONCE Users**

3. VA-ONCE roles map directly to Enrollment Manager roles; therefore, the same level of access and associated facility codes are automatic. **Legacy VA-ONCE Users** are automatically listed as **Approved** Certifying Officials on the **SCO Access Manager** page.

**Note:** If a legacy VA-ONCE user does not successfully gain access, they will subsequently request access using the **Request Access** option; therefore, assigned Oversight and Accountability Division staff may see pending requests that do not have an updated 22-8794. Likewise, existing Certifying Officials transitioning off paper certifications may have a pending access request without an updated 22-8794.

**5.08 Assistant and Read-Only Certifying Official Access Requests and Approval**

 

1. **Access for** **Assistants and Read-Only Certifying Officials****.** Assistants and Read-Only Certifying Officials have the same read-only permissions. While read-only users have the ability to save draft certifications, they cannot submit certifications to VA. Assistant and Read-Only Certifying Official access requests are submitted using the same method performed by Certifying Officials (after ID.me or Login.gov authentication, navigate to the **Landing Page**, select either **Request Access** or **Legacy VA-ONCE Users** and complete the applicable fields). The **Request Access** option routes the request to the Certifying Official to review/approve each request associated with their facility. Assigned Oversight and Accountability Division staff do NOT approve or manage access for Assistants or Read-Only Certifying Officials.

**Note**: If **Legacy VA-ONCE User** is selected, the requestor’s information is cross-referenced with VA-ONCE user data and manual review/approval is bypassed.

2. **SCO Approval**. After submitted by an Assistant or Read-Only Certifying Official, the request proceeds to the Certifying Official for approval. The **SCO Access Manager** page displays a list of pending requests for the specified facility code. Certifying Officials will act to either **Approve** or **Deny** requests.
3. Once approved, the Assistant/Read-Only Certifying Official have access to the requested facility code. If additional facility code(s) need to be assigned, the Certifying Official will select **Mng Facility** to **Assign** additional facility code(s). The Certifying Official will only be able to assign facilities that mirror the ones to which they have access.

**Note**: Schools with Centralized Certification have designated Points of Contact (POCs) at branch campuses versus Certifying Officials on site. POCs must have access to Enrollment Manager in order to provide certification information to students and VA/SAA representatives; however, they do not have the capability to submit certifications and their access is read-only. Certifying Officials are responsible for approving and managing access for POCs.

**5.09 Automatic Email Notification (Requestor/Authorizer)**

1. **Email Notification**. Automatic email notification is sent to the requestor and authorizer based on the following actions performed in the VA Education Platform Portal:

 

2. Requestor (e.g., Certifying Official, Assistant, Read-Only Certifying Official) 
   1. Access Request Confirmed
   2. Access Request Processed
   3. Access Revoked
   4. Access Denied
3. Authorizer (i.e., Assigned Oversight and Accountability Division staff via the ELR of jurisdiction mailbox) 
   1. New Access Request Submitted
   2. Access Revocation Confirmation

**Note**: Certifying Officials will not receive email notification when a request is submitted even though they act to approve and manage access for Assistants and Read-Only Certifying Officials.

**SUBCHAPTER III. NAVIGATING ENROLLMENT MANAGER**

**5.10** **End User Agreement**

**Enrollment Manager End User Agreement.** Unlike VA-ONCE, schools are not required to submit a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU). Instead, each user must agree to the End User Agreement before utilizing Enrollment Manager for the first time. When an authorized user logs into Enrollment Manager for the first time, a pop-up modal appears, and they must agree to the End User Agreement to move forward.

**5.11 Dashboard Page**

1. **Dashboard.** Enrollment Manager is organized into four (4) primary modules each containing numerous sub-category pages. **Dashboard**, **Students**, **Schools**, and **Reports** modules are arranged across the top of the screen. After successful Enrollment Manager login, users are brought to the **Dashboard** module. The **Dashboard** displays a snapshot of all current enrollments, key actions, insights, and information applicable to a Certifying Official’s associated facility code(s).

2. **Your Actions** displays a list of students that require additional action. For example, an enrollment saved as a draft. For schools with Assistants, this feature will streamline the subsequent submission process for Certifying Officials.

![Dashboard Page - Your Actions ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.11 Dashboard Page - Your Actions.png)

**Figure 5.11** **Dashboard Page – Your Actions**

3. **Helpful Resources** include news flash banners, contact information for the ELR of jurisdiction, and links to the SCO Handbook and GI Bill Comparison Tool.

**5.12** **Students Page**

1. **Students.** When a Certifying Official selects the **Students** module, they are brought to the **Students** page where they can **Search My Students**, **Search All Students**, **Create a New Student Profile** for a student not found, **Add Student to School**, navigate to the individual’s **Student Profile** by selecting **Go to Profile**, and view a centralized list of students associated with their facility code(s).

 

2. **Search My Students.**A Certifying Official can search for existing students (i.e., students who have been added to the Certifying Official’s associated schools) on the **Search My Students** page. Numerous filters (e.g., **First Name**, **Last Name**, **Student ID**, **Enrollment Status**, **Training Type**, **Benefit Type**, **Program**, **School**, **Yellow Ribbon**, **STEM**, **Active Student Only**) allow users to narrow the search results displayed on the centralized list of students below the filters.

 

3. **Date Range Filter**. Reserved for future use. Enhanced capability to filter by date range is expected in a future release.

 ![Students Page - Search My Students ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.12 Students Page - Search My Students.png)

**Figure 5.12 Students Page – Search My Students**

**Note**: Legacy VA-ONCE student data has been transferred and both active and inactive students are displayed on the **Search My Students** centralized list. The **Active Student Only** checkbox filter displays legacy VA-ONCE students who were active at the time of migration. The checkbox is automatically checked; however, a Certifying Official can uncheck the box to display both active and inactive students. Certifying Officials use the status to manage student data. An inactive status does not restrict a Certifying Official from submitting certifications. Enhanced student’s status (e.g., Active, Inactive) functionality is expected in a future release.

4. Certifying Officials navigate to an individual’s **Student Profile** by clicking the student’s linked name or highlighting the row and selecting **Go to Profile** in the right hand pane.

**![Students Page - Search My Students - Search Results ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.13 Students Page - Search My Students - Search Results.png)**

**Figure 5.13 Students Page – Search My Students – Search Results**

**Note**: The **Search My Students** centralized list will not display students at newly approved facilities, including those that did not use VA-ONCE, because students have to be associated with the school to appear on the list.

 

5. **Search All Students.** Unlike VA-ONCE where student search was limited to students in the in VA-ONCE data base, Certifying Officials can search for students with existing accounts in VA systems via the Claimant Service. See [Claimant Service](#Claimant Service) for additional information. Certifying Officials begin the search by entering the student’s **First Name**, **Middle Name**, **Last Name**, **Email**, **Phone Number**, and **Date of Birth** (minimum search criteria equals the first two letters of last name or date of birth).

![Students Page – Search All Students ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.14 Students Page – Search All Students.png)

**Figure 5.14 Students Page – Search All Students**

6. Potential students with matching data elements are displayed.The list is organized by **First Name**, **Last Name**, **Benefit Type**, **Claimant Benefit Type**, and **Student ID.** Once a student’s row is highlighted, the right hand pane displays the student’s date of birth, list of schools, and contact information (e.g., email address, mobile phone, home phone, mailing address). The Certifying Official selects **Add Student to School** in the right hand pane to proceed to the next two pages where the student’s **Biographical Information** and **School and Program Information** are displayed.

 

**Note**: Enrollment Manager does NOT display social security numbers.

 

![Students Page – Search All Students – Search Results ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.15 Students Page – Search All Students – Search Results.png)

**Figure 5.15 Students Page – Search All Students – Search Results**

7. **Biographical Information** (1 of 2). The student’s biographical and contact information will be prepopulated with information from Claimant Service. Fields include: **First Name**, **Middle Name**, **Last Name**, **Date of** **Birth**, **Student ID**, **Benefit Type**, **Mobile Phone**, **Home Phone, Email Address**, and **Mailing Address**. See [Claimant Service](#Claimant Service) for additional information.

 

**Note**: **Student ID** is an optional field populated by Certifying Officials. It is primarily used by large schools to help them manage student data. Legacy VA-ONCE Student ID information has been transferred and is displayed on the **Students** page.

 

![Add Student - Biographical Information](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.16 Add Student - Biographical Information.png)

**Figure 5.16 Add Student – Biographical Information**

8. **School and Program Information** (2 of 2). Certifying Officials will select the appropriate options from the following drop-down lists:

 

1. 1. **School** drop-down displays the facility code(s) associated with the logged in Certifying Official.

 

1. 2. **Training Type** drop-down (e.g., IHL, NCD, OJT, Apprenticeship, Flight, VET TEC) displays the training type(s) applicable to the facility.

 

1. 3. **Objective Type** drop-down (e.g., Associate in Arts, Bachelor of Science, Master of Arts, Doctor’s Degree) is enabled and displayed if the **Training Type** is IHL.

 

**Note**: If the student is a guest student, the Certifying Official will select **Guest Student** from the **Objective Type** drop-down. When selected, the **Program** defaults to **Guest Student** and the **Primary School Address Type**, **Primary School State**, and **Primary School Name** fields are enabled and displayed.

 

1. 4. **Program** drop-down is populated by the programs listed in Approval Manager (WEAMS in the interim); in addition, non-matriculated and undeclared are available drop-down options that are enabled and displayed if the Training Type is IHL.

 

1. **Multiple Programs**. Reserved for future use. Enhanced capability to select more than one program (e.g., dual degree, dual objective) is expected in a future release. In the meantime, Certifying Officials will need to select one (1) program from the available **Program** drop-down options and **Add a Custom Remark**, for example, “Student concurrently pursuing 2 degrees – additional program is [program name]”.

**![Figure Add Student - School and Program Information](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.17 Add Student - School and Program Information.png)**

**Figure 5.17 Add Student – School and Program Information**

10. Once the fields on the **School and Program Information** page are complete, the Certifying Official selects **Add Student**. An editable overview of the information is displayed in a grey box.

 

 ![Add Student - School and Program Information – Overview ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.18 Add Student - School and Program Information – Overview.png)

**Figure 5.18 Add Student – School and Program Information – Overview**

11. If the student is taking courses at additional campuses (e.g., branch, extension), the Certifying Official selects **Add Another School** to add associated schools. Once complete, the Certifying Official selects **Add Student.** If the program information needs to be edited after the student has been added, the Certifying Official can make those edits from the **Programs** page within the **Student Profile**. Once a Certify Official has added a student, that student will appear on the **Search My Students** centralized list of students associated with the Certifying Official’s facility code(s).
12. **Create a New Student Profile.** When a student does not exist in VA systems, the Certifying Official will not be able to find the student when searching within **Search All Students**. If a student is not found, Certifying Officials have the ability to add a new student by selecting **Create a New Student Profile.**
13. When **Create a New Student Profile** is selected, the Certifying Official is routed to the **Biographical Information** and **School and Program Information** pages for completion. The Certifying Official will receive alert messages to assist them with tracking the student’s enrollment(s) and checking their eligibility.

**Note**: Certifying Officials need to enter a student’s Social Security number, and veteran’s Social Security number or VA file number for chapter 35, when creating a new student who does not exist in VA Systems. Even though Enrollment Manager does not display Social Security numbers, they are still used to verify students and are in the background.

**![Students Page - Create a New Student Profile ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.19 Students Page - Create a New Student Profile.png)**

**Figure 5.19 Students Page – Create a New Student Profile**

**5.13 Student Profile**

1. **Student Profile**. Actions, such as certifying an enrollment and submitting an amendment, are completed within an individual’s **Student Profile**. **Enrollments**, **Student Info**, **Programs**, **Benefits**, **Notes**, and **History** are sub-category pages displayed within the **Student Profile**.

 

2. **Enrollments.** The **Enrollments** page within the **Student Profile** is where Certifying Officials submit enrollment certifications, amendments, and monthly certifications.

![Student Profile - Enrollments Page ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.20 Student Profile - Enrollments Page.png)

**Figure 5.20 Student Profile – Enrollments Page**

1. 1. **Student Status**. Reserved for future use. Enhanced capability to view a student’s status (e.g., Active, Inactive-Complete, Inactive-Leave of Absence, Inactive-Transferred, Inactive- Unspecified, Disassociate Student) is expected in a future release.

 

1. 2. Similar to the “Certs” page in VA-ONCE, the **Enrollments** page lists all of the student’s enrollments. Information can be filtered by school, status, and type.

![Student Profile - Enrollments Page - Filter ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.21 Student Profile - Enrollments Page - Filter.png)

**Figure 5.21 Student Profile – Enrollments Page – Filter**

1. 3. The **Enrollments** page displays brief details that vary by training type. Additional details including the **Enrollment History** are displayed by clicking˅ to expand. The original enrollment, associated amendments, and corresponding monthly certifications are viewed by clicking **View Details**.

![Student Profile - Enrollments Page – Expanded View ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.22 Student Profile - Enrollments Page – Expanded View.png)

**Figure 5.22 Student Profile – Enrollments Page – Expanded View**

1. 4. Once **View Details** is selected, a summary page displays the details for the selected enrollment, amendment, or monthly certification. The user can view/print the associated form (e.g., 22-1999, 22-1999b, 22-6553c, 22-6553d-1) by selecting **Print** located at the bottom of the screen.

![Student Profile - Enrollments Page - Details ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.23 Student Profile - Enrollments Page - Details.png)

**Figure 5.23 Student Profile – Enrollments Page – Details**

3. **Student Info.** The student’s biographical and contact information is displayed on the **Student Info** page.

![Student Profile – Student Info Page ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.24 Student Profile – Student Info Page.png)

**Figure 5.24 Student Profile – Student Info Page**

4. **Programs**. The student’s program information is displayed on the **Programs** page. If a Certifying Official needs to update a student’s program information, they can edit certain fields (e.g., **Training Type, Objective Type, Program**) or add a new program by selecting **Add Program**. Drop-down selections are dynamic; therefore, displayed options will vary.

 

1. 1. **Example**. When **IHL** is selected as the **Training Type**, the **Objective Type** drop-down selection will display: 
      1. Associate of Art
      2. Associate of Science
      3. Associate Degree Not Elsewhere Classified
      4. Bachelor of Art
      5. Bachelor of Science
      6. Bachelor Degree Not Elsewhere Classified
      7. Master of Art
      8. Master of Science
      9. Master Degree Not Elsewhere Classified
      10. Doctor of Philosophy
      11. Doctors Degree Not Elsewhere Classified
      12. Postdoctoral Degree Not Elsewhere Classified

 

1. 2. **STEM**. The Certifying Official will check the box if the student has been approved for the Edith Nourse Rogers Science Technology Engineering Math (STEM) Scholarship. This box should NOT be checked when the student is enrolled in a STEM program if they are NOT receiving the STEM Scholarship.

 

1. 3. **CIP Code**. This dynamic field will be enabled and displayed if the **STEM** checkbox is checked. The Certifying Official will enter the Classification of Instructional Programs (CIP) code. An automatic CIP Code remark is added to every certification.

**Note:** All STEM Scholarship certifications are routed to the Buffalo Regional Processing Office (RPO).

![Student Profile – Programs Page](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.25 Student Profile – Programs Page.png)

**Figure 5.25 Student Profile – Programs Page**

5. **Benefits.** Similar to the "VA Data" page in VA-ONCE, the **Benefits** page includes information about the benefit type and remaining entitlement. For chapter 33 students, the benefit level is also displayed.

**Note:** Section 308 of the Harry W. Colmery Veterans Educational Assistance Act of 2017 (Public Law 115-48), requires that VA make GI Bill student’s entitlement information available to schools. Students may elect to withhold this information by completing a VA Form 22-0993, *Request to Opt-Out of Information Sharing with Educational Institutions*. If a legacy VA-ONCE student opted-out, their information will not be displayed on the **Benefits** page. Enhanced capability for new opt-out requests is expected in a future release. In the meantime, assigned Regional Processing Office staff will forward opt-out requests to DGIB via [yourIT ServicePortal](https://yourit.va.gov/va?id=va_home) ticket (select: **Report an Issue**, **Report a New Issue**, under **Category** and **Subcategory**, select **Software** and **Desktop Application**, include a remark to forward to DGIB).  

![Student Profile – Benefits Page ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.26 Student Profile – Benefits Page.png)

**Figure 5.26 Student Profile – Benefits Page**

6. **Notes**. The **Notes** page displays all the notes entered by a Certifying Official when adding a student or certifying an enrollment. In addition, Certifying Officials can add directly to the **Notes** page by selecting **Add Note**. The notes are not transmitted to the Regional Processing Offices (RPOs); instead, they are an optional documentation tool for Certifying Officials to capture additional information that is not in the **Remarks** field. **Notes** can be viewed by other Certifying Officials and assigned Oversight and Accountability Division staff.

![Student Profile – Notes Page ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.27 Student Profile – Notes Page.png)

**Figure 5.27 Student Profile – Notes Page**

7. **History**. The **History** page shows the past actions, including all enrollment certifications, amendments, and terminations taken on a student account. Brief details are displayed, including the user who performed the action and the date and time it occurred. The information on the **History** page cannot be edited. In addition, legacy VA-ONCE historical data has been transferred and is displayed on the **History** page. Information is sorted by date and can be filtered by user, action, or school. The **Export** button allows Certifying Officials and assigned Oversight and Accountability Division staff to export historical items to a comma-separated values (csv) file.

![Student Profile – History Page](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.28 Student Profile – History Page.png)

**Figure 5.28 Student Profile – History Page**

**5.14 Schools Page**

1. **Schools.** If a Certifying Official chooses to utilize **Preset Enrollments,** they will navigate to the Schools module to administratively setup **Preset Enrollments** prior to certifying.

![School Page – Preset Enrollments ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.29 School Page – Preset Enrollments.png)

**Figure 5.29 School Page – Preset Enrollments**

 

2. **Preset Enrollments.** Similar to the "Standard Terms" page in VA-ONCE, the **Preset Enrollments** page allows Certifying Officials to enter scheduled enrollment period information for subsequent use. When certifying an enrollment, Certifying Officials have the option to select an enrollment period from a drop-down containing the information entered into **Preset Enrollments**. The **Begin Date**, **End Date**, and **Vacation Period** (if applicable) fields on the enrollment are auto-populated with the values from the selected preset enrollment. This time-saving feature is primarily used for IHL and NCD programs measured in semester or quarter hours. Preset enrollments are not applicable to OJT, APP, or FLT. **Preset Enrollments** fields include:

 

1. 1. **Name**. Certifying Officials will enter an abbreviation for the preset enrollment period (e.g., Fall 2023, Spring A 24). This abbreviation cannot be duplicated for another enrollment period. The names entered into **Preset Enrollments** will subsequently display in the **Enrollment Name** drop-down when certifying the enrollment.

 

1. 2. **School**. A drop-down containing the Certifying Official’s associated facility code(s) and school name(s). Certifying Officials have the option to select one, multiple, or all facility codes.

 

1. 3. **Begin Date** and **End Date**. See [Begin Date and End Date](#Begin Date and End Date) for additional information.

![Preset Enrollments – Basic Information ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.30 Preset Enrollments – Basic Information.png)

**Figure 5.30 Preset Enrollments – Basic Information**

1. 4. **Add Vacation Period.** See [Vacation Period](#Vacation Period) for additional information.

 

1. 5. **Active Checkbox**. The checkbox is automatically checked; however, a Certifying Official can uncheck the box to remove it as a drop-down option when certifying the enrollment.

![Preset Enrollments – Vacation Periods](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.31 Preset Enrollments – Vacation Periods.png)

**Figure 5.31 Preset Enrollments – Vacation Periods**

3. **Notification Emails to Students**. Unlike VA-ONCE where Certifying Officials would select a checkbox for student email confirmation, automated emails are automatically sent to students each time a certification is submitted.

**5.15 Reports**

1. **Weekly Summary and Detail** **Report.** The Weekly **Summary** and **Detail** Reports are primarily used by Certifying Officials; however, internal users also have the ability to generate the reports on demand. Similar to the “Weekly Certs Report” and “Weekly Detail Certs Report” in VA-ONCE, the Weekly **Summary** Report allows users to view the overall number of certifications by type and the **Detail** Report allows users to view the details about each certification submitted in any given week.

 

2. **Weekly Summary Report** includes:
   1. Facility Code
   2. Enrollments (number)
   3. Amendments (number)
   4. Terminations (number)
   5. Monthly Certifications (number)

 

![Screenshot of the Reports section in Enrollment Manager](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.32 Reports Page – Summary Report.png)

**Figure 5.32 Reports Page – Summary Report**

3. **Weekly Detail Report** includes:
   1. Facility Code
   2. Enrollment Type (enrollment, amendment, termination)
   3. Student Name
   4. Status
   5. SCO
   6. Submission Date

 

4. Additional details are displayed by expanding the rows. Expanded information varies by training type.

 

1. 1. For IHL, NCD, and VET TEC, expanded fields contain details regarding credits (e.g., resident hours, online hours, remedial/deficiency hours, clock hours), tuition and fees, Yellow Ribbon, amendments, and terminations.

 

1. 2. For OJT and APP, expanded fields contain details regarding enrollment information (e.g., approved hours, trainee hours per week, prior training time), monthly certifications (e.g., hours trained, month certified), and terminations.

 

1. 3. For FLT, expanded fields contain details regarding total program charges, summary of total hours (e.g., dual, dual simulator hours, solo, ground school, pre/post hours, other), and terminations.

 

5. **Export Reports**. A comma-separated values (CSV) spreadsheet of each record is generated by selecting **Export Reports** located at the bottom of the screen.

**SUBCHAPTER IV. CERTIFICATIONS APPLICABLE TO INSTITUTIONS OF HIGHER LEARNING AND NON-COLLEGE DEGREE FACILITIES**

**5.16 Submitting Enrollment Certifications (IHL/NCD)**

1. **Add Enrollment** (IHL/NCD). From the **Enrollments** page within the **Student Profile**, Certifying Officials select **Add Enrollment** to add a new enrollment. The fields displayed vary depending on the student’s **Training Type** (e.g., IHL, NCD, OJT, APP, Flight). For students with an IHL or NCD training type, the page is organized into the following four (4) sections: **Enrollment Information**, **Credits and** **Tuition**, **Vacation Periods**, and **Remarks and Notes**.

 

2. **Enrollment Information** section includes:

 

1. 1. **School** (\*Required). Populated with the individual student’s associated school (facility code – facility name). If the student has multiple associated schools, the Certifying Official will select from a drop-down list.

 

1. 2. **Enrollment Name**. If a Certifying Official utilized the feature to enter [Preset Enrollments](#Preset Enrollments), a drop-down containing the list of abbreviated enrollment periods would appear in this field. The **Begin Date**, **End Date**, and **Vacation Period** (if applicable) fields on the enrollment would auto-populate with the values from the selected preset enrollment.

 

1. 3. **Begin Date**(\*Required) and **End Date** (\*Required). Generally, Certifying Officials will enter the dates published in the school’s academic calendar.

![Add Enrollment (IHL/NCD) - Enrollment Information ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.33 Add Enrollment.IHL.NCD.Enrollment Information.png)

**Figure 5.33 Add Enrollment (IHL/NCD) – Enrollment Information**

3. **Credits and Tuition** section includes:

 

1. 1. **Resident Credits**. Certifying Officials will enter the number of residential (contains an in-resident component) credit hours the student will take each semester or quarter at the associated school designated in the **School** field. The **Resident Credit** field should not include any online, remedial/deficiency, or clock hours.

 

1. 2. **Note**: This field is disabled if Approval Manager (WEAMS in the interim) indicates the school is online only.

 

1. 3. **Online Credits**. Certifying Officials will enter the number of online (courses taken solely through distance learning) credit hours the student will take each semester or quarter at the associated school designated in the **School** field.

 

1. 4. **Clock Hours**. For programs approved by the State Approving Agency in clock hours, Certifying Officials enter the number of scheduled weekly clock hours in the **Clock Hours** field.

 

1. 5. **Note**: Certifying Officials may also certify practical training (e.g., externship, internship, practicum) in clock hours vs. credit hours if it is advantageous to the student.

 

1. 6. **NCD Program Length Validation**. Reserved for future use. Enhanced capability to integrate approved NCD program hours from Approval Manager and calculate total hours certified is expected in a future release.

 

1. 7. **Online Clock Hours.** Reserved for future use. Enhanced capability for reporting online clock hours is expected in a future release. In the meantime, Certifying Officials must follow normal established procedures (i.e., report the number of weekly scheduled clock hours in the **Clock Hours** field and insert **VBA Remark** “Clock Hours for this student are approved to be taken online.”

 

1. 8. **Remedial/Deficiency Credits**. This dynamic field will be enabled and displayed if the student’s **Objective Type** reflects undergraduate training (e.g., Associate of Arts, Bachelor of Science). If remedial and deficiency courses are required, the Certifying Official will enter the number of credits in the **Remedial/Deficiency Credits** field.

 

**Note 1**: Remedial and deficiency courses offered as independent study (online) cannot be approved and cannot be certified to VA.

 

**Note 2**: Chapter 35 entitlement is not charged for up to 5 months of full-time remedial and deficiency training.

 

1. 9. **Advance Pay Requested by Student**. This checkbox is enabled and displayed on enrollments for chapters 30, 35, and 1606.

 

1. 10. **Full Time Modifier** (chapter 33). This dynamic field is enabled and displayed if the chapter 33 student’s **Objective Type** reflects graduate training (e.g., Master of Arts, Doctors Degree Not Elsewhere Classified). For standard length semesters or quarters, Certifying Officials will enter the number of credits considered full time for that term as published in their school catalog. For nonstandard length semesters or quarters, the number of credits certified will depend on whether the term has an individually defined full-time modifier (IDM). See [School Certifying Official Handbook (On-line)](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/system/templates/selfservice/va_ssnew/help/customer/locale/en-US/portal/554400000001018/content/554400000149088/School-Certifying-Official-Handbook-On-line) for additional guidance disseminated to Certifying Officials.

 

1. 11. **Training Time** (non-chapter 33). This dynamic field is enabled and displayed when the non-chapter 33 student’s **Objective Type** reflects graduate training (e.g., Master of Arts, Doctors Degree Not Elsewhere Classified). The Certifying Official will select the training time from the drop-down list (e.g., full, ¾, ½).

 

1. 12. **Tuition & Fees Amount**. The Certifying Official will enter the allowable tuition and fees associated with the credits/hours certified.

 

1. 13. **Yellow Ribbon**. The Certifying Official will check the box if the student is using Yellow Ribbon for the enrollment period being certified.

 

**Note**: Enhanced capability to enable and display the **Yellow Ribbon** checkbox only for students who meet basic eligibility for the Yellow Ribbon Program is expected in a future release.

 

1. 14. **Yellow Ribbon Amount**. This dynamic field is enabled and displayed if the **Yellow Ribbon** checkbox is checked. The Certifying Official will enter the amount of the school’s contribution.

 

1. 15. **Out of State Charge**.  Reserved for future use. Enhanced capability to enable and display this field when the **Yellow Ribbon** box is checked is expected in a future release. In the meantime, Public IHLs that have students residing out-of-state and who do not meet the criteria of being a “covered individual” will need to report **Out of State Charge** using a **Custom Remark**.

 

**Note**: Since public IHLs no longer charge out-of-state tuition and fee rates to “covered individuals” meeting the requirements in 38 U.S.C. 3679(c), the Yellow Ribbon program is not applicable to those beneficiaries. For GI Bill beneficiaries not meeting the definition of a “covered individual”, the Yellow Ribbon program can be used to cover the difference between the in-state and out-of-state tuition and fee charges.

 

1. 16. **Accelerated Pay Requested by Student**. This checkbox is enabled and displayed on enrollments for chapters 30 and 1606.

**![Add Enrollment (IHL/NCD) - Credits and Tuition ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.34 Add Enrollment.IHL.NCD.Credits and Tuition.png)**

**Figure 5.34 Add Enrollment (IHL/NCD) – Credits and Tuition**

4. **Vacation Period** section includes: 
   1. **Vacation Begin Date**. The 1st calendar day after the last day of class.
   2. **Vacation End Date.** The day before class resumes.

**Note:** For nonstandard length semesters or quarters, vacation periods of seven (7) or more days must be reported in order for VA to properly determine the credit hour equivalent. Certifying Officials will enter the **Begin Date** and **End Date** of the vacation period (if applicable). A vacation period that occurs during a nonstandard term should be reported as the 1st calendar day after the last day of class through the day before class resumes (to include weekends if applicable). If necessary, Certifying Officials may enter multiple vacation periods.

![Add Enrollment (IHL/NCD) - Vacation Periods ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.35 Add Enrollment.IHL.NCD.Vacation Periods.png)

**Figure 5.35 Add Enrollment (IHL/NCD) – Vacation Periods**

5. **Remarks and Notes** section includes:

 

1. 1. **VBA Remarks**. VBA Remarks are predetermined choices that can be selected from a drop-down list. VBA Remarks are used to provide additional context about an enrollment and could increase the efficiency of VA processing; however, they are not usually needed to process a claim.

![VBA Remarks](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.36 VBA Remarks.png)

**Figure 5.36 VBA Remarks**

1. 2. **Add Custom Remark**. If a VBA Remark does not apply, a free text Custom Remark can be added. The use of VBA Remarks is encouraged over Custom Remarks because Custom Remarks prevent automation and delay processing. Custom Remarks should be used when automation should be stopped.

 

1. 3. **Notes**. See [Notes](#Notes) for additional information.

![Notes](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.37 Notes.png)

**Figure 5.37 Notes**

6. Once enrollment information has been entered, the Certifying Official selects **Submit Enrollment**, **Save as Draft**, or **Discard Edits**.

**![Submit, Save, or Discard Options ](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.38 Submit, Save, or Discard Options.png)**

**Figure 5.38 Submit, Save, or Discard Options**

7. **Add Multiple Enrollments** (IHL/NCD). Reserved for future use. Enhanced capability for reporting multiple enrollment periods is expected in a future release. In the meantime, Certifying Officials will need to submit a separate **Enrollment** for each enrollment period. In addition, singular enrollment period submission impacts processing and VCEs need to review and sort multiple certifications.

**5.17 Submitting Amendments (IHL/NCD)**

1. **Amend** (IHL/NCD). Unlike VA-ONCE, amendments, adjustments, and terminations are all completed by selecting **Amend**. To begin the amendment, the Certifying Official will navigate to the **Enrollments** page within the **Student Profile**. The **Enrollments** page shows the past enrollments for the student. Brief details, including the school’s name, facility code, enrollment dates, and status (e.g., in progress, submitted, under review, processed) are displayed for each enrollment. Additional details can be displayed by clicking ˅ to expand.

**Note**: The **Amend** button will not display if the enrollment status is pending SCO review.

![Amend (IHL/NCD)](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.39 Amend.IHL.NCD.png)

**Figure 5.39 Amend (IHL/NCD)**

2. After selecting **Amend**, Certifying Officials are navigated to the **Amend Enrollment** page for that enrollment. The page is organized into the following four (4) sections: **Enrollment Information**, **Credits and Tuition**, **Vacation Periods**, and **Remarks and Notes**.

 

3. **Enrollment Information** (amendment) section includes:

 

1. 1. **School**. The field contains a read-only value for amendments and cannot be edited.

 

1. 2. **Enrollment Name**. The field contains a read-only value for amendments and cannot be edited.

 

1. 3. **Begin Date** and **End Date**. The original dates certified are displayed. These fields can be edited.

**![Amend (IHL/NCD) - Enrollment Information](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.40 Amend.IHL.NCD.Enrollment Information.png)**

**Figure 5.40 Amend (IHL/NCD) – Enrollment Information**

4. **Credits and Tuition** (amendment) section includes:

 

1. 1. **Graduation/End of Term or Course**. This checkbox will be checked when the Certifying Official is reporting graduation or program completion information to VA.

 

1. 2. If **Graduation/End of Term or Course** is selected, a dynamic section titled **Amendment Information** is displayed. The following options appear in the **Amendment Reason** drop-down list:
      1. End of Term or Course (default for NCD)
      2. Graduation (default for IHL)

1. 3. **Amendment Effective Date**. The effective date will automatically display the last day of the enrollment period.

 

1. 4. **Termination.** If the Certifying Official is reporting a termination, they will check the **Termination** checkbox. When a Certifying Official selects the **Termination** checkbox on the **Amend Enrollment** page, the page data is automatically updated to reflect the enrollment's termination. The **Resident**, **Online**, **Clock**, and **Remedial/Deficiency** fields are set to 0 and disabled. When the Certifying Official deselects the **Termination** checkbox, all hour fields reset to their value from the most recent claim (either the original enrollment or the amendment immediately prior). The **Amendment Information** section is also reset and disabled if no hours have changed and the **Graduation/Program Completion** checkbox has not been selected.

 

1. 5. If **Termination** is selected, a dynamic section titled **Amendment Information** is displayed. The following options appear in the **Amendment Reason** drop-down list:
      1. Pre-registered but never attended
      2. Student completed term but non-punitive grades assigned for one or more course(s)
      3. Unsatisfactory attendance, progress, or conduct
      4. Withdraw before beginning of term
      5. Withdraw after drop period – non-punitive grades assigned
      6. Withdraw after drop period – punitive grades assigned
      7. Withdraw during drop period
      8. Withdrawal or interruption (Non-College Degree programs not on a term basis)
      9. Other

![Screenshot illustrating the credits and tuition section in the Amend Enrollment tab](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.41 Amend.IHL.NCD.Termination.png)

**Figure** **5.41 Amend (IHL/NCD) – Termination**

1. 6. **Amendment Effective Date**. The Certifying Official enters the effective date. Some effective dates are auto-populated and cannot be edited, for example, withdraw before beginning of term will auto-populate one day prior to the begin date of the enrollment period.

 

1. 7. **Note:** If a Certifying Official needs to make changes on a terminated enrollment that has been submitted, they need to enter the enrollment again and make each change in sequence.

 

1. 8. **Leave of Absence.** Reserved for future use. Enhanced capability for reporting an NCD Leave of Absence is expected in a future release. In the meantime, Certifying Officials must follow these interim procedures: **Amend** the enrollment, check **Termination**, select **Withdrawal or interruption (Non-College Degree programs not on a term basis**) from the **Amendment Reason** drop-down list, enter the last date of attendance in the **Amendment Effective Date** field, and **Add a Custom Remark** “Student is on leave of absence (LOA).” When the student returns, the Certifying Official will submit a new enrollment by selecting **Add Enrollment**. The **Begin Date** is the first day the student returns from the LOA. The **End Date** will be the adjusted date of expected completion. **Add a Custom Remark** “RESUME – Student returned from LOA. No change in tuition and fees.”

**Note:** When VA processes the LOA, a one-year diary is set on the claim. If the student does not resume training, the award is terminated from the last date of attendance and associated debts created. 

1. 9. **Resident Credits**. The original number of resident credits is displayed. This field can be edited. If an edit is made to this field, a dynamic section titled **Amendment Information** is displayed.

 

1. 11. **Online Credits**. The original number of online credits is displayed. This field can be edited. If an edit is made to this field, a dynamic section titled **Amendment Information** is displayed.

 

1. 12. **Clock Hours**. The original number of clock hours is displayed. This field can be edited. If an edit is made to this field, a dynamic section titled **Amendment Information** is displayed.

 

1. 13. **Remedial/Deficiency Credits**. The original number of remedial/deficiency credits is displayed. This field can be edited. If an edit is made to this field, a dynamic section titled **Amendment Information** is displayed.

 

1. 14. **Tuition & Fees Amount.** The original tuition and fees amount is displayed. This amount can be edited.

**![Amend (IHL/NCD) - Credits and Tuition](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.42 Amend.IHL.NCD.Credits and Tuition.png)**

**Figure 5.42 Amend (IHL/NCD) – Credits and Tuition**

1. 15. If **Amendment Information** is displayed, the following options appear in the **Amendment Reason** drop-down list:
       1. Hours changed between Online and Resident courses
       2. Increase
       3. Pre-registered but reduced prior to first day of term
       4. Reduction (Non-College)
       5. Reduction after drop period – non-punitive grades assigned
       6. Reduction after drop period – punitive grades assigned
       7. Reduction during drop period
       8. Reduction first day of term
       9. Student completed term, but non-punitive grades assigned for one or more courses(s)
       10. Other

 

1. 16. **Amendment Effective Date**. The Certifying Official will enter the effective date. Some effective dates are auto-populated and cannot be edited, for example, reduction first day of term will auto-populate the begin date of the enrollment period.

**![Amendment Information](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.43 Amendment Information.png)**

**Figure 5.43 Amendment Information**

1. 17. **Mitigating Circumstances.** Based on the **Amendment Reason** (e.g., termination or reduction after the drop period – non-punitive grade is assigned), a dynamic section titled **Mitigating Circumstances** is displayed. The following options appear in the drop-down list:
       1. Unknown or unsupported mitigating circumstances
       2. An illness or death in the student's immediate family
       3. An illness or injury afflicting the student during the enrollment period
       4. An unavoidable change in the student's conditions of employment
       5. An unavoidable geographical transfer resulting from the student's employment
       6. Discontinuance of the course by the school
       7. Immediate family or financial obligations beyond the control of the claimant that require them to suspend pursuit of the program of education to obtain employment
       8. Unanticipated active military service, including active duty for training
       9. Unanticipated difficulties with childcare arrangements the student made for the period during which they are attending classes

 

1. 18. Certifying Officials are encouraged to report mitigating circumstances to VA if they know the circumstance and it’s clearly one of the circumstances listed. Documentation of mitigating circumstances provided by the student must be retained within the student’s file.

1. 1. **Vacation Begin Date.** The original date is displayed. This information can be edited.

**SUBCHAPTER VII. GI BILL CHATBOT**

**5.25 Chatbot**

1. **Chatbot.** Billie, the GI Bill Chatbot, is accessed as part of Enrollment Manager. Billie is a self-service tool that is used primarily by new Certifying Officials to provide on-demand answers to frequently asked questions. It provides information on reporting student enrollment and related changes. Answers focus around the following areas:

 

1. 1. **85/15**. Provides general information on 85/15.
   2. **Associated Schools**. Provides general information on schools associated with a Certifying Official’s profile and the differences between main, branch, and extension campuses.
   3. **Course Applicability**. Provides general information on course applicability and guidance on repeating courses.
   4. **Credits and Hours**. Provides information and proper use of certifying resident hours, online hours, remedial/deficiency hours, and clock hours.
   5. **Debts and Overpayments**. Provides general information on school and student debt creation.
   6. **Effective Dates**. Provides information on reporting effective dates used for amendments.
   7. **Flight Training**. Provides information on how to certify flight enrollments and monthly certifications.
   8. **GI Bill Comparison Tool**. Provides general information on the Comparison Tool.
   9. **Legislative and Policy Changes.** Provides information on recent changes and how enrollments are impacted.
   10. **Preset Enrollments**. Provides information on how to create, manage, and select an enrollment.
   11. **Standard, Accelerated, and Decelerated Length Terms**. Provides information on standard and non-standard length semesters and quarters.
   12. **STEM.** Provides general information on the STEM Scholarship and how to certify.
   13. **Supplemental and Concurrent Enrollments**. Provides information applicable to primary and secondary schools and how to certify guest students.
   14. **Timeliness**. Provides timeframe for timely submission.
   15. **Vacation Periods.** Provides information on when and how to certify vacation periods.
   16. **VBA Remarks and Custom Remarks**. Provides information and proper use of VBA Remarks and Custom Remarks and encourages the use of VBA Remarks over Custom Remarks.
   17. **VET TEC**. Provides general information on VET TEC and how to certify.
   18. **Yellow Ribbon**. Provides general information on the Yellow Ribbon Program and how to certify.

**SUBCHAPTER VIII. USING ENROLLMENT MANAGER TO SELECT STUDENT FILES FOR COMPLIANCE SURVEY REVIEW**

**5.26 Administration**

1. **Administration**. Reserved for future use. Admin functionality is expected in a future release.  Since internal users do not have specific schools associated with their user profile, the admin side of Enrollment Manager allows internal users to generate a list of students for any facility code. Similarly, admin functionality allows internal user to generate reports for any facility code.

 

2. Until admin functionality is built, internal users will need to associate schools with their user profile in order to generate student lists and reports. The following steps outline this interim procedure:

 

1. 1. Logon using Single Sign On Internal (SSOi).

 

1. 2. On the VA Education Platform, click the drop-down by the user name located at the top-right of the screen.

 

1. 3. Select **Edit Facility Codes.**

 

1. 4. Internal users can associate facility codes to their user profile by searching/selecting the desired facility code and selecting **Assign.**

 

1. 5. Check the **Include Extensions** box to associate extension campuses.

 

**Note:** Due to limitations with the number of assigned facilities, internal users will **UnAssign** facilities when not in use.    

**5.27 Student File Selection**

1. **Student List.** The student list is accessed on the **Students** Page. With additional fields that can be filtered, the list displays information including the **First Name**, **Last Name**, **Benefit Type**, and **Student ID.**

 

2. **Filter**. Additional fields displayed in **Filter** include **Enrollment Status,** **Training Type,** **Benefit Type**, **Program**, **Yellow Ribbon**, **STEM**, and **Active Student Only.**

![Student Search Filters](https://www.knowva.ebenefits.va.gov/img/edu/10.77 Student Search Filters.png)

**Figure 5.77 Student Search Filters**

1. 1. **Enrollment Status**. Filter by **Enrollment Status** drop-down (e.g., Not Enrolled, In Progress, Pending SCO Review, Submitted, Under Review, Processed).

 

1. 2. **Training Type**. Filter by **Training Type** drop-down (e.g., IHL, Undergrad, NCD, Grad, OJT, Apprenticeship, Flight, VET TEC). Drop-down options display the training type(s) applicable to the facility.

 

1. 3. **Benefit Type**. Filter by **Benefit Type** drop-down (e.g., Unknown, CH33, VET TEC, 1606, CH30, CH35, CH31).

 

1. 4. **Program**. Filter by **Program** drop-down is populated by the programs listed in Approval Manager (WEAMS in the interim); in addition, non-matriculated and undeclared are available drop-down options for IHLs.

 

1. 5. **Yellow Ribbon**. Filter by **Yellow Ribbon** checkbox.

 

1. 6. **STEM**. Filter by **STEM** checkbox.

 

1. 7. **Active Student Only**. Filter by **Active Student Only** checkbox to display students with an active status. If unchecked, both active and inactive students will display on the list.

 

1. 8. **Date Range Filter**. Reserved for future use. Enhanced capability to filter by date range is expected in a future release.

Potential students with matching data elements are displayed.

 

4. **Student Profile**. When a student is selected, users are brought to the individual’s **Student Profile** where additional information can be viewed. See [Student Profile](#Student Profile) for additional information.

 

5. **Enrollments**. The **Enrollments** page within the **Student Profile** displays the certifications certified to VA.
