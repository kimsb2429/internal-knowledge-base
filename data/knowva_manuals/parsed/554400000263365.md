| **II.6.B.1**  **General** | To ensure that as many beneficiary records as possible are successfully transitioned to eMPWR, several data clean-up projects were completed by VA Central Office (VACO) and RPO staff in the months leading up July 8, 2024.  This section is for information only.  No Veteran Claims Examiner (VCE) action is needed.    a.   **Fry Veteran SSNs**will need to be housed within the DGI system. Without the information being present within the record, DGI BTs sent to eMPWR would fail for all Fry recipients. To avoid this, VA has:  (1)  Requested an update to DGI’s Claimant Biography (BIO) screen to include a **Veteran’s SSN** field. This field is required for all Fry claims processed after July 8, 2024.  (See Topic 9: Work Product Creation Screen (Fry) of M22-4, Part II, 2.D.6-10 for further details).  (2)  A Fry Veteran SSN retrieval project was conducted by the Digital GI Bill (DGIB) Payment Team, in conjunction with the DGIB Procedures team so that existing Fry beneficiaries have the Veteran SSN associated without VCE intervention.  See [Topic 2: Conversion Plan](#_Topic_2:_Conversion) for instructions on resolving any errors.  b.   **Remaining 06H (recurring) BTs will cease** and be replaced by one-time 06I transactions. This will include any remaining NCD programs that have not completed by the date of release, as well as all On-The-Job (OJT) and Apprenticeship program recipients. This is being done for multiple reasons, to include:  (1)  Align these benefits/beneficiary records with the majority of those receiving benefits under Chapter 33.  (2)  eMPWR cannot handle recurring payments.  No VCE or RPO action is needed for this effort.  c.   **BDN suspended master record review and clearance**was conducted to ensure that all payable BTs were sent to BDN prior to the transition.  VACO and RPO staff have coordinated to resolve all suspended payments held in BDN.  d.   **BDN receivable migration to eMPWR** was run by the Office of Financial Management (OFM) to support their Participant Systematic Review (PSR)/due process procedures.  No Education Service efforts were needed.  e.  **Payment address migration to eMPWR** for Chapter 33 beneficiaries was completed by the Office of Integration and Technology (OIT) to ensure that the most updated payment address (either from BDN or the Corporate Database (CorpDB) was active as of the conversion to eMPWR.  f.   **Pending work products in DGI** were minimized by the RPOs prior to the June 26, 2024 blackout period as pending work will need to be recreated after the conversion is complete. |

| **II.6.B.2**  **General** | **eMPWR Conversion** work products, if successfully automated, did not make any adjustments/corrections to a beneficiary’s record, nor released any letters to the beneficiary. The conversion has no impact outside of the redirection from BDN to eMPWR.  The conversion will involve the following steps:  a.   All users will be blocked from accessing DGI from the close of business of June 26, 2024, through approximately July 8, 2024.  b.   An **eMPWR conversion** work product will run, attempting to redirect and authorize all records containing any paused or scheduled payments, or those in a hold status.  c.   The initiative resulted in one of two paths:  (1)    **S****uccessfully Authorized Conversion**                                  The batch conversion encountered no offramps causing  the run to fail, resulting in the  conversion of payment rules  allowing for business transactions to be transmitted to eMPWR.                                       (2)    **Offramped/Incomplete Conversions**                                  (a)  If the conversion batch work product has failed to authorize through automation (caused by a system error OR stop automation flag on the file),                                  (b)  Work product is offramped for review by a special RPO Tiger Team,  i.    Reviews include identifying the reason for the offramp, taking any corrective action, and authorizing the conversion work product.  ii.    If the offramp was due to a **stop automation flag** on file, the VCE must confirm that the stop automation reason is still valid.  iii.    If the offramp was due to pending enrollments or amendments in the Enrollment Manager Inbox, the VCE must decline all pending enrollments or amendments before authorizing the conversion work product.  A second work product may need to be created to work these pending enrollments and amendments following normal procedures.  iv.  If the offramp was due to a system error, see M22-4, Part II, 6.A for instructions.  v.   The eMPWR conversion work product should NOT be deleted as this could cause erroneous payments to be sent to BDN during the review but must be authorized. |
